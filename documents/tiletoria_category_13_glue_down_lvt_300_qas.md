## Glue-Down LVT Basics

Summary: Glue-down Luxury Vinyl Tile is a resilient floor covering that is bonded to the prepared subfloor with a suitable adhesive.

### Q001: What is glue-down LVT?

Answer: Glue-down LVT means glue-down Luxury Vinyl Tile. It is a resilient vinyl floor covering that is fixed directly to a prepared subfloor using the correct adhesive system.

### Q002: What does LVT stand for?

Answer: LVT stands for Luxury Vinyl Tile. It usually refers to vinyl tiles or planks that imitate materials such as wood, stone or concrete.

### Q003: Does Tiletoria sell glue-down LVT?

Answer: Yes. Tiletoria’s vinyl offering is focused on glue-down LVT. Customers should confirm the exact available ranges, colours, stock and accessories with a Tiletoria consultant.

### Q004: Does Tiletoria sell click LVT?

Answer: Tiletoria’s chatbot guidance should state that Tiletoria focuses on glue-down LVT, not click LVT. If this ever changes, the product database must be updated before the chatbot answers differently.

### Q005: How is glue-down LVT installed?

Answer: Glue-down LVT is installed by preparing the subfloor, applying the correct adhesive, placing the vinyl tiles or planks into the adhesive, rolling the floor where required, and allowing the system to cure according to the manufacturer instructions.

### Q006: Is glue-down LVT a floating floor?

Answer: No. Glue-down LVT is bonded to the subfloor. A floating floor is not bonded across the whole floor area.

### Q007: Why do customers choose glue-down LVT?

Answer: Customers often choose glue-down LVT because it can provide a stable, low-profile and design-flexible floor when installed on a properly prepared substrate.

### Q008: Is glue-down LVT suitable for homes?

Answer: Yes, glue-down LVT can be suitable for residential applications when the selected product, adhesive and subfloor preparation are appropriate for the room and expected use.

### Q009: Is glue-down LVT suitable for commercial use?

Answer: Some glue-down LVT products are suitable for commercial use, but the product specification, wear layer, adhesive, subfloor and maintenance plan must match the project requirements.

### Q010: What should a customer confirm before buying glue-down LVT?

Answer: The customer should confirm the room type, subfloor condition, moisture risk, traffic level, required accessories, installation method, adhesive system and whether a qualified installer will perform the work.

## Glue-Down LVT Versus Click LVT

Summary: Glue-down LVT and click LVT are different installation systems, and Tiletoria’s chatbot should consistently guide customers toward glue-down LVT.

### Q011: What is the difference between glue-down LVT and click LVT?

Answer: Glue-down LVT is bonded to the subfloor with adhesive, while click LVT uses a locking system and is usually installed as a floating floor.

### Q012: Why should the chatbot not confuse glue-down LVT with click LVT?

Answer: The products use different installation methods, subfloor requirements, accessories and failure risks. The chatbot must keep the guidance specific to glue-down LVT.

### Q013: Can click LVT advice be used for glue-down LVT?

Answer: No. Click LVT and glue-down LVT have different installation systems, so advice about underlay, locking joints or floating movement may not apply to glue-down LVT.

### Q014: Does glue-down LVT need underlay?

Answer: Glue-down LVT is normally installed directly to a properly prepared subfloor with adhesive, not over a soft underlay. Always follow the product and adhesive instructions.

### Q015: Does glue-down LVT have click joints?

Answer: No. Glue-down LVT does not rely on click joints. The tiles or planks are placed into adhesive and bonded to the subfloor.

### Q016: Is glue-down LVT thinner than click LVT?

Answer: Glue-down LVT is often lower profile than many click products, but thickness depends on the specific product range. Confirm the product data sheet before making design or height decisions.

### Q017: Which is better: glue-down LVT or click LVT?

Answer: Neither system is automatically better. Glue-down LVT can be a strong option where a properly prepared subfloor, correct adhesive and professional installation are available.

### Q018: Can glue-down LVT be repaired more easily than click LVT?

Answer: Individual glue-down LVT tiles or planks may be replaceable by a skilled installer, but repair success depends on product availability, adhesive, subfloor condition and installation method.

### Q019: Why might glue-down LVT be preferred in larger or busier areas?

Answer: Glue-down LVT can feel stable underfoot and avoids reliance on click-lock joints, but the subfloor and adhesive system must be correct for the size and use of the area.

### Q020: How should the chatbot respond if a customer asks for click vinyl?

Answer: The chatbot should explain that Tiletoria focuses on glue-down LVT and offer to help the customer choose a suitable glue-down vinyl option, adhesive and preparation products.

## Areas Where Glue-Down LVT Needs Caution

Summary: Glue-down LVT should not be treated as a universal floor covering for wet, exterior, hot, unstable or poorly prepared areas.

### Q031: Should glue-down LVT be used in outdoor patios?

Answer: No. Glue-down LVT should not generally be used on outdoor patios because sunlight, heat, rain and exterior moisture can affect the floor and adhesive system.

### Q032: Should glue-down LVT be used on balconies?

Answer: Balconies are exterior and moisture-exposed areas, so glue-down LVT should not be recommended unless a manufacturer-approved exterior system exists.

### Q033: Can glue-down LVT be used in garages?

Answer: Garages involve vehicle loads, tyres, heat, chemicals and point loads. Glue-down LVT should only be considered if the product manufacturer approves the application.

### Q034: Can glue-down LVT be used in saunas or steam rooms?

Answer: No. High heat and moisture areas such as saunas and steam rooms are not normal applications for standard glue-down LVT.

### Q035: Can glue-down LVT be used around swimming pools?

Answer: Pool surrounds are wet and usually exterior or semi-exterior areas. Standard glue-down LVT should not be recommended for these conditions.

### Q036: Can glue-down LVT be used in laundries?

Answer: LVT may be suitable in some laundry areas, but appliance leaks, moisture, subfloor condition and edge sealing must be considered before installation.

### Q037: Can glue-down LVT be installed in rooms with direct sun?

Answer: Direct sun and heat can affect some vinyl floors. The product data sheet, window exposure, adhesive type and temperature conditions must be checked.

### Q038: Can glue-down LVT be installed over damp concrete?

Answer: No. Damp concrete can cause adhesive failure, odour, mould, bubbling or debonding. Moisture testing and the correct moisture-control system are important.

### Q039: Can glue-down LVT be installed where the floor is uneven?

Answer: No. An uneven subfloor can telegraph through LVT and cause visible imperfections or bonding problems. The floor should be made smooth and flat before installation.

### Q040: What should the chatbot do for high-risk LVT areas?

Answer: The chatbot should escalate to a Tiletoria consultant when the area involves exterior exposure, standing water, excessive heat, heavy loads, damp substrates or commercial risk.

## Subfloor Requirements for Glue-Down LVT

Summary: A successful glue-down LVT installation depends heavily on a clean, dry, smooth, flat and structurally sound subfloor.

### Q041: What subfloor condition is needed for glue-down LVT?

Answer: The subfloor must be clean, dry, smooth, flat, firm and structurally sound before glue-down LVT is installed.

### Q042: Why is subfloor preparation so important for glue-down LVT?

Answer: Glue-down LVT is relatively thin and can show imperfections from the subfloor. Poor preparation can cause visible marks, lifting, bubbling or adhesive failure.

### Q043: What does clean subfloor mean?

Answer: A clean subfloor is free of dust, oil, grease, paint, wax, old adhesive residue, loose material and other contaminants that can interfere with bonding.

### Q044: What does dry subfloor mean?

Answer: A dry subfloor has acceptable moisture levels for the flooring and adhesive system, based on the manufacturer’s requirements and appropriate moisture testing.

### Q045: What does smooth subfloor mean?

Answer: A smooth subfloor has no ridges, holes, trowel marks, grit, joints or irregularities that could telegraph through the vinyl surface.

### Q046: What does structurally sound subfloor mean?

Answer: A structurally sound subfloor is stable, firm and not loose, cracked, crumbling, flexing or contaminated in a way that could affect the LVT installation.

### Q047: Can glue-down LVT hide floor imperfections?

Answer: No. Glue-down LVT can highlight imperfections because the product follows the surface below it. Subfloor preparation is critical.

### Q048: Can glue-down LVT be installed over rough screed?

Answer: Rough screed usually needs smoothing or levelling before glue-down LVT. A compatible primer and self-levelling compound may be required.

### Q049: Can glue-down LVT be installed over dusty screed?

Answer: No. Dusty screed can weaken the adhesive bond. The surface must be properly cleaned, assessed and primed if required.

### Q050: What add-ons support proper subfloor preparation?

Answer: Primer, self-levelling compound, moisture-control products and the correct adhesive are important add-ons to discuss before installing glue-down LVT.

## Moisture Testing and Moisture Control

Summary: Moisture testing is essential because resilient flooring and adhesives can fail when installed over damp substrates.

### Q061: Why is moisture testing important for glue-down LVT?

Answer: Moisture testing helps confirm whether the subfloor is dry enough for the flooring and adhesive system. Without testing, moisture-related failure risk increases.

### Q062: Who should perform moisture testing?

Answer: A qualified installer, flooring contractor or competent professional should perform moisture testing using appropriate methods and manufacturer requirements.

### Q063: Can a floor look dry but still be too wet for LVT?

Answer: Yes. Concrete can appear dry on the surface while still containing moisture that may affect adhesive and flooring performance.

### Q064: What happens if LVT is installed over a damp slab?

Answer: Possible problems include adhesive failure, bubbles, lifting, odour, mould, discolouration and warranty issues.

### Q065: Does Tiletoria’s chatbot provide exact moisture limits?

Answer: The chatbot should not provide exact limits unless they come from the specific product and adhesive data sheets. Moisture limits vary by system.

### Q066: What is relative humidity testing in concrete?

Answer: Relative humidity testing measures moisture conditions within a concrete slab. It is commonly referenced in resilient flooring standards and manufacturer instructions.

### Q067: What is calcium chloride moisture testing?

Answer: Calcium chloride testing estimates moisture vapour emission from a concrete slab surface. It is one of the methods referenced in resilient flooring guidance.

### Q068: Should moisture testing be recorded?

Answer: Yes. Test results should be recorded before installation because they may be needed for warranty or failure investigations.

### Q069: Can a moisture barrier replace moisture testing?

Answer: No. Moisture testing should be done first to understand the risk and determine whether a moisture-control system is needed.

### Q070: What add-on should be discussed when moisture is a risk?

Answer: A compatible moisture-control primer or moisture barrier system should be discussed with Tiletoria when moisture readings are above the adhesive or flooring limits.

### Q086: How smooth does the floor need to be for LVT?

### Q087: What happens if LVT is installed without levelling a poor floor?

### Q088: Can self-levelling compound be used over old tiles before LVT?

### Q089: What add-on should be suggested for uneven floors?

### Q090: When should levelling questions be escalated?

## Glue-Down LVT Installation Process

Summary: The installation process must follow the product and adhesive instructions, including layout, adhesive timing, placement and rolling.

### Q111: What are the main steps in glue-down LVT installation?

Answer: Typical steps include site checks, subfloor preparation, moisture testing, priming or levelling if needed, layout planning, adhesive application, tile placement, rolling and curing.

### Q112: Can glue-down LVT be installed as a do-it-yourself project?

Answer: Glue-down LVT can be more technical than it looks. A qualified installer is recommended, especially where subfloor preparation, moisture or commercial use is involved.

### Q117: Why is rolling important after installing glue-down LVT?

### Q118: Can people walk on glue-down LVT immediately after installation?

### Q119: When can furniture be placed on new glue-down LVT?
