## Tile Selection by Application

Summary: This section explains how customers should select tiles according to the exact area where the tile will be installed.

### Q001: What should customers know about choosing the correct tile for a room?

Answer: Choose according to the room use, floor or wall position, traffic level, water exposure, indoor or outdoor conditions, cleaning needs, and design preference.

### Q002: What should customers know about the first question before choosing tiles?

Answer: First confirm where the tile will be installed because the application controls floor rating, wet-area suitability, outdoor suitability, and slip-awareness requirements.

### Q003: What should customers know about using one tile throughout a home?

Answer: One tile range can sometimes be used throughout a home if every area is suitable for that product, but bathrooms, patios, ramps, stairs, and garages may need different specifications.

### Q004: What should customers know about tile application?

Answer: Application matters because tiles differ in strength, surface finish, water absorption, slip performance, wear suitability, and installation requirements.

### Q005: What should customers know about living-room tiles?

Answer: A durable floor-rated porcelain or ceramic tile is normally suitable for living rooms, subject to the required look, traffic, finish, and budget.

### Q006: What should customers know about kitchen-floor tiles?

Answer: Kitchen floors usually need a durable, easy-clean, floor-rated tile with a practical finish for spills and daily traffic.

### Q007: What should customers know about bedroom tiles?

Answer: Bedroom floors can use many floor-rated ceramic or porcelain tiles because traffic is usually lower than kitchens or passages.

### Q008: What should customers know about passage tiles?

Answer: Passages need durable floor-rated tiles because foot traffic is concentrated in repeated walking routes.

### Q009: What should customers know about bathroom-floor tiles?

Answer: Bathroom floors should use floor-rated tiles with a practical surface finish for wet conditions; highly polished finishes are normally not preferred.

### Q010: What should customers know about bathroom-wall tiles?

### Q011: What should customers know about shower-floor tiles?

Answer: Shower floors need wet-area suitable tiles, correct falls, drainage, waterproofing, adhesive, grout, and professional installation.

### Q012: What should customers know about patio tiles?

Answer: Patios generally need outdoor-suitable floor tiles with appropriate grip, drainage, and exterior installation materials.

### Q013: What should customers know about balcony tiles?

### Q014: What should customers know about pool-surround tiles?

### Q015: What should customers know about garage tiles?

### Q016: What should customers know about stair tiles?

### Q017: What should customers know about commercial-floor tiles?

Summary: This section explains what porcelain tile is, where it is commonly used, and what customers must confirm before purchase.

### Q041: What should customers know about porcelain tile?

Answer: Porcelain tile is a dense ceramic tile with very low water absorption, commonly used for floors, wet areas, and higher-traffic spaces when suitable.
