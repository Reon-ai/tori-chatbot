# Tiletoria Chatbot Knowledge Base: Category 3 - Tile Finishes

This RAG-ready knowledge document contains 300 customer-support questions and answers about tile finishes. Each section is self-contained, uses clear H2 headings, and keeps answers factual, practical, and suitable for a Tiletoria customer-support chatbot.

Source basis: ceramic and porcelain tile finish terminology; Tile Council of North America guidance on glazed and unglazed porcelain; ANSI A137.1 ceramic tile specification context; manufacturer descriptions of matt, gloss, polished, lappato, satin, honed, textured, structured, and grip finishes; and slip-resistance guidance that treats ratings as test classifications rather than safety guarantees.

Business rules applied:
- Do not describe any tile as completely non-slip.
- Do not guarantee suitability for wet, outdoor, sloped, ramp, pool, or commercial use without product-specific confirmation.
- Treat installation guidance as general information, not a substitute for a qualified installer.
- Confirm exact stock, price, shade, calibre, batch, and product technical data with Tiletoria before purchase or installation.
- Escalate high-risk finish questions to a Tiletoria consultant.

## Tile Finish Selection Fundamentals

Summary: This section explains how tile finish affects appearance, cleaning, slip behaviour, and suitability for different areas.

Question 001: What does tile finish mean?

Answer: Tile finish refers to the final surface appearance and texture of a tile. It affects shine, grip, cleaning, visual style, and where the tile may be suitable for use.

Question 002: Why is tile finish important when choosing tiles?

Answer: Tile finish is important because the same colour and size can perform differently depending on the surface. A polished finish may look formal but show marks and become slippery when wet, while a matt or textured finish may be more practical for many floors.

Question 003: Is tile finish the same as tile colour?

Answer: No. Colour is the visual tone of the tile, while finish is the surface effect such as matt, gloss, polished, satin, lappato, textured, or grip. Both colour and finish should be considered before purchase.

Question 004: Is tile finish the same as tile material?

Answer: No. Material refers to what the tile is made from, such as porcelain or ceramic. Finish refers to the surface treatment or surface effect applied to that material.

Question 005: Can two tiles from the same range have different finishes?

Answer: Yes. Some ranges have matching indoor and outdoor versions or wall and floor versions with different finishes. The smoother version may be intended for indoor use, while the more textured version may be intended for outdoor or wet-use areas.

Question 006: Can a tile finish change the look of a room?

Answer: Yes. Gloss and polished finishes reflect more light and can make a space look brighter. Matt, honed, and textured finishes usually create a softer and more natural appearance.

Question 007: Can a tile finish affect safety?

Answer: Yes. Surface texture and finish can affect slip behaviour, especially when the tile is wet, dusty, oily, sloped, or used outdoors. No tile should be described as completely non-slip.

Question 008: Can a tile finish affect cleaning?

Answer: Yes. Smoother glazed surfaces are often easier to wipe, while very textured or structured surfaces can hold dirt more easily. Polished tiles may show footprints, water marks, and residue more easily than matt tiles.

Question 009: Can a tile finish affect installation?

Answer: Yes. Some finishes need extra care during installation, grouting, cleaning, and protection. Polished, textured, and decorative surfaces may require careful grout haze removal and protection from abrasive dirt.

Question 010: What finish is best for a general indoor floor?

Answer: A matt, satin, or lightly textured floor-rated porcelain tile is usually a practical option for many indoor floors. The best finish still depends on room use, lighting, traffic, cleaning expectations, and wet exposure.

Question 011: What finish is best for a wall tile?

Answer: Gloss, satin, matt, structured, and decorative finishes can all work on walls if the tile is suitable for wall installation. Gloss tiles are common on walls because they reflect light and are easy to wipe.

Question 012: What finish is best for wet floors?

Answer: Wet floors usually need a practical matt, textured, or slip-resistant finish. The correct finish depends on the area, slope, water exposure, barefoot or shoe traffic, drainage, and product specification.

Question 013: What finish should be avoided in risky wet areas?

Answer: Highly polished or very smooth glossy floor finishes should be avoided in areas where wet slip risk is a concern, unless the product is specifically suitable for that application and the full site conditions have been assessed.

Question 014: Should customers choose finish from a small sample only?

Answer: A small sample helps show colour and surface feel, but it may not show full variation, shine under lighting, or how the finish behaves across a large area. Viewing multiple pieces or a display is recommended where possible.

Question 015: When should Tiletoria escalate a finish question to a consultant?

Answer: Escalate when the customer asks about ramps, pool surrounds, commercial floors, shower floors, outdoor areas, slip guarantees, cleaning complaints, or whether a specific tile is safe for a regulated area.

## Matt Tile Finishes

Summary: This section explains matt tile finishes, including their typical appearance, advantages, and limitations.

Question 016: What is a matt tile finish?

Answer: A matt tile finish has low surface shine and a softer, less reflective appearance. It is commonly used for floors and modern interior designs.

Question 017: Are matt tiles good for floors?

Answer: Yes. Matt tiles are often practical for floors because they reduce glare and may show fewer smudges than polished tiles. The tile must still be floor-rated and suitable for the intended area.

Question 018: Are matt tiles always slip-resistant?

Answer: No. Matt does not automatically mean slip-resistant. Slip behaviour depends on surface texture, testing, water, slope, cleaning, footwear, and site conditions.

Question 019: Are matt tiles easier to maintain than polished tiles?

Answer: Matt tiles often show fewer footprints and smudges than polished tiles, but they still require correct cleaning. Very rough matt tiles may hold dirt more easily than smooth matt tiles.

Question 020: Can matt tiles be used in bathrooms?

Answer: Yes. Matt floor-rated tiles are commonly used in bathrooms. For shower floors, ramps, or commercial bathrooms, the exact product suitability must be confirmed.

Question 021: Can matt tiles be used outdoors?

Answer: Only if the specific tile is suitable for outdoor use. An indoor matt finish may not have enough texture or weather suitability for an external area.

Question 022: Do matt tiles make a room look dull?

Answer: Not necessarily. Matt tiles can create a calm, natural, and modern look. Lighting, grout colour, tile size, and surrounding finishes strongly affect the final result.

Question 023: Are matt tiles suitable for kitchens?

Answer: Yes. Matt tiles are often practical for kitchens because they reduce glare and can hide minor marks better than polished finishes. They must still be easy enough to clean for the household's needs.

Question 024: Can matt wall tiles be used in showers?

Answer: Yes, if the tile is suitable for shower walls and the installation includes correct waterproofing behind the tile. The tile surface does not replace waterproofing.

Question 025: Do matt tiles need sealing?

Answer: Most glazed ceramic and glazed porcelain matt tiles do not require sealing. Some unglazed, porous, or special-effect surfaces may require manufacturer-specific care.

Question 026: Can a matt tile stain?

Answer: A properly made glazed matt tile is generally resistant to normal household staining, but no surface should be left with aggressive chemicals, oils, cement residue, or dirty water for long periods.

Question 027: Can matt tiles scratch?

Answer: Any tile surface can be scratched by abrasive dirt, sand, dragging furniture, or hard objects. Use entrance mats, felt pads, and correct cleaning to reduce surface damage.

Question 028: What is the best grout colour for matt tiles?

Answer: The best grout colour depends on the design. Similar grout colours create a seamless look, while contrasting grout highlights the tile layout.

Question 029: Do matt tiles show water marks?

Answer: They can, especially dark matt tiles or tiles cleaned with dirty water or incorrect chemicals. Use clean water, suitable detergent, and proper rinsing.

Question 030: What should the chatbot say if a customer wants matt tiles because they think matt means non-slip?

Answer: The chatbot should explain that matt tiles may be more practical than polished tiles in many areas, but matt does not guarantee slip resistance. Product-specific slip suitability must be confirmed for wet, outdoor, sloped, or commercial areas.

## Gloss Tile Finishes

Summary: This section explains gloss tile finishes and where they are typically suitable.

Question 031: What is a gloss tile finish?

Answer: A gloss tile finish has a shiny, reflective surface. It is common on wall tiles and decorative ceramic tiles.

Question 032: Are gloss tiles good for walls?

Answer: Yes. Gloss tiles are very common on walls because they reflect light, are easy to wipe, and can make bathrooms and kitchens feel brighter.

Question 033: Are gloss tiles good for floors?

Answer: Only if the tile is specifically floor-rated and suitable for the area. Glossy floor surfaces can be slippery when wet and may show marks more easily.

Question 034: Can gloss tiles be used in bathrooms?

Answer: Gloss tiles are commonly used on bathroom walls. For bathroom floors, a gloss finish must be selected carefully because wet slip risk may be higher than with more practical floor finishes.

Question 035: Can gloss tiles be used in showers?

Answer: Gloss tiles can be used on shower walls if suitable, but shower floors normally need a more practical floor finish with appropriate wet-use performance.

Question 036: Do gloss tiles make a room look bigger?

Answer: Gloss tiles can reflect light and may make a space feel brighter and visually larger. This effect depends on lighting, colour, tile size, and room layout.

Question 037: Do gloss tiles show marks?

Answer: Gloss tiles can show water spots, fingerprints, soap residue, and cleaning streaks more than some matt finishes. Regular cleaning and proper rinsing help reduce visible marks.

Question 038: Are gloss tiles easy to clean?

Answer: Gloss glazed tiles are generally easy to wipe clean on walls. On floors, cleaning may be more demanding because smudges and footprints can be more visible.

Question 039: Can gloss tiles scratch?

Answer: Glossy surfaces can show scratches more visibly than some matt surfaces. Abrasive cleaning pads, sand, and dragging objects should be avoided.

Question 040: Do gloss tiles need sealing?

Answer: Most glazed gloss ceramic and porcelain tiles do not need sealing. Grout and certain special surfaces may require separate care.

Question 041: Can gloss tiles be used behind a stove?

Answer: Gloss wall tiles are commonly used as kitchen splashbacks if they are suitable for wall use and installed correctly. Heat, cleaning chemicals, and grout maintenance should be considered.

Question 042: Are gloss tiles suitable for commercial bathrooms?

Answer: Gloss wall tiles may be suitable for commercial bathroom walls. Gloss floor tiles should be assessed very carefully because wet slip risk and maintenance requirements are higher.

Question 043: What is the difference between gloss and polished?

Answer: Gloss usually refers to a shiny glazed surface. Polished usually refers to a mechanically polished porcelain surface or polished stone-like surface. The terms are sometimes used loosely, so the product specification should be checked.

Question 044: What is the main risk with gloss floor tiles?

Answer: The main risk is wet slip behaviour, especially in bathrooms, entrances, kitchens, and public areas. The tile must be suitable for the floor and site conditions.

Question 045: What should the chatbot say if a customer asks for glossy bathroom floors?

Answer: The chatbot should explain that glossy bathroom floors can look attractive but may be slippery when wet and may show marks. A matt or slip-resistant floor-rated option is usually more practical for wet areas.

## Polished Porcelain Tile Finishes

Summary: This section explains polished porcelain finishes and the extra selection and maintenance care they may require.

Question 046: What is a polished porcelain tile?

Answer: A polished porcelain tile has a highly reflective surface created by mechanical polishing or a polished surface treatment. It creates a formal, luxury appearance.

Question 047: Is polished porcelain the same as gloss ceramic?

Answer: No. Gloss ceramic usually has a shiny glaze, while polished porcelain is commonly polished mechanically or manufactured to give a high-lustre surface. The product specification should be checked.

Question 048: Where are polished porcelain tiles commonly used?

Answer: Polished porcelain tiles are commonly used in living rooms, reception areas, bedrooms, feature walls, and formal indoor spaces where a reflective luxury look is desired.

Question 049: Are polished porcelain tiles suitable for bathrooms?

Answer: Polished tiles may be used on bathroom walls, but polished floor tiles should be selected carefully because they can become slippery when wet and may show water marks.

Question 050: Are polished tiles suitable for outdoor areas?

Answer: Polished tiles are generally not the preferred choice for outdoor floors because of wet slip risk, glare, and exposure conditions. Use an outdoor-suitable finish where required.

Question 051: Do polished tiles show footprints?

Answer: Yes. Polished tiles can show footprints, smudges, dust, water marks, and cleaning residue more easily than matt tiles.

Question 052: Are polished tiles easy to clean?

Answer: Polished tiles can be easy to wipe, but they often require more frequent cleaning to keep the surface looking clean because marks are more visible.

Question 053: Can polished porcelain stain?

Answer: Some polished porcelain surfaces may be more vulnerable to staining or grout haze because polishing can open microscopic pores. Follow the manufacturer's cleaning and protection instructions.

Question 054: Do polished porcelain tiles need sealing?

Answer: Some polished porcelain tiles may benefit from or require a protective treatment before grouting or after installation, depending on the product. Always follow the manufacturer or supplier guidance.

Question 055: Can polished tiles scratch?

Answer: Yes. Polished surfaces can show scratches and abrasion more visibly. Avoid dragging furniture, abrasive cleaners, construction dirt, and sharp grit.

Question 056: Can polished porcelain be used in commercial areas?

Answer: It may be suitable in selected dry commercial interiors, but traffic, slip risk, maintenance, cleaning, and product specification must be assessed. It is not automatically suitable for every commercial floor.

Question 057: What is the best grout practice for polished tiles?

Answer: Use suitable grout, protect the surface where required, clean grout haze promptly and gently, and avoid abrasive pads. The installer should follow the product and grout manufacturer's instructions.

Question 058: Are polished tiles cold underfoot?

Answer: Tile surfaces can feel cool underfoot regardless of finish. Underfloor heating compatibility depends on the tile, adhesive, substrate, and heating system.

Question 059: Do polished tiles reflect light?

Answer: Yes. Polished tiles reflect more light than matt surfaces, which can make a room look brighter but can also create glare in strong sunlight.

Question 060: What should the chatbot say if a customer wants polished tiles in a wet area?

Answer: The chatbot should explain that polished tiles can be slippery when wet and may require more maintenance. The customer should confirm product suitability with Tiletoria before using polished tiles on wet floors.

## Lappato and Semi-Polished Tile Finishes

Summary: This section explains lappato and semi-polished finishes, which sit between matt and polished looks.

Question 061: What does lappato mean in tiles?

Answer: Lappato means a semi-polished surface that has some shine but is not usually as reflective as a fully polished tile. It often creates a soft, modern sheen.

Question 062: Is lappato the same as semi-polished?

Answer: Yes, lappato is commonly used to describe a semi-polished or partly polished surface. Terminology may vary by manufacturer.

Question 063: Is lappato more practical than polished?

Answer: Lappato can be more subtle than full polished porcelain, but it still needs careful selection for wet or high-traffic areas. It may show marks depending on colour and surface texture.

Question 064: Can lappato tiles be used on floors?

Answer: Yes, if the tile is floor-rated and suitable for the area. Wet floors, outdoor spaces, and commercial areas require product-specific confirmation.

Question 065: Can lappato tiles be used in bathrooms?

Answer: Lappato tiles may be suitable for bathroom walls and selected dry bathroom floors, but wet floor areas must be assessed carefully for slip behaviour.

Question 066: Do lappato tiles show marks?

Answer: They can show footprints, smudges, or cleaning residue, especially darker or smoother lappato surfaces. Regular correct cleaning is important.

Question 067: Are lappato tiles slippery?

Answer: They may be more slippery than matt or textured finishes when wet, depending on the product. Lappato should not be assumed suitable for wet or outdoor floors.

Question 068: Do lappato tiles need sealing?

Answer: Most glazed lappato tiles do not require sealing, but some semi-polished porcelain products may need special grout or cleaning care. Follow supplier guidance.

Question 069: What is the design benefit of lappato tiles?

Answer: Lappato tiles provide a soft sheen that can highlight pattern and texture without the full mirror-like reflection of polished tiles.

Question 070: Can lappato tiles be used in kitchens?

Answer: They may be suitable for kitchens if floor-rated and practical for cleaning. Spills, grease, footwear, and cleaning routine should be considered.

Question 071: Are lappato tiles suitable for commercial use?

Answer: Only if the product specification supports the commercial application and slip, cleaning, and maintenance requirements are acceptable.

Question 072: What is the difference between lappato and satin?

Answer: Lappato is generally partly polished and slightly reflective. Satin is usually smoother and softer than matt but less reflective than polished. Manufacturer terminology can vary.

Question 073: Can lappato finishes have texture?

Answer: Yes. Some lappato tiles combine subtle surface texture with partial polishing, which can highlight parts of the pattern.

Question 074: What should customers check before buying lappato tiles?

Answer: Customers should check area suitability, wet exposure, cleaning expectations, batch, shade, slip information where relevant, and whether any installation protection is required.

Question 075: What should the chatbot say if a customer asks whether lappato is non-slip?

Answer: The chatbot should say no finish is automatically non-slip. Lappato can be attractive but must be checked for wet, sloped, outdoor, or commercial use.

## Satin, Honed, and Natural Tile Finishes

Summary: This section explains softer low-sheen finishes often used for natural, modern, or stone-look designs.

Question 076: What is a satin tile finish?

Answer: A satin finish has a soft, low-to-medium sheen. It is smoother than many matt finishes but less reflective than polished or gloss finishes.

Question 077: What is a honed tile finish?

Answer: A honed finish has a smooth, low-sheen surface, often used to imitate honed natural stone. It gives a refined but not highly reflective look.

Question 078: What is a natural tile finish?

Answer: A natural finish usually refers to a surface intended to look and feel close to the material being represented, such as stone, cement, or wood. The exact meaning depends on the manufacturer.

Question 079: Is satin the same as matt?

Answer: No. Satin usually has slightly more sheen and a smoother visual effect than matt. However, manufacturer descriptions may vary.

Question 080: Is honed the same as polished?

Answer: No. Honed is smooth but low-sheen, while polished is highly reflective. Honed finishes usually create a softer and more natural appearance.

Question 081: Are satin tiles suitable for floors?

Answer: Yes, if they are floor-rated and suitable for the area. Wet or commercial applications need product-specific checks.

Question 082: Are honed finishes good for stone-look tiles?

Answer: Yes. Honed finishes work well with stone-look designs because they create a refined natural appearance without a high-gloss shine.

Question 083: Are natural finishes easy to clean?

Answer: Many natural-look porcelain finishes are practical, but cleaning depends on texture, glaze, colour, and surface structure. Very textured natural finishes may need more careful cleaning.

Question 084: Can satin tiles be slippery?

Answer: Yes, any smooth finish can become slippery when wet. Do not assume satin is suitable for wet or outdoor floors without confirming the product specification.

Question 085: Can honed tiles be used in bathrooms?

Answer: Honed-look tiles may be used in bathrooms if suitable for the area. Shower floors and wet-use floors require more careful slip and installation assessment.

Question 086: Do honed tiles need sealing?

Answer: Most honed-look porcelain or ceramic tiles do not need sealing if they are glazed or dense. Natural stone honed tiles may need sealing, but that is a different product category.

Question 087: Can satin finishes show scratches?

Answer: Smooth satin finishes can show abrasion, especially in strong light or on darker colours. Avoid abrasive dirt and dragging furniture.

Question 088: Do natural finishes vary from tile to tile?

Answer: Yes. Natural-look tiles often use multiple faces and shade variation to imitate stone, cement, or wood. Mixing tiles from several boxes during installation helps blend the variation.

Question 089: What is the main benefit of a low-sheen finish?

Answer: Low-sheen finishes reduce glare and create a softer visual appearance. They are often more forgiving than high-polish surfaces in everyday homes.

Question 090: What should the chatbot say if a customer asks for a natural finish?

Answer: The chatbot should ask or infer whether the customer wants a stone-look, cement-look, wood-look, or low-sheen surface, then recommend checking suitability for the exact room and wet exposure.

## Textured, Structured, and Riven Tile Finishes

Summary: This section explains textured finishes used for grip, realism, and tactile surface effects.

Question 091: What is a textured tile finish?

Answer: A textured finish has a surface that is not completely smooth. Texture can improve realism, create grip, or add a decorative tactile effect.

Question 092: What is a structured tile finish?

Answer: A structured finish has a deliberately shaped or raised surface pattern. It may imitate stone, slate, cement, wood grain, or other natural materials.

Question 093: What is a riven tile finish?

Answer: A riven finish imitates the uneven layered surface of natural split stone or slate. It is often used for rustic or outdoor-style designs.

Question 094: Are textured tiles always slip-resistant?

Answer: No. Texture can help with grip, but slip performance must be based on product information and site conditions. No textured tile should be described as completely non-slip.

Question 095: Are textured tiles harder to clean?

Answer: They can be. Deeper texture can hold dirt, grout haze, soap residue, or outdoor grime more than smooth surfaces. Correct cleaning tools and detergents are important.

Question 096: Can textured tiles be used indoors?

Answer: Yes. Lightly textured tiles can be used indoors if suitable for the room and cleaning expectations. Very rough textures may be less comfortable or harder to maintain indoors.

Question 097: Can textured tiles be used outdoors?

Answer: Some textured tiles are suitable for outdoor use, but the specific product must be rated or recommended for the application. Outdoor use also depends on drainage, slope, and installation.

Question 098: Are structured tiles good for feature walls?

Answer: Yes. Structured wall tiles can create depth and visual interest. They must be installed with suitable adhesive and proper planning, especially if the surface is uneven.

Question 099: Can structured tiles be used on shower walls?

Answer: They may be suitable if the tile is appropriate for shower walls and waterproofing is correctly installed. More texture may require more cleaning because soap residue can collect in the surface.

Question 100: Can riven tiles be used on floors?

Answer: Yes, if the tile is floor-rated and practical for the intended space. Very uneven riven surfaces may be uncomfortable, harder to clean, or unsuitable for some furniture or wheeled movement.

Question 101: Can textured tiles damage mop heads?

Answer: Very rough or abrasive textures may wear mop heads faster and may need a brush or suitable cleaning pad. Avoid abrasive tools that damage the tile surface.

Question 102: Do textured tiles hide dirt?

Answer: They may hide some dust visually, but they can also trap dirt in the texture. Routine cleaning is still important.

Question 103: Do textured tiles need wider grout joints?

Answer: Grout joint width depends on tile size, edge type, manufacturing tolerance, and installation method, not only surface texture. The installer should follow standards and product guidance.

Question 104: Can a levelling system be used with textured tiles?

Answer: A wedge-and-clip levelling system may assist with some floor installations, but it does not fix poor substrate preparation and may not suit every textured edge or tile type.

Question 105: What should the chatbot say if a customer wants textured tiles because they have children?

Answer: The chatbot should explain that textured finishes may be more practical in some areas, but the exact product must still suit the room, cleaning routine, and wet-slip requirements.

## Grip and Slip-Resistant Tile Finishes

Summary: This section explains grip finishes and how to avoid unsafe guarantees in chatbot answers.

Question 106: What is a grip tile finish?

Answer: A grip finish is a surface designed to provide more traction than a smooth finish. It is often used for outdoor, wet, or higher-risk floor areas.

Question 107: Is grip the same as non-slip?

Answer: No. Grip means the surface is designed to improve traction, but no tile is completely non-slip in all conditions.

Question 108: Can Tiletoria call a tile non-slip?

Answer: The chatbot should avoid calling any tile non-slip. It should use safer wording such as slip-resistant, grip finish, or suitable for certain applications subject to product specification and site conditions.

Question 109: What affects slip behaviour besides the tile finish?

Answer: Slip behaviour is affected by water, soap, oil, dust, slope, drainage, footwear, barefoot use, cleaning, maintenance, and the installation environment.

Question 110: Are grip tiles harder to clean?

Answer: They can be harder to clean than smooth tiles because the textured surface may hold dirt. Correct cleaning methods are important.

Question 111: Can grip tiles be used indoors?

Answer: Yes, if the customer accepts the texture and cleaning requirements. Grip finishes are more common in wet or outdoor areas than formal indoor living spaces.

Question 112: Can grip tiles be used in showers?

Answer: Some grip or textured tiles may be suitable for shower floors, but shower floors require product-specific confirmation, correct falls, waterproofing, drainage, adhesive, and grout.

Question 113: Can grip tiles be used around pools?

Answer: Some grip tiles may be suitable for pool surrounds, but pool areas require careful product selection because water, barefoot use, slope, and cleaning chemicals affect safety and durability.

Question 114: Can grip tiles be used on ramps?

Answer: Ramp applications should always be escalated. Slip resistance, slope, drainage, regulations, cleaning, exposure, and user traffic must be assessed.

Question 115: What does R-rating mean on tile finishes?

Answer: R-rating usually refers to a ramp test classification for shod-foot slip resistance. It is a test category, not a guarantee that a tile will be safe in every real-world condition.

Question 116: Does a higher R-rating mean the tile is always safer?

Answer: A higher R-rating can indicate higher slip resistance in that test method, but it may also mean a rougher surface that is harder to clean. The right rating depends on the application.

Question 117: Is R11 suitable for all outdoor areas?

Answer: No. R11 may be suitable for many outdoor applications, but suitability still depends on product, slope, drainage, contamination, cleaning, and project requirements.

Question 118: What finish is safer for wet commercial floors?

Answer: A suitable slip-resistant or grip finish is usually required, but commercial wet floors must be assessed according to product data, site conditions, and applicable standards or project specifications.

Question 119: What should the chatbot say if a customer asks for the safest tile?

Answer: The chatbot should explain that safety depends on the exact area, wet exposure, slope, cleaning, and user traffic. It should recommend confirming the specific product with Tiletoria before purchase.

Question 120: What should the chatbot do if a customer asks whether a tile is guaranteed not to slip?

Answer: The chatbot must not guarantee slip prevention. It should explain that slip-resistant tiles reduce risk under certain conditions but cannot eliminate all slip risk.

## Outdoor Tile Finishes

Summary: This section explains finish selection for exterior floors, patios, balconies, and exposed areas.

Question 121: What finish is best for outdoor tiles?

Answer: Outdoor tiles usually need a textured, grip, or slip-resistant finish that is suitable for external conditions. The exact finish depends on exposure, slope, drainage, and use.

Question 122: Can matt indoor tiles be used outdoors?

Answer: Not automatically. An indoor matt tile may not have the right slip resistance, weather suitability, or surface texture for outdoor use.

Question 123: Can polished tiles be used outdoors?

Answer: Polished tiles are generally not recommended for outdoor floors because wet slip risk, glare, and exposure conditions can be problematic.

Question 124: Can gloss tiles be used outdoors?

Answer: Gloss tiles are generally not preferred for outdoor floors because they can become slippery when wet and may show wear or marks. Use an outdoor-suitable product.

Question 125: Are textured outdoor tiles difficult to clean?

Answer: They may require more cleaning than smooth indoor tiles because dirt can settle into the texture. Outdoor cleaning should suit the tile and avoid damaging chemicals.

Question 126: What finish is best for covered patios?

Answer: A covered patio may still get wind-driven rain, dust, and outdoor dirt. A suitable matt, textured, or slip-resistant outdoor-rated tile is usually more practical than a polished finish.

Question 127: What finish is best for uncovered patios?

Answer: Uncovered patios require an outdoor-suitable surface with appropriate grip and weather performance. Drainage, falls, and correct installation are essential.

Question 128: What finish is best for balconies?

Answer: Balconies require an exterior-suitable finish, correct waterproofing, drainage, falls, and movement joints. Product suitability must be confirmed before installation.

Question 129: What finish is best for outdoor stairs?

Answer: Outdoor stairs need careful slip-resistant product selection, visible edges, correct falls, and safe installation. Escalate stair-specific recommendations to a consultant.

Question 130: What finish is best for braai areas?

Answer: Braai areas need practical cleaning and slip behaviour. A matt or textured outdoor-suitable tile may be appropriate, but grease, soot, heat exposure, and maintenance should be considered.

Question 131: Can outdoor finishes be used indoors?

Answer: Yes, but they may feel rougher and may be harder to clean indoors. Customers should view and feel the finish before choosing it for internal spaces.

Question 132: Do outdoor tiles need to be frost-resistant in South Africa?

Answer: Frost resistance is less critical in many South African regions than in colder climates, but exterior suitability still matters. Product specification and local site conditions should be checked.

Question 133: What is the main installation risk with outdoor finishes?

Answer: The main risks include poor falls, poor drainage, incorrect adhesive, missing movement joints, inadequate substrate preparation, and unsuitable tile selection.

Question 134: Can outdoor tile finish prevent algae growth?

Answer: No tile finish can prevent algae or organic build-up completely. Outdoor areas require proper drainage, sunlight consideration, and regular cleaning.

Question 135: What should the chatbot say about outdoor finish selection?

Answer: The chatbot should say outdoor tile selection must be confirmed against the product specification, exposure, wet conditions, slope, drainage, cleaning plan, and installation method.

Summary: This section explains tile finish choices for bathrooms, showers, splash zones, and wet barefoot areas.

Question 136: What finish is best for bathroom floors?

Answer: A matt, lightly textured, or slip-resistant floor-rated tile is usually more practical for bathroom floors than a polished or very glossy finish.

Question 137: What finish is best for bathroom walls?

Answer: Gloss, satin, matt, structured, and decorative finishes can all work on bathroom walls if the tile is suitable. Cleaning expectations and design preference should guide the choice.

Question 138: What finish is best for shower floors?

Answer: Shower floors normally need a wet-area-suitable surface with appropriate grip, correct falls, waterproofing, drainage, adhesive, grout, and installation quality. Escalate specific product recommendations.

Question 139: Can polished tiles be used on shower walls?

Answer: Polished tiles may be used on shower walls if suitable, but they may show water marks and soap residue. Cleaning expectations should be discussed.

Question 140: Can polished tiles be used on shower floors?

Answer: Polished tiles are generally not recommended for shower floors because wet slip risk is high. Use a suitable wet-area floor finish.

Question 141: Can gloss tiles be used on bathroom walls?

Answer: Yes. Gloss wall tiles are common in bathrooms because they reflect light and are easy to wipe.

Question 142: Can gloss tiles be used on bathroom floors?

Answer: Only if specifically floor-rated and suitable, but gloss floor tiles may be slippery when wet and may show marks. A matt or textured finish is usually more practical.

Question 143: Are textured shower wall tiles hard to clean?

Answer: They can be harder to clean than smooth tiles because soap residue and minerals can collect in the texture. Proper cleaning is important.

Question 144: Do bathroom tiles need waterproofing behind them?

## Cleaning and Maintenance by Tile Finish

Summary: This section explains how different finishes affect day-to-day cleaning and maintenance.

Question 181: Does tile finish affect cleaning?

Answer: Yes. Smooth glazed surfaces are usually easier to wipe, while textured, polished, and dark surfaces may require more careful maintenance.

Question 182: Which finish is easiest to clean?

Answer: A smooth glazed matt, satin, or gloss wall surface is usually easier to clean than deep textured or highly polished floor surfaces. The specific product still matters.

Question 183: Are polished tiles high maintenance?

Answer: They can be higher maintenance because they show footprints, water marks, smudges, and scratches more easily than many matt finishes.

Question 184: Are textured tiles high maintenance?

Answer: They can require more effort because dirt and grout residue can settle into the texture. Regular cleaning with suitable tools is important.

Question 185: Are gloss wall tiles easy to clean?

Answer: Yes. Gloss glazed wall tiles are generally easy to wipe, especially in bathrooms and kitchens, but water spots and streaks may still show.

Question 186: Are matt tiles easy to clean?

Answer: Most matt glazed tiles are easy to maintain with correct cleaning, but rough or micro-textured matt surfaces may need more attention.

Question 187: What cleaning products should be avoided on tile finishes?

Answer: Avoid harsh acids, abrasive powders, waxes, oils, and products not recommended for ceramic or porcelain tiles unless the manufacturer approves them.

Question 188: Can bleach damage tile finishes?

Answer: Occasional diluted use may be tolerated by some tiles, but harsh or repeated chemical use can affect grout, sealers, trims, or certain decorative surfaces. Follow supplier guidance.

Question 189: Can vinegar be used on tiles?

Answer: Vinegar is acidic and may damage grout, natural stone, metal trims, or certain finishes. Use a suitable neutral tile cleaner unless guidance confirms otherwise.

Question 190: Can abrasive pads be used on tile finishes?

Answer: Abrasive pads should be avoided on polished, gloss, metallic, decorative, or delicate surfaces because they can scratch or dull the finish.

Question 191: Why do tiles look cloudy after cleaning?

Answer: Cloudiness may be caused by detergent residue, grout haze, dirty water, hard-water deposits, or incorrect cleaning products. Rinse with clean water and use suitable cleaners.

Question 192: Why do polished tiles show streaks?

Answer: Polished surfaces reflect light and can show detergent residue or dirty water streaks more easily. Use clean water, correct dilution, and dry buffing where appropriate.

Question 193: Why do textured tiles still look dirty after mopping?

Answer: Dirt may remain in the surface texture if the mop only passes over the high points. A suitable brush, microfiber mop, or cleaner may be required.

Question 194: Do tiles need waxing?

Answer: Ceramic and porcelain tiles generally should not be waxed unless the manufacturer specifically recommends it. Wax can create residue and slip risk.

Question 195: What should the chatbot say about cleaning complaints?

## Grout Haze, Sealing, and Surface Protection

Summary: This section explains finish-related issues during installation, grouting, and initial cleaning.

Question 196: What is grout haze?

Answer: Grout haze is a thin residue left on the tile surface after grouting. It must be cleaned correctly and promptly according to grout and tile instructions.

Question 197: Do some finishes hold grout haze more than others?

Answer: Yes. Textured, structured, polished, and porous-looking surfaces can hold grout haze more visibly or more stubbornly than smooth glazed surfaces.

Question 198: Can grout haze damage tile appearance?

Answer: Yes. If left too long or cleaned incorrectly, grout haze can dull the surface or make the tile look dirty. Correct initial cleaning is important.

Question 199: Should polished tiles be protected before grouting?

Answer: Some polished porcelain tiles may need protection or pre-treatment before grouting, depending on the product. Follow supplier or manufacturer instructions.

Question 200: Do textured tiles need extra cleaning after grouting?

Answer: Yes. Textured surfaces may trap grout residue in low points. The installer must clean carefully before residue hardens.

Question 201: Can dark grout stain light tile finishes?

Answer: Dark grout can sometimes leave pigment residue on textured, polished, porous, or light-coloured surfaces. A test area and correct cleaning are recommended.

Question 202: Can light grout affect dark textured tiles?

Answer: Yes. Light grout residue can remain visible in texture. Correct grouting and cleaning technique are important.

Question 203: Do glazed tiles need sealing?

Answer: Most glazed ceramic and porcelain tiles do not need sealing. Grout may still benefit from protection depending on the installation and maintenance plan.

Question 204: Do unglazed tiles need sealing?

Answer: Some unglazed tiles may require sealing or special care depending on porosity, surface, and manufacturer guidance.

Question 205: Does sealing make a tile non-slip?

Question 206: Can a sealer change the appearance of a finish?

Question 207: Should tiles be cleaned before sealing grout?

Question 208: Can construction dirt scratch tile finishes?

Question 209: Who is responsible for removing grout haze?

Question 210: What should the chatbot say about sealing a tile finish?
