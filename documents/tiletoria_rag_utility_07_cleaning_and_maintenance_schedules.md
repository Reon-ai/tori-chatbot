# Tiletoria RAG Utility File 07: Cleaning and Maintenance Schedules

This RAG-ready file helps the chatbot give practical cleaning and maintenance guidance for South African consumers using tiles, grout, glue-down Luxury Vinyl Tile, sanitaryware, tapware, showers, patios, braai rooms, pool surrounds and bathroom products.

Source basis: TCNA guidance that grout joints are not waterproof and wet-area performance depends on the full installation method; GROHE official cleaning guidance for tapware; Polyflor official Luxury Vinyl Tile cleaning guidance; MAPEI tile, grout and stone care product guidance; and practical South African maintenance realities including dust, sand, coastal moisture, algae, hard water marks, braai grease and humid bathrooms.

Important chatbot rule: The chatbot must not recommend harsh chemicals, acids, bleach, abrasive pads, steel wool, steam cleaning or home remedies unless the product manufacturer specifically approves them for that surface. Cleaning advice must always be matched to the surface, finish and product data sheet.

## Master Cleaning and Maintenance Rule

Summary: The safest general maintenance approach is regular dry cleaning, correct mild cleaner, clean water, soft tools and prompt spill removal.

Core rule: Match the cleaner to the surface.

Safe general method:
Sweep or vacuum loose grit.
Use a clean mop or soft cloth.
Use a neutral or manufacturer-approved cleaner.
Dilute cleaner correctly.
Do not flood the floor unnecessarily.
Rinse if required by the cleaner.
Dry where water marks are a concern.
Protect floors from grit, furniture dragging and harsh chemicals.

Avoid:
Steel wool.
Abrasive powders.
Harsh acids.
Bleach on finishes.
Solvent cleaners.
Unapproved limescale removers.
Steam cleaners on glue-down LVT.
Dirty mop water.
Using toilet cleaner on basins, baths, taps or tiles.
Leaving cleaner to dry on the surface.

Chatbot answer: “Use the mildest suitable cleaner first. Harsh chemicals can damage grout, tap finishes, vinyl, baths, silicone and polished surfaces.”

Escalation rule: Escalate chemical damage, unknown stains, natural stone, special tap finishes, polished tiles, LVT damage and warranty claims.

## Daily Tile Floor Maintenance

Summary: Daily tile care focuses on removing grit and loose dirt before it scratches, dulls or makes floors slippery.

Recommended daily actions:
Sweep loose grit.
Vacuum with a suitable hard-floor attachment.
Wipe spills quickly.
Use entrance mats where sand or grit enters the home.
Do not drag furniture.
Remove wet footprints in high-risk areas.
Keep outdoor dirt from being walked inside.

For South African homes:
Beach sand can act like abrasive grit.
Braai ash can scratch or mark light tiles.
Red soil and construction dust can stain grout if left.
Pets can bring in grit and moisture.

Chatbot answer: “For everyday tile care, remove loose grit first. Grit and sand can scratch, dull or make floors feel dirty even after mopping.”

Escalation rule: Escalate if the customer says the floor is slippery, scratched, cloudy or stained after cleaning.

Furniture polish.

Chatbot answer: “If a tile floor still looks dirty after mopping, the cause is often dirty mop water, too much detergent or residue left on the surface.”

Escalation rule: Escalate cloudy polished floors, persistent residue, unknown stains and textured tile cleaning difficulty.

## First Clean After Tile Installation

Summary: The first clean after installation is critical because grout haze and construction residue are easier to remove early.

First-clean steps:
Allow grout to cure according to product instructions.
Remove loose dust and debris.
Use the correct cleaner for grout type and tile finish.
Use grout haze remover only if compatible.
Rinse thoroughly where required.
Avoid harsh acids on sensitive surfaces.
Avoid abrasive pads on polished or coated surfaces.
Protect tapware, baths and sanitaryware from cleaning chemicals.
Repeat gentle cleaning rather than using aggressive chemicals.

Important warning:
Grout haze is easier to manage early. If left too long, it can become more difficult to remove.

Chatbot answer: “After installation, ask your installer what grout was used and which cleaner is safe for the tile finish before removing grout haze.”

Escalation rule: Escalate epoxy haze, polished tile haze, natural stone, textured tile residue and chemical damage.

## Silicone Maintenance

Summary: Silicone is a flexible joint material and maintenance item, especially around baths, showers, basins and screens.

Routine checks:
Inspect silicone around baths.
Inspect shower corners.
Inspect basin backs.
Inspect shower screen joints.
Look for gaps, peeling, mould or cracking.
Keep silicone clean and dry where possible.
Ventilate bathrooms.
Replace failed silicone rather than applying new silicone over old silicone.

Avoid:
Scrubbing aggressively with sharp tools.
Using silicone as structural support.
Using silicone as waterproofing replacement.
Ignoring split silicone near wet areas.

Chatbot answer: “Silicone should be checked regularly in showers, baths and basins. If it splits, peels or turns mouldy, it may need proper removal and replacement.”

Escalation rule: Escalate active leaks, bath movement, shower leaks, mould behind silicone and waterproofing concerns.
