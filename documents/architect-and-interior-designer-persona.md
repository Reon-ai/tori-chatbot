# Architect and Interior Designer Persona

## When to use this file

Use this guide when a customer asks about **architect and interior designer persona** in the South African tile, flooring, bathroomware or contractor context.

Common customer wording and retrieval keywords:
- architect
- designer
- spec
- sample board
- finish schedule
- commercial

## Fast answer

Use specification-grade language. Focus on format, finish, slip risk, maintenance, shade variation, samples, technical datasheets and project continuity.

## Practical guidance for Tori

- Ask for project type, traffic, wet/dry area, indoor/outdoor, required finish and documentation needed.
- Use neutral, professional wording suitable for schedules and client presentations.
- Flag where formal specification requires datasheet confirmation.
- Suggest sample boards, approved alternatives, mock-ups and batch control.
- Do not overstate sustainability, slip rating or commercial suitability.

## Questions to ask before answering fully

- What area is being used: floor, wall, wet area, exterior, commercial, residential?
- What product type, size, substrate and site condition does the customer have?
- Is the customer asking for advice, a quotation, stock, a warranty or a formal specification?

## Recommended answer structure

- Give the direct answer first.
- State the reason in plain language.
- Ask only the missing critical question if needed.
- Recommend the product category or system, not an unsupported product guarantee.
- End with a safe next step: showroom visit, sample, datasheet check, stock check or technical escalation.

## Do not say or assume

- Do not guarantee suitability without confirming area of use, substrate, traffic, exposure and product datasheet.
- Do not confirm stock, lead time, price, shade or batch without live system verification.
- Do not replace product datasheets, SANS/SABS compliance advice, engineering advice or installer responsibility.

## Escalate to a human when

- Escalate failed installations, waterproofing failures, structural cracks, damp, timber/metal substrates, pools, balconies and commercial kitchens.
- Escalate written warranties, architect specifications, insurer confirmations, large projects and legal/compliance questions.

## South African context note

South African advice should be practical, site-aware and conservative. Where a standard, product datasheet or municipal/plumbing/building requirement applies, Tori must say that the final answer should be checked against the latest official datasheet or qualified professional guidance.
