# Tiletoria RAG Utility File 03: South African Consumer Project Planning Guides

This RAG-ready file helps the chatbot guide South African consumers through common renovation and building projects involving tiles, glue-down Luxury Vinyl Tile, sanitaryware, taps, baths, showers, patios and related installation products.

Source basis: South African National Building Regulations awareness through SANS 10400 context, ceramic tile installation principles referenced in SANS 10107-type guidance, TCNA/ANSI tile installation principles, Ceramic Tile Education Foundation installer guidance, manufacturer technical data-sheet practice, and South African site realities such as coastal moisture, winter rain in the Western Cape, humid KwaZulu-Natal conditions, Gauteng screeds and common contractor sequencing issues.

Important chatbot rule: The chatbot may help customers plan, ask better questions and prepare shopping lists. It must not replace a builder, tiler, plumber, waterproofing specialist, architect, engineer, body corporate approval process or local municipal compliance requirement.

## Master South African Project Planning Rule

Summary: South African renovation projects usually succeed when product selection, site conditions, installer responsibility and add-ons are planned before work starts.

Core rule: Plan the project as a system, not as separate products.

The customer should confirm: room use, measurements, water exposure, substrate condition, plumbing position, installer skill, product suitability, add-ons, lead times, waste allowance and maintenance.

The chatbot should encourage: photos, measurements, floor plans, installer feedback and branch confirmation before purchase.

The chatbot should avoid: promising compliance, guaranteeing slip safety, approving waterproofing, confirming plumbing compatibility without site information, or giving exact stock and price.

Safe wording: “This guide helps you plan the project, but final suitability must be confirmed with Tiletoria and the relevant installer or plumber.”

Escalation rule: Escalate wet areas, outdoor areas, ramps, balconies, large-format tiles, glue-down LVT over damp floors, concealed plumbing, wall-hung sanitaryware and commercial projects.

## Bathroom Renovation Sequence

Summary: The order of work matters because plumbing, waterproofing and concealed fittings may be hidden behind tiles.

Typical sequence:
Confirm design and layout.
Measure room and wall areas.
Choose tiles, sanitaryware, mixers and accessories.
Confirm plumbing changes.
Remove old fixtures and tiles.
Repair or prepare substrate.
Install concealed plumbing, frames and mixer bodies.
Pressure test plumbing.
Waterproof required wet areas.
Screed or prepare falls to drains.
Tile floors and walls.
Grout and silicone.
Fit sanitaryware, taps and screens.
Complete first clean.
Test drains, taps, toilets and showers.
Handover care instructions and spare product details.

Key warning: Do not tile over concealed plumbing before pressure testing.

Key warning: Do not install shower tiles before waterproofing is complete.

Key warning: Do not select a concealed mixer after tiling starts.

Chatbot answer: “Bathroom sequencing is critical. Choose concealed mixers, drains, wall-hung frames and waterproofing products before walls are closed or tiled.”

Escalation rule: Escalate if a customer says the plumber and tiler have not agreed on the sequence.
