# Tile m² and Waste Calculator

## Fast answer

Estimate tile quantity from area plus waste. Always round up and recommend spare tiles for future repairs.

## Required inputs

- Room length and width or total m²
- Tile size
- Layout complexity: straight, diagonal, herringbone/patterned
- Number of rooms/thresholds
- Whether spare tiles are required

## Calculation rule

Base tiles required = area in m² × waste factor. Typical waste factors: simple layout 10%, complex layout 12–15%, diagonal/patterned 15% or more. Round up to full boxes using live pack size.

## Worked example

Customer has 40 m² simple floor. Use 10% waste: 40 × 1.10 = 44 m². If box size is live-checked at 1.44 m², boxes = 44 ÷ 1.44 = 30.56, so round up to 31 boxes.

## Answer format

State the estimate, show the assumptions, round up to full sellable units, and warn that final quantities depend on site conditions and product datasheets.

## Warnings

- Do not use box size unless live product data confirms it.
- Do not under-allow waste for large-format, diagonal, patterned or many-cut areas.

## Do not say or assume

- Do not guarantee the quantity as exact.
- Do not ignore product datasheets, pack sizes, site conditions or workmanship.

## Escalate to a human when

- The customer asks for a guaranteed quantity.
- The project is commercial, exterior, wet-area, pool, balcony, failed installation or legally sensitive.
- The inputs are incomplete or suspicious.
