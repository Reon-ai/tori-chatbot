# Tiletoria RAG Utility File 02: Before You Install Inspection and Stop-Work Checklist

This RAG-ready file helps the chatbot guide South African consumers through pre-installation checks for tiles, glue-down Luxury Vinyl Tile, sanitaryware, baths, taps, showers and related products. The purpose is to prevent avoidable mistakes before products are fixed, cut, grouted, glued, connected or built into walls.

Source basis: TCNA guidance that grout joints are not waterproof and wet-area protection depends on the full installation method and substrate; Ceramic Tile Education Foundation guidance on substrate flatness for large-format tile; Polyflor Luxury Vinyl Tile installation guidance on acclimatisation and subfloor preparation; and recognised plumbing guidance that bathroom fittings require appropriate backflow protection. Final approval must always come from the relevant product data sheet, installer, plumber, builder, waterproofing specialist or project professional.

Important chatbot rule: The chatbot must tell the customer to stop installation and escalate when there is visible damage, wrong product, wrong batch, wrong shade, missing accessories, damp substrate, failed waterproofing, incompatible plumbing or uncertainty that could become hidden after installation.

## Master Pre-Installation Rule

Summary: Customers should check all products, site conditions and accessories before installation because installed products are harder to return, replace or claim.

Core rule: Inspect before installing, cutting, gluing, grouting, sealing, drilling or connecting.

Customer-friendly wording: “Please check the product, quantity, colour, batch, accessories and site conditions before your installer starts.”

Business-protection wording: “Once a product is installed, it becomes harder to assess whether the issue was product-related, installation-related or site-related.”

The chatbot should encourage customers to take photos before work starts.

The chatbot should encourage customers to keep packaging labels, batch information, invoices and product instructions.

The chatbot should not approve installation from a description alone when there is a technical risk.

Escalation rule: Stop and escalate if anything looks wrong, does not match the order, is damaged, is missing, or does not match the installer’s requirements.

## Product Box and Label Check

Summary: Product labels should be checked before installation to confirm the correct item, size, batch, shade, finish and quantity.

Check product name: Confirm the product name matches the invoice or quote.

Check product code: Confirm the code matches the selected range.

Check size: Confirm the size on the box matches the order.

Check finish: Confirm matt, polished, lappato, textured, outdoor or other finish is correct.

Check colour: Confirm the colour tone appears consistent with the approved sample or display expectation.

Check shade or batch: Confirm all boxes for one continuous area are from the same shade or batch where possible.

Check calibre or size grouping: Confirm calibre is consistent where marked.

Check quantity: Confirm there are enough boxes, packs, bags, trims, wastes, traps, valves or connectors before work starts.

Check packaging condition: Damaged boxes should be inspected before installation.

Chatbot answer: “Before installation starts, check every box label against your invoice: product name, size, finish, shade, batch, calibre and quantity. Raise any concern before tiles or products are installed.”

Stop-work trigger: If boxes show mixed shade, mixed calibre, wrong size, wrong finish or wrong product code, stop and contact Tiletoria before installation.

## Tile Visual Inspection Before Installation

Summary: Tiles should be checked loose and dry before fixing to identify visible damage, unexpected variation or wrong product.

Open several boxes before installation.

Inspect tiles in good natural or bright light.

Check for obvious chips, cracks, surface marks or wrong finish.

Compare tiles against approved sample or showroom display expectations.

Understand that many modern tiles have intentional variation and multiple faces.

Dry-lay a small area where the tile has strong veining, shade variation or directional pattern.

Mix tiles from several boxes before fixing to blend normal variation.

Do not install tiles that the customer believes are wrong.

Chatbot answer: “Please inspect tiles before installation. If the colour, finish, size, batch or visible quality does not look right, pause the installation and contact Tiletoria before fixing them.”

Stop-work trigger: If the customer or installer is unhappy with appearance before installation, do not proceed until it is checked.

## Shade, Batch and Calibre Check

Summary: Shade, batch and calibre should be checked before installation because future batches may not match exactly.

Shade refers to the colour tone or production shade.

Batch refers to the production run or lot.

Calibre refers to the actual size grouping after manufacturing.

Tiles from different batches may vary in tone, pattern or size.

Use the same batch and calibre in one continuous area where possible.

If mixed batches must be used, the installer should blend and locate them carefully.

Do not start installation if the batch difference is obvious and unacceptable.

Chatbot answer: “For best results, use the same shade, batch and calibre in one continuous area. If boxes differ, stop and confirm with Tiletoria before installation.”

Stop-work trigger: Mixed shade or calibre in one area without customer approval.

## Tile Quantity and Waste Check Before Installation

Summary: Quantity must be confirmed before installation because running short can cause batch mismatches or delays.

Measure the tiled area again before installation starts.

Confirm tile quantity includes waste.

Confirm the layout pattern and waste allowance with the installer.

Confirm extra tiles are available for cuts, breakages and future repairs.

Confirm skirting, steps, niches and feature areas are included in the measurement.

Confirm whether the same batch is available if extra stock is required.

Chatbot answer: “Before work starts, confirm the measured area, waste allowance, layout and spare tiles. Running short later may mean the same batch is no longer available.”

Stop-work trigger: Installer believes there is not enough tile for the planned area and waste.

## Tile Layout Approval

Summary: Tile layout should be approved before adhesive is applied to prevent poor cuts, awkward lines and visual disputes.

Confirm tile direction or grain direction.

Confirm whether tiles will be straight, staggered, diagonal, herringbone or another pattern.

Confirm where full tiles start.

Confirm where cut tiles will fall.

Confirm grout joint width.

Confirm feature wall alignment.

Confirm shower niche and drain alignment.

Confirm skirting height and edge finishes.

Chatbot answer: “Ask your installer to dry-lay or mark out the layout before fixing. Confirm tile direction, cuts, grout joint width and feature placement before adhesive is applied.”

Stop-work trigger: Customer has not approved layout and the installer is about to start fixing visible feature areas.

## Grout Colour and Joint Width Check

Summary: Grout colour and joint width affect the final appearance and must be confirmed before grouting.

Confirm grout colour before grouting starts.

Confirm grout joint width before tiling starts.

Confirm whether rectified or non-rectified tiles require wider or narrower joints.

Confirm whether outdoor, wet-area or textured tiles need a specific grout.

Confirm whether silicone is required at corners and movement joints.

Do not allow a “no grout joint” installation.

Chatbot answer: “Please confirm grout colour and grout joint width before installation. Rectified tiles still need grout joints, and movement-prone corners should not be filled with rigid grout.”

Stop-work trigger: Installer wants to butt tiles with no grout joint or grout the wrong colour.

## Adhesive and Primer Check Before Tiling

Summary: Adhesive and primer must match the tile, substrate and application before any tile is fixed.

Check adhesive is suitable for ceramic, porcelain, large-format, exterior, wet area or wall use as required.

Check primer is required for the substrate or preparation system.

Check the adhesive bag or product data sheet for tile size and application suitability.

Check whether large-format tiles need special adhesive or back-buttering.

Check whether wet or external areas need higher adhesive contact coverage.

Do not use wall tile adhesive for floor installations unless the product is approved for it.

Chatbot answer: “Please confirm the adhesive and primer before installation. The adhesive must match the tile, substrate, size and area, especially for porcelain, large-format, wet or outdoor installations.”

Stop-work trigger: Wrong adhesive is on site, adhesive type is unknown, or installer cannot confirm suitability.

## Substrate Flatness Check for Large-Format Tile

Summary: Large-format tiles require flatter floors and walls because uneven substrates increase lippage and bonding risk.

Ask the installer to check flatness before installation.

Use a straightedge or professional method to identify high and low spots.

Do not rely on levelling clips to fix an uneven substrate.

Correct high spots, low spots, cracks or rough areas before tiling.

Confirm whether self-levelling compound is needed.

Confirm wall flatness before installing large-format wall tiles.

Chatbot answer: “Large-format tiles need a flatter substrate. Levelling clips help align tile edges, but they do not fix a bad floor or wall.”

Stop-work trigger: Substrate is visibly uneven, hollow, dusty, cracked or not flat enough for the selected large-format tile.

## Tile Levelling Clip and Wedge Check

Summary: Levelling systems must be selected correctly for tile thickness, grout joint width and installer workflow.

Confirm clip thickness range matches the tile thickness.

Confirm clip joint width matches the desired grout joint.

Confirm wedges are compatible with the clips.

Confirm enough clips are available for the full project.

Confirm enough reusable wedges are available for the installer’s daily working area.

Confirm pliers are available if the system requires them.

Confirm clips are placed between tiles at joints, not randomly on tile faces.

Chatbot answer: “Please confirm clip size, joint width and tile thickness before installation. Clips are normally single-use; wedges are reusable and should be calculated by the installer’s active working area.”

Stop-work trigger: Clips do not fit tile thickness or the installer intends to use the levelling system to compensate for a poor substrate.

## Movement Joint and Expansion Gap Check

Summary: Movement joints must be planned before tiling because rigid grout cannot handle all movement.

Confirm perimeter gaps are planned.

Confirm movement joints at changes in plane.

Confirm movement joints in large internal floors.

Confirm movement joints in exterior and sun-exposed areas.

Confirm movement joints over structural movement joints.

Confirm movement joint material or profile is available.

Do not fill movement joints with rigid grout.

Chatbot answer: “Ask your installer where movement joints are required before tiling starts. Movement joints help manage expansion and contraction and should not be filled with rigid grout.”

Stop-work trigger: Large, exterior, heated or sun-exposed areas are being tiled with no movement-joint plan.

## Waterproofing Check Before Bathroom or Shower Tiling

Summary: Wet areas should be waterproofed before tiling because tiles and grout are not a waterproofing system.

Confirm waterproofing has been applied where required.

Confirm wall-floor junctions, corners and pipe penetrations are treated.

Confirm shower niches, benches and ledges are waterproofed.

Confirm waterproofing has cured before tiling.

Confirm waterproofing is compatible with adhesive.

Confirm photos are taken before waterproofing is covered.

Remember that grout joints are not waterproof.

Chatbot answer: “Before tiling a shower or wet area, confirm the waterproofing system is complete and cured. Tiles and grout are not a substitute for waterproofing.”

Stop-work trigger: Shower or wet-area tiling is about to start and waterproofing is missing, incomplete, damaged or uncertain.

## Shower Drain and Falls Check

Summary: Shower floors must drain correctly before tiles are installed.

Confirm drain or channel position.

Confirm fall direction.

Confirm water will drain to the waste without ponding.

Confirm tile size suits the planned falls.

Confirm shower channel was selected before screeding.

Confirm waterproofing integrates with the drain system.

Confirm drain remains serviceable for cleaning.

Chatbot answer: “Before shower floor tiling starts, confirm the drain, falls, waterproofing and tile layout. Poor falls can leave standing water even when the tile itself is suitable.”

Stop-work trigger: Shower floor has no clear fall to the drain or the drain/channel is not installed before tiling.

## Outdoor Tile Site Check

Summary: Outdoor tile installations require checks for slope, drainage, movement, sunlight exposure and exterior-rated materials.

Confirm tile is suitable for outdoor use.

Confirm surface finish is appropriate for expected wet conditions.

Confirm adhesive is suitable for exterior use.

Confirm grout is suitable for exterior use.

Confirm drainage and falls are correct.

Confirm movement joints are planned.

Confirm substrate is sound, stable and dry enough for the system.

Confirm cleaning plan for algae and dirt.

Chatbot answer: “Outdoor tiles need more than a textured surface. Confirm outdoor suitability, adhesive, grout, drainage, movement joints and maintenance before installation.”

Stop-work trigger: Outdoor tiling starts with indoor adhesive, no drainage plan or unsuitable indoor tile.

## Ramp and Public Walkway Stop-Work Check

Summary: Ramps and public walkways are safety-sensitive areas and should be reviewed before installation.

Confirm product slip-resistance information.

Confirm the tile is suitable for the application.

Confirm slope and drainage.

Confirm maintenance plan.

Confirm grout, adhesive and movement joints.

Confirm project professional or specifier approval.

Do not promise “non-slip” or “slip-proof”.

Chatbot answer: “Ramps and public walkways should be confirmed with Tiletoria and the project professional before installation. Slip performance depends on tile, slope, drainage, cleaning, footwear and site conditions.”

Stop-work trigger: Customer asks for a guarantee that a tile is non-slip or a ramp is being tiled without professional approval.

## Glue-Down LVT Product Check

Summary: Glue-down Luxury Vinyl Tile must be checked for product, batch, colour, pack quantity and installation method before adhesive is used.

Confirm the product is glue-down LVT, not click vinyl.

Confirm colour, design and batch.

Confirm pack quantity and waste allowance.

Confirm plank or tile direction.

Confirm adhesive is approved for the product.

Confirm trims and transitions are available.

Confirm spare planks or tiles are kept for repairs.

Chatbot answer: “Before installing glue-down LVT, confirm product, colour, batch, quantity, layout, adhesive and trims. Glue-down LVT must not be installed like click vinyl.”

Stop-work trigger: Wrong vinyl type, wrong adhesive, mixed batches or insufficient quantity.

## Glue-Down LVT Subfloor Check

Summary: Glue-down LVT needs a clean, dry, smooth, flat and sound subfloor because imperfections can telegraph through the product.

Check subfloor is dry.

Check moisture readings where required.

Check subfloor is smooth and flat.

Check old tile grout lines are smoothed or levelled.

Check old adhesive, dust, paint, oil or grease is removed or treated.

Check primer and self-leveller are compatible.

Check acclimatisation requirements.

Check site temperature requirements.

Chatbot answer: “Glue-down LVT is only as good as the subfloor below it. The floor must be clean, dry, smooth, flat and sound before adhesive is applied.”

Stop-work trigger: Damp concrete, rough screed, visible grout lines, dusty surface, old adhesive residue or no approved LVT adhesive.

## LVT Acclimatisation and Site Condition Check

Summary: LVT and adhesive should be conditioned according to product instructions before installation.

Confirm boxes are stored flat and indoors.

Confirm product has acclimatised if required by manufacturer instructions.

Confirm room temperature is within product and adhesive limits.

Confirm building is enclosed and weather-tight.

Confirm adhesive is stored correctly.

Confirm underfloor heating is controlled according to instructions.

Chatbot answer: “Please confirm the LVT and adhesive have been conditioned according to the product instructions before installation starts.”

Stop-work trigger: LVT has been stored outside, installed in extreme temperature or installed before required site conditions are met.

## Sanitaryware Box Check

Summary: Sanitaryware should be inspected before installation for cracks, chips, missing parts and correct model.

Check product name and code.

Check colour and finish.

Check visible cracks, chips or glaze defects.

Check whether seat is included with toilet.

Check whether basin waste is included.

Check whether bath waste is included.

Check fixing kit and installation instructions.

Keep packaging and labels until installation is complete.

Chatbot answer: “Please inspect sanitaryware before installation. Report visible damage, missing parts or wrong product before it is fitted.”

Stop-work trigger: Cracks, chips, missing parts, wrong model or wrong colour before installation.

## Toilet Compatibility Check

Summary: Toilet fitment depends on waste outlet, set-out, cistern type, water inlet and space.

Confirm S-trap or P-trap requirement.

Measure set-out from finished wall or floor as applicable.

Confirm pan connector type.

Confirm water inlet position.

Confirm seat is included or selected.

Confirm cistern, flush mechanism or flush plate is included where relevant.

Confirm floor is finished and level.

Confirm wall-hung frame if wall-hung toilet.

Chatbot answer: “Before installing a toilet, confirm the waste outlet, set-out, water inlet, seat, cistern and connector. The toilet may not fit if the waste position is wrong.”

Stop-work trigger: Toilet waste outlet does not match site plumbing or concealed frame/cistern compatibility is uncertain.

## Wall-Hung Toilet and Concealed Cistern Check

Summary: Wall-hung toilets and concealed cisterns must be confirmed before walls are closed and tiled.

Confirm concealed frame is compatible with the pan.

Confirm cistern is compatible with the flush plate.

Confirm wall structure can support the frame.

Confirm finished floor height.

Confirm finished wall depth.

Confirm service access through flush plate or access panel.

Confirm water inlet and waste connection.

Pressure test before closing walls.

Chatbot answer: “A wall-hung toilet is a system. Confirm the frame, cistern, flush plate, pan and wall structure before the wall is closed.”

Stop-work trigger: Wall is about to be closed before frame, cistern, flush plate and service access are confirmed.

## Basin Compatibility Check

Summary: Basin installation depends on basin type, tap holes, waste type, trap position and support.

Confirm basin type: countertop, wall-hung, inset, undercounter, semi-recessed or pedestal.

Confirm tap-hole layout.

Confirm whether basin has overflow.

Select slotted or unslotted waste accordingly.

Confirm trap type and drawer clearance.

Confirm wall support for wall-hung basins.

Confirm mixer height and spout reach.

Confirm angle valves and flexible connectors.

Chatbot answer: “Before installing a basin, confirm tap holes, overflow, waste, trap, mixer height, spout reach and wall or vanity support.”

Stop-work trigger: Wrong waste type, mixer does not fit, trap clashes with vanity drawer or wall support is uncertain.

## Bath Compatibility Check

Summary: Bath installation depends on bath type, waste, overflow, support, mixer, service access and waterproofing details.

Confirm bath size fits the room.

Confirm bath waste and overflow compatibility.

Confirm trap and waste access.

Confirm support frame, feet or cradle.

Confirm bath mixer, spout or floor-standing mixer.

Confirm water supply positions.

Confirm silicone and wall junction details.

Confirm access panel for built-in baths.

Chatbot answer: “Before installing a bath, confirm waste, overflow, support, mixer type, access and sealing details. Bath movement or hidden leaks can cause expensive problems later.”

Stop-work trigger: Bath waste cannot be accessed, support is incomplete or mixer position is not planned.

## Freestanding Bath Check

Summary: Freestanding baths require early planning for waste, floor plumbing, mixer position, weight and service access.

Confirm floor space around bath.

Confirm bath weight and floor support if required.

Confirm waste position.

Confirm trap and service access.

Confirm floor-standing or wall-mounted mixer.

Confirm water pressure and supply positions.

Confirm bath can be moved safely into the room.

Confirm cleaning access around the bath.

Chatbot answer: “Before installing a freestanding bath, confirm waste position, mixer type, floor support, access and plumbing. It is not always as simple as placing the bath anywhere.”

Stop-work trigger: Waste and mixer positions are not planned before floor finishing.

## Tapware and Mixer Check

Summary: Tapware must match water pressure, mounting holes, spout reach, finish and connection requirements.

Confirm water pressure requirements.

Confirm hot and cold supply positions.

Confirm tap-hole count.

Confirm spout reach and height.

Confirm counter thickness.

Confirm angle valves and flexible connectors.

Confirm finish and matching visible accessories.

Flush pipework before connecting mixers.

Chatbot answer: “Before installing a tap or mixer, confirm water pressure, tap holes, spout reach, valves, connectors and pipe flushing.”

Stop-work trigger: Water pressure is unknown for pressure-sensitive mixers or tap does not match basin/bath holes.

## Concealed Mixer Check

Summary: Concealed mixers must be selected, installed and pressure-tested before tiling.

Confirm concealed body and trim are compatible.

Confirm installation depth.

Confirm finished wall build-up including tile and adhesive.

Confirm water pressure.

Confirm outlet positions.

Confirm diverter if multiple outlets.

Confirm service access where applicable.

Pressure test before tiling.

Chatbot answer: “Concealed mixers must be confirmed before tiling. The body, trim, depth, outlets and pressure must all be correct.”

Stop-work trigger: Wall is about to be tiled before concealed body, trim and pressure testing are confirmed.

## Shower Screen and Glass Check

Summary: Shower screens must match opening size, wall strength, fixing method and water-control expectations.

Confirm opening width and height.

Confirm wall is plumb enough for the screen system.

Confirm floor fall and shower layout.

Confirm screen orientation.

Confirm support bar or brace if required.

Confirm silicone and fixing method.

Confirm glass is inspected before fitting.

Confirm waterproofing is not damaged during drilling.

Chatbot answer: “Before fitting a shower screen, confirm size, wall support, fixing method, floor fall and waterproofing protection.”

Stop-work trigger: Shower screen is being fixed into uncertain waterproofed walls or walls are out of square without adjustment plan.

## Waterproofing Penetration Check

Summary: Drilling or fixing through waterproofed surfaces must be controlled to avoid creating leak paths.

Identify waterproofed areas.

Avoid unnecessary drilling in shower walls and wet floors.

Use correct sealant around penetrations where required.

Confirm grab rails, shower screens and accessories have proper wall support.

Confirm installer understands waterproofing protection.

Take photos before walls are closed.

Chatbot answer: “Be careful when drilling into waterproofed shower walls. Fixings must be planned so the waterproofing is not compromised.”

Stop-work trigger: Accessories or glass are being drilled into wet-area walls without waterproofing protection plan.

## Cleaning Product Check Before First Clean

Summary: The first clean should use products compatible with tile, grout, sanitaryware, tap finishes and LVT.

Confirm tile finish before selecting cleaner.

Confirm grout type before selecting haze remover.

Use cement residue remover only where compatible.

Use epoxy haze remover only for epoxy residue where compatible.

Avoid abrasive tools on polished or special finishes.

Avoid harsh acids on natural stone and sensitive surfaces.

Use vinyl-safe cleaner on LVT.

Use finish-safe cleaner on tapware.

Chatbot answer: “Before the first clean, match the cleaner to the surface and residue. The wrong cleaner can damage grout, tap finishes, polished tiles, baths or LVT.”

Stop-work trigger: Installer or cleaner intends to use acid, vinegar, bleach, steel wool or abrasive tools without confirming compatibility.

## Grout Haze Stop-Work Check

Summary: Grout haze should be dealt with correctly during and shortly after installation before it becomes difficult to remove.

Confirm installer performs proper wash-off during grouting.

Avoid too much water during grout cleaning.

Do not clean too early if it weakens grout.

Confirm correct haze remover if residue remains.

Textured tiles need extra cleaning attention.

Polished tiles need non-abrasive cleaning.

Chatbot answer: “Grout haze is easier to manage early. Confirm the correct clean-up process and compatible haze remover before the residue hardens.”

Stop-work trigger: Heavy grout residue is being left on textured or polished tiles with no cleaning plan.

## Installer Question Checklist Before Work Starts

Summary: Customers should ask practical questions before work starts because unclear assumptions often become disputes later.

Ask: Have you checked the product, batch and quantity?

Ask: Have you checked substrate flatness?

Ask: What adhesive will you use?

Ask: Do we need primer or self-leveller?

Ask: What grout joint width will you use?

Ask: Will tiles be mixed from multiple boxes?

Ask: Where will the tile cuts fall?

Ask: Are movement joints needed?

Ask: Who removes grout haze?

Ask: What happens if we discover a problem during installation?

Chatbot answer: “Before installation starts, ask your installer to confirm layout, adhesive, grout joint width, substrate readiness, movement joints and clean-up responsibility.”

Escalation rule: If installer cannot answer technical basics, recommend contacting Tiletoria before proceeding.

## Plumber Question Checklist Before Work Starts

Summary: Plumbing products require compatibility checks before walls are closed, tiles are installed or products are connected.

Ask: Will this toilet fit the existing waste outlet?

Ask: Is this basin waste slotted or unslotted?

Ask: Will the trap fit inside the vanity?

Ask: Is the water pressure suitable for this mixer?

Ask: Are hot and cold supplies correctly positioned?

Ask: Has pipework been flushed?

Ask: Has concealed plumbing been pressure-tested?

Ask: Can the cistern or waste be serviced later?

Ask: Are backflow requirements relevant?

Ask: Are all valves, connectors and fixing kits on site?

Chatbot answer: “Before plumbing starts, ask your plumber to confirm fitment, water pressure, waste type, service access and all required connectors.”

Escalation rule: Escalate concealed mixers, wall-hung toilets, low pressure, leaks and backflow questions.

Do not install if boxes show mixed shade or calibre without approval.

Do not install if visible damage is found.

Do not install if the customer dislikes the colour or finish when opened.

Do not install if waterproofing is missing in a shower.

Do not install if the floor is damp for glue-down LVT.

Do not install if the adhesive is wrong.

Do not install if the installer wants no grout joints.

Do not install if the concealed mixer body and trim are incompatible.

Do not install if the toilet waste does not match the pan.

Chatbot answer: “If any of these issues are present, pause the installation and contact Tiletoria or the relevant installer before proceeding.”

Confirm spare tiles or LVT planks are stored safely.

Keep packaging labels and batch details.

Keep adhesive, grout and cleaner product names.

Keep sanitaryware product codes.

Keep tapware product codes.

Keep warranty information.

Confirm first clean is complete.

Confirm silicone joints are finished.

Confirm plumbing has been tested.

Confirm shower drains properly.

Confirm no visible chips, cracks, leaks or loose items.

Chatbot answer: “After installation, keep product labels, spare pieces, invoices and care instructions. Check the installation before making final payment to the installer.”

Escalation rule: Escalate any visible defect, leak, loose tile, hollow tile, lippage concern, cracked grout or poor drainage before the installer leaves.
