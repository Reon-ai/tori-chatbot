# Category 12: Cleaning, Maintenance and Aftercare

This RAG-ready chatbot document provides factual, self-contained customer support answers for tile cleaning, maintenance and aftercare in a South African retail context. It includes gentle add-on guidance for appropriate cleaners, grout haze removers, sealers, scrub pads and maintenance products without making unsafe product guarantees.

Source basis: TCNA tile and grout cleaning guidance, Ceramic Tile Education Foundation installation and residue guidance, reputable manufacturer aftercare guidance, and general ceramic/porcelain maintenance principles. Final product selection must follow the relevant tile, grout, sealer and cleaner data sheets.

Important chatbot rule: Do not recommend harsh chemicals, acid cleaners, sealers or abrasive tools unless the surface, residue and product compatibility are known. Escalate uncertain cases to a Tiletoria consultant.

## Routine Tile Cleaning Basics

Summary: Routine tile cleaning should remove loose grit and residues without leaving a film that can dull the surface or increase slip risk.

### Q001: What is the best basic routine for cleaning ceramic or porcelain tiles?

Answer: Sweep or vacuum loose grit first, then clean with clean water and a neutral tile cleaner suitable for ceramic or porcelain tiles. Rinse when required by the cleaner instructions and avoid leaving detergent residue on the surface.

### Q002: How often should tiled floors be cleaned?

Answer: Clean tiled floors as often as the area requires based on traffic, dust, spills and weather exposure. High-traffic entrances, kitchens and bathrooms may need more frequent cleaning than bedrooms or low-use areas.

### Q003: Why should loose dust and grit be removed before mopping?

Answer: Dust and grit can act like fine abrasive particles during mopping. Removing loose dirt first helps protect the tile surface, grout joints and cleaning equipment.

### Q004: Can customers use ordinary household detergent on tiles?

Answer: Some mild household detergents may be acceptable, but a neutral cleaner made for tile is safer because it is less likely to leave residue or attack grout, sealers or sensitive finishes.

### Q005: Why do tiles sometimes look dull after cleaning?

Answer: Tiles can look dull if detergent residue, dirty mop water, grout haze, hard-water deposits or incorrect cleaning products remain on the surface. A correct rinse and suitable cleaner can help restore clarity.

### Q006: Should tiles be cleaned with clean or reused mop water?

Answer: Use clean water and change it regularly. Dirty mop water can spread soil over the floor and leave a grey or sticky film.

### Q007: What cleaning add-ons should be considered with a tile purchase?

Answer: A suitable neutral tile cleaner, grout haze remover where cement grout residue may occur, soft scrub pads and grout maintenance products should be considered as part of the complete tile installation and aftercare system.

### Q008: Can a tile floor be over-cleaned?

Answer: Frequent cleaning is normally fine when the correct products are used. Problems usually come from harsh chemicals, abrasive tools, too much detergent, poor rinsing or using the wrong cleaner for the tile finish.

### Q009: What should the chatbot recommend for everyday tile care?

Answer: Recommend sweeping or vacuuming, cleaning with a suitable neutral tile cleaner, using clean water, and avoiding waxes, oils and harsh chemicals unless the product manufacturer specifically allows them.

### Q010: When should cleaning questions be escalated to Tiletoria?

Answer: Escalate when the customer reports staining, etched marks, chemical damage, unknown tile type, natural stone, polished surfaces, heavy grout haze, slip concerns, or a possible installation defect.

## First Clean After Installation

Summary: The first clean after installation is critical because cement grout residue and site dirt can make new tiles look stained, dull or difficult to maintain.

### Q011: What is the first clean after tile installation?

Answer: The first clean is the post-installation cleaning process that removes grout residue, adhesive smears, dust and building-site contamination from newly installed tiles.

### Q012: Why is the first clean important?

Answer: The first clean is important because leftover grout residue can trap dirt, create streaks, make the tile look cloudy and cause ongoing maintenance complaints.

### Q013: Who is responsible for the first clean after installation?

Answer: The installer or contractor usually manages the first clean as part of the installation process. The exact responsibility should be agreed before installation starts.

### Q014: Can the customer move furniture in before the first clean is complete?

Answer: Furniture should only be moved in once the installation has cured sufficiently and the floor has been properly cleaned according to the adhesive, grout and cleaner instructions.

### Q015: What products are normally used for the first clean?

Answer: The product depends on the residue. Cement-based grout haze may require a suitable cement residue remover, while epoxy grout residue needs a specific epoxy haze remover. Always follow product instructions and test first.

### Q016: Can acidic cleaners be used immediately after grouting?

Answer: Do not use acid cleaners unless the grout and tile manufacturer instructions allow it and the grout has cured sufficiently. Incorrect acid cleaning can damage grout, metal trims, natural stone or sensitive surfaces.

### Q017: Why should grout haze be removed early?

Answer: Grout haze is easier to remove before it fully hardens. If left for too long, it may require stronger specialist cleaning and can become more difficult to remove from textured tiles.

### Q018: Should polished tiles be treated carefully during the first clean?

Answer: Yes. Polished tiles may show smears, micro-scratches or chemical effects more easily, so use suitable products, soft tools and test a small area first.

### Q019: What add-on should be offered for new installations?

Answer: A suitable grout haze remover, neutral cleaner and non-abrasive cleaning tools should be offered because they help protect the appearance of the new installation from day one.

### Q020: When should post-installation cleaning be escalated?

Answer: Escalate if the tile is polished, textured, natural stone, unknown, heavily stained, affected by epoxy residue, or if the customer is unsure which grout or cleaner was used.

## Grout Haze and Cement Residue

Summary: Grout haze is a thin residue left after grouting and must be removed with the correct method for the tile surface and grout type.

### Q021: What is grout haze?

Answer: Grout haze is a thin film of grout residue left on the tile surface after grouting. It can look cloudy, dusty, streaky or dull.

### Q022: Is grout haze a tile defect?

Answer: Grout haze is usually a cleaning or installation residue issue, not a tile defect. The surface should be assessed and cleaned with the correct product before assuming the tile is faulty.

### Q023: What causes grout haze?

Answer: Grout haze is caused by grout residue drying on the tile surface, insufficient cleaning during installation, using dirty water, or not completing the final wash properly.

### Q024: Can grout haze make tiles look permanently stained?

Answer: Grout haze can make tiles look stained or dull, but many cases can be improved with the correct cleaner. The result depends on the residue type, tile finish and how long the haze has been present.

### Q025: Can cement grout haze be removed with normal soap?

Answer: Normal soap is usually not ideal for cement grout haze. A suitable cement residue or grout haze remover may be needed, subject to tile and grout compatibility.

### Q026: Can vinegar be used to remove grout haze?

Answer: Vinegar is acidic and should not be recommended as a general solution. It may damage grout, natural stone, metal trims or sensitive surfaces, so a proper tile-safe product is preferred.

### Q027: Why is textured tile harder to clean after grouting?

Answer: Textured tile has surface grooves and grip areas where grout residue can lodge. It often needs careful cleaning, suitable brushes and correct grout haze remover.

### Q028: Can grout haze increase slip risk?

Answer: Yes. Residue from grout haze, detergent or site dirt can affect the feel and cleanliness of the surface. A proper final clean helps restore the intended surface condition.

### Q029: What should the chatbot suggest for visible grout haze?

Answer: Suggest identifying the tile finish and grout type, testing a suitable grout haze remover in a small area, following product instructions, and contacting Tiletoria if the residue is heavy or uncertain.

### Q030: What add-on is relevant for grout haze prevention?

Answer: A proper grout haze remover and neutral maintenance cleaner should be offered when customers buy grout, especially for textured, matt, large-format or dark tiles.

## Neutral Tile Cleaners

Summary: Neutral tile cleaners are generally the safest everyday option for porcelain and ceramic tiles because they clean without aggressive chemical action.

### Q031: What is a neutral tile cleaner?

Answer: A neutral tile cleaner is a cleaning product with a near-neutral pH designed for routine cleaning without strong acidic or alkaline action.

### Q032: Why recommend a neutral cleaner for tiles?

Answer: A neutral cleaner is less likely to damage grout, sealers, silicone, trims or sensitive tile finishes when used correctly.

### Q033: Can neutral cleaner be used on porcelain tiles?

Answer: Yes, a suitable neutral cleaner is commonly recommended for routine porcelain tile cleaning. Always follow the cleaner instructions and rinse if required.

### Q034: Can neutral cleaner be used on ceramic tiles?

Answer: Yes, a suitable neutral cleaner is generally appropriate for routine cleaning of glazed ceramic tiles.

### Q035: Does neutral cleaner remove grout haze?

Answer: Neutral cleaner may remove light dirt and some light residues, but cement grout haze normally requires a specific cement residue remover.

### Q036: Does neutral cleaner remove grease?

Answer: A neutral cleaner can help with light grease, but heavy kitchen grease may need a suitable degreasing product that is compatible with the tile, grout and finish.

### Q037: Why should too much detergent be avoided?

Answer: Too much detergent can leave a sticky or dull residue that attracts dirt and may make the floor feel slippery.

### Q038: Can customers use a neutral cleaner weekly?

Answer: Yes, a suitable neutral cleaner is appropriate for regular maintenance when diluted and used according to the instructions.

### Q039: What should be sold with tiles for long-term cleaning?

Answer: A neutral tile cleaner is a sensible add-on because it supports correct routine maintenance and helps reduce complaints caused by incorrect household cleaners.

### Q040: When is neutral cleaner not enough?

Answer: Neutral cleaner may not remove cement residue, epoxy haze, heavy limescale, rust, oil staining, paint, silicone residue or deep grout discolouration.

## Acidic Cleaners and Cement Residue Removers

Summary: Acidic cleaners can remove cement residues but must be used carefully because they can damage certain materials and surfaces.

### Q041: What is an acidic tile cleaner used for?

Answer: An acidic tile cleaner is generally used for cement-based residues such as cement grout haze, mortar smears or some mineral deposits, subject to product instructions.

### Q042: Can acidic cleaner be used on all tiles?

Answer: No. Acidic cleaners are not suitable for all surfaces and may damage natural stone, metal trims, some decorative finishes, grout or sensitive materials.

### Q043: Can acidic cleaner damage grout?

Answer: Yes. If used incorrectly or too strongly, acidic cleaners can weaken or etch cement-based grout. Follow dilution, dwell time and rinsing instructions carefully.

### Q044: Should acidic cleaners be tested first?

Answer: Yes. Always test on a small inconspicuous area before wider use, especially on polished, textured, decorative, dark or unknown tiles.

### Q045: Can acidic cleaner be used on marble-look porcelain?

Answer: It may be possible on porcelain, but confirm the actual tile surface and manufacturer guidance first. Do not assume a marble-look tile is natural stone or porcelain without checking.

### Q046: Can acidic cleaner be used on natural stone?

Answer: Acidic cleaners should generally be avoided on acid-sensitive natural stone such as marble, limestone and travertine unless a specialist confirms suitability.

### Q047: Why must acidic cleaner be rinsed thoroughly?

Answer: Acid residue left on the surface can continue reacting with grout, trims or residues. Thorough rinsing helps stop the cleaning action and remove loosened material.

### Q048: Can acidic cleaner remove epoxy grout haze?

Answer: Cement residue removers are not normally the correct solution for epoxy haze. Epoxy haze usually requires a specialist epoxy residue remover.

### Q049: What add-on should be suggested after grouting with cement grout?

Answer: A suitable cement grout haze remover can be offered as an add-on, but the customer must use it only according to the tile, grout and cleaner instructions.

### Q050: When should acidic cleaner questions be escalated?

Answer: Escalate when natural stone, metal trims, polished surfaces, decorative tiles, unknown residues, severe haze or possible chemical damage are involved.

## Alkaline and Degreasing Cleaners

Summary: Alkaline cleaners can help remove grease and organic dirt but must match the tile, grout and finish.

### Q051: What is an alkaline tile cleaner used for?

Answer: An alkaline tile cleaner is generally used for grease, oils, organic dirt and heavy soiling where a neutral cleaner is not strong enough.

### Q052: Can alkaline cleaners be used in kitchens?

Answer: A suitable alkaline cleaner may help remove kitchen grease from ceramic or porcelain tiles, but it must be compatible with the grout, trims and finish.

### Q053: Can alkaline cleaner be used every day?

Answer: Strong alkaline cleaners are usually for periodic deep cleaning, not everyday use. Routine maintenance should normally use a suitable neutral cleaner.

### Q054: Can alkaline cleaners damage tile surfaces?

Answer: They can damage sensitive finishes, sealers or adjacent materials if used incorrectly. Always follow product instructions and test first.

### Q055: What should be used for oily marks on porcelain tiles?

Answer: Use a tile-safe degreasing cleaner compatible with porcelain and follow the dilution and dwell-time instructions. Rinse well to avoid residue.

### Q056: Can alkaline cleaner remove soap scum?

Answer: Some alkaline cleaners can help with soap scum, but bathroom deposits may require a cleaner designed for the specific residue and compatible with the surface.

### Q057: Can alkaline cleaner remove grout haze?

Answer: Alkaline cleaners are not normally the primary solution for cement grout haze. A cement residue remover is usually more appropriate for cement haze.

### Q058: Why should strong cleaners not replace routine maintenance?

Answer: Strong cleaners can be unnecessary for routine cleaning and may affect grout, sealers or sensitive finishes if overused.

### Q059: What add-on can be recommended for kitchens?

Answer: A tile-safe neutral cleaner for routine use and a compatible degreaser for occasional deep cleaning can be suggested for kitchen tile maintenance.

### Q060: When should grease or oil cleaning be escalated?

Answer: Escalate if oil staining has penetrated porous grout or stone, if the tile is unsealed natural material, or if the customer has already used harsh chemicals.

## Polished Tile Maintenance

Summary: Polished tiles can look premium but may show smears, footprints, residue and scratching more easily than matt finishes.

### Q061: Are polished tiles easy to clean?

Answer: Polished tiles can be cleaned effectively, but they may show smears, footprints, water marks and detergent residue more easily than matt tiles.

### Q062: What cleaner is best for polished porcelain?

Answer: Use a suitable neutral cleaner and clean water. Avoid waxes, oily products, abrasive powders and harsh chemicals unless the manufacturer approves them.

### Q063: Why do polished tiles show streaks after mopping?

Answer: Streaks often come from too much detergent, dirty mop water, minerals in water, or not rinsing and drying properly.

### Q064: Can abrasive pads be used on polished tiles?

Answer: Avoid abrasive pads on polished tiles because they may dull or scratch the surface. Use soft cloths, suitable mops and non-abrasive tools.

### Q065: Can polished tiles be used in bathrooms?

Answer: Polished tiles may be suitable for walls, but polished floor tiles can be slippery when wet and may show water marks. Confirm suitability before use in wet areas.

### Q066: Can polished tiles be used outside?

Answer: Polished tiles are generally not the first choice for outdoor floors because wet conditions can increase slip risk and maintenance difficulty.

### Q067: Do polished tiles need sealing?

Answer: Many polished porcelain tiles do not require sealing, but some polished or micro-porous surfaces may need special protection according to the manufacturer. Confirm the specific product.

### Q068: Can acid cleaner be used on polished tiles?

Answer: Only use acid cleaner if the tile and cleaner manufacturer instructions allow it. Always test first because polished surfaces can be sensitive.

### Q069: What add-on should be suggested for polished tiles?

Answer: A suitable neutral cleaner, microfiber mop and non-abrasive cleaning tools should be suggested to reduce streaking and protect the finish.

### Q070: When should polished tile care be escalated?

Answer: Escalate when there are scratches, dull patches, chemical marks, unknown residues, wet-floor slip concerns, or a claim that the tile surface is defective.

## Matt Tile Maintenance

Summary: Matt tiles are practical for many floors but still require correct cleaning to prevent residue build-up and dull patches.

### Q071: Are matt tiles easier to maintain than polished tiles?

Answer: Matt tiles often show fewer footprints and smears than polished tiles, but they still need proper cleaning and rinsing to avoid residue build-up.

### Q072: What cleaner should be used on matt tiles?

Answer: Use a suitable neutral tile cleaner for routine maintenance. Stronger cleaners should only be used when needed and if compatible with the tile and grout.

### Q073: Why do matt tiles sometimes look patchy?

Answer: Patchiness can come from grout haze, detergent residue, dirty mop water, uneven drying, hard-water deposits or natural surface variation.

### Q074: Can matt tiles trap dirt?

Answer: Smooth matt tiles normally clean easily, but very textured or grip matt tiles can hold more dirt in the surface profile and need more thorough brushing.

### Q075: Should matt tiles be waxed?

Answer: No. Wax or polish is generally not recommended on ceramic or porcelain tiles because it can create residue, change the surface appearance and affect slip performance.

### Q076: Can acid cleaner be used on matt porcelain?

Answer: A suitable cement residue remover may be used for cement haze if compatible with the tile and grout. Always test first and follow instructions.

### Q077: What is the best mop for matt tiles?

Answer: A microfiber mop or suitable flat mop used with clean water and the correct cleaner is generally effective. Avoid dirty string mops that spread residue.

### Q078: Can matt tiles be used in high-traffic areas?

Answer: Many matt porcelain tiles are suitable for high-traffic areas, depending on the product specification and maintenance routine.

### Q079: What add-on should be offered with matt floor tiles?

Answer: A neutral tile cleaner and, for new installations, a grout haze remover should be offered to help maintain the intended appearance.

### Q080: When should matt tile maintenance questions be escalated?

Answer: Escalate if the customer reports persistent patchiness, unknown chemical use, heavy staining, grout haze that will not remove, or possible manufacturing defects.

## Textured and Grip Tile Cleaning

Summary: Textured and grip tiles need more mechanical cleaning than smooth tiles because dirt can collect in the surface profile.

### Q081: Are textured tiles harder to clean?

Answer: Textured tiles can require more effort than smooth tiles because dirt, grout residue and soap film can collect in the texture.

### Q082: What tools help clean textured tiles?

Answer: A suitable scrubbing brush, non-abrasive pad, microfiber mop and tile-safe cleaner can help clean textured surfaces effectively.

### Q083: Can textured tiles be cleaned with only a mop?

Answer: A mop may not reach dirt held in the texture. Periodic brushing may be needed, especially outdoors, in showers and in high-traffic areas.

### Q084: Why do outdoor textured tiles look dirty quickly?

Answer: Outdoor tiles are exposed to dust, rain, leaves, algae, soil and general weathering. Textured surfaces can hold this dirt more than smooth indoor tiles.

### Q085: Can grout haze be worse on textured tiles?

Answer: Yes. Cement grout residue can lodge in the texture and become difficult to remove if not cleaned properly during installation.

### Q086: What cleaner should be used on grip tiles?

Answer: Use a compatible neutral cleaner for routine cleaning and a suitable residue remover or degreaser for specific issues, following product instructions.

### Q087: Can harsh scrubbing damage textured tiles?

Answer: Aggressive metal brushes or abrasive tools can damage the tile surface or grout. Use tile-safe brushes and pads.

### Q088: Can textured tiles still become slippery?

Answer: Yes. Dirt, algae, soap, grease, detergent residue or water can reduce grip. Cleaning and drainage are essential for slip-risk management.

### Q089: What add-on should be suggested for textured tiles?

Answer: A suitable neutral cleaner, grout haze remover and non-abrasive scrub brush or pad should be suggested because textured surfaces need correct aftercare.

### Q090: When should textured tile cleaning be escalated?

Answer: Escalate for slip incidents, algae growth, heavy grout residue, pool areas, ramps, commercial sites or any claim that the tile is unsafe.

## Bathroom and Shower Cleaning

Summary: Bathroom tiles need regular cleaning to control soap residue, limescale, mould and grout discolouration.

### Q091: How should bathroom wall tiles be cleaned?

Answer: Clean bathroom wall tiles with a suitable tile cleaner, soft cloth or sponge, and rinse where required. Avoid harsh products that may damage grout, silicone or metal fittings.

### Q092: How should shower floor tiles be maintained?

Answer: Shower floors should be cleaned regularly to remove soap, body oils and residues that can affect appearance and slip performance.

### Q093: Why does shower tile become slippery?

Answer: Soap, shampoo, body oils, conditioner, cleaning residue and poor drainage can reduce grip on shower tiles. Regular cleaning and correct falls are important.

### Q094: Can grout in showers be kept white forever?

Answer: Grout can discolour over time due to moisture, soap residue, mould, cleaning habits and ventilation. Regular cleaning and suitable grout protection can help.

### Q095: How can mould be reduced in showers?

Answer: Improve ventilation, dry surfaces where practical, clean regularly, and maintain silicone joints. Persistent mould may indicate poor ventilation, water retention or failed silicone.

### Q096: Can bleach be used on shower grout?

Answer: Bleach may discolour or weaken some materials if overused. Use products compatible with the grout, silicone and tile, and follow instructions.

### Q097: Does cleaning fix a leaking shower?

Answer: No. Cleaning may improve appearance, but leaks usually relate to waterproofing, plumbing, movement joints, silicone failure or installation defects and require inspection.

### Q098: Should silicone be cleaned differently from grout?

Answer: Yes. Silicone is flexible and can be damaged by harsh scrubbing or unsuitable chemicals. Use compatible products and avoid cutting or tearing the joint.

### Q099: What add-on should be recommended for bathrooms?

Answer: A neutral bathroom-safe tile cleaner, suitable grout cleaner and silicone maintenance guidance should be recommended to support long-term hygiene and appearance.

### Q100: When should bathroom cleaning issues be escalated?

Answer: Escalate if there is leaking, loose tiles, cracked grout, failed silicone, persistent mould, water pooling, slip concerns or suspected waterproofing failure.

## Outdoor Tile Cleaning and Algae Control

Summary: Outdoor tiles require maintenance because weather, dust, algae, leaves and poor drainage can affect appearance and slip performance.

### Q101: How should outdoor porcelain tiles be cleaned?

Answer: Remove loose dirt first, then clean with a tile-safe cleaner and suitable brush or mop. Rinse well and ensure drainage does not leave dirty water standing.

### Q102: Why do outdoor tiles become slippery?

Answer: Outdoor tiles can become slippery from algae, moss, mud, dust, leaf tannins, grease, detergent residue or standing water.

### Q103: Can outdoor tiles stay clean without maintenance?

Answer: No. Outdoor tiles are exposed to weather and environmental dirt, so regular cleaning is needed to maintain appearance and surface performance.

### Q104: How can algae on outdoor tiles be reduced?

Answer: Improve drainage, remove leaves and organic matter, clean regularly and use a suitable outdoor tile cleaner or algae treatment compatible with the tile and grout.

### Q105: Can a pressure washer be used on outdoor tiles?

Answer: Pressure washing may be possible but must be used carefully. Excessive pressure can damage grout, silicone, sealers, movement joints or nearby materials.

### Q106: Why is drainage important for outdoor cleaning?

Answer: Poor drainage leaves water and dirt on the surface, which can encourage algae, staining and slip risk.

### Q107: Can outdoor cleaning restore slip resistance?

Answer: Cleaning can remove contaminants that reduce grip, but it does not change the tile's original surface rating. Site conditions and maintenance remain important.

### Q108: What add-on should be suggested for outdoor tiles?

Answer: A suitable outdoor tile cleaner, scrub brush and grout maintenance products should be suggested because exterior tiles need ongoing aftercare.

### Q109: Can household soap be used outdoors?

Answer: Household soap can leave residue and attract dirt if not rinsed properly. A tile-safe cleaner is usually a better recommendation.

### Q110: When should outdoor cleaning be escalated?

Answer: Escalate for ramps, public walkways, pool surrounds, algae-related slip concerns, efflorescence, loose tiles, cracked grout or waterproofing concerns.

## Grout Cleaning and Maintenance

Summary: Grout is more porous and vulnerable than most glazed tile surfaces, so it needs correct cleaning and realistic maintenance expectations.

### Q111: Why does grout get dirty faster than tiles?

Answer: Grout is often more porous and slightly recessed compared with tile, so it can collect dirt, oils, soap residue and moisture.

### Q112: How should grout be cleaned routinely?

Answer: Use a suitable tile and grout cleaner with a soft or nylon brush. Avoid harsh acid or abrasive methods unless the product instructions allow them.

### Q113: Can dirty grout be restored?

Answer: Many grout stains can be improved with correct cleaning, but deep staining, mould, cracking or damaged grout may require repair or replacement.

### Q114: Should grout be sealed?

Answer: Some cement-based grout may benefit from sealing, especially in stain-prone areas. The need depends on the grout type, area and manufacturer instructions.

### Q115: Does grout sealer make grout waterproof?

Answer: No. Grout sealer can help reduce staining and water absorption, but it is not waterproofing and does not replace a proper waterproofing system.

### Q116: Why does grout crack?

Answer: Grout can crack because of movement, poor installation, insufficient joint depth, wrong grout, lack of movement joints or substrate issues.

### Q117: Can cracked grout be fixed with cleaning?

Answer: No. Cleaning cannot fix cracked grout. The cause should be assessed before repair.

### Q118: Can grout colour change after cleaning?

Answer: Cleaning may reveal the original colour or expose uneven staining. Harsh chemicals can also alter grout appearance.

### Q119: What add-on should be suggested with grout?

Answer: A suitable grout sealer, grout cleaner and neutral tile cleaner can be suggested where appropriate to support long-term maintenance.

### Q120: When should grout maintenance be escalated?

Answer: Escalate for cracked, powdery, missing, mouldy, heavily stained or wet grout, especially in showers, balconies, pools or commercial floors.

## Silicone and Sealant Maintenance

Summary: Silicone joints are flexible maintenance items and must be inspected, cleaned and replaced when they fail.

### Q121: What is silicone used for in tiled areas?

Answer: Silicone sealant is used at movement-prone joints such as corners, wall-floor junctions, sanitaryware edges and some perimeter joints.

### Q122: Is silicone the same as grout?

Answer: No. Silicone is flexible sealant, while grout is a rigid joint filler. Silicone is used where movement is expected.

### Q123: Can silicone be cleaned?

Answer: Yes, silicone can be cleaned gently with compatible products, but harsh scrubbing or unsuitable chemicals can damage it.

### Q124: Why does silicone go mouldy?

Answer: Silicone can develop mould due to moisture, soap residue, poor ventilation, standing water or ageing.

### Q125: Can mouldy silicone be restored permanently by cleaning?

Answer: Surface mould may improve with cleaning, but deeply stained or failed silicone often needs removal and replacement.

### Q126: Is silicone permanent?

Answer: No. Silicone is a maintenance item and may need replacement over time, especially in showers and wet areas.

### Q127: Can cracked silicone cause leaks?

Answer: Failed silicone can allow water into vulnerable joints, but leaks may also involve waterproofing, plumbing or installation issues.

### Q128: Should customers buy silicone with tiles?

Answer: For bathrooms, showers, kitchens and movement joints, suitable silicone or matching sealant should be considered as part of the installation system.

### Q129: What add-on should be suggested for bathroom tile jobs?

Answer: A compatible sanitary-grade silicone, grout, tile cleaner and aftercare products should be suggested so the installation is completed with the correct accessories.

### Q130: When should silicone issues be escalated?

Answer: Escalate if silicone failure is linked to leaks, swelling, loose tiles, cracked grout, persistent mould or water escaping from showers.

## Sealers and Protective Treatments

Summary: Sealers can help protect certain grout and porous materials, but most glazed ceramic and many porcelain tiles do not need surface sealing.

### Q131: Do porcelain tiles need sealing?

Answer: Most glazed porcelain tiles do not require sealing, but some polished, unglazed, textured or micro-porous porcelain products may need special treatment according to the manufacturer.

### Q132: Do ceramic tiles need sealing?

Answer: Most glazed ceramic tiles do not need surface sealing. Unglazed ceramic, porous decorative tiles or grout may need protection depending on the product.

### Q133: Does grout need sealing?

Answer: Some cement-based grout may benefit from sealing in stain-prone areas. Epoxy grout and some advanced grouts may not need the same sealing approach.

### Q134: Does sealer make tiles stain-proof?

Answer: No. Sealer improves resistance to staining and absorption but does not make a surface stain-proof, waterproof or maintenance-free.

### Q135: Can sealer change the look of tiles?

Answer: Some sealers can darken, enhance or alter the surface appearance. Always test first.

### Q136: Can sealer be used on polished porcelain?

Answer: Only use a sealer if the tile manufacturer or sealer supplier recommends it for that specific polished porcelain surface.

### Q137: Can sealer solve cleaning problems?

Answer: Sealer cannot fix grout haze, dirt residue, installation defects or chemical damage. The surface must be correctly cleaned before sealing.

### Q138: Should customers seal before or after cleaning?

Answer: Surfaces must be clean and dry before sealing. Sealing over dirt or grout haze can trap the problem under the sealer.

### Q139: What add-on should be suggested with cement grout?

Answer: A compatible grout sealer may be suggested where stain resistance is important, such as kitchens, bathrooms and high-use areas.

### Q140: When should sealing questions be escalated?

Answer: Escalate for natural stone, polished porcelain, unglazed tiles, outdoor areas, wet areas, old stains, unknown sealers or conflicting product instructions.

## Stain Prevention and Stain Removal

Summary: Stains should be identified by source before cleaning because oil, cement, rust, dye, tannin and limescale require different solutions.

### Q141: What causes stains on tile installations?

Answer: Stains can come from grout haze, dirty water, oils, rust, hard water, leaf tannins, dyes, paint, silicone, cleaning chemicals or porous grout.

### Q142: Are porcelain tiles stain-proof?

Answer: Porcelain tiles are generally stain-resistant, but no surface should be treated as stain-proof. Grout, texture, polished surfaces and residue can still hold stains.

### Q143: Why is stain identification important?

Answer: Different stains need different cleaning chemistry. Using the wrong cleaner can fail or cause damage.

### Q144: What should be done after a spill?

Answer: Remove the spill promptly, clean with a suitable product and avoid letting oils, dyes, cement residue or acidic substances sit on the surface.

### Q145: Can rust stains be removed from tiles?

Answer: Rust may require a specialist rust remover compatible with the tile and grout. Test first and avoid unsuitable acids on sensitive materials.

### Q146: Can oil stains be removed from grout?

Answer: Oil can penetrate cement grout. A compatible degreaser or poultice-type treatment may help, but deep stains can be difficult to remove completely.

### Q147: Can leaf stains affect outdoor tiles?

Answer: Yes. Leaves and organic matter can leave tannin stains, especially if left wet on the surface. Regular sweeping helps prevent staining.

### Q148: Can paint or silicone residue be cleaned off tiles?

Answer: Specialist residue removers may be needed. The correct product depends on the residue and tile finish.

### Q149: What add-on helps with stain prevention?

Answer: A grout sealer, neutral cleaner and suitable deep-clean product can help reduce future staining where appropriate.

### Q150: When should stain questions be escalated?

Answer: Escalate if the stain source is unknown, the tile is natural stone or polished, chemicals were used, or the stain may relate to product or installation defects.

## Cleaning Product Safety and Compatibility

Summary: Cleaning products must be compatible with the tile, grout, sealers, silicone, trims and surrounding materials.

### Q151: Why should customers read cleaning product instructions?

Answer: Instructions explain dilution, dwell time, rinsing, surface compatibility and safety precautions. Incorrect use can damage surfaces or leave residue.

### Q152: Why should cleaners be tested first?

Answer: Testing helps confirm that the cleaner does not discolour, etch, dull or damage the tile, grout, sealer or trim.

### Q153: Can different cleaners be mixed?

Answer: No. Cleaning chemicals should not be mixed because reactions can be dangerous and may damage surfaces.

### Q154: Can bleach and acid cleaner be mixed?

Answer: No. Bleach and acid cleaner must never be mixed because dangerous gases can form.

### Q155: Are abrasive powders safe for tile?

Answer: Abrasive powders can scratch or dull some surfaces, especially polished and glazed finishes. Use only if the manufacturer confirms suitability.

### Q156: Can metal scourers be used on tile?

Answer: Metal scourers should generally be avoided because they can scratch surfaces, leave metal marks or damage grout.

### Q157: Can steam cleaners be used on tiles?

Answer: Steam may be suitable in some cases, but it can affect grout, sealers, silicone, resilient flooring or damaged joints. Follow product and equipment guidance.

### Q158: Can wax or polish be applied to porcelain tile?

Answer: Wax or polish is generally not recommended on porcelain or ceramic tiles because it can create residue, attract dirt and affect slip performance.

### Q159: What add-on is safest for routine maintenance?

Answer: A neutral tile cleaner is the safest general add-on for routine ceramic and porcelain tile maintenance.

### Q160: When should cleaner compatibility be escalated?

Answer: Escalate when the tile type is unknown, natural stone is involved, harsh chemicals were used, or the customer reports etching, dulling, staining or slip concerns.

## Efflorescence and Mineral Deposits

Summary: Efflorescence and mineral deposits are usually moisture-related residue issues that require correct diagnosis before cleaning.

### Q161: What is efflorescence?

Answer: Efflorescence is a whitish mineral deposit that can appear when moisture moves soluble salts to the surface and then evaporates.

### Q162: Is efflorescence a tile defect?

Answer: Efflorescence is usually linked to moisture movement, substrate conditions, grout, mortar or site conditions rather than the tile surface itself.

### Q163: Where is efflorescence common?

Answer: It is more common in exterior areas, balconies, damp substrates, retaining walls, pool areas and areas with poor drainage or moisture movement.

### Q164: Can efflorescence be cleaned off?

Answer: Some efflorescence can be removed with dry brushing or suitable cleaners, but the moisture source must be addressed to reduce recurrence.

### Q165: Can acid cleaner remove efflorescence?

Answer: Some mineral deposits may respond to acidic cleaners, but product compatibility must be checked and natural stone or sensitive materials must be protected.

### Q166: Why does efflorescence come back?

Answer: It can return if moisture continues to move through the substrate, grout or installation system.

### Q167: Can sealing stop efflorescence?

Answer: Sealing may not solve efflorescence and can sometimes trap moisture if used incorrectly. The cause must be assessed first.

### Q168: Can outdoor tiles show white marks after rain?

Answer: Yes. White marks may be mineral deposits, efflorescence, cement residue or hard-water deposits. Correct diagnosis is needed.

### Q169: What add-on is useful for mineral residue?

Answer: A compatible mineral deposit or cement residue remover may be useful, but it must be matched to the surface and tested first.

### Q170: When should efflorescence be escalated?

Answer: Escalate for recurring deposits, balconies, waterproofed areas, pool surrounds, damp walls, exterior failures or suspected moisture movement.

## Maintenance by Room Type

Summary: Different rooms expose tiles to different soils, so cleaning advice should match the actual use of the area.

### Q171: How should kitchen floor tiles be maintained?

Answer: Sweep regularly and clean with a neutral tile cleaner. Use a compatible degreaser periodically if cooking oils or heavy grease build up.

### Q172: How should kitchen splashback tiles be cleaned?

Answer: Remove grease regularly with a compatible cleaner and soft cloth. Avoid abrasive pads that can scratch glossy or decorative surfaces.

### Q173: How should entrance tiles be maintained?

Answer: Use mats to reduce grit, sweep often and clean with a suitable tile cleaner. Entrances usually need more frequent maintenance than low-use rooms.

### Q174: How should living-room tiles be maintained?

Answer: Routine sweeping and neutral cleaning are normally enough, provided no waxes, oils or residue-forming products are used.

### Q175: How should bedroom tiles be maintained?

Answer: Bedroom tiles usually need light routine cleaning, but grit should still be removed to protect the surface and grout.

### Q176: How should commercial tile areas be maintained?

Answer: Commercial areas need a planned cleaning schedule, suitable cleaning products and trained cleaners to manage traffic, residue and slip risk.

### Q177: How should tiles in pet areas be cleaned?

Answer: Clean spills promptly with a suitable tile cleaner. Grout may stain if urine or organic spills are left for long periods.

### Q178: How should tiles in braai areas be maintained?

Answer: Remove grease, ash and food spills promptly. A compatible degreaser may be needed periodically, especially on outdoor textured tiles.

### Q179: What add-ons should be suggested for kitchens and entrances?

Answer: A neutral cleaner, periodic degreaser, grout sealer where appropriate and good entrance matting should be considered.

### Q180: When should room-specific cleaning be escalated?

Answer: Escalate for commercial slip concerns, recurring stains, chemical damage, severe grease, grout failure, outdoor algae or unknown tile types.

## Common Cleaning Complaints and Troubleshooting

Summary: Many cleaning complaints are caused by residue, wrong products, site dirt or installation residue rather than tile defects.

### Q181: Why does a new tile floor still look dirty after mopping?

Answer: Common causes include grout haze, detergent residue, dirty mop water, construction dust, hard-water deposits or textured surfaces holding residue.

### Q182: Why are there footprints on new tiles?

Answer: Footprints can be caused by polished surfaces, detergent residue, grout haze, dust or oils. Correct cleaning and rinsing usually help.

### Q183: Why does the floor feel sticky?

Answer: Sticky floors often result from too much detergent, poor rinsing, dirty mop water or incompatible cleaning products.

### Q184: Why does grout look uneven after installation?

Answer: Uneven grout colour can result from inconsistent water use, cleaning too early, different drying conditions, residue or installation technique.

### Q185: Why are there white marks after cleaning?

Answer: White marks can be detergent residue, cement haze, hard-water deposits, efflorescence or grout residue. Identify the source before choosing a cleaner.

### Q186: Why does the floor look cloudy in sunlight?

Answer: Sunlight can reveal grout haze, cleaning residue, streaks or polishing marks that are less visible in normal light.

### Q187: Can cleaning remove scratches?

Answer: No. Cleaning can remove residue and dirt, but it cannot remove actual scratches or surface damage.

### Q188: Can cleaning fix lippage or uneven tiles?

Answer: No. Lippage or uneven tile height is an installation issue, not a cleaning issue.

### Q189: What should the chatbot ask when troubleshooting cleaning complaints?

Answer: Ask what tile type and finish it is, when it was installed, what grout was used, what cleaning products were used, and whether the residue appeared before or after cleaning.

### Q190: When should cleaning complaints be escalated?

Answer: Escalate when the complaint involves possible defects, scratches, chemical damage, slip incidents, installation failure, leaks, heavy haze or unresolved staining.
