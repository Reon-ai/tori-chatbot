# Tiletoria RAG Utility File 08: Budget Drivers and Project Cost Explainers

This RAG-ready file helps the chatbot explain why renovation projects cost more than the main product price. It is designed for South African consumers buying tiles, glue-down Luxury Vinyl Tile, sanitaryware, baths, taps, showers, adhesives, grout, trims, waterproofing and related installation products.

Source basis: TCNA tile installation guidance on movement joints and exterior mortar contact, Ceramic Tile Education Foundation guidance on large-format substrate flatness, Polyflor subfloor and moisture-preparation guidance for vinyl flooring, SANS 10400 Part P drainage and plumbing context for South African bathroom work, and manufacturer technical data-sheet practice. This document does not provide live pricing, labour rates or quotations.

Important chatbot rule: The chatbot may explain cost drivers, but it must not invent current prices, stock levels, labour rates, delivery charges, discounts, installer pricing or quote validity. It must guide customers to confirm actual pricing with Tiletoria and labour costs with their installer.

Chatbot answer: “The tile, LVT or sanitaryware price is only one part of the budget. Preparation, installation products, trims, plumbing, waterproofing and labour can change the final cost.”

## Consumer Budget Conversation Template

Summary: The chatbot should help consumers think through budget without pretending to be a live quoting system.

Template:
“To budget properly, separate the project into product, installation materials, preparation, labour and finishing items. For your project, please confirm the area size, product type, substrate condition, wet or dry use, indoor or outdoor use, layout pattern and required accessories. Tiletoria can confirm product pricing, while your installer should confirm labour and site-preparation costs.”

Use when customer asks:
“How much will this cost?”
“What else must I budget for?”
“Why is this more expensive?”
“Is large format more expensive?”

Giving labour rates.
Promising cheapest solution.
