# Grout Quantity Estimator

## Fast answer

Grout quantity depends on tile size, tile thickness, joint width and area. Small tiles/mosaics and wider joints use more grout.

## Required inputs

- Area in m²
- Tile length and width
- Tile thickness
- Joint width
- Bag size
- Waste allowance

## Calculation rule

Use the grout manufacturer's formula/calculator where available. General principle: smaller tiles, thicker tiles and wider joints increase grout demand. Round up to full bags.

## Worked example

Two bathrooms can have the same m² but different grout usage: 600x600 with 3 mm joints uses far less grout than mosaics with many joints. Ask for tile size and joint width before estimating.

## Answer format

State the estimate, show the assumptions, round up to full sellable units, and warn that final quantities depend on site conditions and product datasheets.

## Warnings

- Do not estimate grout from m² alone when tile size/joint width is unknown.
- Do not claim grout waterproofs a shower.

## Do not say or assume

- Do not guarantee the quantity as exact.
- Do not ignore product datasheets, pack sizes, site conditions or workmanship.

## Escalate to a human when

- The customer asks for a guaranteed quantity.
- The project is commercial, exterior, wet-area, pool, balcony, failed installation or legally sensitive.
- The inputs are incomplete or suspicious.
