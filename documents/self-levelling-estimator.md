# Self-Levelling Compound Estimator

## Fast answer

Self-levelling quantity depends on area, average thickness and product yield. Moisture, DPM and substrate preparation must be checked first.

## Required inputs

- Area in m²
- Average thickness in mm
- Product yield per bag at stated thickness
- Substrate type
- Moisture/DPM condition

## Calculation rule

Estimated bags = area × required thickness ÷ product yield. Use the actual product datasheet yield. Add waste and round up.

## Worked example

Example only: if product covers 5 m² at 3 mm and the area is 20 m² at 3 mm, bags = 20 ÷ 5 = 4 bags, then round/add waste as required.

## Answer format

State the estimate, show the assumptions, round up to full sellable units, and warn that final quantities depend on site conditions and product datasheets.

## Warnings

- Do not use self-levelling to fix structural cracks or damp.
- Do not ignore DPM/moisture testing, especially before LVT/laminate.

## Do not say or assume

- Do not guarantee the quantity as exact.
- Do not ignore product datasheets, pack sizes, site conditions or workmanship.

## Escalate to a human when

- The customer asks for a guaranteed quantity.
- The project is commercial, exterior, wet-area, pool, balcony, failed installation or legally sensitive.
- The inputs are incomplete or suspicious.
