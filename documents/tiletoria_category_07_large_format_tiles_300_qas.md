## Substrate Flatness and Floor Preparation

Summary: This section explains why substrate flatness is the most important technical requirement for successful large-format tile installation.

Question ID: Q041

Question: Why does the floor need to be very flat for large-format tiles?

Answer: Large-format tiles bridge over high and low spots more than smaller tiles. If the substrate is not flat, the tile edges can sit unevenly, adhesive coverage can be poor and lippage can become visible. The floor should be checked and corrected before installation.

Risk level: Medium

Escalation: No, unless site conditions are uncertain

Question ID: Q041

Question: What does substrate mean?

Answer: The substrate is the surface that the tile is installed onto, such as a concrete slab, screed, wall, existing tiled surface or prepared board system. For large-format tiles, the substrate must be flat, stable, clean, dry and suitable for tiling.

Risk level: Medium

Escalation: No, unless site conditions are uncertain

Question ID: Q041

Question: What is the recommended flatness for large-format tile installation?

Answer: International ANSI guidance commonly requires a flatter substrate for tiles with at least one edge of 15 inches, about 381 mm, or greater: no more than 1/8 inch variation in 10 feet and no more than 1/16 inch in 24 inches. In metric terms, this is roughly 3 mm in 3 m and 1.5 mm in 600 mm. Local project specifications and product instructions should still be followed.

Risk level: Medium

Escalation: No, unless site conditions are uncertain

Question ID: Q041

Question: Can adhesive be used to fix an uneven floor under large-format tiles?

Answer: Tile adhesive should not be used as a general floor-levelling product. Minor bedding adjustments may occur during installation, but an uneven floor should be corrected with suitable preparation products before tiling. This is especially important for large-format tiles.

Risk level: Medium

Escalation: No, unless site conditions are uncertain

Question ID: Q041

Question: What happens if large-format tiles are installed on an uneven floor?

## Adhesive Coverage, Trowels and Back-Buttering

Summary: This section explains adhesive selection and application principles for large-format tiles without replacing manufacturer instructions or installer responsibility.

Question ID: Q061

Question: Why is adhesive coverage important for large-format tiles?

Answer: Large-format tiles need strong, even support under the tile. Poor adhesive coverage can leave voids, which may lead to hollow sounds, cracked corners, loose tiles or failure under load. Correct trowel technique and adhesive selection are essential.

Risk level: High

Escalation: Yes

Question ID: Q061

Question: What adhesive coverage is normally expected under tiles?

Answer: Industry guidance commonly expects high mortar contact, with higher requirements in wet, exterior and demanding areas. Interior dry areas are often referenced at a lower minimum than wet or exterior areas, but large-format tiles should be installed to the adhesive manufacturer's instructions and project specification.

Risk level: Medium

Escalation: No, unless site conditions are uncertain

Question ID: Q061

Answer: Wet and outdoor areas generally require very high adhesive contact because voids can trap water and create failure risk. Escalation: No, unless site conditions are uncertain

## Lippage, Warpage and Grout Joints

Summary: This section explains visible edge variation, natural tile tolerances and why grout joints remain necessary with large-format tiles.

Question ID: Q081

Question: What is tile lippage?

Answer: Lippage is when one tile edge sits higher than the edge of the neighbouring tile. Large-format tiles can make lippage more noticeable because the tile surface is bigger and often installed with narrower grout joints.

Risk level: Medium

Escalation: No, unless site conditions are uncertain

Question ID: Q081

Question: Why is lippage more visible with large-format tiles?

Answer: Lippage is more visible because large tiles span a bigger area, and any substrate unevenness or natural tile curvature can show at the edges. Light from windows or downlights can also highlight small height differences.

Risk level: Medium

Escalation: No, unless site conditions are uncertain

Question ID: Q081

Question: Can lippage be completely avoided?

Answer: A good installer can minimise lippage, but the chatbot should not promise zero lippage. Some variation may result from tile manufacturing tolerance, substrate flatness, layout pattern, grout joint width and installation technique.

Risk level: Medium

Escalation: No, unless site conditions are uncertain

Question ID: Q081

Question: What causes lippage in large-format tiles?

Answer: Common causes include uneven substrate, incorrect adhesive coverage, narrow grout joints, unsuitable layout, natural tile warpage, poor trowel technique, rushing the installation or not using appropriate levelling aids correctly.

Risk level: Medium

Escalation: No, unless site conditions are uncertain

Question ID: Q081

Question: What is tile warpage?

Answer: Tile warpage is the natural slight curvature or shape variation that can occur within manufacturing tolerances. It can become more noticeable on longer tiles, especially with plank tiles or offset patterns.

Risk level: Low
