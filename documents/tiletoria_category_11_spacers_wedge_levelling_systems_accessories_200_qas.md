# Category 11: Spacers, Wedge Levelling Systems and Tile Installation Accessories

This RAG-ready document contains customer support questions and answers for tile spacers, wedge-and-clip levelling systems, large-format tile support accessories and related installation add-ons. The answers are written for Tiletoria in South African English, with clear risk control around installer responsibility, substrate preparation, lippage, grout joints and quantity calculations.

Source basis: Tile Council of North America guidance on lippage, grout joints and large-format tile; Ceramic Tile Education Foundation guidance on flat substrates and lippage; ANSI A108/A118/A136 ceramic tile installation principles; reputable manufacturer guidance from Raimondi and RUBI on wedge-and-clip levelling systems; recognised large-format tile installation guidance from adhesive and tile-system manufacturers.

## Spacer Basics
Summary: Tile spacers help create consistent grout joints but do not replace correct layout, substrate preparation or installer skill.

Q001: What are tile spacers?
A: Tile spacers are small plastic pieces placed between tiles during installation to help keep the grout joints consistent. They are installation aids only and do not correct poor substrate preparation, incorrect layout or poor workmanship.

Q002: Why are tile spacers used?
A: Tile spacers help maintain even spacing between tiles so that the finished grout joints look consistent. They also help the installer keep the layout controlled while the adhesive cures.

Q003: Do tile spacers decide the final grout joint width?
A: Tile spacers help set the grout joint width, but the final result also depends on tile size variation, rectification, installation accuracy and the installer’s layout. The installer must still check the installation continuously.

Q004: Can tiles be installed without spacers?
A: Tiles should not be installed with no joint. Even rectified tiles need grout joints to allow for size tolerance, movement, grout filling and proper finishing. Spacers are a practical way to help maintain those joints.

Q005: Are spacers only for floor tiles?
A: No. Spacers can be used for both wall and floor tiles. The correct spacer size depends on the tile type, edge finish, size, layout and application.

Q006: Are spacers reusable?
A: Some loose spacers may be reusable if removed cleanly before grouting and not damaged. Clips used in wedge-and-clip levelling systems are normally not reusable because they break off during removal.

Q007: What happens if spacers are left too deep in the joint?
A: Spacers left too deep can interfere with grout depth and may weaken the grout joint appearance or performance. The installer should remove reusable spacers before grouting unless the specific product is designed to remain in place.

Q008: Are spacers the same as levelling clips?
A: No. Standard spacers mainly control joint width. Levelling clips are part of a wedge-and-clip system that helps reduce lippage between neighbouring tiles while the adhesive cures.

Q009: Do spacers make an installation professional?
A: No. Spacers help with consistency, but a professional result depends on substrate flatness, layout, adhesive coverage, correct trowel technique, grout joints, movement joints and installer skill.

Q010: Should customers buy spacers with their tiles?
A: Yes. Spacers are a small but important installation accessory. When buying tiles, it is sensible to confirm the recommended spacer size, grout joint width and related add-ons such as grout, adhesive, trims and levelling clips.

## Spacer Sizes and Grout Joint Widths
Summary: Spacer size must be selected according to the tile type, edge style, installation area and manufacturer or installer recommendation.

Q011: What size spacer should I use for tiles?
A: The spacer size depends on the tile format, edge type, calibration, application and design. Rectified tiles may allow narrower joints than pressed-edge tiles, but all tiles still need a grout joint.

Q012: Can I use a 1 millimetre spacer?
A: A 1 millimetre joint may be too narrow for many installations and may not allow enough tolerance for tile variation, adhesive movement or grout filling. Confirm with the tile specification and installer before choosing very narrow joints.

Q013: Can I use a 2 millimetre spacer for rectified tiles?
A: A 2 millimetre spacer is commonly used with some rectified tiles, but it is not automatically correct for every tile or site. The installer must check tile size tolerance, substrate condition, layout and grout suitability.

Q014: Can I use a 3 millimetre spacer for floor tiles?
A: A 3 millimetre joint is commonly used for many floor tiles and can give the installer more tolerance than very narrow joints. The correct size still depends on the tile and site conditions.

Q015: Do non-rectified tiles need wider spacers?
A: Usually yes. Non-rectified or pressed-edge tiles normally need wider grout joints to accommodate normal manufacturing size variation and edge tolerance.

Q016: Do outdoor tiles need wider joints?
A: Outdoor installations often need more conservative grout and movement-joint planning because of temperature changes, moisture exposure and movement. The installer must confirm the correct joint width and system for the product and site.

Q017: Do large-format tiles need narrow grout joints?
A: Large-format tiles often look best with neat, consistent joints, but the joint must not be too narrow for the tile tolerance, substrate and installation. Narrow joints require a flatter substrate and higher installer precision.

Q018: Can grout joint width hide small tile size differences?
A: Yes. A slightly wider grout joint can help manage normal size tolerance, especially with pressed-edge tiles. Very narrow joints leave less room for adjustment and can make lippage or size variation more visible.

Q019: Should the grout joint be chosen before installation starts?
A: Yes. The grout joint width should be agreed before installation starts. It affects layout, spacer choice, levelling clip choice, grout quantity, final appearance and customer expectations.

Q020: Can Tiletoria help choose spacer size?
A: Tiletoria can guide customers on common spacer sizes and product-related considerations, but the final spacer and joint selection must be confirmed by the installer according to the tile, substrate and site.

## Cross Spacers, T-Spacers and Horseshoe Spacers
Summary: Different spacer shapes are used for different layouts, edges and installation situations.

Q021: What is a cross spacer?
A: A cross spacer is a four-way spacer used where four tile corners meet. It is common in straight grid layouts.

Q022: What is a T-spacer?
A: A T-spacer is a three-way spacer used at staggered joints, edges or layouts where only three tile edges meet. It is useful for brick-bond or offset patterns.

Q023: What is a horseshoe spacer?
A: A horseshoe spacer is a U-shaped spacer often used for wall tiles, vertical adjustments, heavy tiles or situations where the spacer must be removed more easily.

Q024: Which spacer is best for a straight lay pattern?
A: Cross spacers are commonly used for straight lay patterns because four tile corners meet at the same point. The installer may also use loose spacers or levelling clips depending on the tile size.

Q025: Which spacer is best for a staggered pattern?
A: T-spacers are often used in staggered patterns because the joints do not always meet in a four-way cross. Levelling clips may also be used with larger tiles.

Q026: Can I break cross spacers to make T-spacers?
A: Some installers do this on site, but it is better to buy the correct spacer type where possible. Purpose-made spacers are more consistent and reduce installation frustration.

Q027: Are horseshoe spacers better for wall tiles?
A: Horseshoe spacers can be very useful on walls because they are easy to insert, adjust and remove. The installer should choose the spacer type that best suits the tile and layout.

Q028: Do levelling clips replace cross spacers?
A: Some levelling clips create a set joint width, so they can partly perform a spacing function. However, installers may still use additional spacers at corners, edges or detail areas.

Q029: Can different spacer types be used in one installation?
A: Yes. An installer may use cross spacers, T-spacers, horseshoe spacers and levelling clips in the same project depending on the layout and tile format.

Q030: Should I buy extra spacers?
A: Yes. Spacers are inexpensive and easy to lose or damage on site. It is practical to buy enough spacers for the installer to work efficiently without stopping.

## Wedge-and-Clip Levelling System Basics
Summary: A wedge-and-clip levelling system helps reduce lippage between tiles while adhesive cures, but it does not make an uneven floor flat.

Q031: What is a wedge-and-clip tile levelling system?
A: A wedge-and-clip levelling system uses disposable clips and reusable wedges to help hold neighbouring tile edges closer to the same plane while the adhesive cures. It is especially useful for large-format tiles.

Q032: What does the clip do in a levelling system?
A: The clip sits under the tile edges and between the tiles. It creates the joint spacing and provides a bridge for the wedge to pull neighbouring tile edges into alignment.

Q033: What does the wedge do in a levelling system?
A: The wedge slides through the clip and applies pressure to help bring adjacent tile edges into better alignment. The wedge is normally removed after curing and reused.

Q034: Are wedges reusable?
A: Yes. In most wedge-and-clip systems, wedges are reusable if they are not damaged or contaminated with hardened adhesive. This is why wedge quantity is calculated differently from clip quantity.

Q035: Are clips reusable?
A: No. Levelling clips are normally single-use because the top part is broken off after the adhesive cures. The base remains under the tile edge in the adhesive bed.

Q036: Does a levelling system make tiles level?
A: Not by itself. A levelling system helps align tile edges, but it does not correct an uneven or unsuitable substrate. The floor or wall must still be properly prepared and flat.

Q037: Does a levelling system prevent all lippage?
A: No. It helps reduce lippage, but lippage can still occur if the substrate is not flat, the adhesive coverage is poor, the tile has warpage, the layout is unsuitable or the installer uses the system incorrectly.

Q038: Is a levelling system only for large-format tiles?
A: Levelling systems are most useful on large-format tiles, rectified tiles and layouts where lippage would be more visible. They can also be used on some smaller formats, depending on the installer’s preference.

Q039: Should every large-format tile installation use levelling clips?
A: Levelling clips are strongly worth considering for large-format tiles, but they are not a substitute for proper preparation. A Tiletoria consultant can help customers add the right clips, wedges and pliers to the tile order.

Q040: Can a beginner use a wedge-and-clip system?
A: A wedge-and-clip system can help, but it does not replace tiling skill. DIY users should be cautious with large-format tiles because substrate flatness, adhesive coverage and handling are critical.

## Lippage and Levelling Systems
Summary: Levelling systems help control lippage, but lippage is also affected by tile warpage, substrate flatness, layout and lighting.

Q041: What is tile lippage?
A: Lippage is the difference in height between the edges of neighbouring tiles. It can affect appearance, cleaning and underfoot comfort.

Q042: Why is lippage more visible on large-format tiles?
A: Large-format tiles cover more area and have fewer grout joints, so small differences in height can become more noticeable. Lighting from windows or downlights can also make lippage more visible.

Q043: Can clips remove lippage caused by a bad screed?
A: No. Clips cannot fix a bad screed or uneven substrate. The substrate must be made flat before tiling, especially for large-format tiles.

Q044: Can clips remove lippage caused by tile warpage?
A: Clips can help reduce visible lippage, but they cannot eliminate all effects of tile warpage. Layout, grout joint width and offset pattern must also be planned correctly.

Q045: Do levelling systems remove the need for back-buttering?
A: No. Levelling systems do not replace correct adhesive application. Large-format tiles often need proper adhesive coverage and back-buttering where specified by the installer or adhesive manufacturer.

Q046: Can too much wedge pressure cause problems?
A: Yes. Over-tightening wedges can move tiles, squeeze adhesive unevenly, damage edges or create stress. The system should be tightened firmly but carefully according to manufacturer guidance.

Q047: Can levelling clips lift tiles away from adhesive?
A: Incorrect use can disturb adhesive contact. The installer must bed the tile correctly first, then use the levelling system to align edges without compromising adhesive coverage.

Q048: Should lippage be discussed before installation?
A: Yes. Customers and installers should agree on layout, joint width, tile variation, substrate preparation and expected finish before work starts. This reduces disputes after installation.

Q049: Does a levelling system guarantee a perfect floor?
A: No. It is an aid, not a guarantee. A successful installation still depends on the tile, substrate, adhesive, joint width, layout, movement joints and installer skill.

Q050: How should the chatbot explain lippage safely?
A: The chatbot should say that levelling systems help reduce lippage but do not guarantee a perfectly flat installation. Site conditions and installer workmanship remain critical.

## Clip and Wedge Ratio Principles
Summary: Clips are calculated against the tile layout, while wedges are calculated against the installer’s daily working area because wedges are normally reusable.

Q051: Why are clip and wedge quantities confusing?
A: Clips and wedges are calculated differently. Clips are normally consumed in the installation, while wedges are reusable and only need to cover the amount of tiling being set before the clips are broken off.

Q052: Are clips calculated for the whole job?
A: Yes. Clips are normally calculated for the total tile area or the total number of tile edges that need levelling because each clip is single-use.

Q053: Are wedges calculated for the whole job?
A: Not usually. Wedges are reusable, so the wedge quantity is usually based on how many clips the installer will use in one working cycle or one day, plus a practical allowance.

Q054: What is the simple rule for clips?
A: Buy enough clips for the full installation area, plus extra for cuts, waste, breakages and layout complexity. Clips are usually single-use.

Q055: What is the simple rule for wedges?
A: Buy enough wedges for the maximum number of clips the installer expects to use before removing and reusing wedges. This usually relates to daily production, not the total project size.

Q056: Why can the same wedges be used again?
A: After the adhesive has cured and the clips are knocked off, the wedges are removed and reused on the next section. This is why wedge packs can cover multiple days of work.

Q057: Does every clip need a wedge?
A: Every active clip normally needs a wedge while that section is curing. However, the same wedge can be reused after the clip is removed on a later section.

Q058: Is a 1:1 clip-to-wedge ratio required?
A: A 1:1 ratio is only required for the active working area at one time, not necessarily for the whole project. For the full project, the number of clips is usually much higher than the number of wedges.

Q059: Why do some kits have equal clips and wedges?
A: Starter kits often include equal clips and wedges for convenience. For larger projects, customers usually buy extra clips and keep reusing the wedges.

Q060: What should Tiletoria explain to customers about ratios?
A: Tiletoria should explain that clips are consumables and wedges are reusable tools. The customer should buy clips for the project size and wedges for the installer’s daily working capacity.

## Estimating Clips per Square Metre
Summary: Clip consumption depends on tile size, layout, clip spacing and whether clips are placed on edges, corners or both.

Q061: How do I calculate how many clips I need?
A: Clip quantity depends on tile size, layout, clip spacing and the number of tile edges. The safest approach is to use the levelling system supplier’s calculator or ask the installer to calculate from the actual layout.

Q062: Can clips per square metre be fixed as one number?
A: No. Clips per square metre change significantly between tile sizes. A 600 x 600 millimetre tile and a 600 x 1200 millimetre tile will not use clips in exactly the same way.

Q063: Why do smaller tiles use more clips per square metre?
A: Smaller tiles create more tile edges and joints per square metre, so they often require more clips per square metre than larger tiles.

Q064: Why do larger tiles still need many clips?
A: Larger tiles have longer edges and are more sensitive to lippage, so installers may place several clips along each edge. The exact quantity depends on the format and method.

Q065: How many clips are needed for 600 x 600 millimetre tiles?
A: The exact number depends on layout and clip spacing. As a practical guide, customers should ask the installer or Tiletoria consultant to calculate clips from the actual tile size, room layout and installation method.

Q066: How many clips are needed for 600 x 1200 millimetre tiles?
A: 600 x 1200 millimetre tiles often need multiple clips along the long edges. The exact number must be calculated from the layout and the installer’s chosen clip spacing.

Q067: How many clips are needed for 1200 x 1200 millimetre tiles?
A: 1200 x 1200 millimetre tiles require careful planning and usually several clips per tile edge. The final clip count should be calculated by the installer because handling, edge length and substrate flatness are critical.

Q068: Should cuts be included in the clip calculation?
A: Yes. Cuts, borders, doorways, passages and small areas can increase clip use. It is sensible to add a practical allowance rather than buying the exact theoretical number.

Q069: Should I buy extra clips?
A: Yes. Clips can break, be misplaced or be needed in extra areas. A small extra allowance helps prevent the installer from stopping mid-job.

Q070: Can Tiletoria help estimate clip quantities?
A: Yes. Tiletoria can help estimate clip quantities using tile size and area, but the final quantity should be checked by the installer because the layout and site conditions affect consumption.

## Estimating Wedges for Daily Production
Summary: Wedge quantity should be based on how many clips are active before removal, which usually relates to how many square metres an installer sets per day.

Q071: How do I calculate wedge quantity?
A: Estimate how many clips the installer will use in one day or one curing cycle, then buy at least that many wedges plus a practical allowance. Wedges are normally reused after the clips are removed.

Q072: Why does daily square metre output matter for wedges?
A: The installer only needs wedges for the tiles being installed before the adhesive cures. If the installer lays a smaller area per day, fewer wedges are needed than for a faster team.

Q073: Does one installer need fewer wedges than a team?
A: Usually yes. One installer generally lays fewer square metres per day than a team, so fewer wedges may be active at one time.

Q074: Does a large commercial team need more wedges?
A: Yes. A large team may install more square metres per day, meaning more clips are active at once and more wedges are required.

Q075: Should wedge quantity be based on the total project area?
A: Not directly. Total project area determines clip quantity. Wedge quantity is usually based on the maximum active area being installed before wedges can be reused.

Q076: What is the safest simple customer explanation for wedges?
A: Clips are bought for the whole job. Wedges are bought for the installer’s daily working section because they are reused after the clips are removed.

Q077: What happens if I buy too few wedges?
A: The installer may run out of wedges during the working day and may have to stop, work in smaller sections or leave some clips unused. This can slow the job down.

Q078: What happens if I buy too many wedges?
A: Extra wedges can still be reused on future jobs if they remain in good condition. For installers, owning enough wedges can improve efficiency.

Q079: Can wedges be shared between installers?
A: Yes, but only if there are enough wedges for all active clips being set at the same time. On larger jobs, sharing too few wedges can slow production.

Q080: How should Tiletoria sell wedges without overselling?
A: Tiletoria should explain that wedges are reusable and should be sized to the installer’s working pace. For efficiency, customers should rather have enough wedges for a full day’s installation than too few.

## Wedge Reuse and Care
Summary: Wedges can be reused many times if they are removed correctly, cleaned and not damaged.

Q081: How many times can wedges be reused?
A: Wedges can usually be reused many times if they are not damaged and do not have hardened adhesive that prevents proper fitting. The exact lifespan depends on product quality and site handling.

Q082: How should wedges be cleaned?
A: Wedges should be kept free of wet adhesive during installation and cleaned before adhesive hardens. Hardened adhesive can affect wedge fit and performance.

Q083: Can hardened adhesive damage wedges?
A: Hardened adhesive can make wedges difficult to insert, reduce grip or prevent smooth operation. Dirty wedges can also slow the installer down.

Q084: Can wedges break?
A: Yes. Wedges can break if they are poor quality, over-tightened, misused or removed roughly. Buying a small allowance helps avoid delays.

Q085: Should wedges be stored after the job?
A: Yes. Wedges should be kept for future installations if they are in good condition. They are part of the installer’s reusable accessory kit.

Q086: Are all wedges interchangeable?
A: Not always. Wedges are usually designed for a specific levelling system or compatible clip type. Customers should avoid mixing systems unless compatibility is confirmed.

Q087: Can I reuse wedges from another brand?
A: Only if the wedge is compatible with the clip. Different systems may have different wedge sizes, angles, grip patterns and clip openings.

Q088: Can a damaged wedge affect the tile finish?
A: Yes. A damaged or dirty wedge may apply uneven pressure or scratch delicate surfaces if used carelessly. Protective plates may be needed for sensitive surfaces.

Q089: Should installers own wedges?
A: Professional installers often keep reusable wedges and pliers as part of their tool kit. Customers normally need to buy the consumable clips for the project.

Q090: Should homeowners keep leftover wedges?
A: Yes, if the homeowner paid for them and they are clean. They may be useful for future repairs, additions or other tiling work.

## Clip Selection by Tile Thickness and Joint Size
Summary: Clips must match the tile thickness, joint width and levelling system to work correctly.

Q091: Do clips come in different sizes?
A: Yes. Clips can differ by joint width, tile thickness range, system type and application. The correct clip must match the tile and desired grout joint.

Q092: What does a 1.5 millimetre clip mean?
A: A 1.5 millimetre clip usually creates a minimum joint width of about 1.5 millimetres, depending on the system and installation. The final grout joint must still be suitable for the tile and site.

Q093: What does a 2 millimetre clip mean?
A: A 2 millimetre clip is designed to help create an approximately 2 millimetre joint while levelling tile edges. It must be compatible with the tile thickness and system wedge.

Q094: What does a 3 millimetre clip mean?
A: A 3 millimetre clip is designed to create an approximately 3 millimetre joint and is often used where a wider joint is required. It may give more tolerance than very narrow clips.

Q095: Can the clip replace the spacer?
A: Many clips act as both a spacer and a levelling base. However, extra spacers may still be needed at corners, cuts, walls and layout details.

Q096: Why must clip thickness range be checked?
A: Clips are designed for specific tile thickness ranges. If the tile is too thin or too thick for the clip, the system may not tighten correctly or may damage the installation.

Q097: Can standard clips be used on very thick tiles?
A: Not always. Thick tiles may need clips designed for that thickness. The product packaging or supplier guidance must be checked.

Q098: Can standard clips be used on thin porcelain panels?
A: Not always. Thin panels may require specialised systems and trained installers. Standard clips may not be suitable for very thin or gauged porcelain panels.

Q099: Should clip joint size match the grout joint decision?
A: Yes. The clip size should match the planned grout joint width. Customers should decide the joint width before buying clips.

Q100: Can Tiletoria help choose the correct clip?
A: Yes. Tiletoria can help match clip type to tile size, thickness and grout joint preference, but the installer should confirm compatibility before installation.

## Pliers and Tightening Tools
Summary: Levelling-system pliers improve consistency and reduce hand fatigue, especially on larger jobs.

Q101: What are levelling-system pliers?
A: Levelling-system pliers are tools used to push wedges into clips with controlled pressure. They help improve speed and consistency compared with hand-tightening.

Q102: Are pliers necessary for wedge-and-clip systems?
A: Pliers are strongly recommended for many wedge-and-clip systems, especially on larger jobs. They help apply consistent pressure and reduce installer fatigue.

Q103: Can wedges be tightened by hand?
A: Some wedges can be inserted by hand, but hand-tightening may be slower and less consistent. Pliers are usually better for professional results.

Q104: Can pliers damage tiles?
A: Incorrect use or excessive pressure can cause problems. The installer should set the pliers correctly and avoid over-tightening.

Q105: Are levelling pliers reusable?
A: Yes. Pliers are reusable tools and should be kept with the wedges for future installations.

Q106: Are all levelling pliers the same?
A: No. Some pliers are designed for floors, walls or specific clip systems. Compatibility should be confirmed before purchase.

Q107: Should a DIY customer buy pliers?
A: For a small repair, pliers may not be essential, but for large-format tiles or larger areas they are helpful. Tiletoria can recommend the most practical kit for the project size.

Q108: Do professional installers already have pliers?
A: Many professional installers have their own levelling pliers, wedges and tools. Customers should ask the installer before buying duplicate tools.

Q109: Can pliers control wedge pressure?
A: Yes. Many levelling pliers can be adjusted to control pressure. This helps reduce the risk of over-tightening.

Q110: Should Tiletoria offer pliers as an add-on?
A: Yes. When selling clips and wedges, it is helpful to mention pliers because they make the system easier and more consistent to use.

Q111: What accessories should be considered with large-format tiles?
A: Customers should consider suitable adhesive, primer if needed, spacers or levelling clips, wedges, levelling pliers, grout, silicone, trims and cleaning products.

Q112: Why are add-ons important for large-format tiles?
A: Large-format tiles are less forgiving. The correct accessories help improve adhesive coverage, joint consistency, edge alignment, finishing and maintenance.

Q113: Should levelling clips be sold with 600 x 1200 millimetre tiles?
A: They should be strongly considered because 600 x 1200 millimetre tiles have long edges where lippage can be visible. The installer should confirm the final system.

Q114: Should levelling clips be sold with 1200 x 1200 millimetre tiles?
A: Yes, they should be discussed as part of the installation system. Large square tiles need excellent substrate preparation and edge alignment.

Q115: Should levelling clips be used with rectified tiles?
A: They are often helpful because rectified tiles are usually installed with neater joints, making lippage more noticeable. The installer must still prepare the substrate correctly.

Q116: Do levelling clips reduce the need for skilled labour?
A: No. They help skilled installers achieve better edge alignment, but they do not replace experience, substrate preparation or correct adhesive technique.

Q117: What should a large-format tile quote include?
A: A practical quote should consider tiles, waste allowance, adhesive, primer where needed, grout, silicone, spacers or levelling clips, wedges, trims and appropriate cleaners.

Q118: Why should customers not buy only the tiles?
A: Tiles are only one part of the installation. Without the correct adhesive, grout, spacing and finishing accessories, the final result may be compromised.

Q119: How can the chatbot gently push add-ons?

## Substrate Flatness and Levelling Systems
Summary: Levelling clips are not a substitute for a flat, stable and properly prepared substrate.

Q121: Can levelling clips fix an uneven floor?
A: No. Levelling clips cannot fix an uneven floor. The substrate must be flat, stable, clean and suitable before tiling starts.

Q122: Why is substrate flatness important for levelling clips?
A: If the substrate has high and low spots, the clips may pull tile edges together but cannot correct the underlying unevenness. This can lead to poor adhesive contact or visible lippage.

Q123: What should be checked before using levelling clips?
A: The installer should check substrate flatness, moisture, cracks, contamination, tile size, tile thickness, layout, adhesive type and planned grout joint width.

Q124: Can self-levelling compound be needed before clips?
A: Yes. If the floor is not flat enough, a suitable smoothing or self-levelling compound may be needed before tiling. This must be assessed by the installer.

Q125: Do clips replace self-levelling compound?
A: No. Clips align tile edges during installation. Self-levelling or smoothing compounds prepare the substrate before installation.

Q126: Can clips be used over old tiles?
A: They can be used in some tile-over-tile installations, but the old tiles must be sound, clean, stable and correctly primed or prepared. The installer must confirm suitability.

Q127: Can clips help if adhesive coverage is poor?
A: No. Poor adhesive coverage is a separate failure risk. Clips do not replace correct trowel technique and full bedding of the tile.

Q128: Can clips create voids under tiles?
A: Incorrect use may disturb bedding or create uneven adhesive contact. The installer must bed each tile properly before tightening the system.

Q129: What is the main substrate warning for customers?
A: The floor or wall must be prepared before the levelling system is used. A levelling system helps alignment but does not repair the substrate.

Q130: Should Tiletoria recommend substrate assessment?
A: Yes. For large-format tiles, Tiletoria should encourage customers to have the substrate checked by a qualified installer before installation starts.

## Adhesive Coverage and Trowel Technique
Summary: Levelling systems must be used together with correct adhesive coverage and trowel technique.

Q131: Do levelling clips replace adhesive?
A: No. Adhesive bonds the tile to the substrate. Levelling clips only help align tile edges while the adhesive cures.

Q132: Do levelling clips improve adhesive coverage?
A: Not directly. Adhesive coverage depends on substrate preparation, trowel size, trowel direction, adhesive type, back-buttering and bedding technique.

Q133: Why is adhesive coverage critical with large-format tiles?
A: Large-format tiles have more surface area and are less forgiving. Poor adhesive coverage can lead to hollow sounds, cracks, debonding or edge failure.

Q134: Should large-format tiles be back-buttered?
A: Back-buttering is often recommended or required for large-format tiles to improve contact. The installer should follow the adhesive manufacturer’s guidance and site conditions.

Q135: Can clips compensate for the wrong trowel size?
A: No. The correct trowel size and notch shape must be selected to achieve proper adhesive coverage. Clips cannot compensate for insufficient adhesive.

Q136: Can clips be used with fast-setting adhesive?
A: They can be used if the adhesive and working time are suitable, but fast-setting products require speed and planning. The installer must work within the adhesive open time.

Q137: Can clips be used with large-and-heavy-tile adhesive?
A: Yes, clips are often used with large-format tile installations where suitable large-and-heavy-tile adhesive is selected. The adhesive must match the tile and substrate.

Q138: Can too much adhesive affect clips?
A: Excessive adhesive can squeeze into joints and around clips, making removal and grouting harder. The installer should clean joints before the adhesive hardens.

Q139: Should joints be cleaned before grouting?
A: Yes. Joints should be cleaned to the correct depth before grouting. Clip stems and excess adhesive should not block the grout joint.

Q140: What add-ons should be sold with levelling clips?
A: Suitable adhesive, primer if needed, grout, silicone, trowels, spacers, trims and cleaning products should be discussed with levelling clips as part of a complete installation system.

## Clip Placement and Layout
Summary: Clip placement depends on tile size, edge length, layout and installer method.

Q141: Where are clips placed?
A: Clips are placed under tile edges and between neighbouring tiles, usually along the sides where lippage control is needed. The exact placement depends on tile size and layout.

Q142: Are clips placed at tile corners?
A: Clips are often placed near corners but not necessarily exactly on the corner. The installer should follow the levelling system instructions and tile layout.

Q143: How far apart should clips be placed?
A: Clip spacing depends on tile format, edge length and manufacturer guidance. Longer tile edges usually need more clips than shorter edges.

Q144: Do 600 x 1200 millimetre tiles need clips on the long side?
A: Yes, the long edges usually need careful lippage control and may require multiple clips. The installer must decide the spacing based on the tile and substrate.

Q145: Do square large-format tiles need clips on all sides?
A: They often need clips along multiple sides to control edge alignment. The exact pattern depends on the layout and installation method.

Q146: Are clips needed at perimeter walls?
A: Clips may be used near perimeters where tiles meet other tiles, but perimeter movement gaps must not be filled rigidly. The installer must maintain proper movement allowance.

Q147: Can clips be used on cut tiles?
A: Yes, where the cut tile edge meets another tile and there is enough space to position the clip correctly. Small cuts may require different handling.

Q148: Can clips be used on steps?
A: They may be used in some step installations, but steps are high-risk areas. The installer must confirm edge treatment, nosing, slip resistance, adhesive coverage and movement requirements.

Q149: Can clips be used on walls?
A: Yes, some systems can be used on wall tiles. Wall installations require correct adhesive, support, tile weight assessment and installer skill.

Q150: Should clip placement be planned before adhesive is spread?
A: Yes. The installer should plan clip placement before spreading adhesive so the working area can be completed within the adhesive open time.

## Removing Clips and Wedges
Summary: Clips are removed after curing by breaking the stem in the correct direction, while wedges are recovered for reuse.

Q151: When are clips removed?
A: Clips are normally removed after the adhesive has cured sufficiently, according to the adhesive and levelling system instructions. Removing them too early can disturb the tiles.

Q152: How are clips removed?
A: The exposed clip stem is usually knocked off in the direction of the grout joint after curing. The base remains below the tile edge in the adhesive bed.

Q153: Can clips be removed before adhesive cures?
A: No. Removing clips too early can move the tiles and affect alignment. The installer should wait until the adhesive has cured enough.

Q154: Can clips be removed too late?
A: They can still usually be removed after curing, but hardened adhesive around the clip can make removal or cleanup more difficult. Joints should be kept clean during installation.

Q155: How are wedges removed?
A: Wedges come loose when the clip stems are broken off. They should be collected, cleaned and stored for reuse.

Q156: Can a broken clip damage the tile edge?
A: Incorrect removal or hitting in the wrong direction can chip edges. The installer should follow the system instructions and remove clips carefully.

Q157: Should clips be kicked off?
A: Some installers knock clips off with a mallet or foot after curing, but the direction and force matter. Careless removal can damage tiles.

Q158: What direction should clips be removed?
A: Clips should generally be broken off along the grout joint, not across the tile edge. The installer should follow the specific product instructions.

Q159: What if part of a clip remains above the joint?
A: The installer must remove or correct it before grouting. Obstructions in the joint can affect grout depth and appearance.

Q160: Should the customer remove clips themselves?
A: It is better for the installer to remove clips because timing and technique affect the final finish. DIY removal can chip tiles if done incorrectly.

## Movement Joints, Expansion Gaps and Levelling Systems
Summary: Levelling systems do not replace movement joints, perimeter gaps or flexible sealant joints.

Q161: Do levelling clips replace movement joints?
A: No. Movement joints are still required where specified. Clips only align tile edges and do not absorb building movement.

Q162: Do clips remove the need for perimeter gaps?
A: No. Perimeter gaps are still needed to allow movement at walls and fixed points. These gaps should not be filled with rigid grout.

Q163: Can clips be used across a movement joint?
A: No. Movement joints must remain functional. A levelling clip should not bridge or lock a designed movement joint.

Q164: Why are movement joints important with large-format tiles?
A: Large-format tiles have fewer grout joints and larger tile panels, so movement stress can become more significant. Movement joints help reduce risk of cracking, tenting or debonding.

Q165: Are movement joints only for outdoor areas?
A: No. Movement joints may be needed indoors and outdoors, especially in large areas, sun-exposed areas, heated floors, wet areas and changes in substrate.

Q166: Can silicone be used at perimeter joints?
A: A suitable flexible sealant is often used at changes of plane and perimeter areas, depending on the design. The installer must select the correct product.

Q167: Can grout be used in corners instead of silicone?
A: Corners and changes of plane often need flexible sealant rather than rigid grout. Rigid grout can crack where movement occurs.

Q168: Should levelling clips be used near doorways?
A: They may be used where tiles meet, but doorways can require movement or transition detailing. The installer must not block required movement joints.

Q169: What is the movement-joint warning for chatbot answers?
A: The chatbot should say that levelling clips are not movement joints and do not replace expansion gaps, flexible sealant or installer-designed movement accommodation.

Q170: Should Tiletoria sell silicone with clips?
A: Where appropriate, Tiletoria should remind customers to confirm matching silicone or flexible sealant for corners, perimeters and changes of plane.

Q173: Can clips be used on balconies?

Q174: Can clips be used on ramps?

Q175: Do levelling clips make outdoor tiles safer?

Q176: Can clips be used in showers?

Q179: Should outdoor projects include extra accessories?

## Common Customer Confusion and Practical Explanations
Summary: Most confusion comes from treating wedges and clips as the same type of consumable, when they are not.

Q181: Why did the quote include more clips than wedges?
A: Clips are single-use and must cover the whole job. Wedges are reusable and only need to cover the installer’s active working area, so fewer wedges are normally needed.

Q182: Why did my installer ask for more clips but not more wedges?
A: This is normal. Clips are consumed as the job progresses, while wedges are reused after each section cures.

Q183: Why did my installer ask for more wedges?
A: The installer may be working faster, using more clips per day, or installing larger sections before removing wedges. More wedges can improve daily productivity.

Q184: Why does one installer use fewer wedges than another?
A: Wedge demand depends on how many square metres the installer lays per day and how many clips are active at once. Faster teams need more wedges available.

Q185: Why do clip calculators vary?
A: Calculators use different assumptions about tile size, clip spacing, layout and waste allowance. The actual layout and installer method should guide the final quantity.

Q186: Can I return unused clips?
A: Return eligibility depends on Tiletoria’s returns policy and the product condition. Customers should confirm before purchase, especially for opened packs.

Q187: Can I return used wedges?
A: Used wedges are normally tools that have been used on site and may not be returnable. Customers should confirm Tiletoria’s returns policy before purchase.

Q188: Should a customer buy a starter kit?
A: A starter kit can be practical for small projects or DIY users because it may include clips, wedges and pliers. Larger projects usually need extra clips.

Q189: What is the simplest analogy for clips and wedges?
A: Clips are like consumable spacers that stay behind and break off. Wedges are like reusable tools that move from one day’s work to the next.

Q190: How should staff explain the ratio in one sentence?
A: “Buy clips for the total area, but buy wedges for the installer’s daily working area because wedges are reused.”
