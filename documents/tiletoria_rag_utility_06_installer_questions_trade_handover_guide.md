# Tiletoria RAG Utility File 06: Installer Questions and Trade Handover Guide

This RAG-ready file helps the chatbot guide South African consumers on what to ask tilers, plumbers, waterproofers, vinyl installers, builders and contractors before, during and after installation. It is designed to reduce misunderstandings, missed accessories, installation disputes and avoidable complaints.

Source basis: South African National Building Regulations awareness through SANS 10400 Part P drainage and plumbing context, tile industry movement-joint and exterior installation principles, Ceramic Tile Education Foundation installer-resource guidance, Polyflor resilient flooring installation guidance, and manufacturer technical data-sheet practice. Final technical approval must always come from the relevant installer, plumber, product data sheet or project professional.

Important chatbot rule: The chatbot must not appoint, approve, certify or blame an installer. It may provide practical questions, checklists and handover prompts. It must escalate all safety, waterproofing, plumbing, structural, pressure, commercial and warranty-related issues.

## Master Installer Communication Rule

Summary: Clear questions before installation prevent many South African renovation disputes.

Core rule: Confirm the job scope, product suitability, preparation, accessories, responsibility and handover requirements before work starts.

Customer-friendly wording: “Before your installer starts, confirm the products, layout, preparation, accessories and clean-up responsibility in writing.”

The chatbot should encourage:
Written confirmation.
Photos before and during installation.
Installer approval of products before use.
Clear responsibility for preparation and first clean.
Keeping product labels and invoices.
Stopping work if something looks wrong.

The chatbot should not:
Recommend a specific installer unless Tiletoria has an approved process.
Guarantee workmanship.
Decide fault.
Tell a customer to ignore an installer’s technical concern.
Approve plumbing or waterproofing from chat alone.

Escalation rule: Escalate if the installer and customer disagree about product suitability, substrate readiness, waterproofing, water pressure, quantities or installation method.

## Questions to Ask Any Installer Before Work Starts

Summary: Customers should confirm the basics before any product is cut, fixed, glued, grouted, drilled or connected.

Ask: Have you checked the product name, size, finish and quantity?

Ask: Have you checked the invoice or quote against the product delivered?

Ask: Have you inspected the product before installation?

Ask: Are all required accessories and consumables on site?

Ask: Is the site ready for installation?

Ask: Is the substrate suitable?

Ask: Do you need primer, levelling, waterproofing or repair work before installation?

Ask: What product instructions or data sheets are you following?

Ask: What could cause additional cost or delay?

Ask: What must be confirmed before you start?

Chatbot answer: “Before work starts, ask your installer to confirm the product, quantity, accessories, substrate condition, preparation needs and installation method.”

Escalation rule: If the installer is unsure about product suitability, stop and confirm with Tiletoria before proceeding.

## Installer Scope Confirmation

Summary: Customers should confirm exactly what the installer is responsible for so there is no confusion later.

Confirm who is responsible for:
Removing old tiles or flooring.
Removing rubble.
Preparing the floor or wall.
Primer.
Self-levelling.
Waterproofing.
Tile adhesive or LVT adhesive.
Grout.
Silicone.
Trims.
Movement joints.
Plumbing disconnection.
Plumbing reconnection.
First clean.
Grout haze removal.
Product protection after installation.
Return visit for snagging.

Chatbot answer: “Ask your installer to confirm in writing what is included and excluded. Preparation, waterproofing, trims, silicone and first clean are common areas of misunderstanding.”

Escalation rule: Escalate if waterproofing, plumbing or electrical work is included in a vague contractor quote.

## Tile Installer Pre-Start Questions

Summary: Tiling success depends on substrate condition, layout, adhesive, grout, movement joints and installer workmanship.

Ask: Have you checked the floor or wall flatness?

Ask: Is the substrate clean, dry, sound and stable?

Ask: Are there cracks, hollows, moisture or loose areas?

Ask: What adhesive will you use for this tile?

Ask: Is primer required?

Ask: Is self-levelling or patching required?

Ask: What trowel size will you use?

Ask: Will you back-butter large-format tiles?

Ask: What grout joint width will you use?

Ask: Will you mix tiles from several boxes?

Ask: Where will the cuts fall?

Ask: Are movement joints required?

Ask: Who removes grout haze?

Chatbot answer: “Before tiling starts, confirm substrate readiness, adhesive, grout joint width, tile layout, movement joints and first-clean responsibility.”

Escalation rule: Escalate if the installer wants to tile over a dusty, damp, cracked or uneven substrate without preparation.

## Questions to Ask Before Large-Format Tile Installation

Summary: Large-format tiles need stricter flatness, adhesive coverage, handling and layout planning.

Ask: Have you checked substrate flatness with a straightedge?

Ask: Is self-levelling or wall correction required?

Ask: What adhesive is suitable for this tile size?

Ask: Will you use directional trowelling?

Ask: Will you back-butter the tiles where required?

Ask: Will you use levelling clips?

Ask: What clip size matches the tile thickness and joint width?

Ask: How will you handle and cut the tiles safely?

Ask: Is the wall or floor strong and flat enough?

Ask: What waste allowance do you recommend?

Chatbot answer: “Large-format tiles require a flatter substrate and careful adhesive coverage. Ask your installer to confirm flatness, adhesive, levelling clips and handling before starting.”

Escalation rule: Escalate large-format shower floors, external large-format floors, wall installations and any lippage concern.

## Questions to Ask Before Shower Tiling

Summary: Shower tiling requires waterproofing, falls, drain planning, adhesive, grout and silicone to work as a system.

Ask: Has the shower area been waterproofed?

Ask: What waterproofing system was used?

Ask: Are wall-floor junctions, corners and pipe penetrations sealed?

Ask: Has the waterproofing cured?

Ask: Is the drain or channel installed and compatible?

Ask: Are the floor falls correct?

Ask: Is the tile suitable for the shower wall or floor?

Ask: What adhesive and grout will be used?

Ask: Where will silicone be used?

Ask: How will the shower be tested after installation?

Chatbot answer: “Before shower tiling starts, confirm waterproofing, drain position, falls, adhesive, grout and silicone. Tiles and grout are not the waterproofing system.”

Escalation rule: Always escalate shower waterproofing, wet rooms, drainage, linear channels and shower leak concerns.

Summary: Outdoor tiling must deal with rain, sun, movement, drainage, cleaning and exterior-rated installation products.

Ask: Is the tile suitable for outdoor use?

Ask: Is the adhesive suitable for exterior use?

Ask: Is the grout suitable for exterior use?

Ask: Are falls and drainage correct?

Ask: Are movement joints planned?

Ask: Will the substrate handle outdoor exposure?

Ask: Is waterproofing required?

Ask: Are step trims or nosings required?

Ask: What cleaning and maintenance will be needed?

Ask: How will the area be protected during curing?

Chatbot answer: “For outdoor tiling, ask your installer to confirm tile suitability, exterior adhesive, exterior grout, drainage and movement joints.”

Escalation rule: Escalate balconies, pool surrounds, ramps, public walkways and uncovered patios.

## Questions to Ask Before Balcony Tiling

Summary: Balcony tiling is high-risk because waterproofing and drainage failures can cause serious damage.

Ask: Who is responsible for waterproofing?

Ask: Has the waterproofing system been specified?

Ask: Are falls to outlets correct?

Ask: Are outlets, drains or scuppers clear?

Ask: Is the tile suitable for exterior wet exposure?

Ask: Is the adhesive compatible with waterproofing?

Ask: Are movement joints planned?

Ask: How will edges and upstands be treated?

Ask: Will photos be taken before tiling covers waterproofing?

Ask: Is body corporate approval required?

Chatbot answer: “Balconies need professional waterproofing and drainage planning before tiling. Confirm this before any tile is installed.”

Escalation rule: Always escalate balcony projects.

## Questions to Ask Before Tile-Over-Tile Installation

Summary: Tiling over existing tiles is only suitable if the old tiled surface is sound, clean and prepared correctly.

Ask: Are the existing tiles firmly bonded?

Ask: Are any tiles loose or hollow?

Ask: Is the surface clean and free of grease?

Ask: Is a bonding primer required?

Ask: Is the correct adhesive being used?

Ask: Will the added height affect doors or thresholds?

Ask: Are there hidden moisture or waterproofing issues?

Ask: Will skirtings, stairs or trims still work?

Ask: Who accepts risk if old tiles fail?

Ask: Has Tiletoria confirmed the preparation system?

Chatbot answer: “Tiling over existing tiles is site-dependent. Ask your installer to confirm bond, cleaning, primer, height build-up and adhesive before starting.”

Escalation rule: Always escalate tile-over-tile installations.

## Questions to Ask Before Glue-Down LVT Installation

Summary: Glue-down Luxury Vinyl Tile depends heavily on subfloor preparation, moisture control, adhesive and site conditions.

Ask: Is the subfloor clean, dry, smooth, flat and sound?

Ask: Has moisture been checked where required?

Ask: Are cracks, hollows or rough areas repaired?

Ask: Are old grout lines filled or levelled?

Ask: What LVT adhesive will be used?

Ask: Is primer required?

Ask: Is self-levelling required?

Ask: Has the product acclimatised as required?

Ask: Are room temperature and site conditions suitable?

Ask: Are trims and transitions on site?

Chatbot answer: “For glue-down LVT, ask your installer to confirm moisture, subfloor smoothness, approved adhesive, primer, levelling and trims before installation.”

Escalation rule: Escalate damp floors, existing tiles, underfloor heating, bathrooms, direct sun and commercial LVT.

## Questions to Ask Before Installing LVT Over Existing Tiles

Summary: Existing tile grout lines and unevenness can show through glue-down LVT if not corrected.

Ask: Are the existing tiles firmly bonded?

Ask: Are any tiles cracked, loose or hollow?

Ask: Will grout lines be filled or skimmed?

Ask: Will self-levelling compound be used?

Ask: Is the floor moisture-safe?

Ask: What primer is required?

Ask: What adhesive is approved for the LVT?

Ask: Will door heights and trims still work?

Chatbot answer: “LVT over tiles needs a smooth, stable surface. Ask your installer how grout lines and tile texture will be levelled before adhesive is applied.”

Escalation rule: Always escalate LVT over existing tiles.

## Questions to Ask the Plumber Before Bathroom Work

Summary: Plumbing compatibility should be confirmed before sanitaryware, taps, mixers and baths are installed.

Ask: Does this toilet match the existing waste outlet?

Ask: What is the toilet set-out?

Ask: Is the water inlet in the right position?

Ask: Does this basin need a slotted or unslotted waste?

Ask: Will the trap fit inside the vanity?

Ask: Is water pressure suitable for the mixer?

Ask: Are hot and cold supplies correctly positioned?

Ask: Are angle valves and connectors required?

Ask: Can hidden plumbing be accessed later?

Ask: Will pipework be flushed before connecting mixers?

Chatbot answer: “Ask your plumber to confirm waste positions, water pressure, valves, connectors and future service access before installation.”

Escalation rule: Escalate concealed plumbing, water pressure, wall-hung toilets and leaks.

## Questions to Ask Before Wall-Hung Toilet Installation

Summary: Wall-hung toilets require a compatible frame, cistern, flush plate, wall structure and service access.

Ask: Is the support frame compatible with the pan?

Ask: Is the concealed cistern compatible with the flush plate?

Ask: Can the wall support the system?

Ask: Is the frame set to the correct finished floor height?

Ask: Is the finished wall depth correct?

Ask: Will the flush plate provide service access?

Ask: Is the waste connection correct?

Ask: Has the system been tested before closing the wall?

Chatbot answer: “A wall-hung toilet is a complete system. Ask your plumber to confirm frame, cistern, flush plate, wall structure and service access before tiling.”

Escalation rule: Always escalate wall-hung toilet questions.

## Questions to Ask the Waterproofer

Summary: Waterproofing must be handled as a system with correct surface preparation, reinforcement and curing.

Ask: What waterproofing product or system will be used?

Ask: Is primer required?

Ask: Are corners and wall-floor junctions reinforced?

Ask: Are pipe penetrations sealed with collars or suitable detailing?

Ask: Are niches and benches waterproofed?

Ask: How many coats are required?

Ask: What is the required drying or curing time?

Ask: Is the tile adhesive compatible with the waterproofing?

Ask: Will photos be taken before tiling?

Ask: Is a flood test or water test required?

Chatbot answer: “Ask the waterproofer to confirm the full system, not only the liquid product. Corners, pipe penetrations and curing time matter.”

Escalation rule: Always escalate showers, balconies, wet rooms and waterproofing failures.

## Questions to Ask Before Installing Trims

Summary: Trims must match tile thickness, edge position, finish and environment.

Ask: Where are exposed tile edges?

Ask: What trim profile is needed?

Ask: Does trim depth match tile thickness and adhesive bed?

Ask: What finish should match taps or accessories?

Ask: Are external corners mitred or finished with profiles?

Ask: Are trims suitable for wet or outdoor use?

Ask: Are stair nosings or heavy-duty profiles needed?

Ask: Have all trim lengths been measured?

Chatbot answer: “Ask your installer to confirm trim positions, trim depth, finish and quantity before tiling starts.”

Escalation rule: Escalate steps, ramps, external corners, commercial edges and wet-area trims.

## Questions to Ask Before Choosing Grout Colour

Summary: Grout colour should be confirmed before installation because it changes the final look and maintenance expectation.

Ask: Do you want grout to blend or contrast?

Ask: Is the tile rectified or non-rectified?

Ask: What joint width will be used?

Ask: Is the area wet, outdoor or high traffic?

Ask: Will light grout show dirt too easily?

Ask: Will dark grout show residue or variation?

Ask: Is silicone needed at corners instead of grout?

Ask: Who approves the grout colour before grouting?

Chatbot answer: “Confirm grout colour before grouting starts. Grout colour changes the final appearance and cleaning expectation.”

Escalation rule: Escalate epoxy grout, external areas, showers, pools and natural stone.

## Questions to Ask Before First Clean

Summary: The first clean is critical because wrong cleaners or late grout haze removal can damage finishes or leave residue.

Ask: Who is responsible for the first clean?

Ask: What grout was used?

Ask: What cleaner is safe for this tile finish?

Ask: Is grout haze remover required?

Ask: Is the tile polished, textured, natural stone or LVT?

Ask: Are abrasive pads being avoided?

Ask: Are acidic cleaners safe for this surface?

Ask: Will tapware and sanitaryware be protected from harsh chemicals?

Chatbot answer: “Before cleaning, confirm the surface and grout type. The wrong cleaner can damage tiles, grout, LVT, tap finishes or sanitaryware.”

Escalation rule: Escalate polished tiles, natural stone, epoxy haze, special tap finishes and chemical damage.

## Red Flags Before Work Starts

Summary: Certain warning signs should make customers pause before installation begins.

Red flags:
Installer refuses to inspect product.
Installer ignores batch or shade labels.
Installer says grout joints are unnecessary.
Installer wants to tile a shower without waterproofing.
Installer wants to use indoor adhesive outdoors.
Installer says levelling clips fix a bad floor.
Installer cannot identify adhesive or grout.
Installer wants to install LVT over a damp floor.
Plumber cannot confirm waste compatibility.
Concealed mixer trim is not on site before wall closing.
No plan for bath waste access.
No movement joints in large or outdoor areas.

Chatbot answer: “If any red flag appears, pause the job and confirm with Tiletoria or a qualified professional before continuing.”

Escalation rule: Always escalate red-flag situations.
