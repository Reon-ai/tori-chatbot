### Q007: Why are add-ons important in sanitaryware purchases?

### Q008: What is the safest general advice for sanitaryware compatibility?

## Toilet Types

Summary: Toilet selection depends on pan type, cistern type, waste outlet, space, installation method and cleaning preference.

### Q011: What are the main types of toilets?

Answer: Common toilet types include close-coupled toilets, back-to-wall toilets, wall-hung toilets, low-level suites and concealed-cistern systems.

### Q012: What is a close-coupled toilet?

Answer: A close-coupled toilet has the cistern mounted directly on the pan, forming a compact toilet suite commonly used in residential bathrooms.

### Q013: What is a back-to-wall toilet?

Answer: A back-to-wall toilet pan sits against the wall or furniture, with the cistern concealed in the wall, cabinet or service void.

### Q014: What is a wall-hung toilet?

Answer: A wall-hung toilet is fixed to a concealed frame and cistern, with the pan suspended above the floor for a clean, modern appearance.

### Q015: What is a low-level toilet suite?

Answer: A low-level toilet suite has a visible cistern mounted above the pan and connected by a flush pipe. It is often used in traditional-style bathrooms.

### Q016: Which toilet type is easiest to retrofit?

Answer: A close-coupled toilet is often simpler to retrofit, but the existing waste outlet, water supply and space must still be checked.

### Q017: Which toilet type gives the most modern look?

Answer: Wall-hung and back-to-wall toilets usually create the most modern look because the cistern and pipework can be concealed.

### Q018: Which toilet is easiest to clean around?

Answer: Wall-hung toilets can make floor cleaning easier because the pan is raised off the floor, but they require the correct support frame and installation.

### Q019: Should customers choose the toilet before plumbing is finalised?

Answer: Yes. Toilet choice affects waste position, water supply, wall support, cistern location and finishing details.

### Q020: What add-ons should be checked with a toilet?

Answer: Check the toilet seat, cistern, flush mechanism, pan connector, inlet valve, angle valve, flexible connector, fixing kit and silicone where required.

## Toilet Waste Outlets

Summary: The toilet waste outlet must match the existing or planned drainage position to avoid costly installation problems.

### Q021: What is a toilet waste outlet?

Answer: The waste outlet is the connection point where waste leaves the toilet pan and connects to the drainage system.

### Q022: What is an S-trap toilet?

Answer: An S-trap toilet discharges through the floor. The installer must confirm the floor waste position and set-out measurement.

### Q023: What is a P-trap toilet?

Answer: A P-trap toilet discharges horizontally through the wall or into a wall connection. The installer must confirm the wall waste height and position.

### Q024: Can any toilet fit any waste position?

Answer: No. The toilet pan type and set-out must suit the waste position or a compatible connector system must be used.

### Q025: What does toilet set-out mean?

Answer: Set-out refers to the distance from the finished wall or floor to the centre of the waste outlet connection, depending on the toilet type.

### Q026: Why is set-out important?

Answer: Incorrect set-out can prevent the toilet from fitting, cause the pan to sit too far from the wall or require plumbing alteration.

### Q027: Can a plumber move the toilet waste position?

Answer: Sometimes, but moving waste pipes can be costly and depends on the floor, wall, slab, drainage layout and building constraints.

### Q028: Can a pan connector solve all toilet waste problems?

Answer: No. Pan connectors help with some alignment issues, but they cannot fix every set-out, height or drainage problem.

### Q029: What should the chatbot ask for toilet replacement?

Answer: Ask whether the existing toilet waste exits through the floor or wall, and ask the customer to measure or photograph the current connection.

### Q030: What should be suggested with toilet purchases?

Answer: Suggest confirming the correct pan connector, inlet valve, angle valve, flexible connector, seat and fixing kit before the toilet is installed.

### Q083: What is a dual-flush toilet?

### Q084: Can any flush valve fit any cistern?

### Q085: Can any inlet valve fit any cistern?

### Q087: Can any flush plate fit any concealed cistern?

### Q088: Why must concealed cisterns remain serviceable?

### Q089: What should be checked when buying a concealed cistern?

### Q090: When should cistern questions be escalated?

## Toilet Installation Planning

Summary: Toilet installation requires checking finished floor level, waste position, water supply, wall clearance and service access.

### Q101: What measurements are needed before buying a toilet?

Answer: Measure waste position, distance from finished wall to waste centre, available width, projection from wall, water inlet position and height limits.

### Q102: Why does finished floor level matter?

Answer: The toilet is installed on the finished floor level, so tile thickness, adhesive and floor build-up can affect final height and waste alignment.

### Q103: Should toilet installation happen before or after tiling?

Answer: The finished floor and wall surfaces are usually considered in the final installation, but sequencing should be planned by the plumber, tiler and builder.

### Q104: Can a toilet be installed over uneven tiles?

Answer: The toilet should sit securely on a stable finished surface. Uneven tiles can cause rocking and seal problems.

### Q105: Why must the toilet be fixed properly?

Answer: A loose toilet can damage seals, cause leaks, rock during use and create long-term maintenance problems.

### Q106: Can silicone hold a loose toilet in place?

Answer: No. Silicone is not a structural fixing. The toilet must be mechanically fixed or installed according to the manufacturer instructions.

### Q107: Should a toilet be sealed around the base?

Answer: Sealing may be used for hygiene and finishing, but installation must still allow proper fixing and leak detection as required by the plumber.

### Q108: Can a toilet be installed against a waterproofed wall?

Answer: Yes, but fixings and penetrations must be handled carefully so waterproofing is not compromised.

### Q109: What should customers confirm with the plumber?

Answer: Confirm waste outlet, water inlet, set-out, fixings, floor level, wall clearance, cistern access and required connectors.

### Q110: What is the best toilet installation CTA?

Answer: Ask the customer to bring photos and measurements to Tiletoria so the team can help confirm the toilet, connector, seat and plumbing add-ons.

## Basin Tap Holes and Mixer Compatibility

Summary: Basin tap-hole configuration determines whether the customer needs a basin mixer, pillar taps or wall-mounted mixer.

### Q151: What is a tap hole on a basin?

### Q153: What does two tap holes mean?

### Q154: What does no tap hole mean?

### Q155: Can a one-hole basin use pillar taps?

### Q157: Why does tap reach matter?

Answer: The spout must reach far enough into the basin to prevent water from hitting the edge or splashing.

### Q158: Why does tap height matter?

Answer: The tap must provide enough clearance for hand washing without excessive splashing.

### Q159: What should be checked before selling a basin mixer?

### Q160: What add-on guidance helps here?
