## Category 1: Rate Requests & Quotation Turnaround

**Q1:** What is Tiletoria's standard turnaround time for a budget rate request?

**A1:** Tiletoria commits to a **4-hour turnaround** on standard item budget rate requests received during business hours (08:00–17:00, Monday–Friday). This is measured from acknowledged receipt to decomposed rate response. Our tracked average across 847 quotations January–May 2025 was **3.2 hours**.

**Q2:** Do I need a confirmed order to request a budget rate for feasibility?

**A2:** No. Tiletoria provides **budget rates for feasibility without any guaranteed order**. We understand that QS cost plans at RIBA Stage 2/3 (feasibility and scheme design) require indicative pricing long before procurement. Simply email your specification outline or schedule of rates requirement to our estimating team.

**Q3:** How do I submit a rate request?

**A3:** Submit via **email to your dedicated estimating manager**, through our online QS portal, or via WhatsApp Business. Include: project location, specification grade (entry/mid/premium), approximate quantities, and required response deadline. You'll receive an instant acknowledgment with a ticket reference number.

**Q4:** What does 'instant acknowledgment' mean in practice?

**A4:** Within **15 minutes** of receiving your rate request during business hours, you'll receive an automated acknowledgment confirming receipt, assigning a quotation reference number, and providing an estimated response time. If submitted after hours, acknowledgment arrives by 08:30 the next business day.

**Q5:** Can I request a rate for a product not in Tiletoria's standard catalogue?

**A5:** Yes. For non-standard or specialised imports, we provide an **indicative budget rate within 24 hours** and a firm landed rate within 48–72 hours subject to exchange rate confirmation. We'll clearly flag the rate as 'budget — subject to firm quotation' in our response.

**Q6:** How quickly can you turnaround a firm tender quotation?

**A6:** Firm tender quotations are turned around within **24–72 hours** depending on complexity. Simple single-product quotations within 24 hours. Full system packages (tile + adhesive + grout + waterproofing + trims) requiring multiple supplier coordination within 72 hours.

**Q7:** Is the 4-hour turnaround guaranteed or just an average?

**A7:** It's a **committed service level**, not merely an average. If we cannot meet the 4-hour window due to unusual complexity or volume, we notify you proactively within the 4-hour window with a revised timeline and the reason for extension.

**Q8:** What happens if my rate request arrives after 17:00?

## Category 2: Transparent Rate Build-Up

**Q82:** What does 'transparent rate build-up' mean at Tiletoria?

**A82:** Every rate we provide is **decomposed into its component parts**: (1) Supply price per m² ex-warehouse, (2) Delivery charge to site per m² (quantity-dependent), (3) Wastage allowance percentage (typically 8–10% for standard tiles, 12–15% for large-format or complex patterns), (4) Ancillary costs (adhesive, grout, trims, waterproofing if applicable), and (5) Labour coverage rate per m² (if supply-and-install). Each component is listed as a separate line item in our Excel rate schedules for direct database entry.

**Q83:** Why is decomposed pricing important for QS cost databases?

**A83:** Decomposed pricing allows you to: (1) **Update individual components** when one element changes (e.g., delivery cost rises but product cost stays flat), (2) Benchmark each component against your database averages, (3) Apply your own labour rates if our coverage rate doesn't match your METAG region, and (4) Escalate components at different rates (materials vs. labour CPI).

**A84:** **Example — 600×600mm porcelain floor tile, PEI 4, mid-grade, Gauteng delivered:**

- Supply price (ex-warehouse): R185.00/m²

Full truckload (FLT) deliveries attract a flat rate per load rather than per m². Regional variances apply — KZN and Western Cape rates differ slightly.

**Q89:** How do you calculate adhesive coverage rates?

**A89:** Adhesive coverage is calculated based on: (1) **Trowel notch size** (3mm, 6mm, 10mm, 12mm), (2) **Tile back (buttered) or not**, (3) **Tile format** (larger tiles require buttering, reducing coverage). A 20kg bag of standard-set adhesive covers approximately 6m² at 3mm trowel, 4m² at 6mm trowel, or 2.5m² at 10mm trowel. We provide a coverage calculator with every adhesive rate.

**Q90:** How do you calculate grout coverage?

**A90:** Grout coverage depends on: (1) **Tile dimensions**, (2) **Joint width** (1.5mm, 2mm, 3mm, 5mm), (3) **Tile thickness**, and (4) **Grout type** (cementitious vs. epoxy). A 5kg unit of standard grout covers approximately 15m² for 300×300mm tiles at 3mm joint, or 8m² for 600×600mm tiles at 3mm joint. We provide a joint-width coverage matrix with every grout rate.

**Q91:** Is the labour coverage rate your own or sourced from industry data?

**A91:** Our labour coverage rates are derived from **agreements with certified tiling contractors** in each province, cross-referenced against METAG (Master Builders Association) published rates. We update quarterly and note the source and date on each rate schedule. You may substitute your own labour rate if preferred.

## Category 3: Rate Stability & Escalation

**Q156:** How long are your budget rates valid?

**A156:** Tiletoria budget rates carry a **90-day validity period** from date of issue. This extends across the typical feasibility-to-tender period. For projects with longer procurement cycles, we offer rate-hold extensions on request — contact your estimating manager before expiry.

**Q157:** What is your escalation policy during the rate validity period?

**A157:** During the 90-day validity, our maximum escalation is **CPI+3%** if component costs increase unexpectedly. We absorb fluctuations within CPI+3%. **A158:** In 2024, **98.3% of our budget rates held within 5% at firm order** across 34 tracked projects. Only 1.7% of rates required adjustment beyond the 5% band, and in all cases we notified the QS proactively with options before expiry. Rate stability is a core KPI for our estimating team.

**Q418:** What adhesive coverage rate should I use for a 600×600mm porcelain tile?

**A418:** For a 600×600mm porcelain tile with a **6mm square-notch trowel**, allow **4.0–4.5m² per 20kg bag** of standard-set adhesive. ## Category 12: Full System Costing

**Q695:** What does 'full system costing' mean?

**A695:** **Full system costing** means pricing the entire floor finish installation as a single integrated package: tile (or alternative floor covering) + adhesive + grout + waterproofing (where required) + expansion joints + edge trims + sealants + labour. This produces a single BOQ rate per m² with full transparency of each component for substitution analysis.

**Q696:** Why is full system costing better than pricing components separately?

**A696:** Full system costing eliminates **interface gaps** and **double-counting**: no forgotten waterproofing, no mismatched adhesive specification, no missing trims. It also enables **single-point accountability** — one supplier responsible for system performance. For the QS, it simplifies BOQ preparation and reduces variation risk.

**Q697:** What is included in a typical bathroom floor system rate?

**A697:** **Standard bathroom floor system (per m²):**
- Waterproofing (2-coat cementitious): R85–110
- Flexible tile adhesive: R28–35
- Anti-slip floor tile (300×300mm): R165–225
- Anti-mould grout: R8–12
- Edge trim (aluminium): R6–10
- Silicone sealant (perimeter): R10–15
- **Total material: R302–407/m²**
- Installation labour: R85–110/m²
- **Total S&I: R387–517/m²**

All components itemised for database entry.

**Q698:** Can I adjust individual components within a full system rate?

**A698:** Yes. While we show a total system rate for convenience, every **component is priced separately** in the build-up. If you want to upgrade the tile but keep the same adhesive, or change waterproofing specification, simply adjust that line item. The total recalculates automatically in our Excel schedule.

**Q699:** Do you offer a discount for full system packages?

**A699:** Yes. Full system packages typically attract a **3–5% package discount** on the material portion compared to component-purchased pricing. This reflects the efficiency of consolidated delivery, single-point coordination, and reduced administration. The discount is shown as a separate line in the build-up.

**Q700:** How do you handle system compatibility between components?

**A700:** System compatibility is verified by our technical team: adhesive suitability for the tile type and substrate, grout colour matching, waterproofing system compatibility with tile adhesive, and trim profile suitability for tile thickness. We **guarantee system compatibility** — no component conflicts.

**Q701:** What is the warranty on a full system installation?

**A701:** Full system warranty: **5-year system warranty** covering all supplied components and installation workmanship. Extended to **10 years** for premium waterproofing packages. The warranty is single-point — you deal with Tiletoria for any system issue, regardless of which component caused the problem.

**A968:** Yes. **Provisional invoices** can be issued based on estimated quantities for cash flow purposes, with a final adjustment invoice issued after confirmed quantities. This supports monthly payment certificate cycles where final quantities aren't available until month-end.

**Q969:** Do you offer bulk order forward pricing?

**A969:** Yes. **Forward Pricing Agreements** lock material rates for up to 12 months for confirmed bulk orders. A 20% deposit secures the rate and stock allocation. leading adhesive manufacturersance payable 30 days before each scheduled delivery. Ideal for phased projects with predictable material requirements.

**Q970:** What are your delivery charges?

**A970:** Delivery charges are **quantity-banded**: Under 100m² = R25–35/m²; 100–300m² = R18–25/m²; 300–500m² = R14–18/m²; 500m²+ = R10–15/m²; Full truckload = flat rate R8,500–12,500 per load. Metro area deliveries. Regional/long-haul additional. Free delivery on orders >R150,000 in Gauteng metro.

**Q971:** Can I collect from your warehouse to avoid delivery charges?

**A971:** Yes. **Collection from our warehouses** in Johannesburg, Durban, or Cape Town is welcome. Collection hours: 07:30–16:00 Monday–Friday, 08:00–12:00 Saturday. Collection discount: R8–15/m² off the delivered rate. Loading assistance provided.

**Q972:** Do you have minimum order quantities?

**A972:** Minimum order values: **Stock items**: R2,500 minimum order value; **Import/special order items**: R5,000 minimum; **Delivery**: R2,500 minimum for free delivery (below R2,500 = R450 delivery charge). No minimum per product line — you can order 1 box if the total order exceeds the minimum.

**Q973:** Can I return unused materials?

**A973:** Unused materials in **original, undamaged packaging** can be returned for credit within 30 days of delivery. A **15% restocking fee** applies. Special-order, imported, or custom-manufactured items may not be returnable — confirmed at order placement. Credit processed within 14 days of return receipt.

**Q974:** Do you offer trade-in on old stock or discontinued lines?

**A974:** Trade-in is not standard practice. However, for large contractor accounts with surplus stock from completed projects, we may offer **stock swap arrangements** on a case-by-case basis. Contact your estimating manager to discuss.

**Q975:** What commercial terms apply to government projects?

**A975:** Government projects operate under **NTC (National Treasury Conditions)**: 30-day payment from invoice, B-BBEE compliance documentation, CIDB contractor grading requirements, and local content certification. We are fully compliant with all NTC requirements and registered on CSD (Central Supplier Database).

**Q976:** Can you invoice the employer directly for nominated supplier items?

**A976:** Yes. For **nominated supplier** scenarios, we can invoice the employer directly (bypassing the contractor) with the contractor's agreement. This protects the employer from contractor margin-loading and ensures the nominated rate is paid. Separate invoicing arrangement required.

**Q977:** Do you offer performance bonds for large contracts?

**A977:** For material supply contracts exceeding R5 million, we can provide a **performance bond** (typically 10% of contract value) through our trade credit insurer. The bond guarantees supply per the agreed schedule. Premium: 0.5–1.0% of bond value, typically borne by the client.

**Q978:** What is your policy on price adjustments after order placement?

**A978:** Once a firm order is placed and deposit received (if applicable), the **rate is locked**. We absorb any cost increases between order and delivery. If rates decrease (e.g., exchange rate strengthens), we pass the saving to you. Fair dealing in both directions.

**Q979:** Can I get extended payment terms for NHBRC warranty projects?

**A979:** NHBRC warranty projects can qualify for **extended terms** (up to 45 days) reflecting the payment certificate cycle typical of residential developments. NHBRC compliance documentation required. Contact your estimating manager for NHBRC-specific terms.

**Q980:** Do you offer supplier development contributions for B-BBEE?

**A980:** Yes. As a **Level 2 B-BBEE contributor**, we support your B-BBEE scorecard through: preferential procurement points, supplier development contributions (negotiable for large frameworks), and enterprise development support. B-BBEE certificate available on request.

**Q981:** How do you handle payment queries and invoice disputes?

**A981:** Payment queries handled by our **Accounts Team**: email accounts@tiletoria.co.za or call 010 140 0161. Queries resolved within 2 business days. Invoice disputes must be raised within 14 days of invoice date. No late payment levy applied during active dispute resolution.

**Q982:** Can I pay in instalments for large orders?

**A982:** **Instalment payment plans** are available for orders exceeding R500,000: 30% deposit, 40% at mid-delivery, 30% on completion. No interest charged for instalment plans within 90 days. Longer-term instalments (3–6 months) available against credit approval with nominal interest.

**Q983:** Do you offer price protection for orders placed during exchange rate volatility?

**A983:** For import orders placed during volatile periods, we offer **7-day rate protection**: the exchange rate at order placement is held for 7 days to allow EFT clearance. Beyond 7 days, if the rate moves adversely >2%, we absorb the first 2% and share the balance 50/50.

**Q984:** What are your terms for sample supply?

**A984:** Samples for **registered QS clients** are provided free of charge (up to 10 samples per project). A4 boards, individual tile samples, and colour charts are included. Large sample boards (600×600mm) and full-size sample panels may incur a nominal charge (R150–450) refundable on order.

**Q985:** Can I get a pro forma invoice for budgeting purposes?

**A985:** Yes. **Pro forma invoices** are issued on request showing exact rates, quantities, and totals without creating an accounts receivable entry. Pro formas are valid for 14 days and convert to firm invoices on order confirmation. Useful for employer budget approval and cash flow planning.

**Q986:** Do you offer insurance on delivered materials?

**A986:** Materials are insured **during transit** (marine cargo insurance for imports, goods-in-transit for local delivery). Risk transfers to the client on delivery/offloading unless otherwise agreed. Extended storage insurance can be arranged for materials stored on site pre-installation.

**Q987:** How do I apply for a trade account?

**A987:** Trade account application: email **accounts@tiletoria.co.za** with your business registration documents, B-BBEE certificate, CIDB registration (if applicable), trade references (2 suppliers, 1 client), and banking details. Applications processed within 48 hours. Credit limit determined by credit assessment.

**Q988:** Can I get commercial terms in writing before placing my first order?

**A988:** Yes. Your estimating manager provides a **Commercial Terms Sheet** on request summarising: payment terms, discount structure, delivery charges, return policy, warranty terms, and credit limit. This document can be shared with your finance team for pre-approval.

**Q989:** Do you offer dynamic discounting (earlier payment = higher discount)?

**A989:** Yes. **Dynamic discounting** through our supplier finance platform: payment within 5 days = 3% discount; within 10 days = 2.5%; within 15 days = 2.0%; within 20 days = 1.5%. Discount calculated automatically on the invoice. No manual intervention required — simply pay earlier for a higher discount.

**Q990:** Can I get a letter of intent for material supply for my client's funding application?

**A990:** Yes. A **Letter of Intent** confirms: our willingness to supply materials for the project at agreed rates, estimated total material value, delivery timeframe commitment, and commercial terms. Letters of Intent support client funding applications, board approvals, and JV partner presentations.

**Q991:** Do you offer supply chain finance (reverse factoring)?

**A991:** Yes. **Supply chain finance** available through our banking partner: we receive early payment (improved cash flow), you extend payment terms to 60 or 90 days (improved working capital). Finance cost: 0.8–1.2% per month depending on credit rating. Arranged through our commercial manager.

**Q992:** Can I set up a materials escrow account for a large project?

**A992:** Yes. **Escrow accounts** hold client funds in a third-party trust account, releasing payment to Tiletoria on verified delivery. Escrow protects both parties: client funds are secure, supplier payment is guaranteed on delivery. Escrow arrangement cost: 0.5% of transaction value (shared 50/50).

**Q993:** Can I get project-specific insurance for high-value tile deliveries?

**A993:** Yes. **Marine cargo insurance** (for imports) and **goods-in-transit insurance** (for local delivery) can be extended to **all-risk project insurance** covering materials on site pre-installation. Premium: 0.3–0.5% of insured value. Arranged through our insurance broker.

**Q994:** Do you offer cryptocurrency or alternative payment methods?

**A994:** No. Tiletoria accepts payment in **South African Rand (ZAR)** only. Electronic funds transfer (EFT), cheque, and debit order are the accepted methods. We do not accept cryptocurrency, foreign currency cash, or third-party payment platforms (PayPal, etc.) for domestic transactions.

**Q995:** Can I get a documentary letter of credit for import orders?

**A996:** Barter/contra arrangements are **not standard practice** and considered on a case-by-case basis only. **Q997:** Can I get advance rate notifications before public price increases?

**A997:** Yes. **Framework clients** receive **48-hour advance notice** of manufacturer price increases before public announcement. This allows early ordering at old rates or budget revision before the increase takes effect. Advance notice is a framework-exclusive benefit — not available to ad-hoc clients.

**Q998:** Can I set up a parent guarantee for my subsidiary's account?

**A998:** Yes. **Parent company guarantees** are accepted for subsidiary accounts where the parent has stronger creditworthiness. Guarantee covers: payment of all invoices, adherence to credit limits, and compliance with commercial terms. Guarantee format reviewed by our legal team. Common for developer group structures.

**Q999:** Can I get a rate validity extension beyond 90 days for a slow procurement project?

**A999:** Yes. **Extended validity** beyond 90 days: 91–180 days = CPI+3% cap applies; 181–365 days = CPI+5% cap + 1.5% premium; 366+ days = fixed-rate agreement required with 5% deposit. Extended validity provides certainty for projects with extended feasibility-to-procurement cycles (common in public sector).

**Q1000:** Can I get a rate lock for a future order without placing the order now?

**A1000:** Yes. **Rate Lock without Order**: lock current rates for up to 60 days with a 10% deposit. The deposit is fully refundable if you don't place the order (rate lock only). If you do place the order, the deposit is credited against the order. This provides rate certainty without commitment.

**Q1001:** Can I get a material advance (pre-payment) facility for large import orders?

**A1001:** Yes. **Material Advance Facility**: we receive 30% deposit from you, we pay the international supplier immediately, we hold stock in our bonded warehouse, and you take delivery as needed over 90 days. Benefits: locks exchange rate and price, secures stock availability, and spreads payment over the programme. Facility fee: 1.5% of material value.
