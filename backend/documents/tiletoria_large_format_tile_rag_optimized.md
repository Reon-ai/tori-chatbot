## Large-Format Tile Summary

Summary: Large-format and slab tiles create a premium, seamless look with fewer grout lines, but they require careful planning, correct installation products and experienced handling.

Large-format tiles are tiles with larger-than-standard dimensions, often used to create a more seamless, architectural surface.

They are popular in modern homes, bathrooms, open-plan living areas, commercial spaces, hotel-style interiors, retail showrooms, feature walls and statement floors.

Main customer benefits:
Fewer grout joints.
A more seamless visual effect.
A premium designer look.
Easier routine cleaning compared with many small-format tile layouts.
A larger visual surface that can make spaces feel calmer and more open.
Strong impact in residential and commercial interiors.

Key caution:
Large-format tiles are not just bigger tiles. They need a flatter substrate, suitable adhesive, correct handling, appropriate grout joints, movement joints and skilled installation.

"Large-format tiles give a premium, seamless look with fewer grout joints, but they need careful planning and proper installation. The floor or wall must be flat, the adhesive must suit the tile size, and your installer should confirm handling and layout before work starts."

Tori caution: Always confirm the specific product's water absorption and application suitability.

PEI wear rating:
The sales sheet refers to PEI Class 4 to 5.
Customer meaning: These classes are generally associated with heavy residential and some commercial traffic depending on the tile.
Tori caution: Confirm the PEI rating on the specific product.

MOHS hardness:
The sales sheet refers to MOHS 7 to 8.
Customer meaning: This suggests strong scratch resistance for many normal residential uses.
Tori caution: No tile should be described as scratch-proof; grit, sand, dragging furniture and misuse can still cause damage.

Rectified edges:
The sales sheet refers to precision-cut rectified edges.
Customer meaning: Rectified tiles can allow a cleaner, more modern grout-line appearance.
Tori caution: Rectified tiles still require grout joints and a flat substrate.

Slip resistance:
The sales sheet refers to R9 to R11 depending on finish.
Customer meaning: Slip resistance depends heavily on the tile finish.
Tori caution: Do not call any tile slip-proof or non-slip. Wet surfaces, soap, slope, drainage, footwear and maintenance all affect slip risk.

Breaking strength:
The sales sheet refers to breaking strength above 1300 N.
Customer meaning: Large-format porcelain tiles can be strong when properly installed.
Tori caution: Cracking risk can still come from poor substrate preparation, movement, voids, impact or incorrect installation.

Thermal stability:
The sales sheet refers to frost resistance and suitability for outdoor patios and underfloor heating systems.
Customer meaning: Some products may suit outdoor or underfloor-heating applications.
Tori caution: Confirm product-specific suitability, adhesive system, movement joints and installer requirements.

## Large-Format Tile FAQs

Summary: These short answers help Tori answer common customer questions.

Question: Are large-format tiles good for small bathrooms?
Answer: Yes, they can be excellent because fewer grout lines can make the room feel larger. The layout, cuts and shower-floor falls must be planned carefully.

Question: Are large-format tiles harder to install?
Answer: They require more careful handling, flatter substrates and suitable adhesive, so an experienced installer is recommended.

Question: Do large-format tiles need grout?
Answer: Yes. Rectified large-format tiles still need grout joints.

Question: Do large-format tiles need levelling clips?
Answer: Levelling clips are often useful, especially for large-format floor installations, but they do not fix an uneven substrate.

Question: Can large-format tiles be used outside?
Answer: Sometimes, but only if the specific tile, finish, adhesive, grout, drainage and movement-joint system are suitable for outdoor use.

Question: Can large-format tiles be used in showers?
Answer: They can work well on shower walls when installed correctly. Shower floors need special care because of falls, drainage and slip risk.

Question: Are large-format tiles easy to clean?
Answer: They can be easier to maintain because there are fewer grout joints, but the correct cleaner and maintenance routine still matter.

Question: Do they crack easily?
Answer: Large-format porcelain can be strong, but cracking risk depends on substrate, adhesive coverage, movement joints, impact and installation quality.

Question: Which size should I choose?
Answer: 700 x 1400 mm works well for compact bathrooms and feature walls; 800 x 1600 mm is a strong all-round option; 1200 x 1200 mm suits large open spaces; 1200 x 2400 mm is best for statement areas where handling and access are confirmed.
