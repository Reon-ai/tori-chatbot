## Bathroom rule
Bathrooms are design spaces and technical spaces. Water management matters as much as tile choice.

## Ask for
- Floor only, wall only or full bathroom?
- Shower included?
- New build or renovation?
- Existing tiles removed or tiling over existing?
- Floor size and wall height.
- Drain position and fall.
- Desired tile size.
- Slip concern?
- Sanitaryware/tapware needed?

## Shower rule
Showers must be waterproofed correctly before tiling. Tiles, adhesive and grout do not replace waterproofing.

## Bathroom floor tile selection
Check:
- Slip awareness.
- Ease of cleaning.
- Size and falls to drain.
- Grout joint practicality.
- Coordination with wall tile.

## Wall tile selection
Wall tiles can be ceramic or porcelain depending on range, design and suitability. Wall tiles are generally not automatically suitable for floors.

## Large-format in bathrooms
Large-format tiles look premium but need flat walls/floors and careful planning around falls, drains, niches and cuts.

## Mosaic use
Mosaics can help with shower floors because they follow falls better, but grout and cleaning expectations must be explained.

## Bathroom add-on basket
- Floor tiles.
- Wall tiles.
- Adhesive.
- Grout.
- Waterproofing.
- Silicone/sealant where appropriate.
- Trims.
- Levelling clips/spacers.
- Sanitaryware/taps.
- Drains/wastes where supplied.

## Red flags
- Leaking shower.
- Damp wall.
- Balcony/upper-floor wet area.
- Tiling over existing tiles.
- No fall to drain.
- Smooth tile requested for wet floor.

## Good Tori answer
"For a bathroom, we should plan the tile look and the waterproofing system together. Tell me the floor size, wall height, whether there is a shower, and whether this is a renovation or new build."
