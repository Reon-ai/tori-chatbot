# Kitchen, Laundry, Garage and Braai Room Guide

**Version:** 2026-06-18  
**Market:** South Africa / Southern Africa  
**Business:** Tiletoria customer and staff RAG knowledge base  

## Purpose
Cover common South African household zones that need specific tile/product selection logic.

## Retrieval triggers
- kitchen
- laundry
- scullery
- garage
- braai room
- braai
- splashback
- utility
- pantry

## Kitchen floors
Need durable, easy-clean surfaces. Ask about open-plan flow, cabinetry, pets, children and preferred maintenance level.

## Kitchen splashbacks
Ask:
- Width and height.
- Tile style: subway, mosaic, slab-look, decorative, plain.
- Grout colour.
- Cleaning near hob.
- Edge trims.

## Laundry/scullery
These are wet-risk utility areas. Ask about washing machine, floor drain, water exposure and slip concern.

## Garage
Garages need extra care because of vehicle loads, oil, tyres, dirt and cleaning. Tori must confirm whether the tile is suitable for garage use and avoid recommending wall/soft/unsuitable products.

Ask:
- Residential garage or commercial workshop?
- Vehicle traffic?
- Existing slab condition?
- Oil/chemical exposure?
- Slip expectation?

## Braai room
A braai room may behave like indoor, semi-outdoor or outdoor depending on exposure.

Ask:
- Is it enclosed, covered or open?
- Is rain/wind exposure possible?
- Is there a built-in braai/fireplace?
- Will people enter from pool/garden?
- Is slip resistance important?

## Good guidance
- For kitchens, cleaning and grout colour matter.
- For laundries, water management matters.
- For garages, durability and substrate matter.
- For braai rooms, exposure and slip risk matter.

## Customer answer
"This area can work beautifully, but we need to choose for how the space is actually used. Is it a dry indoor space, a wet utility space, or a semi-outdoor entertainment area?"
