# Contractor Site Readiness and Stop-Work Checks

## Before Pricing or Installation

Check and record:

### Substrate identity

- concrete
- screed
- existing tiles
- plaster or render
- board system
- timber
- metal
- resin or unknown coating

### Surface condition

- sound or friable
- clean or contaminated
- porous or dense
- flatness and levels
- cracks and joints
- hollow or debonded areas
- laitance, curing compound, paint, grease or dust

### Moisture and water

- current moisture result and test method
- active leak or rising damp
- wet-area design
- drainage and falls
- external weather exposure

### Movement and structure

- surface bed or suspended slab
- structural joints
- construction joints
- deflection or vibration
- underfloor heating
- changes between materials

### Finish material

- product code
- tile type and dimensions
- tile thickness
- shade, batch and calibre
- box coverage
- visual variation
- edge and surface condition

### Site logistics

- access and parking
- offloading
- storage
- power and clean water
- lighting
- ventilation
- waste removal
- security
- other trades
- protection after installation

## Stop and Escalate When

- the substrate is moving, crumbling or hollow
- cracks appear active or structural
- moisture is above the selected product-system limit
- water is entering the area
- the substrate is unknown or contaminated
- existing structural joints have no approved treatment
- the waterproofing detail is missing in a wet area
- falls or drain details are wrong
- tile batches, shade or calibre are inconsistent and not approved
- the selected product is not confirmed for the application
- the installation detail conflicts with the manufacturer specification
- a hazardous material is suspected
- the site condition cannot be documented or accepted

## Evidence Pack

Record:

- dated overview photos
- close-up defect photos
- moisture readings
- level or straightedge readings
- substrate notes
- product labels and batch
- approved layout
- client decisions
- technical data sheets
- variation orders
- waterproofing and flood-test sign-off
- handover and protection status

## Tori Response

Tori should not diagnose a site from one sentence. Ask for the key evidence, identify the stop-work risk, and route to technical support when required.
