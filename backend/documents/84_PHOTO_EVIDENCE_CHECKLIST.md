# Photo Evidence Checklist

**Version:** 2026-06-18  
**Market:** South Africa / Southern Africa  
**Business:** Tiletoria / Tori RAG brain  
**Layer:** Phase 3 Control Layer  

## Purpose
Give customers a clear checklist of photos and details to submit for technical support, complaints, damaged goods or product matching.

## Retrieval triggers
- evidence
- photos needed
- what pictures
- complaint photos
- damage photos
- box label
- batch
- shade
- cracked tile
- hollow tiles
- proof

## Purpose
Good evidence helps Tiletoria respond faster and more fairly. Poor evidence creates delays. Tori should request the right photos without overwhelming the customer.

## Universal photo checklist
Ask for:

1. One wide photo of the full area.
2. One close-up of the issue or product.
3. A photo of the box label showing product code, batch, shade or calibre if available.
4. A photo of unused tile/LVT/plank if available.
5. Proof of purchase or quote/invoice number if available.
6. Short written description of what happened.

## Damaged boxes or delivery damage
Ask for:

- photo of damaged box/pallet before unpacking if possible
- close-up of broken pieces
- number of boxes affected
- delivery/collection date
- whether damage was noted on delivery paperwork
- invoice/order number
- whether tiles have been installed or are still unused

## Shade, colour or batch concern
Ask for:

- labels from all boxes involved
- photo of installed area in natural light
- photo of loose tiles from different boxes side by side
- note whether boxes were mixed during installation
- number of m² installed
- number of boxes remaining

## Lippage or uneven tiles
Ask for:

- wide photo across the floor
- close-up across tile edges
- photo with a straightedge if safely possible
- tile size
- installation date
- whether a levelling system was used
- whether substrate flatness was checked

## Hollow sound or loose tiles
Ask for:

- short video tapping affected and unaffected tiles if possible
- wide photo showing affected zones
- installation date
- adhesive used
- substrate type
- whether movement joints exist
- indoor/outdoor/wet area

## Cracking or tenting
Ask for:

- wide photo showing direction/extent of cracking
- close-up of crack
- photo of movement/perimeter joints if visible
- installation date
- substrate/screed details if known
- whether there was heat, sunlight, underfloor heating, water ingress or structural movement

## Wet area or waterproofing concern
Ask for:

- wide photo of shower/bathroom/balcony
- close-up of leak/mould/efflorescence
- where water appears
- when it happens
- what waterproofing system was used if known
- plumber/tiler/builder details if available

## Product matching
Ask for:

- box label
- loose tile photo
- installed tile photo in natural light
- size measurement
- invoice/quote number
- branch of purchase
- approximate purchase date

## Customer-friendly template
> To help the team review this properly, please send: one wide photo, one close-up, a photo of the box label/batch if available, your invoice or quote number if you have it, and a short note explaining what happened and when.

## Privacy note
Ask customers to avoid including unnecessary personal information, people, ID documents, bank cards or unrelated private items in photos.

## Universal Tori rule for this file
Tori must be helpful, practical and commercially safe. Tori may guide, calculate, explain, collect details and prepare a handover, but must not invent live stock, live prices, delivery availability, technical certifications, warranties, legal outcomes, account status, branch decisions or product suitability where site conditions are unknown. When the answer depends on current branch systems, ERP data, site inspection, supplier confirmation or management approval, Tori must clearly say so and route the customer to the correct Tiletoria team.
