# Primer Quantity Calculator

## Required Inputs

Ask for:

1. area in m²
2. substrate type and porosity
3. exact primer or keying system
4. coverage in m²/L per coat
5. number of coats
6. available container sizes

## Formula

`treated_area_equivalent = area_m2 × number_of_coats`

`required_litres = treated_area_equivalent ÷ coverage_m2_per_litre`

`litres_with_allowance = required_litres × 1.10`

Then choose full containers that meet or exceed the result.

## Verified TAL Floor Primer Constants

- approximate coverage: 4–5 m²/L per coat
- coverage depends on porosity and site conditions
- pack sizes listed by TAL: 5 L and 25 L
- very porous floors may require more applications

For conservative planning, use 4 m²/L per coat.

## Worked Example

Inputs:

- area: 30 m²
- coats: 2
- conservative coverage: 4 m²/L per coat
- allowance: 10%

Calculation:

- treated area equivalent = 30 × 2 = 60 m²
- base litres = 60 ÷ 4 = 15 L
- with allowance = 15 × 1.10 = 16.5 L

Buying quantity using 5 L containers:

`ceiling(16.5 ÷ 5) = 4 containers`

Answer:

> “Allow **20 L in total**, or **4 × 5 L containers**, for two coats at 4 m²/L per coat.”

## Substrate Selection Rule

A porous-surface primer is not automatically correct for:

- power-floated or steel-floated concrete
- existing tiles
- glass
- metal
- resin-backed material
- contaminated surfaces
- active moisture

Dense or impervious surfaces may require a manufacturer-approved keying slurry or specialist primer.

## Contractor Warnings

- Cleaning is not the same as priming.
- Priming does not strengthen a weak or friable substrate.
- Remove dust, laitance, curing compounds, grease and other bond breakers.
- Confirm whether coverage is per coat.
- Allow the primer to dry according to the current product instructions.
