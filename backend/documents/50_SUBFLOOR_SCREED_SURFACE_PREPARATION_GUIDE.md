**Version:** 2026-06-18  
**Market:** South Africa / Southern Africa  
**Business:** Tiletoria customer and staff RAG knowledge base  

## Purpose
Teach Tori substrate preparation logic for tiles and glue-down LVT.

## Substrate rule
The finish is only as good as the surface underneath. Tori must prioritise preparation before product choice.

## Good substrate qualities
A suitable surface should generally be:
- Clean.
- Dry where required.
- Sound/solid.
- Flat/level enough for the product.
- Free of dust, oil, paint, loose material and contaminants.
- Properly cured where relevant.
- Free of unmanaged cracks/movement.

## Tile over tile
Tori must be cautious.
Ask:
- Are existing tiles firmly bonded?
- Any hollow or loose tiles?
- Is the surface glazed/polished?
- Is height buildup acceptable?
- Are doors, drains and transitions affected?
- Correct primer/adhesive required?

## Screed too uneven
Recommend self-levelling or remedial preparation where suitable, but do not specify exact product without branch/manufacturer confirmation.

## Dusty or weak screed
Warn that adhesive bonds to the surface. Weak dusty surfaces may fail unless properly prepared.

## Cracks
Cracks need assessment. Do not simply tile over structural or active cracks.

## LVT surface rule
Glue-down LVT is less forgiving than many customers expect. The floor must be smooth because imperfections can telegraph through.

## Red flags
- Damp screed.
- Hollow existing tiles.
- Structural cracks.
- Painted floors.
- Bitumen/old adhesive residues.
- Recently poured screed not cured.
- Outdoor slab without fall/drainage.

## Good answer
"Before choosing the final product, let’s check the surface: is it clean, dry, solid and flat? Are there cracks, loose tiles, damp patches or old adhesive? Those details decide the correct preparation system."
