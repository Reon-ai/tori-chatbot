# Persona 4: Developers — Q&A Knowledge Base

## Category 1: Bulk Procurement & Pricing (62 Q&As)

**Q1:** What volume pricing tiers does Tiletoria offer for residential development projects?
**A:** Tiletoria offers project-specific tender pricing based on committed volumes. Pricing tiers typically kick in at 1,000m², 5,000m², 10,000m², and 20,000m²+ with incremental discounts at each threshold. For projects exceeding 20,000m², a bespoke pricing agreement is negotiated directly with our National Development Accounts team, often including volume rebates and price-lock mechanisms.

**Q2:** Can Tiletoria provide a delivered cost per m² for a 150-unit townhouse complex in Waterfall, KZN?
**A:** Yes. We provide a full landed cost per m² that includes the tile or flooring product, adhesive, grout, trims, waterproofing where applicable, and delivery to site. For a typical mid-market townhouse development in Waterfall using 600x600mm porcelain at R185-R220/m² delivered cost, our QS team can issue a firm project quote within 48 hours of receiving full specifications and quantities.

**Q3:** Does Tiletoria offer price-locking for the duration of a construction programme?
**A:** Yes, for committed development projects Tiletoria offers price-lock agreements of up to 12 months from date of order confirmation. The price-lock is secured against a signed supply agreement and minimum volume commitment. For programmes extending beyond 12 months, structured escalation clauses at CPI+2% or fixed annual increments can be negotiated.

**Q4:** What is the minimum order value to qualify for development pricing?
**A:** Development pricing typically applies to projects with a total material value exceeding R500,000. However, for established developer clients with multiple concurrent projects or pipeline commitments, we can apply development-tier pricing across a portfolio of smaller projects. Contact our Development Accounts team to discuss your specific pipeline.

**Q5:** How does Tiletoria handle call-off pricing for phased developments?
**A:** Call-off orders under a master supply agreement are priced at the agreed project rate, regardless of the individual call-off size. This means a 200m² call-off for Phase 3 attracts the same per-m² rate as the initial 5,000m² bulk order. No surcharges apply to small call-offs provided they fall within the total committed volume.

**Q6:** Can we negotiate volume rebates based on total annual spend across multiple projects?
**A:** Yes. Tiletoria offers annual volume rebate structures for developers committing to R5 million or more in annual purchases. Rebates are typically tiered: 1.5% at R5m, 2.5% at R10m, and 3.5% at R20m+ annual spend. Rebates are calculated quarterly and settled either as credit notes or off-invoice deductions on the following quarter's orders.

**Q7:** How does Tiletoria's pricing compare to importing direct from Spain or Italy?
**A:** In most cases, Tiletoria's landed pricing matches or beats direct import due to our consolidated container volumes (200+ containers/month), established freight contracts, and bulk manufacturing partnerships. Direct import carries hidden costs — customs clearance, demurrage, agent fees, currency risk, and no local warranty or stock backup. Our value proposition includes local stockholding, SANS compliance verification, and immediate replacement availability.

**Q8:** What currencies does Tiletoria transact in, and can we get Euro-based pricing?
**A:** All Tiletoria invoices are in ZAR. However, for large development projects with signed agreements, we can structure Euro-hedged pricing where the base price is fixed in EUR and converted at a locked exchange rate for the contract duration. This protects both parties from ZAR/EUR volatility during the construction programme.

**Q9:** Does Tiletoria offer quantity surveyor support for BoQ preparation?
**A:** Yes. Our project estimating team works directly with your QS to prepare detailed Bills of Quantities including wastage factors (typically 8-12% for tiles, 5% for LVT), adhesive and grout calculations per SANS 10107, and accessory schedules. We provide this service free of charge for projects with a Tiletoria supply agreement.

**Q11:** Can Tiletoria match a competitor's written quote for a development project?
**A:** Yes, through our Price Match programme. Provide us with a formal written quotation from a recognised competitor for identical or materially equivalent products, and we will match or beat that price subject to stock availability and equivalent commercial terms. This applies to full-system quotes (tile + adhesive + grout) on a like-for-like basis.

**Q12:** How are price increases communicated to development clients?
**A:** Development clients with signed supply agreements receive 60 days' written notice of any price adjustment, with increases capped at agreed escalation rates. For non-contracted projects, standard 30-day notice applies. Our account executives provide quarterly price stability reports showing anticipated cost movements driven by exchange rates, freight costs, and raw material trends.

**Q13:** What is the typical price range for developer-grade porcelain tiles suitable for rental units?
**A:** Developer-grade 600x600mm rectified porcelain suitable for rental units typically ranges from R145-R195/m² at development volumes. Hard-wearing 600x600mm glazed porcelain for high-traffic areas ranges from R175-R250/m². These prices exclude adhesive and grout but include delivery for orders over 2,000m² within major metros.

**Q14:** Does Tiletoria offer package pricing for complete bathroom sets in apartment developments?
**A:** Yes. Our apartment bathroom package includes floor and wall tiles, adhesive, grout, silicone, waterproofing membrane, and aluminium trims. For a standard 2-bedroom apartment with two bathrooms, package pricing typically ranges from R12,500-R18,500 per unit complete, depending on tile selection. Volume discounts apply from 50 units upwards.

**Q15:** Can we get provisional pricing for projects still in planning phase?
**A:** Absolutely. We provide budgetary estimates based on indicative specifications for feasibility studies and development financing applications. These are expressed as price ranges (e.g., R180-R220/m² delivered) with a 90-day validity. Once specifications are confirmed, we firm up to fixed project pricing.

**Q16:** How does Tiletoria structure pricing for mixed-product developments (tiles + LVT + laminate)?
**A:** Mixed-product projects qualify for blended volume pricing. The total committed m² across all flooring categories is aggregated to determine the volume tier, with individual product categories priced at their respective tier rates. This maximises your discount compared to treating each category as a separate order.

**Q17:** Are there any hidden costs we should factor into our development cost plan?
**A:** Tiletoria's project quotes are fully transparent with no hidden costs. Delivery, offloading assistance, and standard documentation (datasheets, compliance certificates) are included. Optional extras that may apply include: after-hours delivery (R2,500 surcharge), crane-offloading where site access is restricted (quoted separately), and custom-cutting services. All costs are itemised in our formal proposal.

**Q18:** Does Tiletoria offer early-payment discounts?
**A:** Yes. For clients on standard 30-day terms, a 2.5% settlement discount applies to invoices paid within 7 days. On 60-day terms, a 1.5% discount applies for payment within 14 days. These discounts are cumulative with volume rebates, creating significant savings for cash-positive developers.

**Q19:** What financing options are available for large development projects?
**A:** Tiletoria works with several commercial finance providers to offer supply-chain financing for qualifying developers. Structures include deferred payment programmes aligned with construction drawdowns, and invoice discounting facilities where Tiletoria accepts extended terms backed by a financial institution. Minimum project value for financing is R3 million.

**Q20:** How do we request a formal project tender from Tiletoria?
**A:** Submit your Bill of Quantities, specification schedule, programme timeline, and delivery requirements to our Development Accounts team at projects@tiletoria.co.za. We acknowledge receipt within 24 hours and issue a formal tender within 5-7 working days for standard projects, or 10 working days for complex multi-phase developments.

**Q21:** Does Tiletoria offer different pricing for cash vs credit account purchases?
**A:** For development projects, pricing is generally the same regardless of payment method. However, cash-positive projects that can prepay 50% or more of the order value may qualify for an additional 1-2% prepayment discount. This is negotiated on a project-by-project basis.

**Q22:** What happens if our actual quantities exceed the quoted volumes?
**A:** Additional quantities are supplied at the originally agreed project rate, provided they are ordered within the contract validity period (typically 12 months). If volumes exceed 120% of the original quote, the incremental volume may qualify for a further tier discount, which our team will apply retrospectively.

**Q23:** How does Tiletoria handle under-runs where we order less than quoted volumes?
**A:** Our supply agreements include a volume tolerance of +/-15%. Within this range, no pricing adjustment applies. If the final volume falls more than 15% below the committed minimum, a rate adjustment may apply to the quantities actually supplied, reverting to the appropriate volume tier. We work with developers to avoid this through programme forecasting.

**Q24:** Can we get a price list for standard development-grade products?
**A:** Yes. Our Development Product Guide contains recommended products for the developer market across all categories, with indicative pricing at 5,000m² and 10,000m² volume tiers. This guide is updated quarterly and available from your account executive or via our Spec Lab in Menlyn.

**Q25:** Does Tiletoria offer price differentiation by province?
**A:** Our standard pricing is national, but delivered cost varies by province due to freight differentials. Deliveries to Gauteng and KZN main centres are included in the base price for development orders. Western Cape deliveries carry a freight surcharge of R8-R12/m² for orders under 5,000m²; orders above 5,000m² to WC typically qualify for free delivery.

**Q26:** What is the typical cost difference between Italian and Chinese porcelain for a 100-unit development?
**A:** For 600x600mm rectified porcelain of equivalent technical specification, Italian-sourced product typically costs 25-35% more than premium Chinese product. On a 100-unit development with 800m² of tiling per unit (80,000m² total), this difference could amount to R1.2m-R1.8m. Our value engineering team can advise where Italian origin is essential for specification integrity vs where premium Chinese product meets all technical requirements.

**Q27:** Does Tiletoria offer fixed-price contracts for multi-year developments?
**A:** For developments with programmes exceeding 18 months, we offer structured fixed-price agreements with built-in escalation caps (typically CPI+1.5% per annum). The base price is locked for the first 12 months, with predetermined escalations for subsequent periods. This gives your QS cost certainty for budgeting and cost-to-complete reporting.

**Q28:** How competitive is Tiletoria's LVT pricing for large residential developments?
**A:** Tiletoria's LVT pricing at development volumes is highly competitive, with 2mm glue-down LVT from R95-R125/m² and 4mm click-system LVT from R135-R175/m² at 10,000m²+ volumes. These prices include underlay where specified and are typically 10-15% below specialist flooring distributors due to our integrated supply model and container-scale imports.

**Q29:** Can Tiletoria provide provisional sums for our architect's specification?
**A:** Yes. We work with your project architect to provide provisional sums (PS allowances) for the finishing schedule, expressed as ranges that account for product selection flexibility. These are formatted for direct insertion into the architectural specification and can be firmed up once final product selections are made.

**Q30:** What is the process for negotiating a preferred supplier agreement with Tiletoria?
**A:** Preferred supplier agreements are offered to developers with demonstrated annual spend exceeding R5 million or pipeline exceeding 500 units. The process involves: (1) initial capability meeting, (2) project pipeline review, (3) commercial proposal, (4) B-BBEE and compliance verification, (5) agreement negotiation, and (6) board-level sign-off. The entire process typically takes 4-6 weeks.

**Q31:** Does Tiletoria offer cost breakdowns suitable for our development financing applications?
**A:** Yes. We provide detailed cost breakdowns per unit type, per phase, and per product category, formatted for submission to development financiers. These include itemised costs for tiles, adhesives, grouts, trims, waterproofing, and delivery — all with VAT treatment clearly indicated. Our documentation has been accepted by all major SA development finance institutions.

**Q32:** How does Tiletoria handle price changes between specification and procurement?
**A:** This is a critical risk area for developers. Once a specification is confirmed and a supply agreement signed, pricing is locked for the contract duration. If procurement is delayed beyond the quote validity period (90 days for budget estimates, 12 months for signed agreements), we provide a refreshed quote with any cost movements clearly explained. Our account executives proactively monitor pipeline projects to flag potential price changes.

**Q33:** What are the payment terms for a first-time development client?
**A:** For new developer clients, standard terms are 50% deposit with order, 50% on delivery for the first project, transitioning to 30-day terms upon successful completion and credit assessment. For projects exceeding R10 million, staged payments aligned to construction milestones can be negotiated. Our credit team can provide a decision within 5 working days of receiving financials.

**Q34:** Does Tiletoria provide cost comparisons between different flooring systems for our feasibility studies?
**A:** Yes. Our estimating team regularly prepares flooring system cost comparisons for developer feasibility studies, comparing options such as: porcelain tile vs LVT vs laminate vs engineered wood, including full system costs (product + installation materials + labour allocation). This helps optimise your specification for margin while meeting market expectations.

This removes currency risk from your cost plan. The hedging cost is built into the project pricing or charged as a separate facility fee depending on the structure preferred.

**Q36:** What is the typical lead time from order confirmation to first delivery on a large development?

For container-order products not held in local stock, allow 10-12 weeks from order for sea freight from Europe, or 6-8 weeks from Asia. Our project team will recommend the optimal procurement strategy based on your programme.

Larger format tiles (1200x600, 1200x1200) carry a premium of 15-25% due to higher manufacturing and handling costs. Mosaic and decorative formats are priced separately.

The adhesive cost is similar, but rectified tiles require a more skilled installer (R15-R20/m² labour premium). For developer-grade projects, the improved visual outcome typically justifies the modest cost increase.

## Category 2: Supply Security & Stockholding (62 Q&As)

**Q63:** Does Tiletoria offer dedicated stockholding for development projects?
**A:** Yes. For committed development projects, Tiletoria can dedicate warehouse capacity exclusively to your project's materials. This means your stock is physically segregated in our warehouse, tagged with your project code, and released only against your call-off instructions. Dedicated stockholding is available for projects with material values exceeding R2 million.

**Q64:** What warehouse capacity does Tiletoria have for large development projects?
**A:** Tiletoria operates over over 23,000m² of warehousing across Gauteng, KZN, and the Western Cape, with the capacity to hold 400,000m²+ of tile stock at any given time. Our Johannesburg DC is the largest at 12,000m², followed by Durban (8,000m²) and Cape Town (5,000m²+). This scale ensures we can simultaneously service multiple large developments without capacity constraints.

**Q65:** Can Tiletoria provide a supply guarantee backed by a performance bond?
**A:** Yes. For developments with penalty clauses or critical delivery requirements, Tiletoria can issue a performance bond or provide a parent company guarantee from Invicta Holdings. The bond value is typically 5-10% of the contract value and secures our commitment to deliver according to the agreed programme. Bond costs are either absorbed by Tiletoria or passed through at cost depending on contract value.

**Q66:** How does Tiletoria ensure stock availability for 10,000m²+ orders?
**A:** Large orders are secured through a combination of existing stock, pipeline arrivals, and dedicated procurement. Upon order confirmation, our procurement team verifies availability against current stock and incoming containers. Any shortfall is immediately covered by a dedicated factory order with confirmed production slots and shipping schedules. We never confirm an order we cannot fulfil.

**Q67:** What happens if Tiletoria cannot supply a product after we've specified it?
**A:** This situation is exceptionally rare due to our stockholding capacity and multiple supply sources. However, if it occurs, Tiletoria will: (1) source an equivalent or superior product at the same price, (2) bear any additional cost if the replacement is more expensive, and (3) if no equivalent is available, provide 30 days' notice to allow specification revision without programme impact. This is contractually guaranteed in our supply agreements.

**Q68:** Does Tiletoria have supply commitments from manufacturers for popular development products?
**A:** Yes. Tiletoria holds annual supply agreements with key manufacturers in Italy, Spain, China, India, and Turkey, securing production capacity for our core development ranges. These agreements include minimum volume commitments from manufacturers, priority allocation during supply constraints, and agreed lead times for replenishment.

**Q69:** How much advance notice does Tiletoria need for a 20,000m² order?
**A:** For products held in our local stock, 14-21 days' notice is sufficient to pick, stage, and deliver 20,000m². For products requiring container replenishment, 10-12 weeks from Europe or 6-8 weeks from Asia. For the largest projects, we recommend a kick-off meeting 16 weeks before first delivery to confirm stock plans and container schedules.

**Q70:** Can Tiletoria hold stock for a project with a delayed start date?
**A:** Yes. We offer deferred delivery stockholding where materials are procured and held in our warehouse for up to 6 months before delivery, at no additional storage cost for the first 90 days. Beyond 90 days, a nominal holding fee of 0.5% per month of stock value applies. This is useful for developers securing stock ahead of construction finance approval or seasonal procurement.

**Q71:** What is Tiletoria's stock replenishment lead time for fast-moving development products?
**A:** Our top 200 development SKUs are maintained at 60-90 days' forward cover through continuous replenishment. For these products, typical replenishment from local warehouse to branch is 24-48 hours. Factory-to-warehouse replenishment is 8-10 weeks from Europe, 5-6 weeks from Asia. Our inventory system triggers automatic replenishment when stock falls below defined minimum levels.

**Q72:** Does Tiletoria offer consignment stockholding on development sites?
**A:** Consignment stockholding on site is available for large phased developments. Materials remain Tiletoria's property until drawn down for installation, at which point invoicing occurs. This improves your cash flow and reduces site storage requirements. Consignment terms require site security and insurance arrangements, and are available for projects exceeding R5 million.

**Q73:** How does Tiletoria manage stock allocation when multiple developers want the same product simultaneously?
**A:** Stock allocation prioritises clients with signed supply agreements and confirmed call-off schedules over ad-hoc orders. For high-demand products, our system maintains a "committed stock" view showing what is reserved for projects vs available for new orders. If supply constraints arise, allocation is proportional to committed volumes and programme urgency.

**Q74:** Can Tiletoria guarantee batch consistency across a 15,000m² order delivered over 6 months?
**A:** Yes. For large orders with extended delivery programmes, we procure entire production runs from the manufacturer and hold them in dedicated stock. This guarantees that all 15,000m² comes from the same batch or contiguous production batches with imperceptible variation. Batch numbers are recorded and traceable for every box delivered.

**Q75:** What contingency stock does Tiletoria hold for development call-off orders?
**A:** For projects on dedicated stockholding, we typically hold 10-15% contingency stock above the committed volume to accommodate programme accelerations, damage replacements, and client variations. This contingency is included in the stockholding agreement and released on the same call-off basis as the main stock.

**Q76:** Does Tiletoria have back-to-back supply agreements with its manufacturers?
**A:** Yes. Tiletoria's relationships with manufacturers are governed by formal supply agreements that include volume commitments, quality standards, delivery schedules, and penalty clauses. These back-to-back agreements ensure that our commitments to you are supported by enforceable commitments from our supply chain.

**Q77:** How does Tiletoria handle supply disruptions caused by shipping delays or port congestion?

If a specific supply line is disrupted, we can substitute from alternative manufacturing sources without specification compromise. We also maintain 60-90 days of safety stock for critical development lines. Our logistics team monitors shipping schedules weekly and provides early warning of potential disruptions.

**Q78:** Can Tiletoria provide a stock availability certificate for our development finance application?

**Q80:** Does Tiletoria hold stock of adhesive and grout in addition to tiles?
