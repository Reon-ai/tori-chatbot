# Persona 5: Corporate National Chains — Q&A Knowledge Base

## Category 1: National Coverage & Multi-Site Capability

**Q1:** Does Tiletoria have stockholding facilities in all nine provinces of South Africa?
**A:** Tiletoria operates confirmed stockholding facilities in Gauteng (Johannesburg/Northriding), KwaZulu-Natal (Durban/Cornubia), and the Western Cape (Cape Town). From these three strategic hubs, we deliver to all nine provinces through our own fleet and contracted logistics partners, ensuring national reach with centralised coordination.

**Q2:** Can you deliver to our stores in Limpopo, Mpumalanga, and the Northern Cape?
**A:** Yes. While our primary stockholding is in Gauteng, KZN, and the Western Cape, our logistics network delivers to all nine provinces including Limpopo, Mpumalanga, and the Northern Cape. Standard delivery timeframes to outlying provinces range from 3–7 working days depending on site location.

**Q3:** What is the total warehousing capacity across your national network?
**A:** Tiletoria maintains over over 23,000m² of warehousing space across its national footprint, with a combined delivery capacity of 400 tons per day. This infrastructure is designed to support simultaneous multi-site rollouts for national corporate clients.

**Q4:** Can you handle deliveries to 50 sites simultaneously across three provinces?
**A:** Yes. Our logistics infrastructure is specifically designed for national rollout programs. With 400 tons/day delivery capacity, dedicated fleet allocation for major rollouts, and regional distribution hubs in Gauteng, KZN, and the Western Cape, we can coordinate simultaneous deliveries to multiple sites across provinces within agreed SLA windows.

**Q5:** Do you have a branch network, or do you operate from centralised warehouses only?
**A:** Tiletoria operates a hybrid model — centralised stockholding at our three major regional hubs (Gauteng, KZN, Western Cape) supported by dedicated delivery fleets and logistics coordination centres. This model ensures consistent stock availability, centralised pricing, and standardised quality control across all deliveries nationwide.

**Q6:** What is the lead time for delivery to our sites in the Eastern Cape?
**A:** Deliveries from our KZN Durban hub to Eastern Cape sites typically range from 3–5 working days for standard stock items. For large rollout programs, we establish dedicated delivery schedules aligned to your site programme, often achieving 48–72 hour delivery within committed windows.

**Q7:** Can you deliver to rural sites in the Free State and North West?
**A:** Yes. Our logistics network covers both urban and rural sites across all nine provinces. Rural deliveries may require an additional 1–2 working days and advance coordination for access constraints, but we routinely service retail and hospitality sites in rural and semi-urban locations.

**Q8:** Is stock physically held in all three provinces, or is it only available in Johannesburg?
**A:** Stock is physically held in all three provinces — Johannesburg (Gauteng), Durban/Cornubia (KZN), and Cape Town (Western Cape). This multi-hub model reduces delivery distances, lowers transport risk, and ensures faster replenishment to sites within each region.

**Q9:** How do you manage stock allocation when multiple sites order the same SKU simultaneously?
**A:** National accounts receive dedicated stock allocation protocols. Your national account manager maintains a centralised view of all orders and can ring-fence stock for rollout programs. For ongoing replenishment, our real-time stock visibility system prevents double-allocation and flags low-stock SKUs before they impact your sites.

**Q10:** Can you guarantee the same product will be available in all three hubs at all times?
**A:** For national accounts with agreed SKU lists, we maintain minimum stock levels across all three hubs as part of our SLA. Quarterly stock reviews ensure alignment with your rollout schedule and replenishment forecasts. Critical roll-out SKUs are flagged for priority replenishment and cannot be sold to other customers until your programme commitments are met.

**Q11:** What happens if one hub runs out of stock for a product on our national specification list?
**A:** In the rare event of a hub stock-out, we immediately transfer stock from another hub via our inter-branch transfer network — typically within 24–48 hours. For national accounts, we also maintain safety stock buffers and pre-position stock ahead of known rollout waves to prevent stock-outs entirely.

**Q12:** Can you support a rollout that starts in the Western Cape and moves to Gauteng and KZN sequentially?
**A:** Absolutely. We regularly support phased rollouts across provinces. Your national account manager will pre-position stock at each hub ahead of the programme phase, coordinate site-specific deliveries, and manage inter-hub stock transfers to ensure seamless continuity as the rollout moves between regions.

**Q13:** Do you deliver directly to site, or do we need to collect from your warehouses?
**A:** We deliver directly to site as standard practice for national accounts. Deliveries include offloading at ground level. For sites with specific access constraints (e.g., shopping malls, high-rise buildings), we coordinate delivery windows, crane offloading, and after-hours delivery as required.

**Q14:** What geographic radius does each of your three hubs typically cover?
**A:** Gauteng (Johannesburg) covers the northern cluster — Gauteng, Limpopo, Mpumalanga, North West, and Free State. KZN (Durban) covers the eastern corridor — KwaZulu-Natal and the Eastern Cape. Western Cape (Cape Town) covers the southern and western regions — Western Cape and Northern Cape. This minimises delivery distances and cost.

### Extended Coverage: Product & Technical Deep-Dive

**Q671:** What is the difference between through-body porcelain and glazed porcelain?
**A:** Through-body porcelain has consistent colour throughout the tile thickness — if chipped, the damage is less visible. Glazed porcelain has a coloured glaze over a different body — the glaze provides design and colour, but chips may expose the contrasting body. Through-body is preferred for heavy-duty commercial applications.

**Q672:** Can you provide tiles with metallic or textured surface finishes?
**A:** Yes. Our range includes tiles with metallic glazes (gold, silver, bronze effects), 3D textured surfaces, fabric-look textures, and concrete-effect finishes. These are popular for feature walls, reception areas, and brand-expression elements in flagship stores.

**Q673:** What is a "rectified" large-format tile, and why does it matter?
**A:** Rectified tiles are precision-cut after firing to achieve exact dimensions and sharp, square edges. This allows for very narrow grout joints (1.5–3mm), creating a seamless, premium aesthetic. For large-format tiles (600×600mm+), rectification is essential for professional installation.

**Q674:** Can you provide tiles suitable for underfloor heating systems?
**A:** Yes. Porcelain and ceramic tiles are excellent conductors of heat and are ideal for underfloor heating systems. We specify tiles with thermal compatibility for electric and hydronic underfloor heating, with installation guidelines for heated substrates.

**Q675:** What is the difference between PEI III, IV, and V ratings for our application?
**A:** PEI III is suitable for normal residential foot traffic. PEI IV is for moderate-to-heavy commercial traffic (standard retail stores). PEI V is for heavy commercial and industrial traffic (entrance areas, QSR kitchens, automotive workshops). We recommend PEI IV or V for all commercial applications.

**Q676:** Can you supply tiles with a R11 or R12 slip rating for commercial kitchens?
**A:** Yes. Tiles with R11 and R12 slip ratings (tested to DIN 51130) are available for commercial kitchens, food prep areas, and wet environments. These provide essential slip resistance where grease, water, and food spills create hazardous conditions.

**Q677:** What is the minimum grout joint width you recommend for commercial installations?
**A:** For rectified porcelain tiles in commercial installations, minimum 1.5–2mm grout joints are recommended. For non-rectified tiles, 3–5mm joints accommodate size variation. Joint width is specified in your national specification and must be maintained for warranty validity.

**Q678:** Can you provide tiles with a "soft" or warm underfoot feel for hospitality areas?
**A:** While tiles are inherently hard and cool, LVT (luxury vinyl tile) provides a warmer, softer underfoot feel that is popular in hospitality seating areas, hotel rooms, and boutique retail. LVT can be integrated into your specification for areas where comfort is a priority.

**Q679:** What is the difference between epoxy-based grout and cementitious grout?
**A:** Epoxy grout is chemical-resistant, stain-proof, and ideal for commercial kitchens and food service areas — but more expensive and harder to apply. Cementitious grout is economical, easy to apply, and suitable for most commercial areas — but requires sealing in stain-prone areas.

**Q680:** Can you provide tiles with nano-sealer pre-applied for stain resistance?
**A:** Yes. Some ranges come with factory-applied nano-sealer that provides stain resistance and reduces ongoing maintenance. This is particularly valuable for light-coloured tiles in food service and high-traffic areas where staining is a concern.
