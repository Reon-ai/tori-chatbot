# Failure Diagnostics — Tenting, Cracking, Lippage and Hollow Tiles

**Version:** 2026-06-18  
**Market:** South Africa / Southern Africa  
**Business:** Tiletoria customer and staff RAG knowledge base  

## Purpose
Give Tori a safe diagnostic triage script for common tile failures without assigning blame.

## Rule: diagnose, do not blame
Tori must not blame product, installer, customer or substrate without evidence. It must gather facts and route.

## Common symptoms and possible causes
### Hollow-sounding tile
Possible factors: adhesive coverage, substrate contamination, voids, curing issues, movement, debonding. Needs assessment.

### Tenting or tiles lifting
Possible factors: lack of movement joints, thermal expansion, substrate movement, moisture, bond failure. Urgent escalation.

### Cracked tiles
Possible factors: substrate cracks, structural movement, impact, lack of movement accommodation, voids under tile, inappropriate installation.

### Lippage
Possible factors: uneven substrate, tile warpage tolerance, poor layout, incorrect offset, insufficient joint, installation technique.

### Grout cracking
Possible factors: movement, incorrect mix, poor curing, joint contamination, substrate movement, changes of plane.

## Tori triage fields
- Product name/code and size.
- Date installed.
- Area/application.
- Indoor/outdoor/wet/sun-exposed?
- Substrate type.
- Adhesive/grout used.
- Installer details.
- Photos/video.
- How widespread?
- Any cracks in slab/walls?
- Any water leaks or moisture?
- Were movement joints used?
- Has the issue grown over time?

## Emergency wording
"Please avoid continuing work or forcing tiles down until the issue is reviewed. A tile lifting/tenting issue can indicate movement or bond failure and should be assessed properly."

## Good response
"I’m sorry you’re dealing with this. There are several possible causes, so I don’t want to guess. Please share photos, product details, installation date, area, adhesive used and whether the area is wet, external or sun-exposed. The team can then review the next step."
