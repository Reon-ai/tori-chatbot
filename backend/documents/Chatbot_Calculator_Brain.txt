CALC 01: FLOOR AREA CALCULATOR
================================================================================

BOT INSTRUCTION:
Ask the customer for room dimensions. Break complex shapes into rectangles.
Always deduct fixed items (bathtubs, kitchen islands, built-in cupboards).

RECTANGULAR ROOM:
- Ask: "What is the length and width of the room in metres?"
- Multiply: Area = Length × Width
- Example: 4.2m × 3.5m = 14.7 m²

L-SHAPED ROOM:
- Ask: "Can you split the room into two rectangles?"
- Rectangle A area + Rectangle B area = Total area
- Example: (3m × 4m) + (2m × 2.5m) = 12 + 5 = 17 m²

WITH FIXED ITEMS TO DEDUCT:
| Item | Typical Size | Area to Deduct |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| Standard bath | 1.7m × 0.7m | 1.19 m² |
| Shower enclosure | 0.9m × 0.9m | 0.81 m² |
| Kitchen island | 1.2m × 0.8m | 0.96 m² |
| Built-in wardrobe | 2.0m × 0.6m | 1.2 m² |
| Vanity unit | 1.0m × 0.5m | 0.5 m² |
| Fireplace hearth | 1.5m × 0.5m | 0.75 m² |

RULE: If customer says "I want to tile around the bath, not under it" — DEDUCT the bath area.
RULE: If customer says "floor to ceiling" or "full room" — do NOT deduct. Use total area.

IRREGULAR ROOM (triangles, alcoves):
- Triangle area = (base × height) ÷ 2
- Semi-circle bay window = (π × radius²) ÷ 2
- Add all positive areas, subtract all negative areas (deductions)

SAFETY MARGIN:
- Always round UP to nearest half-square-metre when quoting
- Example: 14.2 m² → quote as 14.5 m² for materials

CALC 02: TILE QUANTITY CALCULATOR
================================================================================

BOT INSTRUCTION:
Step 1: Get floor area from Calc 01
Step 2: Ask which tile size the customer wants
Step 3: Look up tiles per box and coverage per box
Step 4: Add wastage percentage from Calc 09
Step 5: Calculate boxes needed (always round UP)

TILE SIZE REFERENCE TABLE:
| Tile Size (mm) | Tiles per Box | Box Covers |
|----------------|--------------|------------|
| 100 × 100 (mosaic) | 22 sheets | ~1.98 m² |
| 200 × 100 (subway) | 34 pieces | ~0.68 m² |
| 300 × 300 | 17 pieces | ~1.53 m² |
| 400 × 400 | 10 pieces | ~1.60 m² |
| 500 × 500 | 7 pieces | ~1.75 m² |
| 600 × 300 | 8 pieces | ~1.44 m² |
| 600 × 600 | 4 pieces | ~1.44 m² |
| 800 × 800 | 3 pieces | ~1.92 m² |
| 1200 × 600 | 2 pieces | ~1.44 m² |
| 1200 × 200 (wood plank) | 6 pieces | ~1.44 m² |

HOW TO CALCULATE:
- Tiles needed = (Area ÷ Area per tile) × (1 + Wastage%)
- Boxes needed = Tiles needed ÷ Tiles per box → ROUND UP
- Always add: "We recommend ordering one extra box for future repairs"

EXAMPLE CONVERSATION FLOW:
Customer: "I need tiles for my 15 m² kitchen"
Bot: "What size tiles are you considering?"
Customer: "600 × 600"
Bot: "For 15 m² with 600 × 600 tiles laid straight, you need 12 boxes (covers 17.28 m² including 10% wastage). We recommend ordering 13 boxes for spare repairs."

CALC 11: STAIR TILING CALCULATOR
================================================================================

BOT INSTRUCTION:
Stairs have THREE tiled surfaces per step: tread (top), riser (front), and sometimes nosing (edge).
Ask: "How many steps? What is the tread depth, riser height, and stair width?"

SURFACE AREAS PER STEP:
| Surface | Formula | Example (300mm tread, 170mm riser, 1m wide) |
|---------|---------|---------------------------------------------|
| Tread (top) | Depth × Width | 0.30 × 1.00 = 0.30 m² |
| Riser (front) | Height × Width | 0.17 × 1.00 = 0.17 m² |
| Nosing (edge) | Overhang × Width | 0.025 × 1.00 = 0.025 m² |
| TOTAL per step | | 0.495 m² |

QUICK REFERENCE (10 steps at 1m wide):
| Tread Depth | Riser Height | Area per Step | 10 Steps Total |
|-------------|-------------|---------------|---------------|
| 250mm | 170mm | 0.42 m² | 4.2 m² |
| 280mm | 175mm | 0.455 m² | 4.55 m² |
| 300mm | 170mm | 0.47 m² | 4.7 m² |

NOSING OPTIONS:
- Bullnose tile (rounded edge): order matching nosing tiles separately
- Aluminium stair nosing: typically R80-150 per linear metre
- Mitred edge (tile cut at 45°): included in wastage, add 10%

ADD 20% WASTAGE for stair cutting (lots of precision cuts).

EXAMPLE:
Customer: "I have 12 stairs, 280mm tread, 175mm riser, 900mm wide"
Bot: "For 12 stairs at 900mm wide, your total tiled area is approximately 5.5 m². With stair cutting wastage, we calculate for 6.6 m². You'll also need matching bullnose tiles or aluminium stair nosing for the edges — approximately 11 linear metres."
