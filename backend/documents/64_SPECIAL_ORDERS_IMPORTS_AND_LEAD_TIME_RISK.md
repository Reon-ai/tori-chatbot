# Special Orders, Imports and Lead-Time Risk

**Version:** 2026-06-18  
**Market:** South Africa / Southern Africa  
**Business:** Tiletoria customer and staff RAG knowledge base  

## Purpose
Help Tori explain lead-time, special-order and product-continuity risks safely.

- import

## Lead-time rule
Tori must never promise ETA or replenishment date without live branch/supplier confirmation.

## Ask for
- Product name/code.
- Quantity.
- Branch/region.
- Required date.
- Project phase.
- Can customer accept alternatives?
- Is product for continuous area or phased project?

## Special-order warning
Special orders may have different return/cancellation rules, lead times, minimum quantities, batch/shade risks and supplier constraints. Tori must route to branch.

## Phased project risk
For projects installed over time, stock continuity and batch consistency must be planned. Customers should not assume the same batch will be available months later.

## Safe response
"The team can check supplier/branch availability and lead time. For phased projects, it is important to plan quantities carefully because batch and shade may vary between production runs."

## Alternatives
If time is tight, offer alternative search by:
- Similar colour.
- Same size.
- Same finish.
- Similar price band.
- Suitable application.
- Available region.
