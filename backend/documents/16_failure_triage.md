# Tile and Flooring Failure Triage

## Rule

Describe symptoms. Do not declare the cause without inspection and evidence.

## Hollow-Sounding or Loose Tiles

Possible contributors include:

- incomplete adhesive contact
- adhesive skinning
- contamination
- weak substrate
- incorrect adhesive
- movement
- moisture
- poor bedding technique

Ask for:

- area affected
- floor or wall
- tile size
- substrate
- adhesive product
- trowel and back-buttering
- installation date
- photos and lifted-tile evidence if available

## Cracked Tiles

Possible contributors include:

- impact
- substrate crack
- movement joint omission
- deflection
- voids under the tile
- point loading
- thermal or structural movement

The crack pattern and whether it follows a substrate or joint line matter.

## Tenting or Tiles Lifting Together

Possible contributors include restrained movement, missing perimeter or panel movement joints, thermal movement, moisture or loss of bond.

Treat this as a stop-work and safety issue if tiles are lifting sharply.

## Grout Cracking or Popping

Possible contributors include:

- movement
- flexible joint filled with cement grout
- substrate deflection
- weak or over-watered grout
- poor joint cleaning
- early traffic
- inconsistent joint depth

## Lippage

Possible contributors include:

- substrate flatness
- bowed tiles
- layout
- tile-size variation
- workmanship
- incorrect use of levelling systems

Levelling clips do not correct a badly prepared substrate.

## Efflorescence or White Deposits

Possible contributors include moisture moving soluble salts through cementitious materials. Cleaning alone may not solve the moisture source.

## Shade or Batch Concern

Before installation:

- compare labels
- dry-lay
- inspect under suitable lighting
- agree blending and layout

After installation, collect labels, photos, invoices and remaining samples. Do not promise a warranty outcome.

## Complaint Evidence

Collect:

- invoice
- product code
- batch, shade and calibre
- unopened and opened box labels
- site address
- installer details
- substrate and product system
- installation and failure dates
- overview and close-up photos
- affected quantity
- any remedial work already done

## Tori Closing

> “The image or symptom suggests possible causes, but it does not prove one. Please preserve the labels and remaining material and avoid remedial work until the relevant technical or claims team has reviewed the evidence.”
