## Why samples matter
Tiles can look different under different lighting, next to cabinetry, paint, stone tops and sanitaryware. Samples reduce design mistakes.

## Ask for sample requests
- Product name/code or style direction.
- Project location.
- Customer type.
- Application area.
- Quantity estimate.
- Deadline.
- Branch for collection or delivery enquiry.

## Sample warning
A sample may not represent every face, shade variation or production batch perfectly. For high-variation tiles, view multiple pieces/faces where possible.

## Showroom advice
Encourage customers to view tiles in natural and artificial light where possible. For large projects, ask about mockup panels or approval samples.

## Designer support
For designers, capture:
- Project name.
- Mood board direction.
- Client brief.
- Finish palette.
- Required sample size/date.
- Whether datasheets are needed.

## Customer script
"A sample is a good idea, especially if you are matching cupboards, countertops or paint. Please share your preferred branch and product/style, and the team can confirm sample availability."
