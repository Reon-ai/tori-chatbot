## Purpose
Some sites are not suitable for simple product advice. Tori must detect site risk before recommending tiles, LVT, adhesive, waterproofing or trims.

## High-risk areas
### Bathrooms and showers
Risk: waterproofing, falls, drainage, movement, grout misunderstanding.

Ask about shower floor/wall, wet zone, slope, waterproofing and installer.

### Balconies
Risk: waterproofing failure, movement, drainage, public liability, water damage below.

Escalate if leak or waterproofing detail is involved.

### Ramps
Risk: slip safety, slope, public liability.

Do not guarantee suitability. Request product documents and professional sign-off.

### Stairs
Risk: slips, nosing, edges, commercial safety.

Ask about indoor/outdoor, wet exposure, nosing/profile and traffic.

### Pool surrounds
Risk: wet barefoot slip, chemicals, outdoor exposure, drainage.

Require verified product suitability and professional/site review.

### Commercial kitchens/food areas
Risk: grease, wet cleaning, chemical exposure, slip, hygiene.

Escalate to product/spec team.

### Garages
Risk: vehicle loads, oil, hot tyres, cleaning chemicals, substrate.

Do not assume any floor tile is suitable.

## High-risk substrates
### Damp screed
Risk: adhesive failure, efflorescence, LVT bubbling/lifting.

Ask about moisture tests, curing time and damp proofing.

### New screed
Risk: insufficient curing, shrinkage, moisture.

Advise that installation timing depends on screed condition and product system.

### Old tiles
Risk: poor bond, hollow tiles, height issues, contamination.

Ask if existing tiles are sound, clean, fixed and not cracked/hollow.

### Painted floors
Risk: adhesive bond failure.

Escalate for surface preparation guidance.

### Timber/board floors
Risk: movement/deflection.

Require system-specific guidance.

### Cracked slabs
Risk: movement transferring through tile.

Escalate before installation.

## Environmental risks
- coastal salt air
- direct sun and heat
- freeze/thaw in some Southern African regions
- high humidity
- poor ventilation
- heavy rain exposure
- high traffic commercial use

## Product-specific risk
### Large-format tile
Needs flat substrate, suitable adhesive coverage and skilled installation.

### Glue-down LVT
Needs smooth, dry, clean, sound subfloor. Moisture and unevenness are major risks.

### Polished tile
May be slippery when wet; caution in wet areas.

## Safe Tori wording
