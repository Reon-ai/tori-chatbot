# Quote to Branch Handover SOP

**Version:** 2026-06-18  
**Market:** South Africa / Southern Africa  
**Business:** Tiletoria / Tori RAG brain  
**Layer:** Phase 3 Control Layer  

## Purpose
Standardise how Tori collects quote details and sends clean, useful handovers to the correct Tiletoria branch or team.

## Retrieval triggers
- quote
- quotation
- price me
- how much
- send quote
- need tiles
- contractor quote
- bathroom quote
- project quote
- branch handover
- sales lead

## Goal
Tori must turn messy customer conversations into clean quote requests that sales teams can action quickly. The best handover saves staff time and improves conversion.

## Quote types
Classify each quote before capturing fields.

### Retail homeowner quote
A homeowner or renovator needs product and price guidance.

### DIY project quote
Customer may need calculation help, product selection and accessories.

### Contractor/tiler quote
Customer may know m² and wants fast stock/price confirmation.

### Plumber/sanitaryware quote
Customer asks for taps, toilets, basins, mixers, wastes, valves or bathroom accessories.

### Designer/architect quote
Customer needs aesthetics, samples, specs and possibly project pricing.

### Developer/QS quote
Customer needs BOQ, bulk pricing, lead time and product consistency.

### Insurance/replacement quote
Customer needs like-for-like or practical replacement material.

## Universal quote fields
Capture these whenever possible:

- name
- mobile number
- email address
- customer type/persona
- branch or region
- project area: bathroom, kitchen, patio, house, shop, hotel, body corporate, development, insurance claim
- product category
- product name/code if known
- size/finish/colour preference
- m² or room dimensions
- required timing
- delivery or collection
- whether adhesive/grout/trims/levelling/waterproofing must be included
- photos/plans/BOQ available: yes/no

## Fast quote path
When the customer is urgent, ask fewer questions:

1. Product/code or photo.
2. Required quantity/m².
3. Branch/region.
4. Contact number.
5. Delivery or collection.

## Guided quote path
When the customer is unsure, guide first:

1. Ask where the product will be used.
2. Ask indoor/outdoor/wet/commercial/residential.
3. Ask style/colour/size preference.
4. Ask rough area or dimensions.
5. Explain extras required.
6. Offer to prepare quote request.

## Handover summary format
Tori should format the internal handover like this:

```text
TORI QUOTE REQUEST
Customer: [name]
Contact: [mobile/email]
Persona: [retail/contractor/designer/QS/etc.]
Branch/region: [Cape Town/Northriding/KZN/etc.]
Project: [bathroom/patio/house/commercial/etc.]
Product interest: [name/code/category]
Area/quantity: [m²/boxes/dimensions]
Extras: [adhesive/grout/trims/waterproofing/etc.]
Timing: [urgent/this week/future/tender deadline]
Delivery/collection: [preference]
Notes: [important context]
Risk flags: [none / wet area / outdoor / ramp / complaint / insurance / large format]
```

## Customer-facing confirmation
After capturing details, Tori should say:

> Thank you. I have the key details for the quote request. The team will still need to confirm live stock, batch, current price and delivery options before anything is final.

## Quote risk warnings
Include safe warnings where relevant:

### Tiles
Buy enough for the full job plus waste. Future batch/shade matching cannot be guaranteed.

### Large format
Installation quality, flatness and adhesive coverage are critical.

### Outdoor/wet area
Slip suitability and drainage must be checked.

### LVT
Subfloor condition is critical; Tiletoria sells glue-down LVT, not click LVT.

### Special order
Lead time and availability must be confirmed before commitment.

## When to escalate before quote
Escalate first if:

- public safety risk
- active leak
- complaint or claim
- legal threat
- technical certificate needed
- very large project/tender
- price dispute
- stock reservation request

---

## Universal Tori rule for this file
Tori must be helpful, practical and commercially safe. Tori may guide, calculate, explain, collect details and prepare a handover, but must not invent live stock, live prices, delivery availability, technical certifications, warranties, legal outcomes, account status, branch decisions or product suitability where site conditions are unknown. When the answer depends on current branch systems, ERP data, site inspection, supplier confirmation or management approval, Tori must clearly say so and route the customer to the correct Tiletoria team.
