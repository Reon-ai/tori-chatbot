# Trims, Skirting, Nosings and Sealant Calculator

## Linear Products

Measure every exposed edge or required perimeter.

`net_length_m = sum_of_required_lengths`

`order_length_m = net_length_m × 1.10`

`number_of_lengths = ceiling(order_length_m ÷ stock_length_m)`

Do not assume all trims are 2.5 m or 3 m. Use the actual product length.

## Worked Trim Example

- exposed edges: 17.4 m
- trim length: 2.5 m
- allowance: 10%

Calculation:

- order length = 17.4 × 1.10 = 19.14 m
- pieces = ceiling(19.14 ÷ 2.5) = 8 lengths

Purchased length = 20 m.

## Skirting Perimeter

`net_skirting_m = room_perimeter - confirmed_door_openings - confirmed_no_skirting_sections`

Then apply 10% and round to full lengths or full tile boxes.

## Stair Nosings

`nosing_length_m = number_of_nosed_edges × width_of_each_edge_m`

Add landings and return pieces separately.

## Sealant Volume

For a simple rectangular joint estimate:

`sealant_litres = joint_width_mm × joint_depth_mm × joint_length_m ÷ 1000`

`cartridges = ceiling((sealant_litres × 1.10) ÷ cartridge_volume_litres)`

Example:

- width: 6 mm
- depth: 6 mm
- length: 20 m
- cartridge: 0.30 L

Calculation:

- volume = 6 × 6 × 20 ÷ 1000 = 0.72 L
- with allowance = 0.792 L
- cartridges = ceiling(0.792 ÷ 0.30) = 3

## Sealant Limitation

Actual yield depends on:

- joint profile
- backer rod
- nozzle size
- tooling
- waste
- required bond geometry

Manufacturer yield charts override the rectangular estimate.

## Contractor Warnings

- Match trim depth to tile plus the intended adhesive bed and installation detail.
- Measure corners, end caps, connectors and returns separately.
- Movement joints require compatible resilient sealant or profiles; do not fill them with cement grout.
- Stair nosing selection involves safety, edge protection, traffic and finish—not only length.
