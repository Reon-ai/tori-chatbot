## Style directions
### Modern neutral
Large-format greys, greige, stone-look and concrete-look tiles. Works well in open-plan homes, apartments and developments.

### Warm contemporary
Taupe, sand, beige, warm grey and stone textures. Good for homes needing softer warmth.

### Coastal South African
Light stone, sand, soft grey, textured neutrals, easy-clean surfaces and natural materials. Good for Western Cape, Garden Route and KZN coastal projects.

### Industrial / urban
Concrete-look, charcoal, screed-look and matt finishes. Good for lofts, retail, modern kitchens and braai rooms.

### Classic luxury
Marble-look, travertine-look, limestone-look and refined neutrals. Good for bathrooms, entrances and premium homes.

## Grout colour guidance
- Matching grout gives a calmer, seamless look.
- Contrasting grout highlights tile format and pattern.
- Very light grout on busy floors may show dirt more easily.
- Dark grout can frame tiles but may be too strong for some designs.

## Small room advice
Large tiles can make small rooms feel calmer, but layout, cuts and falls matter.

## Dark tile advice
Dark tiles can look dramatic but may show dust, water marks or cleaning residue depending on finish and lighting.

## Light tile advice
Light tiles can open a room but may require practical grout choices in high-traffic areas.

## Customer questions to ask
- What look do you want: calm, warm, dramatic, natural, luxury or industrial?
- Is the room bright or dark?
- Are there pets/children/high traffic?
- Do you prefer low maintenance?
- What colour are your cupboards, walls and counters?
