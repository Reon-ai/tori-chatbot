## Category 1: CPD & Professional Development

**Q1.1:** Does Tiletoria offer CPD-accredited training for architects?

**A:** Yes. Tiletoria's Spec Lab runs SAIA-accredited 'lunch and learn' seminars that carry Category One CPD credits toward your mandatory 25-credit/5-year SACAP cycle. Sessions cover wet area waterproofing, slip resistance standards, large-format installation, and waterproofing system selection.

**A:** Contact the Spec Lab team directly via the website, email, or phone. Sessions can be held at our Menlyn showroom or at your practice premises. We accommodate groups of 6 to 40 architects and typically require 2 weeks' advance notice for scheduling.

## Category 2: Technical Data & Test Certificates

**Q2.1:** Can Tiletoria provide full SANS 1449:2012 test certificates for your tiles?

**A:** Yes. We provide complete SANS 1449:2012 classification certificates for all our porcelain and ceramic tile ranges, including water absorption, breaking strength, modulus of rupture, and dimensional tolerance data. Certificates are issued by SABS-accredited or ISO 17025-certified testing laboratories.

**Q2.2:** What PEI abrasion ratings do your commercial-grade tiles carry?

**A:** Our commercial-performance porcelain tiles carry PEI Class 4 (heavy traffic, moderate abrasion) and PEI Class 5 (heavy traffic, abrasive) ratings. We also stock PEI Class 3 tiles for light commercial and residential applications. PEI ratings are verified by independent laboratory testing.

**Q2.3:** Can you provide DIN 51130 slip resistance test results for your tiles?

**A:** Yes. We provide DIN 51130 slip resistance classifications (R9 through R12) for all floor tiles suitable for commercial and wet area applications. R9 for dry interior areas, R10/R11 for commercial kitchens and bathrooms, R11/R12 for pool surrounds and industrial wet areas.

**Q2.4:** What breaking strength can I expect from your large-format porcelain tiles?

**A:** Our large-format porcelain tiles (1200x1200mm, 1200x2600mm, 1600x3200mm) achieve breaking strengths of 1,300-1,800 N in accordance with SANS 1449:2012 Annex B, well exceeding the minimum 1,000 N requirement for porcelain tiles. Test certificates are available on request.

**Q2.5:** Do your tiles meet the ≤0.5% water absorption requirement for porcelain?

**A:** Yes. All tiles classified as porcelain in our range achieve water absorption rates of ≤0.5%, with many imported Italian and Spanish porcelain tiles achieving ≤0.1%. Full ISO 10545-3 test reports are available for each product line.

**Q2.6:** Can you provide frost resistance test certificates?

**A:** Yes. We provide frost resistance certificates (ISO 10545-12) for all exterior-grade and pool-suitable tiles. Our porcelain tiles from Italian and Spanish manufacturers undergo 100+ freeze-thaw cycles testing, confirming suitability for high-altitude and exposed applications.

**Q2.7:** What dimensional tolerance should I specify for your tiles?

**A:** Our rectified porcelain tiles conform to SANS 1449:2012 dimensional tolerances of ±0.5% on length/width and ±5% on thickness for calibrated products. Rectified tiles achieve tighter tolerances of ±0.2% on length/width, enabling minimal joint widths of 1.5-2mm.

**Q2.8:** Are your test certificates from SABS-accredited laboratories?

**A:** Yes. All test certificates are issued by laboratories accredited to ISO 17025 standards, including SABS Commercial, CSIR Built Environment, and internationally recognised laboratories such as Centro Ceramico Bologna (Italy) and ITC-A (Spain).

**Q2.9:** Can you provide thermal shock resistance data?

**A:** Yes. Thermal shock resistance certificates (ISO 10545-9) are available for our porcelain ranges, confirming resistance to temperature differentials of ≥15°C for standard porcelain and ≥20°C for technical porcelain. This data is critical for underfloor heating and facade applications.

**Q2.10:** Do you have chemical resistance test data for your tiles?

**A:** Yes. We provide chemical resistance classifications (ISO 10545-13) for all commercial-grade tiles. Our glazed porcelain tiles achieve Class A (no visible effect) against standard chemicals including citric acid, sodium hydroxide, and domestic cleaning agents. Special acid-resistant tiles are available for laboratory and industrial applications.

**Q2.11:** What is the Mohs hardness rating of your porcelain tiles?

**A:** Our porcelain tiles typically achieve Mohs hardness ratings of 5-6 (glazed porcelain) to 7-8 (full-body/unglazed technical porcelain). This indicates excellent scratch resistance for commercial high-traffic environments. Specific ratings are listed on each product's technical data sheet.

**Q2.12:** Can you provide stain resistance test results?

**A:** Yes. Stain resistance testing in accordance with ISO 10545-14 is available for all our tiles. Our glazed porcelain tiles achieve Class 4-5 (excellent stain resistance) for common staining agents including coffee, wine, olive oil, and ink. Full test reports with specific stain agents are available.

**Q2.13:** Do you have colour fastness test data for your tiles?

**A:** Yes. Lightfastness testing to ISO 105-B02 is available for all tiles. Our indoor-grade tiles achieve a minimum Blue Wool Scale rating of 6, while exterior-grade tiles achieve rating 7-8, confirming excellent colour stability under UV exposure and artificial lighting.

**Q2.14:** What impact resistance ratings do your tiles achieve?

**A:** Impact resistance data per EN ISO 10545-5 is available. Our standard porcelain tiles achieve impact resistance of 0.50-0.70 Nm, while our technical/structural porcelain achieves 0.80-1.00 Nm, suitable for heavy-duty commercial and industrial applications.

**Q2.15:** Can you provide linear thermal expansion coefficients?

**A:** Yes. The linear thermal expansion coefficient for our porcelain tiles typically ranges from 4.0 to 6.5 x 10⁻⁶ K⁻¹ (ISO 10545-8). This data is essential for calculating movement joint spacing in large-format installations and facade applications.

**Q2.16:** Do you have acoustic performance data for your tiles?

**A:** Impact sound reduction data is available for our tile systems when installed with appropriate acoustic underlayment. Typical ΔLw values range from 14dB to 22dB depending on the substrate and underlayment system. We can provide project-specific acoustic calculations.

**Q2.17:** What is the typical weight per m² for your large-format porcelain tiles?

**A:** Our large-format porcelain tiles (1200x2600mm, 1600x3200mm) in 6mm thickness weigh approximately 14-16 kg/m². Standard 10mm thick porcelain weighs 24-26 kg/m². Weight data is provided on all technical data sheets for structural loading calculations.

**Q2.18:** Can you provide abrasion resistance data for high-traffic commercial entrances?

**A:** Yes. For entrance lobbies and high-traffic commercial areas, we specify tiles with PEI Class 5 and deep abrasion resistance (ISO 10545-6) of ≤120mm³. We also offer technical porcelain with abrasion volumes as low as 50-80mm³ for extreme traffic conditions.

**Q2.19:** Do your tiles comply with SANS 10107 (The Design and Installation of Ceramic Tiling)?

**A:** Yes. All our tiles are specified in accordance with SANS 10107:2017 requirements. We provide method statements, substrate preparation guidelines, and installation detail drawings that align with this standard for warranty compliance.

## Supplementary Section 3: Additional Topics: Emerging Applications, Advanced Sustainability & Specialist Environments

**S3.1:** What is the maximum allowable deflection for a timber floor before tiling?

**A:** For timber floors, maximum allowable deflection is L/360 (span divided by 360) for ceramic tiles and L/480 for porcelain tiles. The floor must also be stable (no bounce), have adequate joist sizing, and be overlaid with cement particle board (minimum 18mm) before tiling.

**S3.2:** Can you provide guidance on tile fixing in coastal environments with high salt exposure?

**A:** Coastal specifications include: salt-resistant porcelain (tested to EN 12370), stainless steel fixings and profiles (316 grade), salt-tolerant adhesives and grouts, enhanced waterproofing, and more frequent maintenance cleaning to remove salt deposits.

**S3.3:** What is the recommended movement joint spacing for a 50m x 30m airport concourse?

**A:** For a 50m x 30m concourse: perimeter movement joints at all edges, intermediate movement joints at 8-10m centres in both directions (creating bays of approximately 8m x 8m), structural movement joints at building expansion joints, and additional joints at changes in substrate or traffic pattern.

**S3.4:** How do I specify tiles for a building that will be constructed over a 5-year phased programme?

**A:** Phased programme specification: select from Permanent Collection products, specify batch consistency requirements, include stockholding clauses, require 12-month discontinuation notice, and plan for potential product evolution between early and late phases.

**S3.5:** What is the correct specification for tiling over a post-tensioned concrete slab?

**A:** Post-tensioned slabs require: allowance for slab shortening (typically 0.1-0.3%), movement joints at closer centres (6-8m), flexible adhesive (S1 classification), perimeter isolation joints, and coordination with PT strand layout to avoid fixing through tendons.

**S3.6:** Can you provide a specification for tile fixing to a lightweight steel deck?

**A:** Steel deck fixing: minimum 1.2mm steel gauge, structural engineer verification of deflection, 22mm cement particle board screwed to deck, flexible adhesive (S1), movement joints at 6m centres, perimeter isolation, and maximum tile weight 25kg/m².

**S3.7:** What is the best approach for tiling over underfloor heating cables?

**S3.8:** How do I handle tile specification when the substrate is unknown at design stage?

**S3.9:** Can you specify tiles for a building with settlement-prone foundations?

**S3.10:** What is the maximum tile size that can be used on a curved wall with 3m radius?

**S3.11:** How should I specify the 'ambient conditions' requirement for tile installation?

**A:** Specify: minimum 5°C and maximum 35°C during installation and for 7 days after; relative humidity <85%; protection from direct sun, wind, and rain; and controlled curing conditions. These ensure proper adhesive curing and long-term performance.

**S3.12:** What should my specification say about tile cutting quality?

**A:** Specify: all cut edges clean and without chips >1mm, cuts straight within 1mm over 600mm, holes core-drilled (not percussion-drilled) with clean edges, and all cut tiles reviewed by architect before fixing. Wet cutting equipment mandatory for porcelain.

**S4.1:** What is the typical drying time for cementitious tile adhesive before grouting?

**A:** Cementitious adhesive requires minimum 24 hours drying time at 20°C before grouting can commence. In cold or humid conditions, extend to 48 hours. Fast-set adhesives can accept grout in 3-6 hours but should not be rushed on large commercial projects.

**S4.2:** Can you provide guidance on tile specification for passive solar design buildings?

**S4.3:** How do I specify tiles for a mixed-use building with residential, retail, and office components?
