# Waterproofing Estimator

## Fast answer

Waterproofing quantity depends on area, number of coats, coverage per litre/kg, corners, bandage and waste.

## Required inputs

- Area to waterproof
- System/product coverage per coat
- Number of coats
- Corners/junctions requiring bandage
- Drying/curing time

## Calculation rule

Estimated litres/kg = area × coats ÷ product coverage. Add waste and bandage quantities for corners, wall/floor junctions and penetrations. Use manufacturer datasheet.

## Worked example

Shower wall and floor total 18 m², product covers 1.5 m²/L per coat, two coats: 18 × 2 ÷ 1.5 = 24 L before waste. Check datasheet and pack sizes.

## Answer format

State the estimate, show the assumptions, round up to full sellable units, and warn that final quantities depend on site conditions and product datasheets.

## Warnings

- Tiles, adhesive and grout are not waterproofing.
- Escalate leaks, balconies, pools and failed waterproofing.

## Do not say or assume

- Do not guarantee the quantity as exact.
- Do not ignore product datasheets, pack sizes, site conditions or workmanship.

## Escalate to a human when

- The customer asks for a guaranteed quantity.
- The project is commercial, exterior, wet-area, pool, balcony, failed installation or legally sensitive.
- The inputs are incomplete or suspicious.
