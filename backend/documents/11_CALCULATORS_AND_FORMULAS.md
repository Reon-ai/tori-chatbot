# Calculators and Formulas

## Purpose

Tori helps customers estimate quantities.

All calculations are estimates for planning. Final quantities must be confirmed by the installer and Tiletoria branch.

## Universal Calculation Rules

Always:

- Use metric.
- Use m² for area.
- Use linear metres for trims.
- Add wastage.
- Round up.
- Round boxes up to full boxes.
- Mention that product-specific coverage may vary.
- Ask for missing dimensions.

Never:

- Invent live product pack sizes.
- Invent exact branch prices.
- Invent labour rates.
- Promise exact final quantity without site confirmation.

# Calculator 1: Floor or Wall Area

## Formula

Area m² = length × width

For multiple rooms:

Total area = room 1 + room 2 + room 3

## Example

Room 4.2 m × 3.5 m = 14.7 m²

## Tori Answer

> Your room is 14.7 m² before wastage. For tiles, we should add wastage and then round up to full boxes.

---

# Calculator 2: Tile Quantity With Wastage

## Formula

Required m² = measured area × (1 + wastage %)

## Suggested Wastage Guide

Simple floor or wall: 10%

Large-format tile: 10% to 15%

Diagonal or herringbone: 12% to 20%

Bathrooms with many cuts: 12% to 15%

Mosaics or feature work: 15% to 20%

Stairs: 15% to 25%

Repairs where batch matching matters: buy extra/spares if available.

## Tori Answer

> For a simple layout, use about 10% wastage. For large-format, diagonal layouts, bathrooms with many cuts or high-variation tiles, allow more.

---

# Calculator 3: Boxes Needed

## Formula

Boxes needed = required m² ÷ m² per box

Round up to the next full box.

## Example

Area with wastage: 32.5 m²  
Box coverage: 1.44 m²  
Boxes = 32.5 ÷ 1.44 = 22.57  
Round up = 23 boxes

## Safe Answer

> You would need about 23 boxes, but the branch must confirm the exact m² per box for that product.

# Calculator 4: Tile Area by Size

## Formula

Tile area m² = tile length in metres × tile width in metres

## Common Examples

600 mm × 600 mm = 0.6 × 0.6 = 0.36 m² per tile

600 mm × 1200 mm = 0.6 × 1.2 = 0.72 m² per tile

700 mm × 1400 mm = 0.7 × 1.4 = 0.98 m² per tile

1200 mm × 1200 mm = 1.2 × 1.2 = 1.44 m² per tile

## 700 x 1400 Box Example

If a 700 mm × 1400 mm tile has 2 tiles per box:

0.98 m² × 2 = 1.96 m² per box

## Safe Answer

> A 700 × 1400 tile covers 0.98 m² per tile. If there are 2 tiles per box, that is 1.96 m² per box.

# Calculator 5: Adhesive Estimate

## Basic Formula

Adhesive kg = area m² × estimated kg per m²

## Practical Coverage Guide

Small wall tiles: about 3 to 4 kg/m²

Standard floor tiles: about 4 to 6 kg/m²

Large-format tiles: about 6 to 8 kg/m²

Outdoor/wet/high-demand areas: product/system-specific, often higher.

## Safe Answer

> Adhesive coverage depends on tile size, trowel notch, substrate flatness, back-buttering and product type. I can estimate, but the adhesive bag and installer must confirm final coverage.

# Calculator 6: Grout Estimate

## Formula

Approximate grout kg per m²:

((Tile length mm + Tile width mm) ÷ (Tile length mm × Tile width mm)) × joint width mm × joint depth mm × grout density

Then multiply by total m².

Use density around 1.6 as a planning estimate unless the grout supplier gives another figure.

## Example

600 × 600 tile  
3 mm joint  
10 mm joint depth  
Density 1.6

((600 + 600) ÷ (600 × 600)) × 3 × 10 × 1.6  
= 0.16 kg/m² approximately

For 30 m²: 30 × 0.16 = 4.8 kg  
Add waste and round up.

## Safe Answer

> Grout use depends heavily on tile size, joint width and tile thickness. Smaller tiles use much more grout than large tiles.

# Calculator 7: Linear Metres for Trims or Skirting

## Formula

Linear metres = exposed edge length or room perimeter needing trim

Add 10% wastage.

## Example

Visible tile edges: 6.8 m  
With 10% wastage: 6.8 × 1.10 = 7.48 m  
Round up to available trim lengths.

## Safe Answer

> Measure every exposed edge that needs a trim, then add about 10% for cutting and joins.

---

# Calculator 8: Self-Levelling Compound

## Formula

Self-leveller kg = area m² × thickness mm × 1.6

Use manufacturer coverage if available.

## Example

Area 20 m²  
Average thickness 3 mm  
20 × 3 × 1.6 = 96 kg

If bags are 20 kg: 96 ÷ 20 = 4.8  
Round up = 5 bags

## Safe Answer

> Self-leveller quantities depend on average thickness. A floor that is 2 mm low uses much less than one that is 8 mm low.

---

# Calculator 9: Screed Volume

## Formula

Volume m³ = area m² × thickness in metres

## Example

Area 28 m²  
Thickness 25 mm = 0.025 m  
Volume = 28 × 0.025 = 0.70 m³

## Safe Answer

> Screed calculations need area and thickness. Drying time is just as important as quantity; tiling too early can cause failure.

---

# Calculator 10: Waterproofing Area

## Formula

Waterproofing area = floor area + wall areas + upturns + overlaps

## Shower Example

Shower floor: length × width  
Walls: wall width × waterproofing height  
Add corners, overlaps and pipe penetrations.

## Safe Answer

> Waterproofing should be calculated as a system, not only by floor area. Include walls, corners, upturns, pipe penetrations and overlaps.

---

# Calculator 11: Glue-Down LVT

## Formula

Required LVT m² = measured floor area × (1 + wastage %)

Suggested wastage:

Simple rooms: 5% to 10%

Complex rooms or diagonal layouts: 10% to 15%

## Safe Answer

> For glue-down LVT, allow wastage and keep spare planks/tiles for future repairs. The subfloor preparation is critical.

---

# Calculator 12: Wedge and Clip Levelling System

## Planning Rule

Clip quantities depend on tile size and layout.

Typical planning estimates:

600 × 600: about 4 clips per tile

600 × 1200: about 6 to 8 clips per tile

700 × 1400: about 8 to 10 clips per tile

Large slabs: installer-specific layout required.

## Formula

Estimated clips = number of tiles × clips per tile

Add 10% for wastage.

## Important Rule

Levelling clips help control lippage. They do not replace a flat substrate, correct adhesive coverage or skilled installation.

## Safe Answer

> I can estimate clips from the tile size and tile count, but your tiler should confirm the clip layout because cuts, perimeter rows and tile pattern affect the final number.

# Calculator 13: Complete Project Starter Budget

## Include

- Tiles or LVT.
- Wastage.
- Adhesive.
- Grout.
- Primer.
- Self-levelling if needed.
- Waterproofing if wet area.
- Trims.
- Spacers or levelling clips.
- Silicone.
- Delivery.
- Labour.
- Removal and disposal.
- Plumbing or electrical changes.
- Spare tiles.

## Safe Answer

> A proper project budget is not only the tile price. Preparation, waterproofing, trims, adhesive, grout, wastage, labour and delivery can change the total significantly.
