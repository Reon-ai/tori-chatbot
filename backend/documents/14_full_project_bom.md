# Complete Contractor Bill of Materials

## Purpose

When the user asks for a complete project quantity, do not answer with tiles alone.

Build a bill of materials from the following sections and mark each line as:

- required
- not required
- unknown—needs confirmation

## Project Inputs

Collect:

- customer or project reference
- site area and nearest branch
- room or zone names
- floor and wall dimensions
- tile or LVT product and pack coverage
- substrate
- wet, dry, indoor, outdoor, suspended, heated or commercial
- product system
- programme and delivery constraints

## Quantity Sections

### Finish material

- floor tiles
- wall tiles
- mosaics or feature tiles
- glue-down LVT
- skirting pieces or tile allocation
- spare batch stock when approved

### Substrate preparation

- repair mortar
- screed
- self-levelling compound
- primer
- keying slurry or specialist primer
- crack or joint treatment specified by the system

### Waterproofing

- primer
- waterproofing compound
- reinforcing membrane
- corner/interface details
- drain or penetration details
- sealant
- protection and flood-test sign-off

### Installation

- adhesive
- additives where specified
- grout
- spacers
- levelling clips
- reusable wedges or caps
- tile trims
- movement-joint profiles
- backer rod and sealant
- stair nosings
- transition profiles

### Cleaning and protection

- appropriate initial cleaner
- sponges and cleaning tools
- floor protection
- protection from following trades
- maintenance guidance

## Rules

- Use the dedicated calculator file for every quantity.
- Do not invent prices.
- Do not invent stock.
- Do not assume all products are available at one branch.
- Do not omit movement joints or waterproofing because the customer did not mention them; mark them for confirmation.
- Do not double-count wastage. Apply it once per line.
- State which quantities rely on product coverage that still needs verification.

## Contractor Closing

> “This is the planning bill of materials. Before ordering, we should verify the exact product pack sizes, technical system, branch stock, batch, delivery and site measurements.”
