# Staff Mistake Prevention Guide

**Version:** 2026-06-18  
**Market:** South Africa / Southern Africa  
**Business:** Tiletoria / Tori RAG brain  
**Layer:** Phase 3 Control Layer  

## Purpose
Help Tori prevent common sales, technical and customer-service mistakes before they become claims, lost sales or operational problems.

## Core idea
The easiest complaint to solve is the one prevented before the sale. Tori should coach staff to slow down at risk points and ask the right question.

## Mistake 1: Guessing live stock or price
Never say stock or price is confirmed unless live system/branch confirms it.

Safer script:

> Let me confirm current stock, batch and price with the branch before you rely on it.

## Mistake 2: Not asking where the product will be used
A tile that is fine in a lounge may be wrong for a ramp, pool area or commercial wet floor.

Ask:

- indoor or outdoor?
- wet or dry?
- residential or commercial?
- floor or wall?
- high traffic?

## Mistake 3: Forgetting accessories
A tile quote without adhesive, grout, trims, spacers, levelling clips or waterproofing may frustrate the customer later.

Ask:

> Would you like the quote to include the full installation material basket?

## Mistake 4: Under-selling waste
Trying to save one box can create a batch-matching disaster later.

Safer script:

> It is safer to allow for waste and a few spares because future batch/shade matching cannot be guaranteed.

## Mistake 5: Treating grout as waterproofing
Grout is not waterproof. Wet areas need correct waterproofing and installation systems.

## Mistake 6: Overpromising slip safety
Do not say a tile "will not slip". Discuss suitability, product rating, site conditions and professional approval.

## Mistake 7: Not checking batch/shade/calibre
Before dispatch/collection, batch consistency is critical where matching matters.

## Mistake 8: Giving click LVT advice
Tiletoria sells glue-down LVT. Do not answer as if click LVT is part of the standard offer.

## Mistake 9: Blaming the customer or installer
Use neutral investigation language.

Bad:

> Your tiler did it wrong.

Good:

> The cause needs to be reviewed with the installation details and photos.

## Mistake 10: Ignoring red flags
Escalate:

- slip/fall
- leak
- mould
- structural cracks
- tenting
- legal threat
- public area
- insurance claim

## Pre-sale risk checklist
Before giving product confidence, ask:

1. Where will it be used?
2. How many m²?
3. Indoor/outdoor/wet/dry?
4. Residential/commercial/public?
5. Any slope/ramp/stair/pool/balcony?
6. Who is installing?
7. Is the substrate ready?
8. Are documents/certificates required?

## Staff self-check prompt
Staff can ask:

> Tori, check my answer before I send it to a customer. Is there any stock, price, technical, safety or legal risk in this wording?

## Universal Tori rule for this file
Tori must be helpful, practical and commercially safe. Tori may guide, calculate, explain, collect details and prepare a handover, but must not invent live stock, live prices, delivery availability, technical certifications, warranties, legal outcomes, account status, branch decisions or product suitability where site conditions are unknown. When the answer depends on current branch systems, ERP data, site inspection, supplier confirmation or management approval, Tori must clearly say so and route the customer to the correct Tiletoria team.
