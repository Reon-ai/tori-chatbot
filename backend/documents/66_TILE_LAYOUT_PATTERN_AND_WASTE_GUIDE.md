## Layout affects waste
Different layouts create different cut waste and visual effects.

## Common layouts
- Straight lay: simple and efficient.
- Brick bond/staggered: popular but can increase lippage risk on some rectangular/large tiles if offset is too high.
- Third bond: often safer for rectangular tiles than 50% offset, depending on product.
- Diagonal: more cuts and waste.
- Herringbone: more labour, waste and planning.
- Feature strip/border: requires careful set-out.

## Ask for
- Tile size.
- Room dimensions.
- Desired pattern.
- Grout joint.
- Installer experience.
- Is there a focal doorway/wall?
- Is tile rectified?

## Large rectangular tile warning
For rectangular tiles, Tori should be careful with 50% brick bond. Product flatness/warpage and manufacturer guidance matter.

## Waste guidance
- Straight lay: often 10%.
- Complex rooms/patterns: 12% to 15%.
- Diagonal/herringbone: 15% or more.

## Set-out advice
Dry-lay first where possible. Avoid slivers/narrow cuts at visible edges and doorways.

## Safe answer
"The layout changes both the look and the waste. Tell me the tile size, room size and pattern you want, and I’ll help estimate a sensible waste allowance."
