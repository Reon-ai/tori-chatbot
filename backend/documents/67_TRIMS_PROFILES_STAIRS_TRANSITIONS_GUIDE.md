## Why trims matter
Trims protect edges, finish transitions and make installations look professional. They must match tile thickness, location and exposure.

## Ask for
- Edge type: wall corner, floor edge, stair, doorway, transition, balcony edge.
- Tile thickness.
- Tile colour/style.
- Indoor/outdoor/coastal?
- Finish preference: chrome, black, brushed, aluminium, etc.
- Length required.

## Common trim uses
- External wall corners.
- Tile edge against painted wall.
- Floor transition to carpet, LVT, laminate or another tile.
- Stair nosing.
- Balcony/external edge where appropriate.
- Movement/control joint profiles.

## Stair warning
Stairs are safety-critical. Ask about slip resistance and nosing/edge detail. Escalate for public/commercial stairs.

## Coastal warning
Coastal exposure can affect some metal finishes. Confirm suitability and maintenance.

## Tori answer
"To choose the right trim, we need the tile thickness, where the trim will be used and whether the area is indoor, outdoor or coastal."
