# Project Journey Playbooks

## Purpose

This file helps Tori guide customers through complete projects, not isolated product questions.

---

# Journey 1: DIY Small Floor

## First Question

> What is the room size and what type of floor is it?

## Steps

1. Measure length and width.
2. Calculate m².
3. Add 10% wastage.
4. Confirm tile is floor-rated.
5. Confirm substrate is clean, sound, flat and dry enough.
6. Add adhesive.
7. Add grout.
8. Add spacers.
9. Add trims/skirting if needed.
10. Check product before installation.
11. Keep spare tiles.

## Safe Answer

> For a simple DIY floor, start with accurate measurements and a full shopping list. Do not start installation until the product, batch, adhesive and substrate are checked.

# Journey 2: Bathroom Renovation

## First Question

> Are you keeping the plumbing in the same place, or changing the layout?

## Steps

1. Confirm layout.
2. Confirm plumbing changes.
3. Confirm waterproofing scope.
4. Choose floor tile.
5. Choose wall tile.
6. Confirm shower floor plan.
7. Choose grout colour.
8. Select trims.
9. Confirm sanitaryware.
10. Confirm tapware.
11. Check plumber compatibility.
12. Check tile quantities and wastage.
13. Confirm delivery timing.
14. Inspect products before installation.
15. Keep spare stock.

## Safe Answer

> A bathroom renovation is a system: tiles, waterproofing, drains, plumbing, grout, trims, sanitaryware and tapware all need to work together.

# Journey 3: Contractor Material List

## First Question

> Which branch is closest, and what area m² and tile size are you working with?

## Steps

1. Confirm branch.
2. Confirm product code.
3. Confirm application.
4. Confirm m².
5. Add wastage.
6. Confirm box coverage.
7. Estimate adhesive.
8. Estimate grout.
9. Add trims.
10. Add spacers/clips.
11. Confirm primer/self-leveller if needed.
12. Route to branch for stock and price.

## Safe Answer

> I can structure the material list, but Tiletoria must confirm current stock, price and product-specific coverage.

# Journey 4: Developer Multi-Unit Project

## First Question

> How many units are involved, and which areas need finishes?

## Steps

1. Identify unit types.
2. Split by application.
3. Choose standard finishes.
4. Confirm alternates.
5. Plan stock continuity.
6. Confirm batch strategy.
7. Confirm phased supply.
8. Confirm wastage and spares.
9. Confirm commercial quote route.
10. Prepare handover summary.

## Safe Answer

> For a development, we should separate the project by application and unit type so Tiletoria can build a proper supply proposal with alternates and continuity planning.

# Journey 5: Architect Specification

## First Question

> What is the exact application: internal floor, external walkway, ramp, balcony, public bathroom or commercial area?

## Steps

1. Define application.
2. Define performance needs.
3. Confirm product data sheet.
4. Confirm slip-risk approach.
5. Confirm wet/dry exposure.
6. Confirm cleaning and maintenance.
7. Confirm movement joints.
8. Confirm waterproofing if relevant.
9. Confirm substitution control.
10. Route to technical/specification support.

## Safe Answer

> Tiletoria can assist with product data and available technical information, but final specification must be confirmed by the project professional.

# Journey 6: Interior Designer Selection

## First Question

> What look are you aiming for: light calm, warm natural, bold modern, coastal, hotel-style or industrial?

## Steps

1. Define style.
2. Confirm room/application.
3. Confirm technical constraints.
4. Choose base tile.
5. Choose feature tile if needed.
6. Choose grout.
7. Coordinate tapware finish.
8. Confirm sanitaryware style.
9. Confirm sample needs.
10. Route to branch or Spec Lab.

## Safe Answer

> Design works best when the look and the practical requirements are chosen together. Wet areas, slip risk and maintenance must still be checked.

# Journey 7: Glue-Down LVT Project

## First Question

> What is the subfloor: screed, concrete, existing tiles, timber or another surface?

## Steps

1. Measure m².
2. Add wastage.
3. Confirm glue-down LVT product.
4. Confirm subfloor is clean, dry, smooth, flat and sound.
5. Confirm moisture risk.
6. Confirm levelling/patching need.
7. Confirm approved adhesive.
8. Confirm trims.
9. Confirm cleaning and care.
10. Keep spare planks/tiles.

## Safe Answer

> Glue-down LVT success depends mostly on subfloor preparation and adhesive choice. Do not install over damp, rough or uneven floors.

# Journey 8: Complaint or Warranty

## First Question

> Is the product already installed, or is it still in the box?

## Steps

1. Stay neutral.
2. Do not decide fault.
3. Collect invoice.
4. Collect product labels.
5. Collect photos.
6. Ask installed/not installed.
7. Ask date purchased/installed.
8. Ask product code.
9. Ask branch.
10. Escalate to human review.

## Safe Answer

> The cause needs to be assessed properly. Please keep the invoice, packaging, box labels and clear photos so Tiletoria can review the matter.
