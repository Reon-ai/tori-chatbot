# Product Selection by Application

## Purpose

Use this file when customers ask which product to use in a specific area.

Tori must not approve final suitability without product-specific data and site-specific checks.

---

# Indoor General Floors

## Priorities

- Floor-rated product.
- Suitable wear resistance.
- Practical finish.
- Easy cleaning.
- Good grout colour.
- Enough spare stock.
- Correct adhesive and grout.
- Movement joints where required.

## Safe Answer

> For general indoor floors, choose a floor-rated tile with a practical finish and confirm the correct adhesive, grout and wastage. Keep spare tiles from the same batch for future repairs.

---

# Bathrooms

## Priorities

- Water exposure.
- Slip risk.
- Waterproofing.
- Falls to drain.
- Cleaning.
- Grout colour.
- Correct adhesive and grout.
- Silicone at movement/change-of-plane areas.
- Plumber coordination.

## Safe Answer

> In bathrooms, the tile choice must work with waterproofing, falls, drainage, adhesive, grout and maintenance. Tiles and grout are not the waterproofing system.

---

# Shower Walls

## Priorities

- Waterproofed substrate.
- Suitable adhesive.
- Correct grout.
- Movement/change-of-plane joints.
- Cleaning.
- Tile size and layout.
- Niche details.
- Drainage and ventilation.

## Safe Answer

> Shower walls need proper waterproofing behind the tile. The tile finish matters, but the waterproofing and installation system are what protect the building.

---

# Shower Floors

## Priorities

- Slip risk.
- Falls to drain.
- Tile size compatible with slope.
- Drain type.
- Waterproofing integration.
- Grout joints.
- Maintenance.
- Professional installer.

## Safe Answer

> Shower floors are high-risk. The tile size, slip resistance, falls, drain and waterproofing must work together. Please confirm the product and layout with Tiletoria and the installer before purchase.

---

# Kitchens

## Priorities

- Easy cleaning.
- Stain resistance.
- Slip risk when wet or greasy.
- Practical grout colour.
- Compatibility with cabinets and appliances.
- Correct trims and movement joints.
- Maintenance.

## Safe Answer

> For kitchens, choose a practical floor-rated tile or suitable LVT, with a grout colour that does not show every mark. Cleaning and slip risk matter because kitchens get water, oil and food spills.

---

# Patios and Outdoor Areas

## Priorities

- Outdoor suitability.
- Surface texture.
- Drainage.
- Slope.
- Exterior adhesive.
- Exterior grout.
- Movement joints.
- Sun and thermal movement.
- Algae and dirt cleaning.

## Safe Answer

> Outdoor tiles need the correct tile, adhesive, grout, drainage, slope and movement joints. A textured tile alone does not make an outdoor area safe.

---

# Balconies

## Priorities

- Waterproofing.
- Drainage.
- Falls.
- Movement joints.
- Edge details.
- Weight/load considerations.
- Building/body corporate rules.
- Professional specification.

## Safe Answer

> Balconies are high-risk because waterproofing and drainage failure can damage the building. Please involve Tiletoria and the responsible contractor or professional before selecting the product.

## Escalate

Always escalate balcony questions.

# Ramps

## Priorities

- Slip risk.
- Gradient.
- Wet/dry conditions.
- Drainage.
- Public access.
- Cleaning.
- Footwear.
- Traffic.
- Professional sign-off.

## Safe Answer

> Ramps are safety-sensitive. No tile should be treated as slip-proof. Slip performance depends on tile surface, slope, drainage, cleaning, footwear and site use. A professional should confirm suitability.

## Escalate

Always escalate ramp questions.

---

# Pool Surrounds

## Priorities

- Barefoot slip risk.
- Water exposure.
- Chemicals.
- Drainage.
- Heat underfoot.
- Maintenance.
- Edge trims.
- Professional review.

## Safe Answer

> Pool surrounds need careful review because the surface is often wet and used barefoot. Confirm product-specific data, drainage, cleaning and edge details before purchase.

## Escalate

Always escalate pool surrounds.

# Rental Properties

## Priorities

- Durable.
- Neutral.
- Easy to clean.
- Easy to replace.
- Standard sizes.
- Spare stock.
- Practical grout.
- Chrome or easy-care tapware.

## Safe Answer

> For rentals, choose durable, neutral, easy-to-clean products and keep spare tiles or LVT planks for future repairs.

---

# Sectional Title / Body Corporate Properties

## Priorities

- Body corporate rules.
- Plumbing changes.
- Waterproofing.
- Acoustic requirements.
- Floor level changes.
- Lift and delivery access.
- Working hours.
- Debris removal.
- Common property boundaries.

## Safe Answer

> Before buying for a sectional-title unit, check body corporate rules for plumbing, waterproofing, floor finishes, noise, working hours and delivery access.

---

# Coastal Homes

## Priorities

- Moisture.
- Salt air.
- Ventilation.
- Corrosion risk for fittings.
- Outdoor algae risk.
- Waterproofing.
- Tap finish care.
- Cleaning products.

## Safe Answer

> For coastal homes, confirm moisture exposure, ventilation, outdoor cleaning and tap finish care before selecting products.
