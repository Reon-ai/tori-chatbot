# Installation Preparation and Stop-Work Checks

## Purpose

This file helps Tori prevent avoidable installation failures.

The most important rule:

> If something looks wrong before installation, stop before fixing the product.

Installed products are much harder to assess, remove, return or replace.

---

# Universal Pre-Installation Check

## Use When

Customer says:

- “Tiler starts tomorrow.”
- “We received the tiles.”
- “Can we install?”
- “The boxes look different.”
- “The colour seems off.”
- “Some tiles are chipped.”
- “The batch numbers differ.”

## Checklist

Before installation, check:

- Product name/code.
- Size.
- Finish.
- Colour.
- Quantity.
- Shade.
- Batch.
- Calibre.
- Box labels.
- Visible damage.
- Surface appearance.
- Tile faces/variation.
- Correct adhesive.
- Correct grout.
- Layout direction.
- Wastage allowance.
- Installer has seen the product.
- Customer has approved the appearance.

## Safe Answer

> Before installation starts, check product code, shade, batch, calibre, finish, quantity and visible condition. Open several boxes and view tiles together. If anything looks wrong, stop and contact Tiletoria before fixing the product.

## Stop-Work Trigger

Stop if:

- Wrong product.
- Wrong size.
- Mixed shade/batch/calibre.
- Visible damage.
- Missing labels.
- Customer unhappy with variation.
- Installer unsure.
- Wrong adhesive.
- Waterproofing not complete in wet area.

# Tile Blending Check

## Use When

Product has natural variation, stone look, marble look, cement look, wood look or V3/V4 rating.

## Checklist

- Open several boxes.
- Dry-lay representative tiles.
- Blend from multiple boxes.
- Avoid repeated faces close together.
- Confirm customer accepts appearance.
- Keep spare tiles.

## Safe Answer

> Blend tiles from several boxes during installation, especially for V3/V4, marble-look, stone-look, cement-look and wood-look tiles. This prevents patches or repeated patterns.

# Substrate Check for Tiles

## Checklist

Substrate must be:

- Clean.
- Sound.
- Stable.
- Dry enough for the system.
- Flat enough for tile size.
- Free from dust, oil, paint, loose material and contaminants.
- Suitable for the selected adhesive.
- Correctly primed where required.

## Safe Answer

> Tiles are only as good as the surface below them. The substrate must be clean, sound, stable, dry enough and flat enough for the tile size.

## Stop-Work Trigger

Stop if:

- Loose screed.
- Dusty surface.
- Damp surface.
- Paint or oil contamination.
- Large cracks.
- Hollow areas.
- Uneven substrate for large format.
- No primer where required.

---

# New Screed Check

## Key Rule

New screed must cure and dry before tiling.

## Safe Answer

> New screed needs enough drying time before tiling. Tiling too early can trap moisture and cause failure. The installer should confirm moisture and readiness before installation.

## Stop-Work Trigger

Stop if:

- Screed is still damp.
- No drying time allowed.
- Moisture readings are not checked where required.
- Surface is weak, dusty or cracked.

# Shower and Wet-Area Check

## Checklist

Confirm:

- Waterproofing system complete.
- Waterproofing cured.
- Drain installed.
- Falls to drain correct.
- No ponding.
- Tile size suits falls.
- Adhesive suitable.
- Grout suitable.
- Corners and changes of plane planned.
- Shower screen/door details considered.

## Safe Answer

> Before tiling a shower or wet area, confirm the waterproofing system is complete and cured. Tiles and grout are not a substitute for waterproofing.

## Stop-Work Trigger

Stop if waterproofing is missing, damaged, incomplete or uncertain.

---

# Outdoor Tile Check

## Checklist

Confirm:

- Tile suitable for outdoor use.
- Adhesive suitable for exterior use.
- Grout suitable for exterior use.
- Drainage/falls correct.
- Movement joints planned.
- Substrate sound.
- Cleaning plan for algae and dirt.
- Edge details safe.

## Safe Answer

> Outdoor tiles need more than a textured surface. Confirm outdoor suitability, adhesive, grout, drainage, movement joints and maintenance before installation.

## Stop-Work Trigger

Stop if:

- Indoor adhesive is being used outside.
- No drainage plan.
- No movement joints.
- Tile not confirmed for outdoor use.

# Glue-Down LVT Check

## Checklist

Confirm:

- Product is glue-down LVT.
- Subfloor is clean.
- Subfloor is dry.
- Subfloor is smooth.
- Subfloor is flat.
- Subfloor is sound.
- Old grout lines are smoothed/levelled.
- Approved adhesive selected.
- Layout direction confirmed.
- Product stored correctly.
- Manufacturer instructions followed.

## Safe Answer

> Glue-down LVT is only as good as the subfloor below it. The floor must be clean, dry, smooth, flat and sound before adhesive is applied.

## Stop-Work Trigger

Stop if:

- Damp concrete.
- Rough screed.
- Visible grout lines.
- Dusty surface.
- Old adhesive residue.
- Wrong adhesive.
- Product stored outside.
- Site temperature not suitable.

# Sanitaryware Check

## Checklist

Before installation, check:

- Product name/code.
- Colour/finish.
- Cracks.
- Chips.
- Glaze defects.
- Missing parts.
- Fixing kit.
- Seat included or excluded.
- Waste included or excluded.
- Installation instructions.
- Plumbing compatibility.

## Safe Answer

> Inspect sanitaryware before installation. Report visible damage, missing parts or wrong product before it is fitted.

## Stop-Work Trigger

Stop if cracks, chips, missing parts, wrong model or wrong colour are found before installation.
