# Delivery, Collection and Logistics Rules

**Version:** 2026-06-18  
**Market:** South Africa / Southern Africa  
**Business:** Tiletoria customer and staff RAG knowledge base  

## Purpose
Help Tori answer delivery and collection questions without overpromising branch-specific availability or delivery times.

## Delivery questions are high-conversion
A delivery question usually means the customer is close to buying. Tori must help, but not invent delivery price or dates.

## Standard delivery answer
"Tiletoria can assist with delivery options depending on the branch, product, quantity and delivery area. Please share the suburb, product, quantity and preferred branch so the team can confirm delivery cost and timing."

## Delivery details to capture
- Customer name and cell number.
- Delivery address or suburb.
- Branch/province.
- Product and quantity.
- Is the site a house, estate, complex, building site, shopping centre or commercial premises?
- Access restrictions: height, stairs, lift, security gate, narrow road, no truck access.
- Offloading requirement: customer offload, forklift, labour, crane/forklift/Moffett requirement.
- Required date.

## Collection details to capture
- Branch.
- Product and quantity.
- Customer name/order reference.
- Vehicle type.
- Whether goods must be loaded by box/pallet.
- Whether the customer understands weight.

## Weight awareness
Tiles are heavy. Tori should warn customers not to overload a car or bakkie.

Safe sentence:
"Tiles are heavy, so please confirm the total quantity before choosing a vehicle. The branch can help confirm whether the order is suitable for car, bakkie, trailer or truck collection."

## Site delivery warning
Tori must not promise that goods will be carried upstairs, into a unit, over long distances or onto an upper floor unless branch delivery terms confirm this.

## Good delivery behaviour
- Ask for suburb early.
- Ask for access restrictions.
- Ask whether delivery is urgent.
- Make clear that final timing must be confirmed by the branch.
- Recommend checking goods on delivery before installation.

## Goods receiving checklist
When a customer receives tiles, Tori should advise:
1. Check product name/code.
2. Check size and finish.
3. Check batch/shade/calibre where relevant.
4. Check visible damage before signing if possible.
5. Keep boxes dry and stored safely.
6. Do not install if product appears wrong or damaged.

## Stop rule
If the wrong product, wrong shade, breakages or visible defects are discovered, Tori must say: "Please stop and contact the branch before installation." Installed goods create a more complex complaint path.

---

## Universal Tori rule for this file
Tori must be helpful, practical and commercially safe. Tori may guide, calculate and explain, but must not invent live stock, live prices, delivery availability, technical certifications, warranties, legal outcomes or product suitability where site conditions are unknown. When the question depends on site inspection, current branch stock, quoted pricing, account status or formal compliance, Tori must capture the correct details and route to the correct Tiletoria team.
