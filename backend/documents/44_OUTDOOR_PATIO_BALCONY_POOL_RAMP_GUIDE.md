# Outdoor, Patio, Balcony, Pool and Ramp Guide

**Version:** 2026-06-18  
**Market:** South Africa / Southern Africa  
**Business:** Tiletoria customer and staff RAG knowledge base  

## Purpose
Provide high-safety guidance for external and wet/sloped applications in Southern African conditions.

## Outdoor is not just indoor with sunlight
External areas face rain, thermal movement, UV, wind, dirt, cleaning, slope and slip risk. Tori must ask more questions before recommending.

## Ask for
- Is it covered or fully exposed?
- Is the area flat, sloped or a ramp?
- Is it near a pool, braai, garden or entrance?
- Is it residential or commercial/public?
- Existing substrate: concrete, screed, old tiles, waterproofed slab?
- Any water pooling?
- Any cracks?
- Required look/colour/size.
- Children/elderly/public users?

## Slip guidance
Outdoor wet areas generally need more slip resistance than indoor dry spaces. R-ratings can help compare surfaces, but final suitability depends on the exact application, slope, contamination, footwear, cleaning and expected users.

## Balcony above occupied space
This is high-risk. Tori must not solve casually.

Say:
"A balcony above a room or living area needs proper waterproofing and drainage before tile selection. Tiles and grout are not waterproofing. This should be checked properly before installation."

## Ramp rule
Ramps are high-risk because slope increases slip risk. Tori must escalate to branch/technical/spec team and avoid making a guarantee.

## Pool surround rule
Ask about wet barefoot use, drainage, texture, cleaning and heat underfoot. Avoid polished/glossy recommendations.

## Outdoor installation warnings
- Use correct exterior-suitable adhesive/system.
- Do not bridge movement joints.
- Include perimeter and movement joints.
- Ensure falls/drainage.
- Confirm waterproofing where needed.
- Confirm slip suitability.

## Good answer
"For outdoor use, especially patios, balconies, pools and ramps, we need to confirm exposure, slope, wetness and substrate before choosing. Please share photos and the area dimensions so the team can recommend a suitable option safely."
