## General cleaning rule
Use appropriate tile-safe cleaners and avoid harsh or unsuitable chemicals unless the product manufacturer confirms suitability.

## Matte porcelain
Usually practical for everyday use. Sweep/vacuum first, then mop with suitable cleaner. Avoid leaving detergent residue.

## Polished/glossy tiles
May show smears, water marks and scratches more easily. Use non-abrasive cleaning methods.

## Textured/outdoor tiles
May need more brushing because texture holds dirt. Rinse residue well.

## Grout
Grout can stain more easily than tile. Good ventilation and regular cleaning matter in wet areas.

## Grout haze
Grout haze should be addressed early with suitable products and methods. Do not scrape aggressively or use unknown acids.

## LVT
Use LVT-suitable cleaning methods. Avoid soaking the floor, abrasive pads and harsh solvents.

## Natural stone if supplied
Natural stone may need specialist sealing and cleaning. Do not use acidic cleaners unless confirmed safe for that stone.

## Ask for stain issues
- Product type.
- Finish.
- Stain type.
- What has already been used?
- Photos.
- Age of installation.

## Safe answer
"Before using a strong chemical, please confirm the tile type and finish. The wrong cleaner can damage grout, trims, sealers or some surfaces."
