# Persona 9: Smaller Flooring Merchants (Wholesale) — Q&A Knowledge Base

## Category 1: Wholesale Account Setup

**Q1:** How do I open a wholesale account with Tiletoria?
**A:** Contact our wholesale team on 021 555 5700 or email wholesale@tiletoria.co.za. You'll need to complete a wholesale account application form, provide your company registration documents, VAT certificate, valid trade references from two existing suppliers, and banking details. Our wholesale team will review your application within 3-5 business days and advise on credit terms or pro forma arrangements.

**Q2:** What documents do I need to submit for a wholesale account application?
**A:** You'll need: (1) Company registration documents (CCR14 or equivalent), (2) VAT registration certificate, (3) Proof of business address (utility bill or lease agreement), (4) Banking details for EFT payments, (5) Two trade references from existing building material or tile suppliers, and (6) ID copies of the owner/director(s). For CCs or trusts, all member/trustee IDs are required.

**Q3:** How long does wholesale account approval take?
**A:** Standard wholesale account approval takes 3-5 business days from receipt of a complete application. If trade references respond promptly, it can be as quick as 2-3 days. Pro forma accounts (payment upfront) can often be opened within 24-48 hours. Credit account applications may take 5-7 business days depending on credit reference turnaround.

**Q4:** Can I start buying on a cash basis while my credit application is being processed?
**A:** Yes — most new merchants start on a pro forma (payment upfront) basis while their credit application is being assessed. This allows you to begin trading immediately. Once your credit application is approved, your account is converted to 30-day terms and any pro forma purchases are reflected on your credit record with us.

**Q5:** What trade references do you accept for a new wholesale account?
**A:** We require two trade references from existing building material suppliers, tile distributors, sanitaryware suppliers, or builders merchants you've traded with for at least six months. References from hardware stores, plumbing suppliers, or building material wholesalers are preferred. References from cash-only suppliers carry less weight than those who extended credit terms.

**Q6:** I'm a new business with no trade references. Can I still get a wholesale account?
**A:** Yes, but you'll start on a pro forma (payment upfront) basis. After six months of consistent purchasing and payment history with Tiletoria, you can apply for credit terms. Some new merchants also provide a personal surety from the owner/director or a bank guarantee to accelerate credit approval.

**Q7:** Do I need a physical showroom to qualify for a wholesale account?
**A:** Not necessarily. While most of our wholesale customers operate from showrooms or shop-front premises, we also supply contractors' yards, builders merchants with dedicated flooring sections, and specialist bathroom retailers. Your business model needs to demonstrate a route to market for tile and flooring products.

**Q8:** Can I have multiple wholesale accounts if I have more than one branch?
**A:** Yes, many of our multi-branch customers operate separate wholesale accounts per branch for stock management purposes. Alternatively, you can operate a single group account with multiple delivery addresses. Your account manager will advise on the best structure based on your business setup and credit profile.

**Q9:** What is the minimum turnover requirement for a wholesale account?
**A:** We don't enforce a strict minimum turnover threshold. We work with merchants turning R5 million to R50 million annually. What matters more is your business model, market reach, and commitment to stocking and promoting our product ranges. Even smaller specialist retailers are welcome if they demonstrate growth potential.

**Q10:** Is there a membership fee or annual subscription for wholesale accounts?
**A:** No, there is no membership fee or annual subscription to maintain a wholesale account with Tiletoria. Your account remains active as long as you maintain regular purchasing activity (at minimum, one order every six months) and settle any outstanding amounts within agreed terms.

**Q11:** Can I visit your warehouse before opening an account to see the stock range?
**A:** Absolutely — we encourage prospective wholesale customers to visit our warehouses. Our Cape Town (Paarden Eiland), Durban, Johannesburg, and Paarden Eiland facilities are open for trade viewings by appointment. Contact the wholesale team to arrange a guided tour with one of our account managers.

**Q12:** What is the difference between a wholesale account and a retail cash sale?
**A:** Wholesale accounts receive preferential pricing significantly below our retail price list, access to exclusive wholesale-only product ranges, dedicated account management, delivery services, credit terms (once approved), volume discount structures, and first access to clearance and promotional stock. Retail cash sales are at marked prices with none of these benefits.

**Q13:** Do you offer wholesale accounts to online-only retailers?
**A:** We evaluate online-only retailers on a case-by-case basis. Key considerations include your existing web traffic, customer base, digital marketing capability, and whether you hold physical stock. We generally prefer merchants with a showroom or trade counter, but proven e-commerce operators in the building materials space are considered.

**Q14:** Can my buying manager be the primary contact for the wholesale account, or does it need to be the owner?
**A:** Your buying manager can absolutely be the primary day-to-day contact for ordering, stock queries, and range discussions. However, the owner or MD must sign the credit application and account opening documents. We recommend designating your buying manager as an authorised contact on the account for operational efficiency.

**Q15:** What happens after I submit my wholesale application — what is the process?
**A:** (1) Application received and acknowledged within 24 hours, (2) Credit department verifies documents and contacts trade references (Days 1-3), (3) Credit assessment completed (Days 3-5), (4) Account approved with credit limit and terms, or pro forma status advised, (5) Welcome pack issued with price lists, ordering details, and account manager contact, (6) First order can be placed immediately upon approval.

**Q16:** If my credit application is declined, can I reapply later?
**A:** Yes, you can reapply after six months. Common reasons for initial decline include insufficient trading history, adverse credit bureau information, or weak trade references. During the six-month period, trading on a pro forma basis and building a payment history with Tiletoria significantly strengthens a reapplication.

**Q17:** Do you offer a trial period or first-order discount for new wholesale accounts?
**A:** New wholesale accounts receive our standard wholesale pricing from day one. During pre-season show periods (typically February-March and August-September), new accounts can also access show pricing on their first order if they attend or place orders during the promotional window. Speak to your account manager about current new-account incentives.

**Q18:** Can I get a wholesale account if I'm based outside South Africa in a neighbouring country?
**A:** Yes, we supply merchants across Southern Africa. Accounts for Botswana, Namibia, Mozambique, Zimbabwe, and Zambia are handled through our export division. Terms may differ — typically pro forma or confirmed LC for the first 12 months. Export documentation and cross-border logistics are arranged through our team.

**Q19:** What is your policy on account confidentiality — will my competitors know I'm buying from you?
**A:** We treat all customer information as strictly confidential. We do not disclose your purchasing activity, pricing, or account details to other merchants, including those in the same town or region. Your commercial relationship with Tiletoria is private, and we expect the same discretion from our wholesale customers regarding our pricing.

**Q20:** How do I update my account details if my business address or banking details change?
**A:** Email your updated details to accounts@tiletoria.co.za with "Account Update" in the subject line. Attach supporting documentation (new utility bill for address changes, stamped bank letter for banking changes). Changes are processed within 2-3 business days. Banking detail changes require verification for security purposes.

**Q21:** Do I need to sign a wholesale supply agreement or contract?
**A:** Yes, all wholesale accounts sign our standard Wholesale Trading Terms and Conditions. This covers pricing confidentiality, payment terms, returns policies, delivery terms, and minimum marketing standards for displaying our brands. The agreement is not an exclusive supply contract — you're free to buy from other suppliers.

**Q22:** Can my Tiletoria wholesale account be suspended or closed?
**A:** Accounts may be suspended for persistent late payment (accounts over 60 days overdue), fraudulent activity, or breach of trading terms. We'll always attempt to resolve issues through discussion before any suspension. If you wish to close your account voluntarily, simply settle any outstanding balance and notify your account manager.

**Q23:** What is the difference between the wholesale accounts at Tiletoria branches — is pricing the same nationwide?
**A:** Wholesale pricing structures are consistent nationwide, but freight costs vary by region, affecting delivered pricing. Each branch manages its own wholesale accounts, but your account manager can coordinate orders from multiple branches. National group accounts receive unified pricing and centralised reporting.

**Q24:** Who will be my main point of contact once my wholesale account is active?
**A:** You'll be assigned a dedicated wholesale account manager based at your nearest Tiletoria branch. For larger accounts, you may also have access to a regional wholesale manager and our head office category management team for range planning and strategic discussions.

**Q25:** Can I get a price list before opening an account to evaluate your competitiveness?
**A:** We provide indicative pricing on request to serious prospective wholesale customers who have submitted an account application or are in the process of doing so. Full wholesale price lists are issued upon account approval. Our account managers can also quote on specific product categories you're interested in during the evaluation phase.

## Category 2: Wholesale Pricing & Margins

**Q26:** What kind of markup can I expect as a wholesale customer buying from Tiletoria?
**A:** Tiletoria's wholesale pricing is structured to allow you a 25-45% markup to retail, depending on the product category. Fast-moving ceramic wall tiles typically allow 30-35% markup, while specialist porcelain and imported designer tiles can support 40-45%. Your account manager can provide category-specific margin guidance.

**Q27:** How often do wholesale price lists get updated?
**A:** Wholesale price lists are updated quarterly (March, June, September, December) to reflect exchange rate movements, factory price adjustments, and freight cost changes. Major price movements due to currency volatility may trigger interim adjustments. You'll receive 14 days' notice of any price increases.

**Q28:** Is there a volume discount structure on top of wholesale pricing?
**A:** Yes. Beyond standard wholesale pricing, additional volume rebates apply at quarterly and annual tiers based on your total purchase value. Typical thresholds are R250,000, R500,000, R750,000, and R1 million+ per quarter. Your account manager will confirm your specific rebate structure upon account opening.

**Q29:** What is pre-season show pricing, and how does it work?
**A:** Pre-season show pricing is promotional wholesale pricing offered during our annual buying windows (February-March for winter stock and August-September for summer ranges). Prices are typically 5-15% below standard wholesale for forward orders placed during these periods. Stock is delivered according to agreed schedules.

**Q30:** How do I access pre-season show pricing if I can't attend the trade show in person?
**A:** Show pricing is available to all active wholesale accounts during the promotional window, regardless of attendance. Your account manager will send you the show catalogue and price list via email. Orders can be placed by email, phone, or through your online portal. Early ordering secures the best stock availability.

**Q31:** Can you recommend retail pricing for my market, or is that my decision?
**A:** We provide a Manufacturer's Recommended Selling Price (MRSP) on most wholesale price lists as a guideline. However, retail pricing is entirely your commercial decision. We encourage you to price according to your local market conditions, competitor positioning, and service level. The MRSP is a reference point only.

**Q32:** How do I know if my current supplier's pricing is competitive against Tiletoria's wholesale prices?
**A:** Our account managers are happy to do a competitive price comparison on specific product categories or SKUs you're currently buying elsewhere. Submit your current price list or key product prices (with supplier names redacted if preferred), and we'll benchmark against our wholesale pricing. No obligation to switch.

**Q33:** Does the exchange rate affect my wholesale pricing, and how is this managed?
**A:** Yes, most of our imported product pricing is linked to EUR, USD, or factory currency. We fix pricing at landed cost when stock arrives and is costed into our system, so your price list reflects actual landed costs rather than daily spot rates. Quarterly price list updates capture cumulative exchange rate and cost movements.

**Q34:** Are there settlement discounts for early payment?
**A:** Yes, accounts that settle within 7 days of statement date qualify for a settlement discount — typically 2.5-3% depending on your account tier. This is over and above any volume rebates. To qualify, payment must clear into our account within the 7-day window. Discounts are credited to your account monthly.

**Q35:** What is the price difference between local SA-manufactured tiles and imported tiles at wholesale?
**A:** Locally manufactured tiles (Johnson Tiles, Ceramic Industries) are generally 15-30% cheaper at wholesale than equivalent-quality European imports, due to shorter supply chains and no import freight costs. However, European imports command higher retail prices and margins. We recommend a mix: local products for volume, imports for margin.

**Q36:** How do I protect my margins when competing against competitor retailers, competitor retailers, or competitor retailers?
**A:** Focus on products and services the chains can't match: exclusive ranges (our exclusive mosaic range, our laminate flooring, our laminate flooring), personalised service, local stock availability, installation support, and product knowledge. Avoid competing solely on price for commodity products. Your account manager can help you develop a differentiated product mix.

**Q37:** Can I get special pricing for a large project order that exceeds my normal stock purchases?
**A:** Yes, for significant project orders (typically R100,000+ wholesale value), your account manager can negotiate project-specific pricing with our commercial team. This considers the total volume, product mix, delivery schedule, and payment terms. Project pricing is quoted per job and doesn't affect your standard wholesale pricing.

**Q38:** Are there price differences between buying ex-branch and having stock delivered?
**A:** Wholesale price lists are quoted ex-branch (EXW). Delivery costs are additional and calculated based on distance, volume, and weight. For larger orders within 60km of a branch, delivery may be included depending on your account tier. Your account manager can provide a delivered pricing schedule for your area.

**Q39:** How do I know when a price increase is coming so I can stock up beforehand?
**A:** We provide 14 days' written notice of all price increases via email to all active wholesale accounts. Your account manager will also call key accounts to discuss the impact. Smart merchants typically place forward orders before the effective date to lock in current pricing — this is standard practice and encouraged.

**Q40:** Do you price match against other tile wholesalers if I find a product cheaper elsewhere?
**A:** We evaluate price match requests case by case. If you can demonstrate that an identical or directly comparable product is available from a reputable competitor at a lower wholesale price, we'll review our pricing. We need the competitor's written quote and product specification to verify comparability.

**Q41:** What is the typical margin difference between ceramic wall tiles and porcelain floor tiles at retail?
**A:** Ceramic wall tiles are more commoditised with typical retail markups of 25-35%. Porcelain floor tiles, particularly large-format (600x600mm and above) and rectified porcelain, support markups of 35-45% due to their technical advantages, durability ratings, and perceived value. Polished porcelain commands the highest margins.

**Q42:** Are there any hidden costs I should factor into my landed cost besides the wholesale price?
**A:** Beyond the ex-branch wholesale price, factor in: (1) Delivery/freight costs if applicable, (2) Your financing cost if buying on credit (30-day terms), (3) Handling and warehousing costs, (4) Breakage and damage allowance (typically 2-3%), (5) Display and marketing costs, and (6) Shrinkage and slow-moving stock risk.

**Q43:** How do your wholesale prices for laminate flooring compare to other laminate distributors?
**A:** our laminate flooring and our laminate flooring exclusive ranges are competitively priced against Kronotex, Finfloor, and leading adhesive manufacturersterio distribution. Because these are exclusive Tiletoria brands, you won't face direct price competition from other merchants carrying the same brand — a significant margin protection advantage.

**Q44:** Can I get a fixed price agreement for 12 months if I commit to a volume target?
**A:** For larger wholesale accounts with proven track records and committed annual volume targets (typically R2 million+), we can discuss fixed-price agreements on core ranges for 12 months. This provides certainty for your retail pricing and margin planning. Speak to your regional wholesale manager about qualifying.

**Q45:** What is the price differential between full container load (FCL) and less-than-container load (LCL) wholesale pricing?
**A:** Full container load purchases typically attract a 5-10% price advantage over LCL/break-bulk due to lower per-unit freight costs. However, FCL requires significant capital commitment and carries higher inventory risk. Most smaller merchants prefer LCL purchasing through our stock — you benefit from our container volumes without the capital tie-up.

**Q46:** How do I calculate my actual gross margin after accounting for returns, damages, and slow stock?
**A:** Start with your retail selling price minus wholesale cost. Then deduct: (1) Delivery costs to customer (if absorbed), (2) Returns and damages (typically 3-5% of sales), (3) Slow-moving stock markdowns (typically 5-10% of inventory value annually), (4) Display and showroom costs (2-3% of sales), and (5) Finance costs on stock holding. Your net margin should still target 18-28%.

**Q47:** Do you offer introductory pricing or first-order incentives for merchants switching from a competitor?
**A:** We occasionally run merchant acquisition promotions, particularly during pre-season show periods. These may include enhanced pricing on your first two orders, extended payment terms for the first month, or free marketing materials. Ask your account manager about any current new-merchant incentives.

**Q48:** How does the rebate system work — is it a retrospective discount or upfront?
**A:** Volume rebates are calculated retrospectively based on your quarterly or annual purchases and credited to your account. Credits can be offset against future purchases or, for accounts in good standing, applied as a payment reduction on your next statement. Rebate structures are agreed upfront so you know exactly what you're working towards.

**Q49:** What is the wholesale price difference between standard grade and first-grade tiles?
**A:** Standard grade (or commercial grade) tiles are typically 10-20% cheaper at wholesale than first-grade equivalent. However, first-grade tiles command higher retail prices and have lower defect rates, reducing returns and customer complaints. For retail showrooms, we recommend stocking first-grade. Standard grade suits contractor-focused yards where price is paramount.

**Q50:** Can I request a custom price list filtered only to the categories I stock?
**A:** Yes, your account manager can provide a customised price list showing only the product categories you actively trade in. This makes ordering and price checking more efficient. As your range expands, the price list can be updated. You can also access the full catalogue anytime through our online portal.

## Category 3: MOQ & Ordering Flexibility

**Q51:** What is the minimum order quantity (MOQ) for wholesale orders?
**A:** Standard wholesale MOQ is one full pallet per tile size/design, typically ranging from 40m² to 80m² depending on tile format. For laminate flooring, MOQ is typically 20-30 cartons. For sanware, MOQ varies by product — 5-10 units for fast-moving items, single units for high-value items like baths and vanities.

**Q52:** Can I order less than a full pallet if I'm testing a new product in my market?
**A:** Yes, we accommodate sample/test orders below standard MOQ for new products or new customers. Typically, you can order a quarter-pallet (10-20m²) or even a few boxes for product evaluation. These are priced at standard wholesale rates. Speak to your account manager to arrange — we want you to range the right products for your market.

**Q53:** Do you offer mixed pallet options so I can get variety without over-committing?
**A:** Yes, mixed pallets are available for selected product categories, particularly ceramic wall tiles and decorative mosaic sheets. A mixed pallet might contain 4-6 different designs in the same size format. This is ideal for building display boards or testing multiple colours without holding full pallets of each.

**Q54:** What is the smallest order value you'll deliver to outlying areas like Kimberley or East London?
**A:** For outlying deliveries, we typically require a minimum order value of R8,000-R12,000 to justify the freight cost, depending on distance from the nearest branch. For smaller orders, you can arrange your own transport collection ex-branch, or we can hold stock until your next order reaches the minimum.

**Q55:** Can I place a single order across multiple branches and have it consolidated?
**A:** Consolidated orders across branches can be arranged for larger accounts. Your primary account manager coordinates stock availability across branches and arranges consolidated delivery from the branch with the majority of stock, or split deliveries if required. A consolidation fee may apply depending on logistics complexity.

**Q56:** How quickly can I get stock for an emergency jobber order from a contractor?
**A:** For stock items at your nearest branch, same-day or next-day collection is available during branch hours. If you need delivery, next-day service applies within 60km of major branches. For outlying areas, 2-3 days is typical. Having an accurate stock availability check before promising your contractor is key — call your account manager or check online.

**Q57:** Can I place a backorder for out-of-stock items, or do I need to wait for them to arrive?
**A:** Yes, backorders are accepted and encouraged. When you place a backorder, the stock is allocated to you upon arrival at our warehouse. For fast-moving SKUs, we recommend placing backorders immediately to secure your allocation, particularly for imported lines where restock lead times are 6-12 weeks.

**Q58:** Is there a maximum order size, or can I order multiple containers?
**A:** There is no maximum order size. We regularly fulfil multi-pallet and multi-container orders for larger merchants. For very large orders (R500,000+), we recommend discussing with your account manager in advance to ensure stock availability, delivery scheduling, and any special pricing considerations.

**Q59:** Can I modify or cancel an order after it has been placed?
**A:** Orders can be modified or cancelled if they haven't yet been picked or dispatched from our warehouse. For import forward orders, cancellations are generally accepted up until the stock departs the factory. Once stock is in transit, cancellation may incur freight and handling costs. Contact your account manager immediately if you need to change an order.

**Q60:** Do you offer order consolidation — holding my smaller orders and shipping them together?
**A:** Yes, for accounts in outlying areas, we offer weekly or fortnightly consolidation. You place orders throughout the week, and we consolidate for delivery on your scheduled delivery day. This reduces per-order freight costs and ensures you always meet minimum order values. Your account manager will set up a delivery schedule.

**Q61:** What is a typical pallet quantity for 600x600mm porcelain floor tiles?
**A:** A standard pallet of 600x600mm porcelain tiles contains approximately 40m² (roughly 110-112 tiles), depending on thickness and packaging. This represents 4-5 cartons. A full truckload (FTL) carries approximately 24-28 pallets, or 960-1,120m² of this format.

**Q62:** Can I split a pallet with another merchant in my area if we both want the same product but can't justify a full pallet each?
**A:** We don't facilitate merchant-to-merchant pallet splitting directly, but if both merchants have Tiletoria wholesale accounts, each can order a half-pallet or quarter-pallet at standard wholesale pricing. Alternatively, one merchant can order the full pallet and arrange their own resale to the other party.

**Q63:** How do I place a forward order for stock that hasn't arrived in SA yet?
**A:** Forward orders are placed through your account manager using our import pipeline reports. These show products currently in production, in transit, or arriving at port. You place your order with an estimated delivery date, and stock is allocated to you upon arrival. A deposit may be required for large forward orders.

**Q64:** What deposit is required for forward orders on incoming container stock?
**A:** For forward orders under R50,000, no deposit is typically required for established accounts. For orders between R50,000 and R150,000, a 30% deposit secures your allocation. For orders over R150,000, a 50% deposit is standard. Deposits are offset against your final invoice when stock arrives.

**Q65:** Can I order samples of new products before committing to a pallet quantity?
**A:** Yes, sample tiles and sample boards are available for most products. Individual sample tiles are free for active wholesale accounts (up to 10 tiles per month). Full sample boards can be purchased at cost or provided free with a committed forward order. Contact your account manager to request samples.

**Q66:** Is there a cut-off time for placing orders for next-day delivery?
**A:** Orders for next-day delivery must be placed by 2:00 PM the previous business day for standard stock items. For large or complex orders (5+ pallets), 24 hours' notice is preferred. Orders placed after 2:00 PM are processed for delivery the following day. Friday afternoon orders are delivered on Monday.

**Q67:** Can I set up a standing order for fast-moving products that I reorder regularly?
**A:** Yes, standing orders are available for core range products that you sell consistently. Your account manager sets up a recurring order schedule (weekly, fortnightly, or monthly) with agreed quantities. You can modify quantities or skip deliveries with 48 hours' notice. Standing orders ensure you never run out of bestsellers.

**Q68:** What happens if the stock I ordered arrives damaged or short?
**A:** Report any shortages or damage within 24 hours of delivery. We verify against the picking slip and dispatch docket. Shortages are replaced or credited immediately. Damaged stock is collected and replaced at our cost if the damage occurred in transit. Damage must be noted on the delivery note at time of delivery.

**Q69:** Can I order products that are not in your standard stock range but that you can import for me?
**A:** Yes, we facilitate special imports for wholesale customers. You specify the product (size, design, finish, quantity), and we source from our factory network. Special imports require full-container quantities (FCL) or significant LCL volumes. Lead times are 8-16 weeks depending on source country. A deposit of 50-70% is required.

**Q70:** How flexible are you on MOQs for exclusive ranges like our exclusive mosaic range or our laminate flooring?
**A:** Our exclusive ranges have standard MOQs that align with factory minimums. For our exclusive mosaic range, minimums vary by product line — typically 20-50m² per design. For our laminate flooring, full-carton orders apply (typically 2.5-3m² per carton). Your account manager can advise on specific MOQs and may accommodate flexibility for range launches.

**Q71:** Can I place an order now and delay delivery until my showroom expansion is complete?
**A:** Yes, we can hold stock for up to 30 days at our warehouse at no charge for established accounts. For longer holding periods, a nominal storage fee may apply. Your account manager will confirm stock has been allocated to you and will coordinate delivery when you're ready. Advance payment may secure longer holding.

**Q72:** What is the process for ordering custom-made or bespoke mosaic patterns?
**A:** Custom mosaic designs are available through our exclusive mosaic range division. Submit your design or concept to your account manager, who liaises with the our exclusive mosaic range team. Custom orders require minimum quantities (typically 50-100m² depending on complexity) and carry a 10-20% premium over standard designs. Lead time is 10-14 weeks.

**Q73:** Can I get a pro forma invoice before placing an order to confirm the total cost?
**A:** Yes, pro forma invoices are available on request. Submit your product list and quantities to your account manager, and a detailed pro forma including product costs, VAT, and delivery charges will be issued within 24 hours. Pro forma invoices are valid for 7 days, after which prices may be subject to change.

**Q74:** Do you offer a 'call-off' system where I pay for stock upfront but take delivery in batches?
**A:** Yes, call-off arrangements are available for larger accounts. You commit to and pay for a quantity upfront (at a negotiated price), then call off stock as needed over an agreed period — typically 30-90 days. This secures your stock and pricing while managing your cash flow and warehouse space.

**Q75:** If I order a product and it doesn't sell in my market, can I exchange it for something else?
**A:** Standard exchanges are subject to our returns policy — stock must be in original packaging, undamaged, and returned within 30 days. A restocking fee of 10-15% may apply. For slow-moving stock exchanges outside this window, discuss with your account manager. We may offer exchange options on a case-by-case basis, particularly if you're replacing with a higher-velocity product.

## Category 4: Stock Range & Selection

**Q76:** What is the core wholesale range that most independent merchants should stock?
**A:** Our recommended core range for independents includes: (1) 300x600mm and 400x800mm ceramic wall tiles in white, beige, grey (top sellers), (2) 600x600mm and 600x1200mm porcelain floor tiles in neutral tones, (3) A wood-look porcelain option, (4) A-look porcelain option, (5) 200x100mm subway tiles, and (6) Mosaic accent strips. This gives you 40-60 SKUs covering 80% of typical consumer demand.

**Q77:** What are your current bestselling tile SKUs that I should definitely have in my range?
**A:** Consistent bestsellers include: large-format grey porcelain floor tiles (600x600 and 600x1200), white and grey-look porcelain, wood-look porcelain planks (200x1200mm), neutral ceramic wall tiles (300x600mm and 400x800mm), and classic white subway tiles. Your account manager can provide a current bestseller report with exact SKU references and monthly sales velocity.

**Q78:** Should I focus on imported European tiles or locally manufactured tiles for my market?
**A:** A balanced mix works best. Local SA tiles (Johnson Tiles, Ceramic Industries) offer better margins on volume sales and faster restocking. European imports from Spain and Italy command higher retail prices and attract premium customers. We recommend 60-70% local for volume and cash flow, 30-40% imports for margin and showroom differentiation.

**Q79:** What exclusive ranges does Tiletoria offer that I can't buy from other wholesalers?
**A:** Tiletoria's exclusive wholesale ranges include: our exclusive mosaic range (decorative mosaic tiles and feature strips), our laminate flooring laminate flooring, our laminate flooring LVT/vinyl flooring, and selected imported tile lines with territorial exclusivity arrangements. These ranges protect you from direct price competition with other merchants carrying the same brands.

**Q80:** How often do you add new products to your wholesale range?
**A:** New products are introduced quarterly, aligning with seasonal buying cycles. Major range launches coincide with pre-season shows in February-March and August-September. We typically add 15-30 new SKUs per quarter across tiles, flooring, and sanware. Your account manager will brief you on upcoming launches and provide samples.

**Q81:** What large-format tile sizes do you stock, and are they popular in secondary markets?
**A:** We stock large-format porcelain from 600x1200mm up to 1200x1200mm and 1200x2600mm slabs. While 600x1200mm has strong demand across all markets, the larger formats (1200x1200mm+) sell primarily in affluent urban and coastal markets like leading adhesive manufacturerslito, George, and Stellenbosch. For smaller towns, focus on 600x600mm and 600x1200mm.

**Q82:** Do you stock outdoor/patio tiles suitable for the South African climate?
**A:** Yes, our outdoor range includes 20mm thick porcelain pavers (600x600mm, 600x1200mm) with R11-R13 slip ratings, suitable for direct laying on grass, gravel, or pedestals. We also stock anti-slip textured porcelain floor tiles (R10-R11) for patio and pool surround applications. These are strong sellers in coastal and braai-focused markets.

**Q84:** Do you stock rectified tiles, and what is the price difference vs non-rectified?
**A:** Yes, we stock extensive rectified tile ranges, particularly in porcelain floor tiles 600x600mm and above. Rectified tiles are typically 10-20% more expensive at wholesale than equivalent non-rectified, but they command higher retail prices due to the premium look of minimal grout joints. Rectified is recommended for premium showrooms.

## Category 5: Lead Times & Import Planning

**Q101:** What are the current lead times for tiles imported from Spain and Italy?
**A:** Standard factory lead times from Spain and Italy are 6-8 weeks from order confirmation to ex-factory. Add 2-3 weeks for sea freight to Durban or Cape Town, plus 1 week for customs clearance and inland transport to our warehouses. Total pipeline: approximately 10-12 weeks from factory order to our warehouse shelf.

**Q102:** How long do Brazilian porcelain tiles take to arrive?
**A:** Brazilian tiles have shorter lead times than European imports — typically 4-6 weeks factory lead time plus 2-3 weeks sea freight to Durban. Total pipeline is approximately 7-9 weeks. Brazilian products are popular for their competitive pricing and reasonable quality, making them a good mid-range option.

**Q103:** What about tiles from Turkey, India, and China — what are those lead times?
**A:** Turkish tiles: 6-8 weeks factory + 3-4 weeks sea freight = 10-12 weeks total. Indian tiles: 4-6 weeks factory + 4-5 weeks sea freight = 9-11 weeks total. Chinese tiles: 4-6 weeks factory + 5-6 weeks sea freight = 10-12 weeks total. These sources offer price-competitive options, particularly for ceramic wall tiles and mosaics.

**Q104:** When should I place my pre-season orders to ensure stock arrives for the building season?
**A:** For the September-April building season (South African summer/autumn), place your pre-season orders by June-July to ensure stock arrives by September. **Q105:** How do I know what stock is currently in the import pipeline and when it will arrive?
**A:** Your account manager provides regular pipeline reports showing stock currently on order, in production, in transit, and at port. **Q106:** What happens if there are customs delays at Durban or Cape Town port?

If customs delays affect your forward order, your account manager will advise you of the revised arrival date and adjust delivery scheduling accordingly.

**Q107:** Can I place a forward order for stock that hasn't left the factory yet?

**Q108:** How accurate are your estimated arrival dates for incoming containers?

**Q256:** How do I advise a customer on whether to choose porcelain or ceramic tiles?

Porcelain is harder, more durable, and frost-resistant but costs more. Ask about the application, traffic level, and budget to guide the recommendation.

**Q257:** What is the difference between PEI 1, 3, and 5 ratings, and why does it matter?
**A:** PEI (Porcelain Enamel Institute) ratings indicate wear resistance: PEI 1 = wall use only, PEI 2 = light-traffic residential floors (bedrooms), PEI 3 = medium-traffic residential floors (living areas, kitchens), PEI 4 = heavy-traffic residential and light commercial, PEI 5 = heavy commercial traffic. Recommend PEI 3+ for general residential floors.

**Q258:** How do I explain rectified vs non-rectified tiles to a customer?
**A:** Rectified tiles are mechanically cut after firing to achieve precise, uniform dimensions with minimal size variation. This allows for very narrow grout joints (1.5-2mm) and a seamless, premium look. Non-rectified tiles have natural size variation and require wider grout joints (3-5mm). Rectified tiles cost more but deliver a superior aesthetic.

**Q259:** What slip resistance rating should I recommend for different applications?
**A:** R9 = dry interior floors (living areas, bedrooms). R10 = general residential wet areas (kitchens, bathrooms). R11 = outdoor patios, pool surrounds, commercial kitchens. R12-R13 = commercial wet areas, ramps, industrial applications. Always recommend R10 or higher for bathrooms and R11+ for outdoor areas in South Africa.

They must also withstand commercial cleaning equipment and chemicals. Always verify the PEI rating and breaking strength (measured in Newtons) for commercial applications.

## Category 24: Financial Management & Cash Flow

**Q566:** How should I manage my cash flow as an independent tile merchant?
**A:** Key principles: maintain 4-6 weeks of stock cover (not 12+), negotiate favourable payment terms with suppliers, collect from contractor customers promptly (don't let them stretch beyond 30 days), time your pre-season orders to align with cash availability, and maintain a cash reserve for 2-3 months of operating expenses.

**Q567:** What stock turn ratio should I aim for, and how do I calculate it?
**A:** Aim for 4-6 stock turns per year. Calculate: Cost of Goods Sold / Average Inventory Value. Higher is generally better (less cash tied up), but too high means you may be losing sales due to stock-outs. Track monthly and by category — some categories (fast ceramics) should turn 8-12x, while premium imports may turn 2-3x.

**Q568:** How much should I reinvest in stock vs taking profit out of the business?
**A:** A healthy balance: reinvest 60-70% of net profit into the business (stock range expansion, showroom improvements, marketing), and draw 30-40% as owner's income. In growth phases, reinvest more. Many failing merchants draw too much too early — build the business first, then enjoy the rewards of a well-established operation.

**Q569:** Should I use an overdraft or revolving credit facility to fund my stock purchases?
**A:** A revolving credit facility or overdraft is useful for managing cash flow timing — particularly around pre-season ordering when large payments coincide with slower collection periods. However, avoid using credit facilities to fund fundamentally unprofitable operations. The cost of credit (prime + 2-4%) must be factored into your pricing.

**Q570:** How do I know if I'm pricing my products correctly?
**A:** Regularly review: your gross margin by category (should be 25-45%), competitor pricing on key products, your mark-up consistency, and the impact of discounting on overall profitability. If you're consistently winning every quote, you're probably too cheap. If you're losing everything, you may be too expensive or not selling value effectively.

**Q571:** What financial records should I keep for effective business management?
**A:** Essential records: monthly P&L statement, balance sheet, stock valuation, aged debtor analysis, aged creditor analysis, cash flow forecast, and category sales analysis. Review these monthly. Use accounting software (Sage, Xero, Pastel) to automate reporting. Your accountant can set up a management reporting pack.

**Q572:** How do seasonal fluctuations affect cash flow in the tile business?
**A:** The tile business is seasonal: strong sales September-April (building season), slower May-July, and variable December-January. Cash flow peaks follow sales by 30-60 days (depending on your payment terms). Plan for: stronger stock investment in August-September, reduced ordering in April-May, and conservative cash management in slow months.

**Q573:** Should I offer credit to my retail customers, or cash only?
**A:** Most independents offer cash terms to retail customers (payment on order or before delivery). Credit to retail customers should be very limited — only for established relationships or large projects with written contracts. Your credit risk is with contractor trade accounts, not retail consumers. Retail lay-bys are safer than credit.

**Q574:** How do I manage the cash flow impact of placing large pre-season orders with Tiletoria?
**A:** Strategies: spread orders across the buying window rather than one large order, use our forward order system with staged payments, time your order to coincide with strong cash collection periods, negotiate extended terms for pre-season orders (we often offer 45-60 days for large seasonal commitments), and consider trade finance for major stock builds.

**Q575:** What key performance indicators (KPIs) should I track monthly as a merchant?
**A:** Track: sales revenue and gross profit by category, stock turn ratio, average transaction value, number of transactions, debtor days (how quickly contractors pay), creditor days (how quickly you pay suppliers), gross margin %, net profit %, and customer mix (retail vs contractor split). Review trends monthly and benchmark against industry norms.

**Q576:** How do I know when I'm overtrading — growing sales but running out of cash?
**A:** Overtrading signs: sales growing but bank balance shrinking, increasing reliance on overdraft, taking longer to pay suppliers while debtor days increase, stock levels growing faster than sales, and frequent stock-outs on fast-movers while slow-movers pile up. If you recognise these signs, slow down ordering and focus on collections.

**Q577:** What is the right level of debtors (money owed by customers) for a merchant my size?
**A:** Debtors should not exceed 15-20% of monthly turnover if you offer 30-day terms. If debtors exceed one month's turnover, you have a collection problem. Aim for average debtor days of 35-45 days on 30-day terms. Over 60 days indicates serious collection issues. Be prepared to put slow-paying accounts on hold.

**Q578:** How should I structure my business — sole proprietor, CC, or company?
**A:** For turnover above R5 million, a private company (Pty) Ltd is recommended for limited liability protection and professional image. CCs are acceptable for smaller operations but note that new CCs can no longer be registered under South African law. Consult your accountant and attorney for advice specific to your situation.

**Q579:** Do I need business insurance, and what type?
**A:** Essential insurance includes: commercial property insurance (building and stock), public liability insurance (customers in your store), business interruption insurance, vehicle insurance (for delivery vehicles), and fidelity/guarantee insurance (if you have employees handling cash). Professional indemnity insurance if you provide design advice.

**Q580:** How do I prepare for my financial year-end as a wholesale customer?
**A:** At year-end: ensure all stock is counted and valued, reconcile your Tiletoria account (and all supplier accounts), review aged debtors and chase outstanding payments, claim all volume rebates due, update asset registers, and schedule a meeting with your accountant. A clean year-end makes for accurate financial statements and tax returns.

**Q581:** What tax considerations apply to the building materials retail business?
**A:** Key considerations: VAT registration (required above R1 million turnover), provisional tax payments (twice yearly), PAYE and UIF for staff, dividends tax if you draw dividends, and capital gains tax on asset sales. Maintain accurate records and use a qualified accountant. SARS scrutiny of building materials businesses has increased — compliance is essential.

**Q582:** How can I improve my stock management to free up cash?
**A:** Implement: regular stock counts (quarterly full counts, monthly cycle counts), ABC analysis (focus management attention on high-value items), set reorder points based on sales velocity, identify and clear slow-movers promptly, negotiate sale-or-return on new ranges, and use our inventory planning templates. Every rand freed from stock is a rand in your bank account.

**Q583:** Should I take on debt to fund showroom expansion or new branch openings?
**A:** Expansion debt is appropriate if: the market opportunity is proven, you have a detailed business plan with financial projections, the debt service (repayments) is covered 1.5x by projected cash flows, you maintain a cash reserve after expansion, and you have a fallback plan if projections aren't met. Don't over-leverage — the building industry is cyclical.

**Q584:** How do I value my business if I want to sell or bring in a partner?
**A:** Business valuation methods for retailers: (1) Asset-based (stock + assets - liabilities), (2) Earnings multiple (annual profit x 3-5 for established merchants), (3) Turnover multiple (annual sales x 0.3-0.8 depending on profitability). A well-run merchant with strong customer relationships and consistent profits commands a premium valuation.

**Q585:** What financial red flags should I watch for that indicate my business is in trouble?
**A:** Red flags: declining gross margins, increasing debtor days, frequent stock-outs on bestsellers while overstocked on slow-movers, inability to pay suppliers within terms, declining average transaction value, losing key contractor customers, and negative cash flow for 2+ consecutive months. Address these early — denial is the enemy of business survival.

**Q596:** What software systems do I need to run an efficient tile retail business?

Consider: a room visualiser tool for customer engagement, a website with product gallery, and social media management tools.

**Q597:** How do I manage my time effectively as an owner-operator of a tile business?
**A:** Prioritise: customer-facing activities (sales, contractor relationships), stock management, financial oversight, and strategic planning. Delegate routine tasks where possible. Set aside dedicated time for admin (e.g., Friday afternoons). Visit sites with contractors periodically — it builds relationships and market knowledge. Don't get trapped behind a desk.

**Q598:** What legal compliance requirements apply to my tile retail business?
**A:** Compliance requirements: company registration and annual returns (CIPC), SARS tax compliance (income tax, VAT, PAYE), BEE documentation (if supplying corporate/government), consumer protection compliance (CPA — returns policies, warranty disclosure), occupational health and safety compliance, and municipal business licensing and zoning compliance.

**Q599:** How do I protect my business against fraud and bad debt?
**A:** Fraud prevention: verify new contractor references thoroughly, set conservative initial credit limits, check ID documents for large cash transactions, implement dual authorisation for large refunds, reconcile stock and cash daily, and watch for suspicious patterns (frequent returns, altered receipts). **Q600:** What succession planning should I consider for my tile business?
**A:** Even if you're not planning to exit soon, prepare: train a key person who can run the business in your absence, document your systems and procedures, build a strong management team, maintain clean financial records, and understand your business valuation. A business that can run without you is more valuable and less stressful.

## Category 27: Stock Management & Warehousing

**Q626:** How should I organise my warehouse for efficient tile stock management?
**A:** Organise by: product category (wall tiles, floor tiles, mosaics, flooring), then by size within category, then by colour within size. Keep fast-moving products near the dispatch area. Store heavy pallets on lower racks. Label all locations clearly. Maintain clear aisles for forklift access. Separate damaged/returned stock from saleable inventory.

**Q627:** What is FIFO, and why is it important for tile stock management?
**A:** FIFO (First In, First Out) means selling the oldest stock first. It's critical for tiles because: older stock may be from a different batch than newer arrivals, and you want to avoid holding stock so long it becomes dated or discontinued. When receiving new stock of an existing product, rotate the pallets so older stock is accessible first.

**Q628:** How often should I count my stock, and what method should I use?
**A:** Conduct a full stock count quarterly. Perform cycle counts monthly (counting a portion of your range each month so everything is counted at least once per quarter). Investigate and resolve all variances immediately. Accurate stock records prevent lost sales (thinking you're out of stock when you're not) and identify shrinkage issues early.

**Q629:** What is the best way to store mosaic sheets and small-format tiles?
**A:** Store mosaic boxes flat, never on edge (sheets can crack). Keep them in original packaging until sale. Store in a dry area — moisture can damage the paper or mesh backing. Keep different mosaic types clearly labelled and separated. Handle gently during picking — mosaic sheets are more fragile than standard tiles.

**Q630:** How do I manage damaged stock that can't be sold as first quality?
**A:** Segregate damaged stock immediately in a designated "damaged" area. Assess: can it be sold as "standard/commercial" grade at a discount? Can individual good pieces be used as samples? Can it be sold as job lots to budget contractors? As a last resort, dispose of it. Document all damage and claim against your insurance or supplier if applicable.

**Q631:** Should I invest in a forklift, or can I manage with manual pallet jacks?

## Category 28: Installation & Technical Support

**Q641:** What is the correct adhesive for porcelain floor tiles vs ceramic wall tiles?
**A:** Porcelain floor tiles require a high-quality polymer-modified cementitious adhesive (flexible, C2 classification) due to their low porosity and weight. Ceramic wall tiles can use a standard cementitious adhesive (C1 classification). Large-format tiles (600x600mm+) require adhesive with extended open time and non-slip properties. Always follow the adhesive manufacturer's recommendations.

**Q642:** How do I advise customers on expansion joints, and why are they important?
**A:** Expansion joints accommodate movement in the building structure and prevent tile cracking. General rules: perimeter joints (10mm gap around room edges), structural joints (over existing building expansion joints), and intermediate joints every 8-10 metres in large areas. Fill with flexible silicone sealant, not grout. For outdoor areas, increase to every 6-8 metres.

**Q643:** What is the difference between cement grout and epoxy-based grout, and when should each be used?
**A:** cement-based grout is affordable, easy to apply, and suitable for most residential applications. It requires sealing and can stain. Epoxy grout is waterproof, stain-resistant, chemical-resistant, and ideal for bathrooms, kitchens, and commercial areas. However, epoxy is harder to apply, more expensive, and has a slight sheen. Recommend epoxy for wet areas and white/light grout installations.

**Q644:** How do I know if a substrate is suitable for tiling?
**A:** A suitable substrate must be: structurally sound (no movement or flex), clean (free of dust, grease, and loose material), dry (moisture content within adhesive manufacturer's limits), level (within 3mm over 2 metres using a straight edge), and stable (cured concrete, seasoned screed). Substandard substrates cause the majority of tiling failures.

**Q645:** What is "lippage" and how can it be minimised?
**A:** Lippage is the height difference between adjacent tiles, creating an uneven surface. It's minimised by: proper substrate preparation (must be flat), using a levelling system (clips and wedges) especially for large-format tiles, selecting skilled tilers, choosing tiles with tight calibration (rectified), and using the correct adhesive bed thickness. ASTM standards allow maximum 1mm lippage for glazed tiles.

**Q646:** Can tiles be installed over existing tiles, or must they be removed first?
**A:** Tiles can be installed over existing tiles if: the existing tiles are firmly bonded (not hollow-sounding), the surface is clean and free of contaminants, and the additional height won't cause problems with doors, skirtings, and fittings. Use a suitable tile-on-tile adhesive and ensure the existing surface is roughened for adhesion. When in doubt, remove first.

The waterproofing membrane (liquid or sheet) must be applied to walls and floors before tiling, with particular attention to corners, joints, and penetrations (pipes). A 48-hour flood test should be conducted before tiling. Recommend qualified waterproofing contractors.

**Q655:** How do I advise customers who want to tile over underfloor heating screeds?
**A:** Key advice: the screed must be fully cured (minimum 28 days), moisture content must be within adhesive manufacturer's limits, use flexible adhesive and grout rated for underfloor heating, allow the heating system to run for 2 weeks before tiling to drive out residual moisture, and leave perimeter expansion joints. **Q656:** What is the maximum tile size that can be used on walls, and what preparation is needed?
**A:** Large-format tiles (600x1200mm, 1200x1200mm) can be used on walls but require: a flat, stable substrate, a high-quality non-slip adhesive with extended open time, back-buttering of tiles (apply adhesive to both wall and tile), a suitable levelling system, and potentially mechanical support during curing. Experienced tilers are essential for large wall formats.

**Q657:** How do I advise on tile slip ratings for elderly customers or wet areas?
**A:** For elderly customers or safety-critical wet areas, recommend: R10 minimum for bathroom floors, R11 for shower floors and pool surrounds, and R12 for commercial wet areas. **Q723:** Are porcelain tiles more environmentally friendly than ceramic tiles?
**A:** Porcelain tiles are denser and more durable (longer lifespan = less replacement), but their production requires higher firing temperatures (more energy). Ceramic tiles use less energy to produce but may need replacement sooner. Overall, both are environmentally favourable compared to many alternatives — they're inert, recyclable, and made from natural materials (clay, sand, feldspar).

**Q724:** Can I market my business as "green" or "eco-friendly" if I stock some sustainable products?
**A:** Be careful with green marketing claims — they must be substantiated to avoid greenwashing accusations. You can highlight specific eco-friendly products in your range, promote recycling of tile offcuts where possible, and implement sustainable practices in your own operations (recycling packaging, energy-efficient lighting). **Q725:** Does Tiletoria have any sustainability initiatives I should know about?
**A:** Tiletoria is committed to responsible sourcing and environmental stewardship. We work with factories that meet international environmental standards, optimise container utilisation to reduce transport emissions, and continuously improve our warehouse energy efficiency. We're exploring carbon offset programmes for our import logistics. Updates on sustainability initiatives are shared with wholesale customers.

**Q726:** Are laminate and LVT flooring products environmentally friendly?
**A:** Modern laminate and LVT products from reputable manufacturers use sustainably sourced wood fibres (for laminate core), low-VOC emissions adhesives, and recycled content where possible. our laminate flooring and our laminate flooring products meet European E1 emission standards for formaldehyde. LVT is fully recyclable at end of life through specialised recycling programmes.

**Q727:** How can I reduce waste in my own business operations?
**A:** Waste reduction strategies: recycle cardboard packaging and wooden pallets, donate damaged but usable tiles to Habitat for Humanity or community projects, optimise delivery routes to reduce fuel consumption, use energy-efficient LED lighting in your showroom, implement paperless invoicing where possible, and encourage customers to take only the samples they need.

**Q728:** Do customers in South Africa care about sustainability when buying tiles?
**A:** Sustainability awareness is growing, particularly among younger, urban, and affluent consumers. However, price, aesthetics, and durability remain the primary purchase drivers for most South African consumers. Stock a few sustainable options for the environmentally conscious segment, but don't make sustainability the central pillar of your range unless your market specifically demands it.

**Q729:** What is the lifecycle environmental impact of tiles compared to other flooring materials?
**A:** Tiles (ceramic and porcelain) compare favourably: they're made from abundant natural materials, have very long lifespans (20-50+ years), are inert and don't emit VOCs, are easy to clean without chemicals, and can be recycled as aggregate at end of life. Compared to vinyl sheet flooring or carpet, tiles generally have lower long-term environmental impact.

**Q730:** Can old tiles be recycled, and do you have any recycling programmes?
**A:** Old tiles can be crushed and recycled as aggregate for concrete, road base, or drainage material. While widespread consumer tile recycling isn't yet established in South Africa, the construction industry is increasingly recycling demolition waste. We can provide information on waste management companies that accept ceramic and porcelain tile waste for recycling.

## Category 37: Product Warranties & Guarantees

**Q826:** What warranties does Tiletoria offer on its tile products?

**Q827:** What is covered under the tile warranty, and what is excluded?
**A:** Warranties cover manufacturing defects: cracks, chips, glazing defects, colour variation beyond declared tolerances, and premature wear under normal use. Exclusions include: damage from improper installation, substrate movement, misuse, neglect, damage from harsh chemicals, normal wear and tear, and damage from impact or accidents. Installation must be by a qualified tiler for warranty to be valid.

**Q828:** How do I process a warranty claim for my customer?
**A:** Warranty claim process: (1) Document the defect with clear photos, (2) Record batch numbers from the packaging, (3) Note the date and place of purchase, (4) Submit the claim to your account manager with supporting documentation, (5) We'll assess and either approve replacement, offer a credit, or arrange an inspection. Claims are typically resolved within 7-14 days.

**Q829:** What is the warranty on our premium tapware range products?
**A:** our premium tapware range offers a 5-year warranty on taps and showers against manufacturing defects in materials and workmanship. Ceramics (toilets, basins) carry a 10-year warranty. The warranty covers cartridges, finishes, and mechanical components under normal domestic use. Commercial warranties are typically 1 year. Warranty cards are included with each product.

**Q830:** How does the warranty on laminate flooring work?
**A:** our laminate flooring warranties: AC3 grade = 15 years residential, AC4 = 20 years residential, AC5 = 25 years residential/commercial. The warranty covers: wear-through of the decor layer, staining, fading from normal light exposure, and structural integrity under normal use. Warranty does not cover: water damage from flooding, scratches from moving furniture without protection, or installation errors.

**Q831:** What documentation should I give my customers regarding warranties?
**A:** Provide: the manufacturer's warranty card or document, a copy of the purchase invoice (proof of purchase is required for claims), care and maintenance instructions, and your business contact details for warranty queries. For laminate and LVT, ensure the warranty is registered with the manufacturer if required. Keep a copy of all warranty documentation on file.

**Q832:** Can a warranty be voided, and what are the common reasons?
**A:** Warranties can be voided by: improper installation (not by a qualified professional), use of incorrect adhesives or grouts, failure to follow maintenance instructions, misuse or abuse, modifications to the product, installation in unsuitable environments (e.g., outdoor use of indoor-only products), and failure to report defects within the warranty period. Educate customers on proper care.

**Q833:** What happens if a product is discontinued and my customer needs a warranty replacement?
**A:** If the exact product is discontinued, we'll provide the closest available equivalent at the same or better specification. If the replacement is a higher-grade product, we typically honour it at no additional cost. We maintain records of discontinued products and their closest replacements. Your account manager handles discontinued product substitutions.

**Q834:** Do warranties transfer if the property is sold?
**A:** Most manufacturer warranties are tied to the original purchaser and proof of purchase. However, some warranties (particularly laminate flooring) transfer to subsequent owners if the warranty card was properly registered. Advise customers to keep all warranty documentation and receipts for potential future claims, even if they plan to sell.

**Q835:** How do I handle a warranty claim where the defect is clearly due to installation error?
**A:** If investigation shows the issue is installation-related (not a manufacturing defect), explain this to your customer with evidence and reference to installation standards (SANS 10107). Be diplomatic — the customer may not want to hear it. Offer to facilitate contact with a qualified tiler for remedial work. Sometimes a goodwill gesture (discounted replacement tiles) preserves the relationship even when not strictly liable.

**Q877:** What is the difference between first-grade and commercial-grade tiles?
**A:** First-grade tiles meet the manufacturer's full quality standards with minimal visual defects. Commercial-grade (or standard-grade) tiles may have slight colour variations, minor glaze imperfections, or size tolerances that don't affect performance but are visible on close inspection. Commercial-grade is 10-20% cheaper and suitable for budget projects, rental properties, and areas where minor imperfections are acceptable.

**Q878:** How do I read and understand tile box markings and labels?
**A:** Tile box markings typically include: product name and code, size (e.g., 600x600mm), thickness, quantity per box, area per box (m²), country of origin, batch number (critical for colour consistency), shade number, calibre (size grouping), PEI rating (for floor tiles), slip rating (R9-R13), and date of manufacture. Train staff to check batch numbers when selling multiple boxes for the same job.

**Q879:** What is "calibre" and why does it matter?
**A:** Calibre refers to the actual size grouping of tiles. Due to firing shrinkage variations, "600x600mm" tiles may actually measure 598x598mm or 602x602mm depending on the batch. Tiles of the same calibre number have consistent sizing and should be used together. Mixing different calibres results in uneven grout lines and lippage. Always check calibre numbers when using tiles from different batches.

**Q880:** Do you stock tile spacers, and what sizes are available?
**A:** We stock plastic tile spacers in standard sizes: 1mm, 1.5mm, 2mm, 3mm, 4mm, 5mm, and 10mm. Cross spacers and T-spacers are available. We also stock levelling systems (clips and wedges) for large-format tiles. Spacers are profitable add-on sales — ensure your staff recommend the correct size for the tile being purchased.

**Q881:** What is "rectified" and why do rectified tiles cost more?
**A:** Rectified tiles are mechanically trimmed after firing to achieve precise, uniform dimensions. This allows for very narrow grout joints (1.5-2mm) and a premium, seamless appearance. The additional manufacturing process (precision cutting and grading) adds cost. Rectified tiles are worth the premium for customers who want a high-end, contemporary look.

**Q882:** How do I calculate how many tiles my customer needs for their project?
**A:** Calculation method: measure the wall/floor area (length x width in metres), add 10-15% for cutting waste (15-20% for diagonal patterns), divide the total area by the coverage per box. Round up to the nearest full box. Also account for any feature strips, borders, or design elements. Our staff can verify your calculations if needed.

**Q883:** What is "box coverage" and why is it important?
**A:** Box coverage is the area (in m²) that one box of tiles will cover. It's calculated from the tile dimensions and quantity per box. Always sell in full boxes — don't split boxes unless absolutely necessary. Customers should be advised to keep any leftover tiles for future repairs. Knowing exact box coverage ensures accurate ordering.

**Q884:** Do you stock tile levelling systems, and which do you recommend?
**A:** Yes, we stock tile levelling systems (clips and wedges) which are essential for large-format tile installation (600x600mm and above). Levelling systems prevent lippage by holding tiles at a consistent height while the adhesive cures. We stock several brands and can recommend the most suitable system based on your customers' preferences and tile types.

**Q885:** What is "lappato" finish, and when should it be used?
**A:** Lappato is a semi-polished finish — between matte and fully polished. It offers a subtle sheen that adds depth and luxury without the high reflectivity of full polish. Lappato is popular for floor tiles in living areas and bedrooms where customers want some shine but are concerned about slipperiness or maintenance of fully polished tiles.

**Q886:** Do you stock flexible adhesive and grout for plywood or timber substrates?
**A:** Yes, flexible (polymer-modified) adhesives and grouts are available for tiling over timber or plywood substrates, which expand and contract with humidity changes. Standard adhesives will crack on timber substrates. Always confirm the substrate type with your customer before recommending adhesive — this is a common cause of installation failure.

**Q887:** What is "double-charging" or "double-loading" in porcelain tiles?
**A:** Double-loaded porcelain has a second layer of coloured material pressed into the tile body before firing. This creates a deeper, more durable surface pattern that wears better than single-layer tiles. Double-loaded tiles are recommended for high-traffic commercial areas. The term refers to the manufacturing process, not the price.

**Q888:** How do I tell if a tile is porcelain or ceramic?
**A:** Key differences: porcelain is denser, heavier, and has lower water absorption (<0.5%) than ceramic. On the back of the tile, porcelain has a finer, more uniform body colour that matches the surface (especially through-body porcelain). Ceramic has a coarser, usually reddish or white body. The box label will clearly state whether the tile is porcelain or ceramic.

**Q889:** What does "full-body" or "through-body" porcelain mean?
**A:** Full-body (through-body) porcelain means the colour and pattern run through the entire thickness of the tile, not just the surface layer. If the surface chips, the damage is less visible because the body matches the surface. This contrasts with glazed porcelain where the body is a different colour underneath the glaze. Full-body is preferred for high-traffic commercial floors.

**Q890:** Do you stock anti-fatigue or cushioned floor tiles for commercial kitchens?
**A:** For commercial kitchens requiring anti-fatigue properties, we recommend: rubber flooring systems (through partners), specialised commercial kitchen vinyl, and certain textured porcelain tiles with ergonomic properties. Pure anti-fatigue matting is typically not a tile product — we can advise on complementary flooring systems for kitchen environments.

**Q891:** What is "inkjet printing" in tile manufacturing?
**A:** Inkjet printing is a digital decoration technology that applies high-resolution images to tile surfaces. It allows manufacturers to create highly realistic reproductions of natural materials (wood, stone) with variation between tiles (like natural materials). Most modern decorative tiles use inkjet technology. It's cost-effective and enables rapid design changes.

**Q892:** Do you stock tiles with nano-technology or self-cleaning surfaces?
**A:** Some of our premium ranges feature nano-technology surface treatments that make tiles easier to clean and more resistant to staining. These treatments create a hydrophobic (water-repelling) surface that prevents dirt and grime from adhering. Ask your account manager about current nano-treated products in our range — availability varies by season.

**Q893:** What is "PEI rating" and what do the numbers mean?
**A:** PEI (Porcelain Enamel Institute) rating measures a glazed tile's resistance to abrasion: PEI 0 = wall use only, PEI 1 = very light traffic (bathrooms, no footwear), PEI 2 = light traffic (bedrooms, bathrooms), PEI 3 = medium traffic (residential floors, normal footwear), PEI 4 = heavy traffic (commercial entrances, restaurants), PEI 5 = very heavy traffic (public buildings, shopping centres).

**Q894:** Do you stock tiles suitable for direct laying on sand or grass (pedestrian only)?
**A:** Yes, 20mm thick outdoor porcelain pavers can be laid directly on grass, gravel, sand, or rubber pads without adhesive or cement screed. This is ideal for temporary installations, garden paths, or areas where a permanent installation isn't desired. We also supply adjustable pedestal systems for raised outdoor flooring over concrete.

**Q895:** What is the difference between "polished" and "glazed" porcelain?
**A:** Polished porcelain has a glossy surface achieved by mechanical polishing of the tile body (like polishing). The polish goes through the full body. Glazed porcelain has a glossy glass-like coating applied to the surface. Polished porcelain is generally harder and more durable but can be more slippery. Glazed porcelain offers more design variety.

**Q897:** What is "gauged" porcelain, and what are its applications?
**A:** Gauged porcelain refers to large-format porcelain slabs (typically 1200x2600mm or larger, 3-6mm thick) that are manufactured to precise thickness tolerances. They're used for: wall cladding, countertop surfaces, furniture facings, and flooring. Gauged porcelain requires specialised cutting equipment and installation techniques. **Q898:** Do you stock tiles specifically for lift interiors (elevator cabs)?
**A:** Yes, lift interior tiles include: thin, lightweight porcelain panels (to minimise weight), large-format tiles for minimal joints, mirror-finish tiles for spaciousness, and decorative feature panels. **A:** Tile trims are profiles that protect and finish exposed tile edges. The goal is to recover cash, not maximise margin.
