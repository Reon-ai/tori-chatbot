# Large Format Tile Guide

## Purpose

Use this file for large-format tiles such as 600 × 1200, 700 × 1400, 800 × 1600, 1200 × 1200 and larger.

## Simple Definition

Large-format tiles are bigger tiles that create fewer grout lines and a premium, modern look.

They require more careful planning and installation.

## Benefits

- Modern appearance.
- Fewer grout lines.
- Larger visual flow.
- Premium feel.
- Good for open-plan spaces.
- Can make areas feel more spacious.

## Risks

- Requires flatter substrate.
- Heavier to handle.
- More difficult cuts.
- Higher adhesive coverage requirements.
- More lippage risk.
- More breakage risk during handling.
- Needs skilled installer.
- May need levelling clips.
- Delivery and access matter.
- Waste can be higher.

## First Question

> What size tile are you using, and is it going on a floor, wall, bathroom, shower, patio or another area?

## Substrate Flatness

Large-format tile needs a flatter surface than small tiles.

Safe answer:

> Large-format tiles need a very flat substrate. A levelling clip system can help control tile-to-tile lippage, but it cannot fix a floor that is not flat.

## Adhesive Coverage

Large-format tiles often need:

- Correct trowel notch.
- Back-buttering where required.
- Full support under the tile.
- Suitable adhesive.
- Skilled trowel direction.
- Avoiding voids.

Safe answer:

> Large-format tile installation depends heavily on adhesive coverage. Voids under tiles can lead to hollow sounds, cracks or failure.

## Handling and Access

Check:

- Can the boxes fit through doors?
- Can tiles fit in lifts?
- Are stairs narrow?
- Is there enough manpower?
- Is the site clear?
- Are cutting tools suitable?
- Is the delivery route safe?

Safe answer:

> Before buying very large tiles, confirm site access, handling and cutting. Large tiles are not only a product choice; they are a logistics choice.

## Large Format on Walls

Check:

- Wall substrate.
- Weight.
- Adhesive.
- Mechanical support where required.
- Bathroom/wet area details.
- Tile layout.
- Cuts around openings.
- Installer skill.

Safe answer:

> Large-format wall tiles need the correct substrate and adhesive. The installer must confirm that the wall can carry the tile and that the fixing method is suitable.

## Large Format in Showers

Escalate if used on shower floors.

For shower walls:

- Waterproofing still required.
- Tile size affects cuts and layout.
- Fewer grout lines does not remove waterproofing needs.
- Corners and penetrations matter.

Safe answer:

> Large-format shower walls can look excellent, but waterproofing, substrate, adhesive and penetrations still matter. Fewer grout lines do not make the shower waterproof.

## Offset / Brick Pattern Caution

Rectangular large-format tiles may have warpage or bowing. Large offsets can increase lippage risk.

Safe answer:

> For rectangular large-format tiles, the installer should confirm the recommended offset and joint width. Large brick-bond offsets can increase lippage risk.

## Recommended Customer Checklist

Ask customer for:

- Tile size.
- Area m².
- Floor or wall.
- Indoor/outdoor/wet area.
- Substrate type.
- Is the floor flat?
- Is there lift/stair access?
- Is the installer experienced with large format?
- Is a levelling clip system planned?
- What grout joint is planned?
