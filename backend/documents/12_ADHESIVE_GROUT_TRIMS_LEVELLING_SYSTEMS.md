# Adhesive, Grout, Trims and Levelling Systems

## Purpose

Use this file when customers ask what else they need with tiles.

---

# Tile Adhesive

## What It Does

Tile adhesive bonds the tile to the substrate.

## What Affects Adhesive Choice

- Tile type.
- Tile size.
- Indoor or outdoor use.
- Wall or floor.
- Wet or dry area.
- Substrate.
- Underfloor heating.
- Large format.
- Existing tile-over-tile.
- Exterior exposure.
- Manufacturer instructions.

## Safe Answer

> Adhesive choice depends on tile size, substrate and application. Large-format, wet-area, outdoor and tile-over-tile installations usually need more careful adhesive selection.

## Never Say

Do not say:

- “Any adhesive will work.”
- “Use the cheapest glue.”
- “You do not need primer.”
- “You can tile over anything.”

---

# Primer

## What It Does

Primer helps prepare the substrate and improve bonding where required.

## Use When

- Dusty or porous screed.
- Self-levelling compound.
- Tile-over-tile system.
- Specific adhesive instructions require it.
- Problem substrates.

## Safe Answer

> Primer depends on the substrate and adhesive system. The installer should confirm whether the surface needs priming before adhesive is applied.

---

# Grout

## What It Does

Grout fills the joints between tiles.

## Grout Is Not Waterproofing

Important:

> Grout joints are not waterproof.

## What Affects Grout Choice

- Joint width.
- Tile type.
- Wet or dry area.
- Indoor or outdoor use.
- Colour choice.
- Cleaning requirements.
- Chemical exposure.
- Movement.
- Commercial use.

## Safe Answer

> Grout colour affects the final look and maintenance. In wet areas, grout does not replace waterproofing.

---

# Silicone and Movement at Changes of Plane

## Use When

- Wall meets floor.
- Internal corners.
- Bath edge.
- Shower corners.
- Countertop edges.
- Expansion/movement areas.

## Safe Answer

> Changes of plane and movement areas often need flexible sealant rather than rigid grout. Your installer should confirm the correct detail.

---

# Tile Trims

## What They Do

Trims finish exposed tile edges and transitions.

## Common Uses

- External tile corners.
- Shower niches.
- Wall tile edges.
- Step edges.
- Floor transitions.
- Skirtings.
- LVT transitions.

## Safe Answer

> Trims protect exposed edges and improve the finish. Measure all exposed edges in linear metres and add cutting allowance.

---

# Spacers

## What They Do

Spacers help keep grout joints consistent.

## Safe Answer

> Spacers help create consistent joints, but the final joint width must suit the tile, edge type, size and installation method.

---

# Wedge and Clip Levelling System

## What It Does

A wedge and clip system helps align adjacent tiles while adhesive cures.

It is especially useful for large-format tiles.

## What It Does Not Do

It does not:

- Flatten a bad substrate.
- Replace correct adhesive coverage.
- Replace skilled installation.
- Eliminate movement joints.
- Guarantee perfect installation.
- Fix bowed or warped tiles.

## Safe Answer

> A wedge and clip system can help control lippage between tiles, but the floor still needs to be flat and the tiler must use the correct adhesive and trowel method.

## Calculation

Use:

Estimated clips = number of tiles × clips per tile

Planning guide:

- 600 × 600: about 4 clips per tile.
- 600 × 1200: about 6 to 8 clips per tile.
- 700 × 1400: about 8 to 10 clips per tile.

Add 10% for waste.

## Customer Education

Explain:

- Clips sit between tiles at the grout joint.
- Wedges push through clips to align tile edges.
- Clips are broken off after curing.
- The base of the clip remains under the tile edge.
- Grout is applied after clips are removed and joints are cleaned.

---

# Movement Joints

## What They Do

Movement joints allow the tile layer to accommodate movement.

## Where They Matter

- Large floor areas.
- Doorways and transitions.
- Perimeters.
- Outdoor areas.
- Sun-exposed areas.
- Heated floors.
- Over structural joints.
- Commercial floors.

## Safe Answer

> Movement joints are part of a proper tile installation. The installer or project professional should plan them, especially in large, outdoor or sun-exposed areas.

## Never Say

Do not say levelling clips or anti-fracture membranes remove the need for movement joints.
