# New Sales Consultant 30 Day Training

**Version:** 2026-06-18  
**Market:** South Africa / Southern Africa  
**Business:** Tiletoria / Tori RAG brain  
**Layer:** Phase 3 Control Layer  

## Purpose
Provide a practical 30-day learning path for new Tiletoria sales consultants using Tori as a daily trainer.

## Retrieval triggers
- 30 day training
- new consultant
- new staff
- sales consultant training
- onboarding
- first month
- training plan

## Training goal
A new consultant should become confident enough to guide customers safely, capture useful quote details, avoid risky promises and know when to escalate.

## Week 1: Foundations
### Day 1: Tiletoria basics
Learn product categories, branches, customer personas and Tori's role.

Staff prompt:

> Tori, explain Tiletoria's main product categories like I am a new sales consultant.

### Day 2: Tile fundamentals
Learn porcelain vs ceramic, rectified vs non-rectified, finishes, sizes, shade variation and batch.

### Day 3: Measuring and m²
Learn length x width, waste allowance, boxes, UOM and why customers must buy enough.

### Day 4: Room applications
Learn product selection by bathroom, kitchen, patio, garage, living area and commercial area.

### Day 5: Quote capture
Practise collecting clean quote details.

## Week 2: Accessories and systems
### Day 6: Adhesives
Learn adhesive basics and why site conditions matter.

### Day 7: Grout
Learn grout colour, joint width, grout haze and cleaning basics.

### Day 8: Trims/profiles
Learn edges, transitions, stairs and finishing details.

### Day 9: Levelling systems
Learn wedge and clip basics, spacer logic and large-format risk.

### Day 10: Waterproofing and wet areas
Learn why grout is not waterproof and when to escalate.

## Week 3: Customer journeys
### Day 11: DIY homeowner
Practise simple explanations and patient guidance.

### Day 12: Renovator
Learn project planning, budgeting and risk checks.

### Day 13: Tiler/contractor
Practise fast technical questions and branch handover.

### Day 14: Plumber/sanitaryware
Learn compatibility questions and plumber handover.

### Day 15: Designer/architect/QS
Learn spec pack and datasheet request logic.

## Week 4: Risk, complaints and excellence
### Day 16: Stock/price boundaries
Learn why not to guess live data.

### Day 17: Returns and complaints
Learn neutral evidence-based language.

### Day 18: Technical failures
Learn signs of tenting, cracking, hollow tiles, lippage and moisture.

### Day 19: Objection handling
Practise price, value, trust and availability objections.

### Day 20: Gold-standard responses
Practise writing perfect customer replies.

## Final 10-day reinforcement
Repeat daily practice:

- one calculator exercise
- one product selection scenario
- one complaint scenario
- one quote handover
- one objection response

## New consultant daily prompt
> Tori, give me one sales training scenario for today. Test me with the customer's question, then show me the ideal response and what I should not say.

## Competency checklist
A new consultant should be able to:

- calculate m² and boxes
- explain waste allowance
- ask the right room/application questions
- identify wet/outdoor/slip risks
- capture a clean quote request
- avoid stock/price hallucination
- request photos for complaints
- escalate red flags
- explain product value without attacking competitors

## Manager review questions
Managers can ask:

- Can the consultant explain porcelain vs ceramic?
- Can they quote without missing accessories?
- Can they identify a risky bathroom/outdoor/ramp scenario?
- Do they avoid promising live stock?
- Do they know when to escalate?

---

## Universal Tori rule for this file
Tori must be helpful, practical and commercially safe. Tori may guide, calculate, explain, collect details and prepare a handover, but must not invent live stock, live prices, delivery availability, technical certifications, warranties, legal outcomes, account status, branch decisions or product suitability where site conditions are unknown. When the answer depends on current branch systems, ERP data, site inspection, supplier confirmation or management approval, Tori must clearly say so and route the customer to the correct Tiletoria team.
