# Persona 3: Smaller Contractors — Q&A Knowledge Base

> **Persona:** Owner-operator tiling contractor, 2-5 permanent staff + casuals, KZN/Gauteng/WC, 2-15 units/month, 50-300m² jobs, sole decision-maker. Top concerns: stock-outs, pricing consistency, credit access, and speed.

---

## Category 1: Trade Account Setup & Management (60 Q&As)

**Q1:** How do I open a trade account with Tiletoria?
**A:** Visit any Tiletoria trade counter with your company registration documents, ID, proof of address, and latest 3 months' bank statements. Complete the trade account application form at the counter or request one via WhatsApp. Most accounts are processed within 48-72 hours.

**Q2:** What documents do I need to apply for a trade account?
**A:** You'll need your CIPC company registration certificate or business name registration, your ID document, proof of residential address (utility bill not older than 3 months), latest 3 months' business bank statements, and a completed credit application form. If you're VAT registered, include your VAT certificate.

**Q3:** How long does trade account approval take?
**A:** Standard trade account approval takes 48-72 hours from the time all documents are submitted. If additional credit checks are required, it may extend to 5 business days. You'll receive an SMS and WhatsApp confirmation once approved.

**Q4:** Can I start buying before my trade account is fully approved?
**A:** Yes, you can make cash purchases immediately at any trade counter while your application is being processed. Keep your receipts — once your trade account is active, those purchases can be retrospectively linked to your account for volume tracking.

**Q5:** What's the difference between a cash trade account and a credit trade account?
**A:** A cash trade account gives you access to trade pricing (15-25% below retail) but requires payment at the time of purchase or collection. A credit trade account extends 30-day payment terms subject to credit approval and a set limit.

**Q6:** What credit limit can I expect as a new smaller contractor?
**A:** Initial credit limits for smaller contractors typically range from R15,000 to R50,000 depending on your turnover, credit history, and time in business. Limits are reviewed every 6 months and can be increased based on payment history and volume.

**Q7:** How do I increase my credit limit?
**A:** Contact your dedicated account manager or the trade counter supervisor. You'll need to provide updated bank statements showing increased turnover and demonstrate 3+ months of consistent payment within terms. Limit increase requests are processed within 3-5 business days.

**Q8:** Can I get 60-day or 90-day payment terms instead of 30 days?
**A:** Standard terms are 30 days. Extended terms of 60 days may be considered for established contractors with 12+ months of consistent payment history and monthly purchases exceeding R50,000. Apply through your account manager with supporting financials.

**Q9:** Do you offer credit to contractors who are blacklisted or have poor credit?
**A:** Each application is assessed individually. If you have credit challenges, you may still qualify for a cash trade account with full trade pricing benefits. Credit accounts require a satisfactory credit check, but we work with contractors to find a suitable arrangement.

**Q10:** Can I have trade accounts at multiple Tiletoria branches?
**A:** Yes, your trade account is valid across all Tiletoria branches — Cape Town (Paarden Eiland), Johannesburg (Northriding) and Menlyn (Spec Lab), Cornubia, and Paarden Eiland. You can collect from or request delivery from any branch.

**Q11:** Is there a minimum monthly spend to maintain a trade account?
**A:** There is no strict minimum monthly spend for cash trade accounts. For credit accounts, we expect some activity every 90 days. If your account is dormant for 6 months, it may be reviewed for deactivation, but you can reactivate at any time.

**Q12:** I'm a one-man tiler with no registered company — can I still get trade pricing?
**A:** While formal trade accounts require business registration, informal tilers can access competitive pricing at the trade counter. We encourage you to formalise your business to unlock full trade benefits, credit facilities, and volume rebates.

**Q13:** My business is registered in my wife's name — can I apply for the trade account?
**A:** Yes, provided you have a letter of authority from the registered owner authorising you to transact on behalf of the business, along with both parties' ID documents. Alternatively, the registered owner can apply and designate you as the authorised purchaser.

**Q14:** Do I need to renew my trade account annually?
**A:** Trade accounts are reviewed annually. You'll be asked to submit updated CIPC documents, proof of address, and recent bank statements. Your account manager will contact you when renewal is due — usually a straightforward process if your business details haven't changed.

**Q15:** Can I add additional authorised buyers to my trade account?
**A:** Yes, you can add up to 3 authorised buyers (e.g., your site foreman, wife/partner, or delivery coordinator). Submit their full names, ID numbers, and contact details in writing to your account manager or at the trade counter.

**Q16:** What happens if I exceed my credit limit mid-project?
**A:** Contact your account manager immediately. For active projects, we can often arrange a temporary limit increase or release reserved stock against a partial payment. We understand that project timelines don't always align with statement cycles.

**Q17:** Can I get a trade account if I'm based in PE but work mostly in East London?
**A:** Yes, your PE trade account gives you access to trade pricing nationwide. For East London projects, you can arrange delivery through the Paarden Eiland (delivery charges apply) or collect from PE and transport yourself.

**Q18:** How do I know if my credit account application has been approved?
**A:** You'll receive an SMS confirmation and a WhatsApp message from your branch with your account number and credit limit. Your account manager will also call to introduce themselves and explain how to use your account.

**Q19:** Can I use my Tiletoria trade account at Spec Lab?
**A:** Yes, your Tiletoria trade account is valid at Spec Lab Menlyn (Menlyn). The same pricing, terms, and credit limits apply. Spec Lab offers additional specifier-grade products and services for higher-end residential projects.

**Q20:** What is the interest rate on overdue credit account balances?
**A:** Interest is charged at 2% per month (24% per annum) on overdue balances. To avoid interest, ensure payment reaches us by the 30th day from statement date. If you're experiencing cash flow issues, contact your account manager before the due date.

**Q21:** Do you require personal surety for credit accounts?
**A:** For credit limits up to R50,000, personal surety is generally not required for registered companies with 2+ years' trading history. For higher limits or newer businesses, the directors may be asked to sign personal surety.

**Q22:** Can I get a pro forma invoice to secure a job before opening a trade account?
**A:** Yes, any branch can provide a pro forma invoice at trade pricing to help you secure a tender or quote. Once your account is approved, the pro forma can be converted to a formal order.

**Q23:** How do I close or suspend my trade account?
**A:** Submit a written request to your account manager. All outstanding balances must be settled, and any unused credits will be refunded. Accounts can be suspended (e.g., for seasonal breaks) and reactivated later without reapplying.

**Q24:** Do you offer a trial period for new trade account holders?
**A:** New trade accounts start on cash terms for the first month, after which credit terms are activated (if approved). This allows both parties to establish a trading relationship before credit is extended.

**Q25:** What's the difference between a trade account and a normal retail purchase?
**A:** Trade accounts offer 15-25% below retail pricing, access to dedicated trade counters, 30-day credit terms (if approved), dedicated account managers, WhatsApp stock checking, delivery prioritisation, and volume rebate eligibility. Retail purchases are at marked prices with immediate payment.

**Q26:** Can I change my trading name on my account?
**A:** Yes, submit your updated CIPC registration documents and a letter requesting the name change. The update takes 2-3 business days. Your account number and credit limit remain unchanged.

**Q27:** I'm a subcontractor — can my main builder pay Tiletoria directly on my behalf?
**A:** Yes, third-party payments are accepted provided your account is in good standing. The builder must reference your Tiletoria account number when making payment. Notify your account manager in advance of any third-party payments.

**Q28:** Do you keep my account information confidential?
**A:** Absolutely. All account and personal information is handled in accordance with POPIA (Protection of Personal Information Act). Your information is never shared with third parties without your consent.

**Q29:** Can I get a consolidated statement if I buy from multiple branches?
**A:** Yes, request a consolidated statement from your account manager. Statements can be emailed monthly or accessed on request. Each transaction shows the branch of purchase for easy tracking.

**Q30:** What happens if my business banking details change?
**A:** Notify your account manager immediately and provide a new bank confirmation letter or updated bank statement. This ensures your account details remain current and prevents payment reconciliation delays.

**Q31:** Do I need NHBRC registration to open a trade account?
**A:** No, NHBRC registration is not required to open a trade account. However, having NHBRC registration may strengthen your credit application and is recommended if you enrol houses for warranty.

**Q32:** Can a CC (Close Corporation) still open a trade account?
**A:** Yes, existing CCs can still open trade accounts. You'll need your CK1/CK2 documents and a resolution from the members authorising the account. Note that CIPC no longer registers new CCs.

**Q33:** I'm a plumber — can I open a trade account even though I also do tiling?
**A:** Yes, multi-trade contractors are welcome. Your trade account gives you access to our full range including tiles, adhesives, sanware, and waterproofing. Your account manager can recommend cross-category bundles.

**Q34:** Do you offer joint accounts for business partnerships?
**A:** Yes, partnership applications require all partners' IDs, the partnership agreement, and a resolution signed by all partners specifying authorised signatories. Each partner's credit profile is assessed for credit accounts.

**Q35:** How do I update my contact details on my trade account?
**A:** WhatsApp your updated cell number, email, or address to your account manager, or email the changes to accounts@tiletoria.co.za. Updates are processed within 24 hours.

**Q36:** Can I set a purchase limit for my authorised buyers?
**A:** Yes, you can set individual purchase limits for each authorised buyer. This is useful for controlling site foreman or delivery coordinator purchases. Contact your account manager to set these limits.

**Q37:** What happens to my trade account if I sell my business?
**A:** Trade accounts are not transferable. The new owner must apply for their own account. Any outstanding balance remains the responsibility of the original account holder until settled.

**Q38:** Do you offer credit insurance or payment protection?
**A:** We don't offer credit insurance directly, but we recommend contractors consider business credit insurance to protect against client non-payment, which can affect your ability to pay suppliers.

**Q39:** Can I get a letter of good standing from Tiletoria?
**A:** Yes, request a letter of good standing from your account manager. This confirms your account is active and in good order. Useful for tender applications and builder relationships.

**Q40:** What is the fastest way to open a trade account?
**A:** The fastest way is to visit your nearest trade counter with all documents ready (ID, CIPC docs, 3 months bank statements, proof of address). Complete the application on-site, and approval typically takes 48-72 hours.

**Q41:** Can I apply for a trade account online?
**A:** Initial applications can be started by requesting a form via WhatsApp or email, but you'll need to visit a trade counter in person to submit documents and sign the application. Online-only applications are being developed.

**Q42:** Do I get a trade card or account number?
**A:** You'll receive a unique trade account number. Present this number or your ID at the trade counter for quick service. Some branches issue trade cards — ask at your local counter.

**Q43:** What is the age requirement for a trade account?
**A:** The business must be registered, and the applicant/director must be 18 years or older. There is no maximum age limit.

**Q44:** Can I get trade pricing on sanware and adhesives too?
**A:** Yes, your trade account gives you access to trade pricing across all categories — tiles, adhesives, grouts, waterproofing, sanware, flooring, and accessories. Volume discounts apply across the total basket.

**Q45:** Do you verify my home address or business address?
**A:** We verify both. Proof of residential address (utility bill) is required for credit checks, and your business address is used for deliveries and correspondence.

**Q46:** Can I use my accountant or bookkeeper as the point of contact?
**A:** You can designate an additional contact person (e.g., your bookkeeper) to receive statements and pricing, but the account holder remains the primary responsible party for payments.

**Q47:** What credit bureau do you use for checks?
**A:** We use major credit bureaus including TransUnion and Experian. A credit check is only conducted with your consent as part of the application process.

**Q48:** Can I get a credit account without a credit check?
**A:** No, credit checks are mandatory for credit accounts as part of responsible lending practices. Cash trade accounts don't require credit checks.

**Q49:** How do I dispute something on my account statement?
**A:** Contact your account manager or the accounts department within 7 days of statement issue. Provide details of the disputed transaction, and we'll investigate and respond within 5 business days.

**Q50:** Do you offer introductory pricing for new trade account holders?
**A:** New trade account holders receive our standard trade pricing immediately. Additional welcome promotions are run periodically — ask your account manager about current new-account incentives.

**Q51:** Can I get a temporary credit limit increase for a big project?
**A:** Yes, contact your account manager with details of the project, expected value, and duration. Temporary increases are assessed case-by-case and may require a deposit or partial prepayment.

**Q52:** What happens if my client doesn't pay me — can I get an extension?
**A:** Contact your account manager before your due date to discuss payment arrangements. We understand contractor cash flow challenges and can often arrange a short extension or payment plan for trusted accounts.

**Q53:** Do you offer account statements via WhatsApp?
**A:** Yes, you can request your current balance and recent transactions via WhatsApp. Full PDF statements are emailed monthly or on request.

**Q54:** Can I set up automatic payments from my bank?
**A:** Yes, set up a debit order or scheduled EFT from your bank using your Tiletoria account number as reference. Notify us if you set up a debit order so we can note it on your account.

**Q55:** Is there a joining fee for trade accounts?
**A:** No, there is no joining fee or annual membership fee for trade accounts. You only pay for the products you purchase.

**Q56:** Can my trade account be used for personal/residential purchases?
**A:** Trade accounts are for business use. However, many contractors use their trade account for their own homes — just ensure this doesn't conflict with your business tax position. We don't restrict personal use.

**Q57:** Do you offer training or onboarding for new trade account holders?
**A:** Yes, your account manager will walk you through the product range, ordering process, WhatsApp stock checks, and delivery options. Branch open days and product demonstrations are also held regularly.

**Q58:** Can I get references from other contractors who use Tiletoria?
**A:** We can connect you with contractors in our network at networking events and braai days. Due to privacy, we don't share contact details directly, but our contractor community is active and supportive.

**Q59:** What makes a trade account application get rejected?
**A:** Common reasons include incomplete documentation, unsatisfactory credit check, business not registered, or inconsistent banking history. If rejected, we'll explain why and guide you on reapplying.

**Q60:** How do I reactivate a dormant trade account?
**A:** Contact your nearest branch or account manager. Dormant accounts can usually be reactivated within 24 hours with updated contact details. Credit limits may need to be reassessed if dormant for over 12 months.

## Category 2: Trade Pricing & Margins (60 Q&As)

**Q1:** How much discount do I get with a trade account?
**A:** Trade account holders receive 15-25% below retail pricing, depending on the product category. Tile categories typically see the deepest discounts, while specialty items like premium sanware may have smaller margins.

**Q2:** Is trade pricing the same across all branches?
**A:** Yes, trade pricing is standardised across all Tiletoria branches. You won't get different prices in Durban vs Cape Town vs Johannesburg for the same SKU.

**Q3:** How does volume pricing work?
**A:** Volume pricing kicks in at project level. Projects requiring 500m²+ of tile typically qualify for additional discounts beyond standard trade pricing. Speak to your account manager for a project-specific price.

**Q4:** Do I get better pricing if I buy more than one product category?
**A:** Yes, bundled purchases (e.g., tiles + adhesive + grout + waterproofing) can attract additional margin. Your account manager can structure a package deal for full-project material supply.

**Q5:** How long are quotes valid for?
**A:** Standard trade quotes are valid for 14 days. Project quotes for 500m²+ are valid for 30 days. If pricing changes during the quote period, we'll honour the quoted price on stock already in the country.

**Q6:** Can I get a formal quote for a tender?
**A:** Yes, we provide formal trade quotes on letterhead with full product descriptions and specifications suitable for tender submission. Allow 24-48 hours for formal tender quotes.

**Q7:** Do your prices include VAT?
**A:** All displayed prices and quotes exclude VAT unless stated otherwise. VAT at 15% is added at checkout or invoicing.

**Q8:** How often do prices change?
**A:** Prices are reviewed quarterly based on exchange rates, container costs, and supplier pricing. Major price adjustments are communicated 14 days in advance via WhatsApp and email to all trade account holders.

**Q9:** Can I lock in a price for a future project?
**A:** For confirmed projects with a purchase order, we can lock pricing for up to 30 days. For large projects (1000m²+), extended price holds of 60-90 days may be negotiated with a deposit.

**Q10:** Do I get notified before prices go up?
**A:** Yes, all trade account holders receive advance notification of price increases via WhatsApp broadcast and email. Typically 14 days' notice is given, allowing you to place forward orders at current pricing.

**Q11:** Is there a price difference between cash and credit trade purchases?
**A:** No, trade pricing is the same whether you pay cash or use your credit facility. Early payment discounts may apply to credit accounts — check with your account manager.

**Q12:** Can I get a price list to keep in my bakkie?
**A:** Yes, request a current trade price list from your account manager or at the trade counter. Price lists are also available via WhatsApp as a PDF. Top 200 fast-moving SKUs are included.

**Q13:** Do you price match competitors like competitor retailers Trade or competitor retailers Pro?
**A:** We regularly benchmark our pricing against the market. If you have a genuine lower written quote for an identical product, bring it to your account manager. We'll do our best to match or better it.

**Q14:** How do I know I'm getting the right trade price at the counter?
**A:** Always quote your trade account number before the transaction is processed. The trade price will automatically pull up on the system. If in doubt, ask the counter staff to confirm your trade discount percentage.

**Q15:** Are there hidden charges on trade accounts?
**A:** No hidden charges. You pay the quoted product price plus VAT. Delivery, if requested, is charged separately. Credit accounts incur interest only if payment exceeds 30-day terms.

**Q16:** Can I get a breakdown of pricing for a full bathroom installation?
**A:** Yes, your account manager can provide a full material list with pricing for tiles, adhesive, grout, waterproofing, trims, spacers, and sanware. Bring your plans or dimensions for accurate quantities.

**Q17:** Do trade prices apply to special orders and imported products?
**A:** Yes, trade pricing applies to our full range including special-order imported products. Lead times and minimum order quantities may apply for non-stock items.

**Q18:** What is the typical markup I should add for my clients?
**A:** Most smaller contractors mark up materials by 15-25% when billing clients. This covers your time sourcing, handling, warranty risk, and cash flow. Your Tiletoria trade pricing is designed to support this margin structure.

**Q19:** Can I get project pricing for a 15-unit complex I'm tiling?
**A:** Absolutely. Bring your BOQ or material schedule to your account manager. Project pricing for multi-unit developments offers enhanced discounts beyond standard trade, especially for 1000m²+ total volumes.

**Q20:** Do you offer early settlement discounts on credit accounts?
**A:** Yes, a 2.5% early settlement discount applies to credit account balances settled within 7 days of statement date. This is automatically calculated on your statement.

**Q21:** Can I get a provisional price for budgeting purposes?
**A:** Yes, request a budgetary estimate from the trade counter or your account manager. These are non-binding but use current trade pricing to help you prepare client quotes. Formal quotes can be issued when specifications are confirmed.

**Q22:** Do prices differ between the trade counter and the showroom?
**A:** Showroom retail prices are higher than trade counter prices. Always purchase through the trade counter or quote your account number to ensure you receive trade pricing.

**Q23:** How does the exchange rate affect pricing?
**A:** Most of our imported products are priced in Euros or US Dollars. When the Rand weakens significantly, price increases may be necessary. We hedge currency exposure where possible to provide pricing stability.

**Q24:** Can I get a price commitment for a 6-month project?
**A:** For large projects with confirmed schedules, we can structure a supply agreement with fixed pricing for up to 6 months. A deposit or monthly minimum commitment is typically required.

**Q25:** Do you offer clearance or end-of-line pricing to trade?
**A:** Yes, clearance stock is offered to trade account holders first, often at 30-50% below trade price. Clearance notifications are sent via WhatsApp broadcast. Act fast — clearance stock moves quickly.

**Q26:** Can I see my purchase history and average pricing?
**A:** Yes, your account manager can provide a purchase history report showing products, quantities, and average pricing over any period. This helps you track costs and plan future projects.

**Q27:** Is there a minimum order value for trade pricing?
**A:** No minimum order value for standard trade pricing. For project pricing and volume discounts, minimum volumes (typically 500m² for tiles) apply.

**Q28:** Do you offer retrospective volume rebates?
**A:** Yes, quarterly volume rebates are calculated based on total purchases across all categories. Thresholds and rebate percentages are communicated at the start of each quarter.

**Q29:** How do I get the best possible pricing from Tiletoria?
**A:** Maximise your discount by: buying across multiple categories, consolidating purchases to hit volume tiers, paying early for the settlement discount, attending trade days for special pricing, and building a relationship with your account manager.

**Q30:** Do trade prices include delivery?
**A:** No, delivery is charged separately based on distance and load size. However, large orders (R20,000+) within 30km may qualify for free delivery — ask your account manager.

**Q31:** Can I get a quote in Excel format for my records?
**A:** Yes, request an Excel quote from your account manager. This is useful for importing into your own quoting system or BOQ.

**Q32:** What is the price difference between porcelain and ceramic tiles for trade?
**A:** At trade, ceramic wall tiles start from around R80-120/m², while porcelain floor tiles range from R150-400/m² depending on size and origin. Your trade discount applies to both categories.

**Q33:** Do adhesive and grout prices fluctuate like tile prices?
**A:** Adhesive and grout prices are more stable as they're locally manufactured. Price adjustments typically happen annually rather than quarterly. Stock up during promotional periods.

**Q34:** Can I get a price estimate without my trade account number?
**A:** Informal estimates can be given at the counter, but trade pricing is only confirmed when your account number is verified. Open a trade account to lock in the best prices.

**Q35:** How do I compare Tiletoria pricing with what I'm currently paying?
**A:** Bring your recent invoices from your current supplier (with prices visible) to your account manager. We'll do a line-by-line comparison on comparable products.

**Q36:** Are there additional discounts for NHBRC-registered contractors?
**A:** NHBRC registration doesn't attract a separate discount, but registered contractors often qualify for higher credit limits and are prioritised for project pricing.

**Q37:** Do you offer package pricing for bathroom sets?
**A:** Yes, bathroom set packages (tiles + sanware + fittings + accessories) are available at bundled pricing. Ask your account manager for current bathroom package deals.

**Q38:** Can I get trade pricing on samples?
**A:** Sample pieces are generally provided free to trade account holders for client presentations. Full-size paid samples are available at trade price if you need to leave with a client.

**Q39:** How do I request a price match?
**A:** Provide your account manager with a written quote from a competitor for the exact same product (brand, size, colour, batch). The quote must be current (within 14 days). We'll verify and match where possible.

**Q40:** Do prices go down when the Rand strengthens?
**A:** We review pricing quarterly in both directions. Sustained Rand strengthening may lead to price reductions on imported products, which we pass on to trade customers.

**Q41:** Can I get pricing for a client who wants a specific Italian tile?
**A:** Yes, we import from Italy, Spain, France, Turkey, Brazil, India, and China. If we don't stock the specific tile, we can source it or recommend the closest equivalent from our range.

**Q42:** Is there a difference between trade counter pricing and phone/email pricing?
**A:** No, pricing is consistent across all channels. However, the trade counter may offer clearance items or on-the-day specials that aren't advertised elsewhere.

**Q43:** Can I get a discount on my first order?
**A:** New trade account holders sometimes qualify for a first-order promotional discount. Ask your account manager about current new-customer incentives.

**Q44:** How do quantity breaks work on adhesives and grouts?
**A:** Adhesive and grout pricing typically has breaks at 20 bags, 50 bags, and 100+ bags. Your trade price already includes the base discount, and volume tiers apply on top for large projects.

**Q45:** Do you offer trade pricing to labour-only contractors?
**A:** Labour-only contractors who don't purchase materials can still open a cash trade account for sundries (spacers, trims, tools) at trade pricing. For material procurement, we work with your client's account or direct payment.

**Q46:** Can I get an all-in price per square metre for tiling a bathroom?
**A:** Your account manager can provide an all-in material price per m² including tiles, adhesive, grout, and waterproofing. Labour is excluded as you provide your own installation service.

**Q47:** How does Spec Lab pricing compare to trade counter pricing?
**A:** Spec Lab carries premium and designer ranges with higher base prices, but trade discounts still apply. For mid-market residential work, the trade counter typically offers better value.

**Q48:** Can I get a fuel levy or transport cost breakdown?
**A:** Delivery charges are quoted as a single rate based on distance and load size. If you need a detailed breakdown for a tender, your account manager can provide one.

**Q49:** Are your trade prices published anywhere?
**A:** Trade prices are confidential to account holders and not published publicly. Your price list is provided on account opening and updated regularly.

**Q50:** Can I negotiate pricing for a very large project?
**A:** For projects exceeding R500,000 in materials, pricing is negotiable at management level. Contact your account manager to escalate to the branch manager for project-specific pricing.

**Q51:** Do you offer price protection on forward orders?
**A:** Yes, if you place a forward order and pay a deposit, the quoted price is protected even if exchange rate changes lead to price increases before delivery.

**Q52:** How do I know if a product is on promotion?
**A:** Trade promotions are announced via WhatsApp broadcast and in-branch signage. Your account manager also flags relevant promotions based on your purchase history.

**Q53:** Can I get pricing for underfloor heating systems?
**A:** Yes, we supply and price underfloor heating systems at trade rates. Systems start from approximately R450-650/m² depending on the system type and coverage area.

**Q54:** Do you offer different pricing tiers for different contractor sizes?
**A:** All trade account holders receive the same base trade discount. Volume tiers and project pricing are based on purchase volume rather than business size, so smaller contractors buying in volume get the same rates as larger ones.

**Q55:** What is the typical price range for 600x600mm porcelain tiles at trade?
**A:** 600x600mm porcelain floor tiles at trade typically range from R120-280/m² for standard ranges, and R280-450/m² for premium imported ranges.

**Q56:** How much should I budget for adhesive per square metre?
**A:** At trade pricing, quality tile adhesive costs approximately R25-45/m² depending on tile size and adhesive type. Large-format tiles require premium adhesive at the higher end.

**Q57:** Do you offer trade pricing on tools and tiling equipment?
**A:** Yes, tile cutters, trowels, spacers, leveling systems, and other tools are available at trade pricing. Full tool lists are available at the trade counter.

**Q58:** Can I get a cost-per-square-metre breakdown for different tile sizes?
**A:** Your account manager can provide a comparative cost breakdown showing material cost per m² for different tile sizes (e.g., 600x600 vs 800x800 vs 1200x600) including adhesive and grout.

**Q59:** How do I factor Tiletoria pricing into my client quotes?
**A:** Use your trade price as the base cost, add your markup (typically 15-25%), add delivery if applicable, and present the total to your client. Your account manager can help you structure professional client quotes.

**Q60:** Who do I speak to if I think I've been overcharged?
**A:** Return to the trade counter with your receipt or invoice, or contact your account manager immediately. We'll review the transaction and process a refund or credit note if an error occurred.

## Category 3: Stock Availability & Checking (60 Q&As)

**Q1:** How do I check if a product is in stock before I drive to the branch?
**A:** The fastest way is to WhatsApp your branch with the product code or description. Responses are typically within minutes during trade counter hours. You can also call the trade counter directly.

**Q2:** What's the WhatsApp number for stock checks?
**A:** Each branch has its own WhatsApp number for stock checks. Your account manager will share the number when your account is opened. Save it in your phone for quick access.

**Q3:** How accurate is the WhatsApp stock check?
**A:** WhatsApp stock checks reflect the live system and are accurate at the time of checking. However, stock moves quickly during trade hours, so we recommend confirming availability before leaving the site and reserving if needed.

**Q4:** Can I reserve stock while I arrange a bakkie or transport?
**A:** Yes, trade account holders can reserve stock for up to 24 hours (48 hours by arrangement) with no deposit. After that, the reservation is released unless extended.

**Q5:** What happens if I reserve stock and don't collect it?
**A:** Unclaimed reservations are automatically released after 24 hours (48 hours by arrangement). Frequent no-shows may affect your reservation privileges. If you're running late, a quick WhatsApp call extends the hold.

**Q6:** Do you guarantee stock availability for my confirmed projects?
**A:** For confirmed projects with a purchase order or deposit, we guarantee stock availability for the agreed delivery schedule. Walk-in purchases are subject to available stock.

**Q7:** What are the "top 50 contractor SKUs" and why do they matter?
**A:** These are the 50 fastest-moving tile SKUs purchased by contractors — typically neutral colours in 600x600 and 800x800 porcelain, white wall tiles, and standard grey floor tiles. We guarantee these are always in stock.

**Q8:** How do I find out which products are in the top 50?
**A:** Ask for the "Contractor Fast-Mover List" at the trade counter or via WhatsApp. This list is updated quarterly and shows the most reliable stock items for quoting with confidence.

**Q9:** What happens if my chosen tile is out of stock?
**A:** Your trade counter consultant will check alternative branches for availability, suggest the nearest equivalent from stock, or provide the lead time for replenishment (typically 8-12 weeks for imports).

**Q10:** How do I ensure batch consistency for my project?
**A:** Always specify the batch number when ordering, and order your full quantity in one go. If the project requires multiple deliveries, we allocate stock from the same batch and reserve it against your order.

**Q11:** What is "shade variation" and how do I manage it?
**A:** Shade variation refers to natural colour differences between tile batches. All tiles have a shade rating (V1-V4) on the box. Always check the shade number on the box, mix tiles from multiple boxes during installation, and order 10% extra from the same batch.

**Q12:** Can you check stock at other branches and transfer it?

## Category 7: Product Technical Data (55 Q&As)

**Q1:** Where do I find technical datasheets for your tiles?
**A:** Technical datasheets are available at the trade counter, via WhatsApp request, or from your account manager. Datasheets include PEI ratings, water absorption, slip ratings, breaking strength, and SANS compliance data.

**Q2:** What is a PEI rating and why does it matter?
**A:** PEI (Porcelain Enamel Institute) rating indicates tile wear resistance. PEI I = walls only, PEI II = light residential floors, PEI III = normal residential/light commercial, PEI IV = commercial, PEI V = heavy commercial. Most residential floors need PEI III+.

**Q3:** What PEI rating do I need for a residential home entrance?
**A:** Home entrances, passages, and living areas should use PEI III or higher due to foot traffic. PEI IV is recommended for rental properties or homes with pets.

**Q4:** What is the difference between R9, R10, and R11 slip ratings?
**A:** R9 = low slip resistance (dry indoor areas), R10 = normal slip resistance (general residential), R11 = enhanced slip resistance (bathrooms, patios, commercial), R12-R13 = high slip resistance (wet areas, pools, industrial). SANS 1449 references these ratings.

**Q5:** What slip rating is required for a wet room or shower floor?
**A:** SANS 10107 recommends R11 or higher for wet room floors and shower areas. We stock anti-slip tiles specifically rated for wet-area application.

**Q6:** Do you provide SANS compliance certificates for your tiles?
**A:** Yes, SANS compliance certificates are available on request for all tiles that carry certification. Your account manager can provide these for NHBRC and council submissions.

**Q7:** What is water absorption and why does it matter?
**A:** Water absorption measures how much water a tile absorbs. Porcelain tiles have <0.5% absorption (impervious), ceramic 0.5-3% (vitreous), and some wall tiles 3-10% (semi-vitreous). Low absorption is critical for outdoor and wet-area tiles.

**Q8:** What is the breaking strength of your porcelain tiles?
**A:** Our porcelain tiles exceed the SANS minimum breaking strength of 1,300N for wall tiles and 1,800N for floor tiles. Most porcelain floor tiles test at 2,000N+. Datasheets show exact values.

**Q9:** What is "rectified" vs "pressed" edge?
**A:** Rectified tiles are cut to precise dimensions after firing, allowing narrower grout joints (1.5-2mm). Pressed (cushion edge) tiles have slight size variation and require wider grout joints (3-5mm). Rectified tiles cost more but give a sharper, more modern look.

**Q10:** What does "calibre" mean on the tile box?
**A:** Calibre refers to the tile's thickness. Always check the calibre number on the box — tiles of different calibres from the same range should not be laid together as it creates lippage. Same calibre = same thickness.

**Q11:** Can I mix tiles from different boxes during installation?
**A:** Yes, in fact you should mix tiles from 3-4 different boxes during installation. This blends any natural shade variation and prevents visible colour-blocking on the floor.

**Q12:** What is "rectified" in relation to large-format tiles?
**A:** Large-format tiles (600x1200mm and larger) are almost always rectified. This allows for minimal grout joints and the seamless look that clients expect. Always use a levelling system with rectified large-format tiles.

**Q13:** Do your tiles comply with NHBRC requirements?
**A:** Yes, our tile range includes products that meet NHBRC specifications for enrolled homes. Compliance certificates and method statements can be provided on request.

**Q14:** What is the difference between glazed and unglazed porcelain?
**A:** Glazed porcelain has a glass-like surface layer available in various colours and finishes. Unglazed (full-body) porcelain has the same colour and pattern throughout, making it more slip-resistant and suitable for heavy commercial use.

**Q15:** What does "V1, V2, V3, V4" mean on the box?
**A:** This is the shade variation rating. V1 = uniform colour (minimal variation), V2 = slight variation, V3 = moderate variation, V4 = substantial variation. Always check this rating and inform your client before installation.

**Q16:** Can I use wall tiles on the floor?
**A:** No — wall tiles are not designed to withstand foot traffic and typically have lower PEI ratings and breaking strength. Using wall tiles on floors voids warranties and may result in cracking.

**Q17:** Can I use floor tiles on the wall?
**A:** Yes, floor tiles can be installed on walls and are often used for feature walls and bathroom walls. Note that floor tiles are heavier — ensure the wall structure can support the weight.

**Q18:** What is "rectified" vs "non-rectified" in terms of installation?
**A:** Rectified tiles require a perfectly flat substrate due to the minimal grout joint. Any substrate irregularity will show as lippage. Non-rectified (pressed) tiles are more forgiving with slight substrate imperfections.

**Q19:** What adhesive coverage do I need per square metre?
**A:** Standard adhesive coverage is 3-5kg/m² depending on trowel size and tile format. Large-format tiles need more adhesive (5-7kg/m²) and a notched trowel. Always check the adhesive manufacturer's specifications.

**Q20:** How much grout do I need per square metre?
**A:** Grout coverage depends on tile size and joint width. As a guide: 300x300mm tiles at 3mm joints = ~0.5kg/m². 600x600mm tiles at 2mm joints = ~0.3kg/m². 100x100mm mosaics at 5mm joints = ~1.2kg/m².

**Q21:** Do you provide NHBRC Enrolment Forms and Method Statements?
**A:** Yes, NHBRC-compliant method statements and product certificates are available from your account manager. These documents support your NHBRC enrolment and inspections.

**Q22:** What is the recommended expansion joint spacing for tile installations?
**A:** Perimeter expansion joints are required at all edges. Intermediate expansion joints should be installed every 8-10 metres in both directions for interior floors, and every 4-6 metres for exterior installations.

**Q23:** Can I install tiles over existing tiles?
**A:** Yes, provided the existing tiles are soundly bonded, clean, and the surface is primed. Use a flexible adhesive designed for tiling over tiles. Total thickness build-up must be checked against door clearances and trim transitions.

**Q24:** What is the minimum curing time before grouting?
**A:** Standard adhesive requires 24 hours before grouting. Rapid-set adhesive allows grouting after 3-4 hours. Full adhesive curing is 28 days, though foot traffic can resume after 24 hours with standard adhesive.

**Q25:** How long before a tiled floor can take foot traffic?
**A:** With standard adhesive: 24 hours for foot traffic, 72 hours for normal use. With rapid-set adhesive: 4 hours for foot traffic, 24 hours for normal use. Full curing is 28 days for both.

**Q26:** Do you have technical data for your adhesives and grouts?
**A:** Yes, technical datasheets for all adhesives, grouts, waterproofing, and ancillary products are available at the trade counter or via WhatsApp. Data includes mixing ratios, coverage, curing times, and compliance standards.

**Q27:** What is the SANS standard for tiling in South Africa?
**A:** SANS 10107 is the code of practice for tiling. It covers substrate preparation, adhesive selection, installation methods, and quality standards. SANS 1449 covers ceramic tiling and related products.

**Q28:** Can I get a product certificate of conformity?
**A:** Yes, certificates of conformity are available on request for products that carry SABS or equivalent certification. Your account manager will arrange these.

**Q29:** What is the difference between "polished" and "glazed" porcelain?
**A:** Polished porcelain is mechanically polished after firing, creating a high-gloss surface. Glazed porcelain has a glass layer applied before firing. Polished porcelain requires sealing and is more slippery when wet.

**Q30:** What is "lapato" or "semi-polished" finish?
**A:** Lapato (semi-polished) tiles have a partially polished surface that gives a soft sheen without the full gloss of polished tiles. They're less slippery than fully polished and hide scratches better.

**Q31:** Do I need to seal porcelain tiles?

**Q32:** What is the " Mohs hardness" rating of your tiles?
**A:** Most porcelain tiles rate 5-7 on the Mohs hardness scale. PEI rating is more commonly used for floor tile selection. Your account manager can provide specific Mohs ratings on request.

**Q33:** Can I get frost-resistance data for outdoor tiles?
**A:** Yes, frost-resistance ratings are available on the technical datasheet. All porcelain tiles with <0.5% water absorption are frost-resistant. Some ceramic tiles are also rated for frost — check the datasheet.

**Q34:** What is the chemical resistance of your tiles?
**A:** Porcelain tiles generally have excellent chemical resistance. Technical datasheets rate resistance to common household chemicals, acids, and alkalis. This is important for commercial kitchen and industrial applications.

**Q35:** Do you provide fire-rating certificates?
**A:** Ceramic and porcelain tiles are naturally fire-resistant (Class A/Class 0). Fire-rating certificates can be provided for commercial projects requiring fire-compliance documentation.

**Q36:** What is "biocuttura" or "monocuttura"?
**A:** These refer to Italian manufacturing processes. Monocuttura tiles are fired once with the glaze applied to the raw body. Biocuttura (double-fired) involves a first firing of the body, then glaze application and a second firing. Most modern tiles are monocuttura.

**Q37:** What is the recommended substrate flatness for tiling?
**A:** SANS 10107 requires the substrate to be flat to within 3mm over a 2-metre straight edge for tiles up to 600x600mm. For tiles larger than 600x600mm, flatness should be within 2mm over 2 metres.

**Q38:** Can I get a comparison sheet between similar products?
**A:** Yes, your account manager can provide a side-by-side comparison of similar products showing technical specifications, origin, price, and recommended applications.

**Q39:** What is the "Crazing Resistance" rating?
**A:** Crazing resistance indicates how well a glazed tile resists fine surface cracking (crazing) under stress. Our glazed tiles meet SANS requirements for crazing resistance. Full-body porcelain doesn't craze as there's no glaze layer.

**Q40:** Do your tiles contain any harmful substances (lead, etc.)?
**A:** Our tiles comply with international standards for chemical safety. Lead-free glazes are standard. Full material safety datasheets (MSDS) are available on request.

**Q41:** What is the difference between "through-body" and "glazed" porcelain?
**A:** Through-body (full-body/unibody) porcelain has the same colour and pattern throughout the entire thickness. Glazed porcelain has a surface glaze layer over a different-coloured body. Chips on through-body tiles are less visible.

**Q42:** Can I get thermal expansion data for outdoor tiling?
**A:** Yes, thermal expansion coefficients are available on technical datasheets. Expansion joints must be designed to accommodate thermal movement — your account manager can advise on spacing for your climate zone.

**Q43:** What is the recommended slope for a shower floor?
**A:** SANS 10107 recommends a minimum fall of 1:50 to 1:100 (1-2%) toward the shower drain for effective water drainage. Pre-formed shower trays can simplify this.

**Q44:** Do you provide installation guides?
**A:** Yes, product-specific installation guides are available at the trade counter and via WhatsApp. These cover substrate prep, adhesive selection, fixing methods, and grouting.

**Q45:** What is "lippage" and how do I avoid it?
**A:** Lippage is the difference in height between adjacent tiles. It's caused by substrate unevenness, inconsistent adhesive bed, or mixing calibres. Use a levelling system for large-format tiles and ensure substrate flatness.

**Q46:** Can I get a warranty document for tiles I'm installing?
**A:** Manufacturer warranties are available for most products. These cover manufacturing defects when installed per SANS 10107. Your account manager provides warranty documents on request.

**Q47:** What does "rectified" mean for mosaic sheets?
**A:** Most mosaic sheets are mounted on mesh or paper backing. "Jointless" mosaic designs exist where individual pieces butt together. Standard mosaic sheets require grouting between individual tesserae.

**Q48:** How do I read the batch information on the box?
**A:** The box shows: product code, description, size, quantity per box, shade/tone number, calibre, date of manufacture, country of origin, and SANS/ISO compliance marks. Always check shade and calibre before installing.

**Q49:** Do you have products specifically for industrial applications?
**A:** Yes, our commercial/industrial range includes heavy-duty porcelain (PEI V), anti-slip industrial tiles, and chemical-resistant options. Your account manager can recommend suitable products.

**Q50:** What is the impact resistance of porcelain tiles?
**A:** Porcelain tiles have high impact resistance due to their density and low porosity. Specific impact resistance data (measured in Newtons) is available on technical datasheets for commercial specifications.

**Q51:** Can I use tiles with underfloor heating?
**A:** Yes, all our porcelain and ceramic tiles are compatible with underfloor heating. Ensure the adhesive and grout are also rated for underfloor heating (thermal cycling). We supply compatible systems.

**Q52:** What is the maximum temperature for underfloor heating under tiles?
**A:** Underfloor heating systems typically operate at 25-29°C floor surface temperature. The thermostat should be limited to 29°C floor temperature to prevent thermal stress on the tile and adhesive.

**Q54:** What is the "rectified" tolerance for your tiles?
**A:** Rectified tiles have dimensional tolerances of ±0.5mm on length and width, and ±0.3mm on thickness. This precision allows for minimal grout joints. Actual tolerances are shown on each product's datasheet.

## Category 8: Tile-Specific Contractor Queries (55 Q&As)

**Q1:** Should I recommend porcelain or ceramic for a residential bathroom floor?
**A:** Porcelain is recommended for bathroom floors due to its low water absorption (<0.5%), higher durability, and better slip resistance. Ceramic wall tiles are fine for bathroom walls but not recommended for floors.

**Q2:** What size tile is best for a small bathroom?
**A:** 600x600mm or 800x800mm tiles make small bathrooms appear larger due to fewer grout joints. Avoid small tiles in compact spaces as they create a busy look and more grout lines to clean.

**Q3:** Can I use 1200x600mm tiles in a standard residential home?
**A:** Yes, 1200x600mm is increasingly popular in residential homes. Ensure your substrate is very flat (2mm over 2m), use a levelling system, and allow for the higher adhesive cost (5-7kg/m²).

**Q4:** What tile size should I use for kitchen floors?
**A:** 600x600mm is the standard for kitchen floors. 800x800mm or larger creates a more premium look. Consider the client's budget, kitchen size, and substrate condition when recommending.

**Q5:** Are wood-look tiles practical for homes?
**A:** Yes, wood-look porcelain is extremely popular. It offers the warmth of wood with the durability of porcelain. Available in plank formats (150x900mm, 200x1200mm) with realistic textures. Perfect for wet areas where wood can't be used.

**Q6:** What tile do you recommend for a rental property?
**A:** For rental properties, recommend PEI IV-rated porcelain in neutral colours (grey, beige). These withstand tenant wear, are easy to maintain, and appeal to a broad market. Avoid polished finishes in rentals.

**Q7:** Can I use the same tile indoors and outdoors?
**A:** Yes, if it's a porcelain tile with <0.5% water absorption (frost-resistant) and R10+ slip rating. Many of our porcelain ranges are suitable for both applications. Check the technical data to confirm.

**Q8:** What is the best tile for a patio or veranda?
**A:** Outdoor tiles should be frost-resistant porcelain with R11 slip rating minimum. Textured or structured finishes provide better grip. Avoid polished tiles outdoors. Consider 600x600mm or 800x800mm for clean lines.

**Q9:** How do I handle large-format tiles without breaking them?
**A:** Use a large-format tile cutter (1200mm+ bed) or a wet saw. Handle with suction cups. Score once firmly — don't score multiple times. Support the full tile when cutting and during transport.

**Q10:** What is the best tile for a swimming pool surround?
