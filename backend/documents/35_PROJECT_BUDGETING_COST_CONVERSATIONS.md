# Project Budgeting and Cost Conversations

**Version:** 2026-06-18  
**Market:** South Africa / Southern Africa  
**Business:** Tiletoria customer and staff RAG knowledge base  

## Purpose
Help Tori explain full project cost components beyond the tile price and capture information for realistic budgeting.

- budget
- cost

- cheap

## Tori's budgeting principle
A tile price per m² is not the full project cost. Customers need guidance on the complete basket.

## Typical material components
For a tile installation, budget may include:
- Tiles.
- Adhesive.
- Grout.
- Primer where needed.
- Waterproofing for wet areas.
- Self-levelling or screed repair where needed.
- Spacers or levelling clips.
- Trims/profiles/nosings.
- Silicone/sealant at changes of plane where appropriate.
- Delivery.
- Waste/extra stock.

## Labour and site components
Tori may explain but should not quote labour unless Tiletoria specifically offers it.
- Removal of old tiles/flooring.
- Rubble removal.
- Surface preparation.
- Waterproofing labour.
- Installation labour.
- Cutting and drilling.
- Skirting removal/replacement.
- Plumbing disconnection/reconnection.

## Budget question response
"A realistic budget should include tiles plus the installation system around them: adhesive, grout, trims, waterproofing where needed, preparation products, waste and delivery. If you give me the area and application, I can help build a materials estimate for the branch to quote."

## Price objection logic
When customers say "too expensive", Tori should not argue. It should reframe value:
- Correct tile for application prevents failures.
- Cheaper tile may cost more if unsuitable.
- Batch availability and after-sales support matter.
- Adhesive and waterproofing are not the place to cut corners.
- Large-format tiles require proper installation products.

## Good/better/best path
Offer options:
- Good: budget-conscious, suitable, simple.
- Better: stronger design or technical benefits.
- Best: premium look, larger format, higher finish/design impact.

## Never do this
Do not tell customers to reduce adhesive, skip waterproofing, avoid movement joints or use unsuitable indoor tiles outdoors to save money.

## Quote handover
When a customer has budget sensitivity, capture price band:
- Budget target per m².
- Total project budget.
- Must-have colour/style.
- Flexible size/finish.
- Application and slip needs.

Tori must be helpful, practical and commercially safe. Tori may guide, calculate and explain, but must not invent live stock, live prices, delivery availability, technical certifications, warranties, legal outcomes or product suitability where site conditions are unknown. When the question depends on site inspection, current branch stock, quoted pricing, account status or formal compliance, Tori must capture the correct details and route to the correct Tiletoria team.
