# Core Design Principles

## 1. Start With Application

The room controls the product choice.

- Bathroom: wet area, cleaning, slip, grout, waterproofing.
- Kitchen: cleaning, spills, cabinetry.
- Living area: flow, light, furniture, maintenance.
- Patio: drainage, slip, outdoor system.
- Commercial: traffic, cleaning, safety.

## 2. Choose the Mood

Common Tiletoria-friendly style directions:

- Light calm.
- Warm natural.
- Contemporary stone.
- Modern cement.
- Cape coastal.
- Hotel bathroom.
- Industrial loft.
- Soft luxury.
- Bold contrast.
- Family practical.
- Rental neutral.
- Developer standard.

## 3. Control Contrast

Too much contrast can make small rooms busy.

Grout contrast changes the final look.

Safe answer:

> Matching grout gives a softer seamless look. Contrasting grout highlights the tile shape and joints.

## 4. Consider Light

Tiles look different under:

- Showroom lighting.
- Natural light.
- Warm LED.
- Cool LED.
- Small bathrooms.
- South-facing rooms.
- Outdoor daylight.

Safe answer:

> Always view a sample in your own light before making the final decision, especially with warm neutrals, greys and marble-look tiles.

## 5. Think Maintenance

Beautiful products must still be practical.

Check:

- Texture.
- Grout colour.
- Cleaning.
- Limescale.
- Outdoor dirt.
- Children/pets.
- Rental use.
- Coastal conditions.

# Style Direction: Light Calm

## Use For

- Small bathrooms.
- Apartments.
- Minimal spaces.
- Coastal homes.
- Soft modern renovations.

## Guidance

Use light stone-look, soft concrete-look or warm off-white tiles.

Pair with:

- Similar grout.
- Chrome or brushed metal.
- Natural wood vanity.
- Soft lighting.

## Caution

Very white floors can show dirt and hair.

---

# Style Direction: Warm Natural

## Use For

- Family homes.
- Renovations.
- Winelands feel.
- Calm contemporary interiors.

## Guidance

Use beige, sand, greige, limestone-look or warm stone-look tiles.

Pair with:

- Warm grout.
- Timber tones.
- Brushed brass or chrome.
- Soft green or terracotta accents.

## Caution

Warm tones must be checked in real light; some can look pink or yellow depending on lighting.

---

# Style Direction: Bold Modern

## Use For

- Guest bathrooms.
- Feature walls.
- Modern apartments.
- Designer spaces.

## Guidance

Use charcoal, black, strong stone, dramatic marble or contrast finishes.

Pair with:

- Controlled lighting.
- Simple sanitaryware.
- One metal finish.
- Practical grout.

## Caution

Dark tiles show dust, water marks and limescale more than customers expect.

---

# Style Direction: Cape Coastal

## Use For

- Coastal homes.
- Holiday apartments.
- Paternoster / West Coast style.
- Relaxed bathrooms and patios.

## Guidance

Use soft stone, warm white, sand, greige, muted blues/greens and natural textures.

Pair with:

- Easy-care finishes.
- Good ventilation.
- Corrosion-aware tapware.
- Practical outdoor cleaning plan.

## Caution

Salt air and moisture affect metal finishes and maintenance.

---

# Style Direction: Hotel Bathroom

## Use For

- Premium renovations.
- Main en-suites.
- Spec Lab clients.
- Designer projects.

## Guidance

Use large-format porcelain, calm grout, elegant tapware and good lighting.

Pair with:

- Niche planning.
- Linear drains where appropriate.
- Quality waterproofing.
- Consistent metal finish.
- Good mirrors and lighting.

## Caution

Hotel-look bathrooms can become expensive because layout, waterproofing, plumbing, large-format installation and trims must be done well.

# Architect / Specifier Support

- Product data.
- Application suitability.
- Slip-risk caution.
- Wet-area system thinking.
- Movement joints.
- Maintenance assumptions.
- Substitution controls.
- Formal handover.

## Safe Answer

> For specification work, Tiletoria can help with product-specific data and available technical information, but final project suitability should be confirmed by the responsible professional team.
