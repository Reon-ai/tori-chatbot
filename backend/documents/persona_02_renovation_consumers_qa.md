Materials spend R80k-R400k, all-in budgets R600k-R1.2m. Professional installers engaged. Expects white-glove service, private consultation, sample loans, and design credibility. ## Category 3: Aesthetic Selection & Coordination

**Q131:** How do I choose between warm and cool tones for my tiles?
**A:** Your choice depends on several factors: the natural light in your space (north-facing rooms suit warm tones), existing finishes like cabinetry and countertops, and the overall mood you want to create. Our design advisors can assess your space and recommend the optimal colour temperature.

**Q132:** Will you help me match tiles to my existing floor tiles?
**A:** Yes, matching to existing finishes is one of our specialties. Bring a sample or photograph of your existing tiles to the consultation, and our advisor will identify the closest matches from our current collections.

**Q133:** How do I coordinate wall tiles with floor tiles in a bathroom?

## Category 4: Technical Specifications

**Q196:** What is a PEI rating and why does it matter?
**A:** PEI (Porcelain Enamel Institute) ratings indicate a tile's resistance to abrasion and wear. PEI 1 is for walls only; PEI 2 for light-traffic floors; PEI 3 for residential floors; PEI 4 for commercial/light industrial; PEI 5 for heavy commercial. For home renovations, PEI 3-4 is generally recommended.

**Q197:** What slip resistance rating do I need for bathroom floors?
**A:** For bathroom floors, we recommend R10 or R11 slip resistance ratings. For shower floors and wet room areas, R11 or R12 provides additional safety. Our advisors will identify the appropriate rating for each application.

**Q198:** What does R9, R10, R11, R12 mean for slip resistance?
**A:** These are DIN-rated slip resistance classifications: R9 is suitable for dry interior areas; R10 for areas with occasional wetting (bathrooms, kitchens); R11 for wet areas (showers, pool surrounds); R12 for commercial wet areas and ramps.

**Q199:** What is a rectified edge and do I need it?
**A:** Rectified tiles are mechanically trimmed after firing for precise, uniform dimensions and perfect 90-degree edges. They allow for minimal grout joints (1.5-2mm) and a seamless, modern look. Rectified tiles cost more but deliver a superior finish — ideal for premium renovations.

**Q200:** What is water absorption and why does it matter?
**A:** Water absorption measures how much water a tile absorbs. Porcelain absorbs less than 0.5% (often <0.05%), making it virtually waterproof. Ceramic absorbs 3-10%, suitable for walls and light-use floors. For bathrooms and wet areas, porcelain is essential.

**Q201:** What is the difference between glazed and full-body porcelain?
**A:** Glazed porcelain has a printed, glass-like surface layer over a porcelain body. Full-body (through-body) porcelain has consistent colour and composition throughout, so chips or wear are less visible. Full-body is preferred for high-traffic commercial areas.

**Q202:** What does SANS 1449 mean for tiles?
**A:** SANS 1449 is the South African National Standard for ceramic tiles. It specifies requirements for dimensions, surface quality, strength, water absorption, and resistance to chemicals and staining. All tiles sold by Tiletoria meet or exceed SANS 1449.

**Q203:** Do your tiles comply with NHBRC requirements?
**A:** Yes, all our tiles and associated waterproofing and installation systems comply with NHBRC (National Home Builders Registration Council) requirements. We can provide technical data sheets and compliance certificates for your project records.

**Q204:** What is breaking strength and what should I look for?
**A:** Breaking strength measures the load a tile can withstand before breaking. For floor tiles, look for breaking strength above 1,300N. Our porcelain tiles typically exceed 2,000N, making them suitable for all residential and commercial applications.

**Q205:** Are your tiles frost-resistant for outdoor use?
**A:** Yes, our outdoor-rated porcelain tiles are frost-resistant. Porcelain's extremely low water absorption means it won't crack in freeze-thaw cycles. This makes it ideal for patios, pool surrounds, and outdoor entertainment areas throughout South Africa.

**Q206:** What is the Mohs hardness scale rating for your tiles?
**A:** Porcelain tiles typically rate 6-8 on the Mohs hardness scale, meaning they resist scratching from most household materials. Ceramic tiles rate 4-6. For comparison, diamonds rate 10 and steel knives rate 5.5.

**Q207:** What is chemical resistance and do I need to worry about it?
**A:** Chemical resistance measures a tile's ability to withstand exposure to cleaning chemicals, acids, and alkalis. Our glazed porcelain tiles offer excellent chemical resistance (Class A or B). This is particularly important for kitchens, bathrooms, and commercial spaces.

**Q208:** What does "shade variation" mean on tile labels?

**Q209:** What is the difference between ceramic and porcelain tiles?
**A:** Porcelain is denser, harder, and less porous than ceramic because it's fired at higher temperatures and made from finer clay. Porcelain is suitable for floors, walls, and outdoor use. Ceramic is generally lighter, easier to cut, and more economical — suitable for walls and light-traffic floors.

**Q210:** What tile thickness should I choose?
**A:** Standard wall tiles are 8-10mm thick. Standard floor tiles are 9-11mm. Outdoor pavers are 20mm thick for pedestal installation. Large-format porcelain slabs can be 6mm (for walls) or 12mm (for floors). Your tiler will advise on the right thickness for your application.

**Q379:** Is NHBRC documentation required for my renovation?
**A:** NHBRC documentation is required for new home construction and major alterations. For bathroom and kitchen renovations in existing homes, your tiler should still follow NHBRC guidelines for waterproofing and installation, though formal registration may not be required.

**Q380:** What substrate preparation is required before tiling?
**A:** Substrates must be structurally sound, level (maximum 3mm deviation over 2m), clean, dry, and free of contaminants. New screeds require 28 days curing. Existing tiles must be assessed for adhesion. Proper preparation is critical for a successful installation.

**Q381:** Do large-format tiles require special installation techniques?
**A:** Yes, large-format tiles require back-buttering (applying adhesive to both substrate and tile), specialised lifting equipment, perfectly flat substrates, and adhesives with extended open time. Only experienced tilers should attempt large-format installation.

**Q382:** What is back-buttering and why is it important?
**A:** Back-buttering is applying adhesive to the back of the tile as well as the substrate. This ensures 100% adhesive coverage, eliminating voids that can cause cracking. It is essential for all large-format tiles (600x600mm and above).

**Q383:** Can tiles be installed over existing floor tiles?
**A:** Yes, provided the existing tiles are soundly bonded, level, and properly primed. A flexible, polymer-modified adhesive must be used. The existing tiles must be cleaned and abraded, and a suitable primer applied. Your tiler should assess the existing installation first.

**Q384:** What is the minimum drying time before tiles can be grouted?
**A:** Standard adhesive requires at least 24 hours curing before grouting. Rapid-set adhesives may allow grouting in 3-6 hours. Large-format tiles and wet areas should cure for 48 hours before grouting.

**Q385:** Do you provide installation training for tilers?
**A:** We periodically host installer training sessions and product workshops in partnership with adhesive and tool manufacturers. Contact our technical team for information on upcoming training events.

**Q386:** What expansion joints are required in a tiled floor?
**A:** Perimeter joints (minimum 5mm) are required where tiles meet walls. Intermediate expansion joints should be placed every 8-10 metres in large areas and across doorways. All joints must be filled with flexible silicone or compressible joint filler.

**Q387:** Can your technical team visit my site?
**A:** For substantial projects, our technical team can arrange site visits to assess substrate conditions, discuss installation methodology, and provide on-site guidance. A call-out fee may apply, credited against large orders.

**Q388:** What is the correct way to install mosaic tiles?
**A:** Mosaics on mesh backing should be installed using a fine-notched trowel and white adhesive (to prevent colour bleeding through translucent mosaics). Ensure the substrate is perfectly flat, as mosaics will telegraph any irregularities.

**Q389:** Do you supply tile cutting and drilling accessories?
