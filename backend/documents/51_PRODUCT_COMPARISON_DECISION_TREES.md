## Tile vs LVT
Ask:
1. Is the area wet, outdoor or shower? If yes, LVT is not the default answer; check suitability carefully.
2. Is the customer after warmth/comfort and fast renovation? Glue-down LVT may be considered for suitable indoor dry areas.
3. Is the surface uneven/damp? Resolve before LVT.
4. Is durability/water/heat/outdoor required? Porcelain tile is often stronger, but exact product must match application.

## Porcelain vs ceramic
- Porcelain: generally denser and commonly used for floors, higher traffic and many modern formats.
- Ceramic: often cost-effective and widely used for walls and some floors depending on product.
- Do not assume every ceramic is floor-suitable or every porcelain is outdoor-suitable.

## Matte vs polished
- Matte: practical, softer reflection, often better for everyday floors.
- Polished/glossy: premium look but may show marks and may be more slippery when wet.
- Wet/slip areas: confirm suitability; do not recommend polished just for appearance.

## Rectified vs non-rectified
- Rectified: mechanically finished edges, often used with narrower grout joints when installed correctly.
- Non-rectified: slightly more variation; often uses wider grout joints.
- Tori should not promise "no grout". Tiles need grout joints.

## Indoor vs outdoor
Ask:
- Exposure to rain?
- Slip risk?
- Sun/thermal movement?
- Drainage/fall?
- Substrate/waterproofing?

## Adhesive decision tree
Ask:
- Tile type and size.
- Floor or wall.
- Indoor/outdoor/wet.
- Substrate.
- Large format?
- Time/traffic requirements.
Then route to product-specific recommendation.

## Grout decision tree
Ask:
- Joint width.
- Tile colour.
- Floor/wall/wet area.
- Maintenance preference.
- Desired contrast.

## Trim decision tree
Ask:
- Edge, corner, stair, transition or expansion detail?
- Tile thickness.
- Finish colour.
- Indoor/outdoor/coastal exposure?

## Sanitaryware decision tree
Ask:
- New build or renovation.
- Existing plumbing positions.
- Space available.
- Product style.
- Technical sheet needed.
