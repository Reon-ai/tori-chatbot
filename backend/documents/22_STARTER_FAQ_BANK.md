# Starter FAQ Bank

## Purpose

This is a starter answer bank for common questions.

---

# Q: How many tiles do I need?

A: Measure the length and width of the area in metres, multiply them to get m², then add wastage. A simple layout usually needs about 10% wastage. More complex layouts, large-format tiles, bathrooms with many cuts or diagonal patterns may need more. Final quantities should be rounded up to full boxes and checked by the branch and installer.

---

# Q: How many m² is a 700 mm x 1400 mm tile?

A: A 700 mm × 1400 mm tile is 0.7 m × 1.4 m = 0.98 m² per tile. If there are 2 tiles per box, the box covers 1.96 m².

---

# Q: What is the difference between ceramic and porcelain?

A: Porcelain is generally denser and less absorbent than standard ceramic, so it is often used for floors and higher-wear areas. Ceramic can be excellent for walls and some floors if the product is rated for that use. Always check the specific product before buying.

---

# Q: What does rectified mean?

A: Rectified tiles have mechanically finished edges, which can create a cleaner modern look and often allow narrower grout joints. They still need grout joints and a flat surface.

---

# Q: What does V-rating mean?

A: V-rating describes shade and pattern variation. V1 is uniform, V2 is slight variation, V3 is moderate variation and V4 is substantial/random variation. V3 and V4 tiles should be viewed and blended from multiple boxes before installation.

---

# Q: Can I install tiles without grout joints?

A: No. Tiles should not be installed hard against each other. Grout joints allow for tile tolerance, alignment and finishing.

---

# Q: Are tiles waterproof?

A: Tile surfaces can be water-resistant depending on the product, but tiles and grout are not the waterproofing system. Wet areas need proper waterproofing behind or below the tiles.

---

# Q: Can I use indoor tiles outside?

A: Not unless the product is suitable for outdoor use and the full installation system is correct. Outdoor areas need suitable tile, adhesive, grout, drainage, slope, movement joints and maintenance.

---

# Q: Are textured tiles non-slip?

A: No tile should be called slip-proof. Slip performance depends on tile surface, slope, drainage, cleaning, contaminants, footwear and site use.

---

# Q: What tile should I use for a ramp?

A: Ramps are safety-sensitive and must be professionally reviewed. Tiletoria can help with available product data, but final suitability depends on gradient, drainage, traffic, maintenance and project requirements.

---

# Q: Can I use wall tiles on floors?

A: Wall tiles should not be used on floors unless the product is specifically rated for floor use. Floor traffic can damage unsuitable wall tiles.

---

# Q: Can floor tiles go on walls?

A: Some floor tiles can go on walls, but weight, substrate, adhesive and installation method must be confirmed, especially for large-format tiles.

---

# Q: What else do I need with tiles?

A: You may need adhesive, grout, spacers, trims, primer, self-leveller, waterproofing, silicone, levelling clips and cleaning products. The exact list depends on the tile, area and substrate.

---

# Q: Do I need levelling clips?

A: Levelling clips are useful for controlling lippage, especially with large-format tiles. They do not fix an uneven substrate and do not replace skilled installation.

---

# Q: Can I tile over existing tiles?

A: It may be possible only if the existing tiles are firmly bonded, clean, sound and compatible with the correct primer/adhesive system. Loose, hollow or contaminated tiles must be addressed first.

---

# Q: Can I install glue-down LVT over tiles?

A: It may be possible only if the existing tiles are sound and the grout lines are properly levelled. Glue-down LVT needs a clean, dry, smooth, flat and sound subfloor.

---

# Q: Can I steam-clean glue-down LVT?

A: No. Do not steam-clean glue-down LVT. Use a damp mop and compatible cleaner, and avoid flooding the floor.

---

# Q: What is grout haze?

A: Grout haze is a fine residue left on the tile surface after grouting. It should be removed with the correct method for the tile and grout type. Avoid harsh acid on sensitive surfaces.

---

# Q: What must I check before installing tiles?

A: Check product code, shade, batch, calibre, finish, quantity, visible damage and box labels. Open several boxes and view the tiles together. If anything looks wrong, stop before installation.

---

# Q: I have a complaint. What must I send?

A: Please send your invoice, product name/code, box labels, batch/shade/calibre details, clear photos, date installed, branch purchased from and whether the product is installed or still boxed. The cause needs to be assessed before any outcome is confirmed.
