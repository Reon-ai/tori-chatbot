## Bathroom Floor Tile Selection

Summary: Bathroom floor tiles must be selected for floor use, water exposure, cleanability, and practical surface grip.

Question ID: CAT05-Q001
Question: What tile should I choose for a bathroom floor?
Answer: Choose a floor-rated tile with a finish suitable for wet domestic use, usually a matt or lightly textured porcelain or ceramic floor tile. Confirm product specification, slip-resistance information, grout joint, drainage, and installation method before purchase.

Question ID: CAT05-Q002
Question: Can I use any floor tile in a bathroom?
Answer: No. The tile must be suitable for floor use and practical for water exposure. Highly polished or very smooth tiles can become slippery when wet and should be selected carefully.

Question ID: CAT05-Q003
Question: Are porcelain tiles good for bathroom floors?
Answer: Yes, suitable porcelain tiles are commonly used for bathroom floors because they are dense, durable, and have very low water absorption. The finish and wet-area suitability still need to be checked.

Question ID: CAT05-Q004
Question: Are ceramic tiles good for bathroom floors?
Answer: Ceramic floor tiles can be suitable when rated for floor use and appropriate for wet domestic conditions. Wall-only ceramic tiles should not be used on bathroom floors.

Question ID: CAT05-Q005
Question: Should bathroom floor tiles be matt or gloss?
Answer: Matt or lightly textured tiles are generally more practical for bathroom floors. Gloss or polished floor tiles may be slippery when wet and may show marks more easily.

Question ID: CAT05-Q006
Question: What tile colour works best for bathrooms?
Answer: Light neutrals can make bathrooms feel larger and brighter, while darker tones can look dramatic but may show water marks, soap residue, or dust more easily.

Question ID: CAT05-Q007
Question: What tile size is best for a bathroom floor?
Answer: The best size depends on layout, falls, drain position, and cuts. Larger tiles reduce grout lines but need flatter substrates; smaller tiles can work better in sloped shower areas.

Question ID: CAT05-Q008
Question: Can large-format tiles be used in bathrooms?
Answer: Yes, if the substrate is flat enough, the layout is planned, and the correct adhesive and fixing method are used. They may not suit small shower floors with complex falls.

Question ID: CAT05-Q009
Question: Are small tiles better for wet bathroom floors?
Answer: Small tiles can help on sloped shower floors because they follow falls more easily. They still need correct waterproofing, adhesive, grout, and drainage.

Question ID: CAT05-Q010
Question: Should the same tile be used on bathroom floors and walls?
Answer: It can be done if the tile is suitable for both surfaces. Check tile weight, wall condition, adhesive, and installation method before using floor tiles on walls.


## Shower Floor Tile Selection

Summary: Shower floor tiles require wet barefoot suitability, safe drainage design, and conservative slip-resistance wording.

Question ID: CAT05-Q011
Question: What tile should I use on a shower floor?
Answer: Use a tile suitable for wet floor use, able to follow the required fall to the drain, and practical to clean. Smaller tiles or mosaics are often used on shower floors.

Question ID: CAT05-Q012
Question: Can I use polished porcelain on a shower floor?
Answer: Polished porcelain is generally not recommended for shower floors because it can be slippery when wet. Confirm a suitable wet-area surface before purchase.

Question ID: CAT05-Q013
Question: Can I use large tiles on a shower floor?
Answer: Large tiles can be difficult because the floor must slope to the drain. They may work with a linear drain, but the designer and installer must confirm falls, cuts, drainage, and surface suitability.

Question ID: CAT05-Q014
Question: Are mosaics good for shower floors?
Answer: Mosaics are commonly used because they can follow slopes and provide many grout joints. They must still be floor-rated and suitable for wet use.

Question ID: CAT05-Q015
Question: Are all mosaics suitable for shower floors?
Answer: No. Some mosaics are decorative wall products only. Shower-floor mosaics must be suitable for floors, wet conditions, backing compatibility, adhesive, and grout.

Question ID: CAT05-Q016
Question: What slip rating is needed for a shower floor?
Answer: There is no single rating for every shower. Shower floors should be assessed as wet barefoot areas, considering product surface, drainage, slope, cleaning, and user needs.

Question ID: CAT05-Q017
Question: Can a shower floor tile be guaranteed non-slip?
Answer: No. No tile should be called non-slip or slip-proof. Slip risk depends on water, soap, slope, cleaning residue, bare feet, user behaviour, and site conditions.

Question ID: CAT05-Q018
Question: Why do shower floors need a fall?
Answer: A fall allows water to drain away instead of pooling. Pooling water increases slip risk, staining, soap build-up, mould risk, and possible water-related failures.

Question ID: CAT05-Q019
Question: Who decides the fall on a shower floor?
Answer: The fall should be designed and installed by the builder, plumber, designer, or qualified installer according to the drainage system and applicable requirements.

Question ID: CAT05-Q020
Question: Can a flat tile be used in a walk-in shower?
Answer: It depends on tile size, drain type, slip suitability, and whether the floor can be formed with correct falls. Large flat tiles may need cutting or a linear-drain design.

Question ID: CAT05-Q032

Question ID: CAT05-Q033

Question ID: CAT05-Q035

Question ID: CAT05-Q038

Question ID: CAT05-Q039
