# Cleaning and Maintenance

## Purpose

Use this file when customers ask how to clean or maintain tiles, grout, glue-down LVT, tapware, sanitaryware and outdoor areas.

## Master Rule

Start with the mildest compatible method.

Avoid harsh chemicals unless the manufacturer instructions allow them.

---

# Porcelain and Ceramic Tiles

## Routine Cleaning

- Sweep or vacuum loose dirt.
- Damp mop with clean water or compatible mild cleaner.
- Rinse if cleaner residue remains.
- Dry where water marks are a concern.
- Avoid waxy build-up.

## Safe Answer

> For regular tile cleaning, sweep first, then damp mop with a mild compatible cleaner. Avoid leaving cleaner residue because it can make floors look dull or sticky.

---

# Grout

## Routine Cleaning

- Use mild cleaner.
- Use soft brush if needed.
- Avoid aggressive acids.
- Rinse well.
- Improve ventilation in wet areas.
- Seal grout where appropriate and product instructions require it.

## Safe Answer

> Grout can hold dirt more than the tile surface. Start with a mild cleaner and soft brush. Avoid aggressive chemicals unless confirmed safe for that grout and tile.

---

# Grout Haze

## What It Is

Grout haze is a fine residue left on the tile surface after grouting.

## Safe Answer

> Grout haze should be removed with the correct method for the tile and grout type. Do not use harsh acid on sensitive surfaces. If the haze is severe or the tile is delicate, ask Tiletoria or the grout supplier before cleaning.

---

# Polished Tiles

## Care

- Sweep grit regularly.
- Use soft mop.
- Avoid abrasive pads.
- Avoid harsh chemicals.
- Dry to reduce water marks.

## Safe Answer

> Polished tiles can show marks and fine scratches more easily. Keep grit off the floor and avoid abrasive cleaning pads.

---

# Outdoor Tiles

## Common Issues

- Dirt.
- Algae.
- Leaves.
- Mud.
- Pool residue.
- Drainage staining.

## Care

- Sweep regularly.
- Wash with compatible outdoor cleaner.
- Keep drains clear.
- Maintain falls/drainage.
- Treat algae carefully.
- Avoid slippery residues.

## Safe Answer

> Outdoor tiles need regular cleaning because algae, dust and moisture can affect slip risk. Keep drainage clear and avoid cleaners that leave a slippery film.

---

# Glue-Down LVT

## Routine Cleaning

- Sweep or vacuum.
- Damp mop only.
- Use compatible LVT cleaner.
- Wipe spills.
- Use furniture pads.
- Protect from dragging.
- Avoid flooding.

## Do Not

- Do not steam clean.
- Do not flood mop.
- Do not use harsh solvents.
- Do not use abrasive pads.
- Do not polish unless product instructions allow it.

## Safe Answer

> Do not steam-clean glue-down LVT. Use a damp mop and compatible cleaner, and avoid flooding the floor.

---

# Black or Special-Finish Tapware

## Care

- Use mild soap/water where appropriate.
- Dry with soft cloth.
- Avoid abrasive pads.
- Avoid harsh chemicals.
- Avoid acidic cleaners.
- Avoid bleach unless product instructions allow it.
- Manage limescale gently.

## Safe Answer

> Black and special-finish tapware needs gentle cleaning. Avoid abrasive pads, harsh chemicals and acidic cleaners unless the product instructions specifically allow them.

---

# Sanitaryware

## Care

- Use non-abrasive bathroom cleaner.
- Avoid dropping heavy objects.
- Avoid harsh scouring pads.
- Clean limescale with compatible products.
- Keep seat hinges and fittings dry where possible.

## Safe Answer

> Clean sanitaryware with a compatible non-abrasive cleaner and avoid harsh scouring pads that can damage surfaces or fittings.

---

# Shower Glass

## Care

- Squeegee after use.
- Use compatible glass cleaner.
- Manage limescale early.
- Improve ventilation.
- Avoid abrasive pads.

## Safe Answer

> Shower glass stays cleaner when water is removed after use. Limescale is easier to control if cleaned regularly before it builds up.

---

# Chemical Damage

## Safe Answer

> If a surface has changed colour, gone dull, etched or stained after chemical use, stop using the chemical and send photos plus the product details to Tiletoria for review.

## Escalate

Always escalate chemical damage.
