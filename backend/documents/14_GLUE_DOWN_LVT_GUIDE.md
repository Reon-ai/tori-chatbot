# Glue-Down LVT Guide

## Purpose

Use this file for all Tiletoria LVT questions.

## Critical Tiletoria Rule

Tiletoria sells **glue-down LVT**.

Tori must not answer as if Tiletoria sells click LVT unless a future product file specifically confirms a click product.

## What Glue-Down LVT Is

Glue-down LVT is luxury vinyl tile or plank that is bonded to the prepared subfloor with approved adhesive.

## Best Uses

Glue-down LVT may be suitable for many residential and commercial interiors when the product, adhesive and subfloor are correct.

Common areas:

- Bedrooms.
- Living rooms.
- Kitchens where approved.
- Offices.
- Retail interiors where product-rated.
- Renovation floors where subfloor can be made smooth and dry.

## Not Suitable Without Specialist Confirmation

Do not approve glue-down LVT for:

- Showers.
- Wet rooms.
- Damp floors.
- External areas.
- Rough screeds.
- Uneven floors.
- Floors with visible grout lines unless levelled.
- Active moisture problems.
- Subfloors with old adhesive contamination.
- Areas outside product rating.

## First Question

> What is the subfloor: screed, concrete, existing tiles, timber or another surface?

## Subfloor Rule

The subfloor must be:

- Clean.
- Dry.
- Smooth.
- Flat.
- Sound.

## Safe Answer

> Glue-down LVT requires a clean, dry, smooth, flat and sound subfloor, plus approved adhesive. If the subfloor is uneven or damp, the installation can fail or imperfections can show through.

## Existing Tiles Under LVT

Possible only if system is approved and the surface is prepared correctly.

Check:

- Existing tiles are firmly bonded.
- No hollow or loose tiles.
- Grout lines levelled/smoothed.
- Surface cleaned and primed as required.
- Moisture not trapped.
- Correct leveller/compound used.
- Manufacturer/installer approval.

Safe answer:

> Glue-down LVT over existing tiles may be possible only if the tiles are sound and the grout lines are properly levelled. The installer must confirm the system before adhesive is used.

## Moisture

Moisture is a major risk for glue-down LVT.

Safe answer:

> Do not install glue-down LVT over damp concrete or an active moisture problem. Moisture can affect adhesive and cause failure.

## Cleaning

Do:

- Sweep or vacuum.
- Damp mop.
- Use compatible pH-neutral cleaner where appropriate.
- Wipe spills.
- Use furniture protectors.
- Follow product instructions.

Do not:

- Steam clean.
- Flood mop.
- Use harsh solvents.
- Use abrasive pads.
- Use unapproved polish.
- Drag heavy furniture.

Safe answer:

> Do not steam-clean glue-down LVT. Use a damp mop and compatible cleaner, and avoid flooding the floor.

## LVT Quantity

Formula:

Required m² = measured area × (1 + wastage)

Wastage:

- Simple room: 5% to 10%
- Complex layout: 10% to 15%

Keep spares for repairs.

## Add-On Shopping List

Possible items:

- Glue-down LVT.
- Approved adhesive.
- Primer if required.
- Self-leveller/patching compound if required.
- Moisture barrier system if specified.
- Trims/transitions.
- Skirting or quarter-round.
- Roller/tooling by installer.
- Cleaner/care product.

## Stop-Work Triggers

Stop if:

- Damp subfloor.
- Rough screed.
- Loose existing tiles.
- Visible grout lines not levelled.
- Wrong adhesive.
- Product stored outside.
- No site conditioning.
- Installer treats glue-down LVT like click vinyl.
- Customer wants it in a shower.
